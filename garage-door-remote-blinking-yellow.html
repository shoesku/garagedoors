What is a Blinking Yellow Light on a Garage Door Remote?

A blinking yellow light on a garage door remote typically indicates a weak signal. This could be caused by a variety of reasons, ranging from a low battery to a malfunctioning remote. One of the most common causes of a weak signal is due to interference from other electronic devices.

What to Do When Your Garage Door Remote Starts Blinking Yellow

When your garage door remote starts blinking yellow, there are several steps you can take in order to troubleshoot the issue. First, check the batteries in the remote to make sure they are still good. If the batteries are low or dead, replace them with fresh, new ones.

Next, check the remote for any signs of damage such as scratches or cracks. If there are any signs of damage, the remote may need to be replaced. If there is no visible damage, you can try resetting the remote. To reset the remote, press and hold the “learn” button for 10 seconds until the yellow light stops blinking.

Checking for Interference from Other Electronic Devices

If the reset does not work, you can try checking for interference from other electronic devices. This is especially true if you have recently added any new electronic devices to your home. Some of the most common sources of interference include wireless routers, cell phones, and microwave ovens.

To check for interference, try turning off or unplugging these devices and then try using the garage door remote. If the yellow light stops blinking and the remote functions properly, you will know that the interference is coming from one of these devices.

Replacing the Garage Door Remote

If the interference is not coming from any of the electronic devices in your home, you may need to replace the garage door remote. If this is the case, you will need to purchase a new remote that is compatible with your garage door opener.

Once you have purchased a new remote, you will need to program it to your garage door opener. To do this, press and hold the “learn” button on the garage door opener until the yellow light begins to blink. Then, press and hold the “learn” button on the new remote for about five seconds until the yellow light on the opener stops blinking.

Conclusion

A blinking yellow light on a garage door remote typically indicates a weak signal. This can be caused by a variety of reasons, ranging from a low battery to a malfunctioning remote. If the battery is low or dead, replace it with a fresh, new one. If there is no visible damage to the remote, try resetting it by pressing and holding the “learn” button for 10 seconds. If resetting the remote does not work, check for interference from other electronic devices or replace the remote with one that is compatible with your garage door opener.
