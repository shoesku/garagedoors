How To Change The Key For Your Garage Door

Best Garage doors and references for house or residential with custom material and more design. We Share more picture design with HD quality images.

If you’re the owner of a garage door, you know how important it is to keep the doors secure. If your key is lost or stolen, it can be difficult to replace it, and if the key is copied, someone else may have access to your property. To prevent any potential security risks, it’s important to change your garage door key periodically. Here’s how to do it.

Step 1: Identify Your Garage Door Lock

The first step in changing your garage door key is to identify the type of lock your garage door has. There are two main types of locks for garage doors: cylinder locks and deadbolts. Cylinder locks are the most common type and are usually found on residential garage doors. They have a single keyhole and require a key to unlock. Deadbolts are typically found on commercial garage doors and require a key to both unlock and lock the door.

Step 2: Get the Right Key

Once you’ve identified the type of lock on your garage door, you’ll need to get the right key. If you have a cylinder lock, you can purchase a replacement key from your local hardware store. If you have a deadbolt, you’ll need to have a locksmith come out and make a new key for you. Keep in mind that both types of keys are specific to the type of lock they are used with, so you may need to purchase more than one key.

Step 3: Change the Lock

Once you have the right key, it’s time to change the lock. If you have a cylinder lock, you can do this yourself. Simply unscrew the screws on the back of the lock and remove the old key. Insert the new key and replace the screws. If you have a deadbolt lock, it’s best to have a locksmith come out and change the lock for you.

Step 4: Make Copies of the Key

Once the lock is changed, you’ll need to make copies of the key. This ensures that you have a spare key in case the original is lost or stolen. You can purchase a copy of the key from your local hardware store or have a locksmith make a copy for you. Be sure to store the spare key in a safe place.

Step 5: Test the Lock

Once you’ve completed the steps above, it’s time to test the lock. Insert the key into the lock and turn it to make sure the door unlocks and locks properly. If the lock works properly, you’ve successfully changed the key for your garage door.

Conclusion

Changing the key for your garage door is an important step in keeping your property secure. By following the steps outlined above, you can easily change the key for your garage door and make sure that only authorized people have access to your property. Make sure to store the spare key in a safe place and test the lock regularly to ensure it’s working properly.
