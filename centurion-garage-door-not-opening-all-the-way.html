Troubleshooting A Centurion Garage Door That Won't Open All The Way

Introduction

Ah, the joys of homeownership. When everything is working as it should, you can open and close your garage door with the simple push of a button. But when the door won't open all the way, it can be a major headache. If you have a Centurion garage door that won't open all the way, you're in luck. We've put together this troubleshooting guide to help you identify the problem and find a solution. Let's get started.

Common Causes of Centurion Garage Door Malfunctions

Centurion garage doors are designed to be durable and reliable, but like any mechanical device, they can malfunction from time to time. The most common causes of Centurion garage door malfunctions are:

  • Obstructions in the track
  • Dirt and debris in the track
  • Incorrectly adjusted force settings
  • Incorrectly aligned sensors
  • Worn out or broken springs
  • Worn out or broken drive chains

Inspect the Track

The first step in troubleshooting a Centurion garage door is to inspect the track. If there are any obstructions in the track, such as stones, dirt, or debris, they need to be removed. If the track is bent or damaged, it needs to be repaired or replaced. Also, make sure that the rollers in the track are in good condition and properly lubricated.

Check the Force Settings

The next step is to check the force settings. If the force settings are too high, the door may not be able to open all the way. To adjust the force settings, you'll need to consult the manual that came with your Centurion garage door. Once the force settings are properly adjusted, the door should open and close smoothly.

Align the Sensors

The sensors that are located on either side of the garage door need to be properly aligned in order for the door to open and close properly. If the sensors are not aligned, the door may not open all the way. To adjust the sensors, you'll need to consult the manual that came with your Centurion garage door.

Inspect the Springs and Chains

The springs and chains in your Centurion garage door need to be in good condition in order for the door to open and close properly. If the springs are worn out or broken, they need to be replaced. The same is true for the chains. If the chains are worn out or broken, they need to be replaced.

Test the Door

After you have inspected the track, adjusted the force settings, aligned the sensors, and inspected the springs and chains, it's time to test the door. Open and close the door several times and make sure that it opens and closes all the way. If the door opens and closes all the way, you're done. If not, you may need to call a professional.

Conclusion

We hope this troubleshooting guide has helped you identify the problem and find a solution for your Centurion garage door that won't open all the way. If the door still won't open all the way, you may need to call a professional for assistance. Good luck and happy troubleshooting!
