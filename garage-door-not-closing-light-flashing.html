Garage Door Not Closing Light Flashing - What To Do?

Garage door not closing light flashing is a common issue for many homeowners. If your garage door is not closing, the light on the opener will flash - but what do you do? In this article, we’ll discuss the most common causes of this problem, and how to fix them. We’ll also provide tips to help you prevent this issue from occurring in the future. Read on to learn more!

What Causes Garage Door Not Closing Light Flashing?

There are several common causes of a garage door not closing light flashing. The most common cause is a malfunctioning garage door opener. The opener may be damaged, or the sensors may be misaligned, causing the opener to think the door is still open. Another cause is a damaged door or broken springs, which can prevent the door from closing properly. Finally, bad weather or debris can also cause the opener to malfunction.

How to Fix Garage Door Not Closing Light Flashing

If you’re having issues with your garage door not closing light flashing, there are several steps you can take to fix the problem. The first step is to check the sensors on the opener. Make sure they are aligned correctly and that there is nothing blocking them. You should also check the tracks and rollers to make sure they are in good shape. Finally, check the springs to make sure they are not broken or damaged.

How to Prevent Garage Door Not Closing Light Flashing

To help prevent the garage door not closing light flashing, there are a few things you can do. First, make sure the opener is regularly serviced by a professional. This will help ensure the opener is functioning properly and that no issues arise. Additionally, make sure the tracks, rollers, and springs are in good condition. Finally, clear any debris or bad weather from the area around the opener to help prevent any malfunction.

Conclusion

Garage door not closing light flashing can be a nuisance, but it’s usually easy to fix. Start by checking the sensors on the opener, and then check the tracks, rollers, and springs. If all these items are in good condition, the problem is likely with the opener. To prevent this issue from occurring in the future, make sure the opener is serviced regularly and that the area around the opener is clear of debris or bad weather. With these tips, you should be able to keep your garage door functioning properly.
