The Ultimate Guide To Replacing Your Rubber Garage Door Bottom Weather Seal

Are you looking for the best way to fix the weather seal around the bottom of your garage door? If so, then you’ve come to the right place. Replacing your rubber garage door bottom weather seal is an important task that will help keep the elements out of your garage. Here, we’ll guide you through the process, step by step. In no time at all, you’ll have your garage door weatherproofed and ready to go.

Step 1: Measure for the Right Fit

Before you purchase a new weather seal, you need to measure the size of the garage door’s bottom rail. Measure the length and width of the rail and make sure to record the measurements accurately. Once you know the size you need, you can then purchase the right weather seal replacement kit.

Step 2: Remove the Old Seal

Before you can install the new seal, you need to remove the old one. To do this, you’ll need a putty knife and a pair of pliers. Use the putty knife to gently pry the old seal away from the rail. Once you’ve removed the old seal, use the pliers to pull out any nails that may remain in the rail.

Step 3: Cut the New Seal to Size

Now it’s time to cut the new seal to size. Most rubber garage door bottom weather seal replacement kits come with a piece of vinyl that needs to be cut to the size of your garage door rail. Use a measuring tape and a sharp pair of scissors to cut the vinyl to size. Make sure to measure twice and cut once to get the perfect size.

Step 4: Attach the New Seal

Once your new seal is cut to size, it’s time to attach it to the rail. Start by attaching one end of the seal to the top of the rail. Then, use a staple gun to attach the rest of the seal to the rail. Make sure to pull the seal tightly as you staple it in place.

Step 5: Secure the Seal

Once the seal is attached to the rail, you need to secure it in place. This can be done by using nails or screws. Nails are preferred, as they provide a tighter seal. Use a hammer to drive the nails into the rail, making sure the head of the nail is flush with the rail.

Step 6: Test the Seal

Once you’ve finished securing the seal, it’s time to test it out. Open and close the garage door a few times to make sure the seal is working properly. If you feel any air coming in around the seal, then you need to go back and re-secure it.

Step 7: Reattach the Garage Door

Once you’ve tested the seal and everything is working properly, it’s time to reattach the garage door. To do this, you’ll need to hook the garage door opener back up to the door. Make sure to follow the manufacturer’s instructions for reattaching the garage door.

Conclusion

Replacing the weather seal around the bottom of your garage door is an important task that will help keep the elements out of your garage. By following the steps outlined above, you should have no problem replacing your rubber garage door bottom weather seal replacement kit. Once you’ve finished the job, you can be sure that your garage door is properly sealed and ready to take on the elements.
