What are Garage Door Extension Springs?

Garage door extension springs are a type of spring system that helps to open and close a garage door. They are typically installed along the sides of the door, and they are connected to the garage door’s tracks. The springs are designed to help lift the weight of the door, making it easier for a person to open and close the door. They are usually made from metal and can last for years if properly maintained.

Garage door extension springs come in two different types. The first type is known as a torsion spring, and the second type is known as an extension spring. Torsion springs are designed to provide a more powerful lift than extension springs, but they require more maintenance and can be harder to install. Extension springs are usually less powerful, but they are easier to install and require less maintenance.

Benefits of Garage Door Extension Springs

Garage door extension springs can provide a number of benefits to homeowners. First, they make it easier to open and close the garage door. This can be especially helpful when garage doors are large and heavy. Extension springs also help to reduce wear and tear on the door, as they help to evenly distribute the weight of the door when it is opened and closed. In addition, these springs can help to reduce energy costs, as they help to keep the door tightly sealed when it is closed.

Extension springs can also help to increase the safety of a garage door. These springs can help to prevent the door from suddenly snapping open or slamming shut, which can be a hazard. This is especially important for homes with children or pets.

Tips for Installing Garage Door Extension Springs

Installing garage door extension springs can be a complicated process, so it is important to follow some basic tips. First, make sure you have all the necessary tools, including a power drill, a wrench, and a level. Additionally, make sure the area around the door is clear and free of debris. This will help to make the installation process easier.

Next, measure the size of the existing garage door and purchase extension springs that are the correct size for the door. It is important to buy the correct size to ensure the springs are able to lift the full weight of the door.

Once the springs have been purchased, it is time to begin the installation process. For torsion springs, begin by attaching the spring to the header bracket, then attach the other end of the spring to the roller bracket. For extension springs, attach one end of the spring to the roller bracket and the other end to the wall bracket.

Finally, it is important to adjust the tension on the springs to ensure they are providing the correct amount of lift for the door. This can be done by adjusting the tension rods located near the spring. Make sure to use caution when adjusting the tension, as over-tightening the rods can cause damage to the door.

Conclusion

Garage door extension springs are an important part of any garage door system. They help to make it easier to open and close the door, as well as helping to reduce wear and tear on the door. Additionally, they can provide increased safety and help to reduce energy costs. Installing extension springs can be a complicated process, so it is important to follow the tips outlined above to ensure the springs are installed correctly and are providing the correct amount of lift for the door.
