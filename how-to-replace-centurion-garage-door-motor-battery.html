How To Replace Centurion Garage Door Motor Battery

Introduction

Garage door motors are a great convenience for many homeowners. They allow you to open and close your garage door with the press of a button, instead of having to manually lift the door. However, like any other machine, garage door motors require regular maintenance and occasional repairs. One of the most common repairs is replacing the motor battery, which can be a tricky process. In this article, we will discuss the process of how to replace a Centurion garage door motor battery.

Tools Needed

Before you begin the process of replacing your Centurion garage door motor battery, you will need to gather the necessary tools. The tools you will need include a Phillips-head screwdriver, a pair of pliers, a flathead screwdriver, a new garage door motor battery, and a pair of safety glasses. Make sure you have these tools before you begin the process.

Step 1: Unplug the Garage Door Motor

The first step in replacing your Centurion garage door motor battery is to unplug the motor from the power source. This can usually be done by unplugging the power cord from the wall outlet. Make sure the power is off before proceeding to the next step.

Step 2: Remove the Cover

The next step is to remove the cover from the garage door motor. This is usually done by unscrewing the screws that are holding the cover in place. Once you have removed the screws, carefully lift the cover off of the motor. Make sure to keep track of all the screws you removed, as you will need them to reassemble the motor later.

Step 3: Locate the Battery

Once you have removed the cover from the garage door motor, you will be able to see the battery. The battery may be located in a compartment on the side of the motor or it may be attached directly to the motor itself. In either case, make sure to take note of its location.

Step 4: Disconnect the Old Battery

Once you have located the battery, the next step is to disconnect the old battery. This is usually done by using a pair of pliers to disconnect the wires that are connecting the battery to the motor. Make sure to take note of which wire is connected to which terminal, as this will make it easier to connect the new battery.

Step 5: Replace the Battery

Now that the old battery has been disconnected, you can replace it with the new one. Make sure to connect the wires to the new battery in the same way they were connected to the old one. Once the new battery has been connected, you can replace the cover of the motor and secure it with the screws you removed earlier.

Step 6: Test the Motor

Once you have replaced the battery and secured the cover, the next step is to test the motor. Connect the power cord to the wall outlet and press the button on the motor to open and close the garage door. If the motor is working properly, the garage door should open and close with no problems.

Conclusion

Replacing the battery in a Centurion garage door motor is a relatively simple process, but it requires attention to detail. Make sure you have the necessary tools and take your time when replacing the battery. If done properly, your garage door motor should be working like new in no time.
