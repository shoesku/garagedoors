How To Fix Garage Door Panels That Are Not Aligned

Best Garage doors and references for house or residential with custom material and more design. We Share more picture design with HD quality images.

If you own a garage at home, then you know how important it is to keep the garage door functioning properly. After all, it not only provides a secure entryway to your home, but can also be an important part of the overall aesthetic and curb appeal of your home. Unfortunately, over time garage door panels can become misaligned and can cause problems with the door’s operation. In this article, we’ll discuss the reasons why your garage door panels might become misaligned and what you can do to fix them.

Why Do Garage Door Panels Become Misaligned?

Garage door panels can become misaligned for a variety of reasons. One of the most common causes is because of wear and tear on the door’s hinges. Over time, hinges can become worn, bent, or even broken, which can cause the panels to become misaligned. In addition, a poorly maintained or aged garage door opener can also cause this problem, as the opener can be unable to properly lift the door and the panels may become dislodged.

How to Fix Garage Door Panels that are Not Aligned

Fortunately, there are a few steps that you can take to fix misaligned garage door panels. The first step is to make sure that all of the door’s hinges are properly lubricated and functioning correctly. If you have an aged or poorly maintained garage door opener, you may need to have it serviced or replaced. Additionally, you can also make sure that the door’s tracks are clean and free of any debris, as this can also cause misalignment of the panels.

Realign the Panels

Once you’ve completed the above steps, you’ll want to realign the panels. To do this, you’ll need to open the door and manually move the panels back into place. You may want to use a pair of pliers to make the process easier. Once the panels are back in place, you’ll want to close the door and make sure that it is functioning properly. If the door is still not working properly, you may need to contact a professional garage door repair service.

Replace the Panels

If the misalignment of your garage door panels is too severe, you may need to replace them. Generally, you’ll want to stick with the same type and size of panels as the old ones. Once you’ve purchased the new panels, you’ll need to remove the old ones and then install the new ones. It is important to ensure that the panels are properly aligned and that all of the door’s components are functioning correctly before closing the door. If you’re unsure about how to replace the panels, it’s best to contact an experienced garage door repair service.

Conclusion

Garage door panels can become misaligned for a variety of reasons, including wear and tear on the door’s hinges, an aged or poorly maintained garage door opener, or debris in the door’s tracks. Fortunately, there are a few steps that you can take to fix misaligned garage door panels, such as making sure that all of the door’s hinges are properly lubricated and functioning correctly, ensuring that the door’s tracks are clean and free of debris, and realigning the panels if necessary. If the misalignment is too severe, you may need to replace the panels. If you’re unsure about how to complete any of these steps, it’s best to contact an experienced garage door repair service.
