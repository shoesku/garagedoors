Garage door header size LVL, or laminated veneer lumber, is an important component of a garage door system. The purpose of the LVL is to provide support to the garage door, helping to ensure that the door opens and closes smoothly and safely. LVL also helps to protect the door from damage caused by wind, rain, and other weather conditions. In order to get the most benefit out of the LVL, it is important to understand the difference between the two different types of LVL and how to determine the correct size. This article will provide a brief overview of LVL and how to choose the right size for your garage door.

Types of LVL

There are two main types of LVL: the post-and-beam type and the truss type. The post-and-beam type is made up of two vertical boards that are connected by a horizontal beam. This type of LVL provides support to the garage door and helps to distribute the weight of the door more evenly. The truss type, on the other hand, is made up of two horizontal boards that are connected by a vertical beam. This type of LVL is generally more rigid than the post-and-beam type and provides more support for the door. Both types of LVL are available in standard sizes and can be customized for a specific door size.

How to Determine the Correct Size

When determining the correct size for your garage door header size LVL, the first step is to measure the width and height of your garage door. It is also important to measure the amount of space between the door and the ceiling. This measurement will help you determine the size of the LVL that you need. Once you have the measurements, you can use a chart to determine the size of the LVL that you need. It is also important to consider any additional weight that the LVL may need to support, such as a garage door opener or a door that has been modified with additional hardware.

Benefits of Using LVL

LVL is an incredibly strong and durable material that can provide a long-lasting and secure solution for your garage door system. LVL is also much lighter than traditional wood, making it easier to install and maintain. In addition, LVL is resistant to moisture and rot, which can help to extend the life of your garage door system. Finally, using LVL can help to reduce noise levels in your garage, making it a more pleasant space to work in.

The Cost of LVL

The cost of LVL varies depending on the size and type of LVL that you choose. Generally, the post-and-beam type is more expensive than the truss type. However, the cost of LVL is typically much lower than the cost of traditional wood. Additionally, the cost of installation is also lower when compared to traditional wood. This makes LVL an affordable and cost-effective solution for your garage door system.

Where to Buy LVL
