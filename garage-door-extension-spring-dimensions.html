Everything You Need To Know About Garage Door Extension Spring Dimensions

Garage door extension spring dimensions are an important factor to consider when you’re installing a new garage door or maintaining an existing one. Without the correct dimensions, the door won’t open and close properly, and could end up costing you more in the long run. This guide will cover everything you need to know about extension springs, including what they are and how to measure them.

What Are Garage Door Extension Springs?

Garage door extension springs are components of the garage door system that are responsible for providing the tension needed to open and close the door. They are placed on either side of the door and connected to a pulley system that helps with the lifting and lowering of the door. In order to work correctly, they must be the right size and tension.

What Are the Different Types of Extension Springs?

There are two main types of extension springs: standard and heavy duty. Standard springs are used on doors that are up to 7 feet high and weigh up to 150 pounds. Heavy duty springs are used on doors that are higher than 7 feet and weigh more than 150 pounds. Depending on the door size and weight, you may need to use a combination of both types of springs.

How to Measure Garage Door Extension Spring Dimensions

Measuring garage door extension spring dimensions can be done easily by using a tape measure. The first step is to measure the spring from end to end, including the hooks at either end. This measurement is known as the “unloaded length” and it is important to get this right as it will affect how the door opens and closes. The next step is to measure the inside diameter of the spring, which is the distance between the two coils. This measurement will tell you what size of spring you need to purchase.

How to Install Extension Springs

Installing garage door extension springs is a job that should only be done by a professional. Attempting to install them yourself can be dangerous and could lead to further damage to your door. When installing the springs, it is important to make sure that the tension is set correctly. Too much tension could cause the door to open too quickly, while too little tension could cause the door to open slowly or not at all.

How to Replace Extension Springs

Replacing garage door extension springs is also a job best left to a professional. The process is similar to installing them, but it is important to make sure that the tension is set correctly. This is done by adjusting the springs with a wrench. Once the tension is set, the springs should be tested to make sure they are working correctly.

How to Maintain Extension Springs

Maintaining garage door extension springs involves regularly checking the tension and lubricating them. The tension should be checked about once a month to make sure it is correct and that the springs are not overstretched. The springs should also be lubricated at least twice a year, using a light machine oil or a silicone spray. This will help to keep the springs in good condition and prevent them from corroding.

How to Test Extension Springs

Testing garage door extension springs is a simple process and can be done by manually opening and closing the door. The door should open and close smoothly and with the same amount of effort each time. If the door is difficult to open or close, the tension may need to be adjusted. If the door moves too quickly or too slowly, the springs may need to be replaced.

Conclusion

Garage door extension spring dimensions are an important factor to consider when installing or maintaining a garage door. These measurements need to be accurate in order for the door to open and close properly. It is important to measure the springs correctly and to adjust the tension regularly. If you’re unsure about how to do this, it is best to leave it to a professional.
