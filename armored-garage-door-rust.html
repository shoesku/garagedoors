Armored Garage Door Rust: A Guide To Understanding And Preventing

In 2023, the issue of armored garage door rust continues to be a problem for many homeowners across the world. Rust is a form of corrosion that can occur on metal surfaces, including garage doors. As the name implies, armored garage doors are designed to be extra durable and resistant to rust, but unfortunately, they still can be affected by it. This guide will explain what causes rust, how to prevent it, and how to repair damage when it occurs.

What Causes Armored Garage Door Rust?

Rust is caused when iron and oxygen react in the presence of moisture, creating a reddish-brown oxide. This reaction is accelerated by salt, which can be found in some areas of the country. It is also no surprise that garage doors are more susceptible to rusting due to the fact that they are exposed to the elements. The combination of moisture and salt can cause the metal to corrode quickly, leading to the formation of rust.

How to Prevent Armored Garage Door Rust

Fortunately, there are several steps homeowners can take to protect their garage door from rust. One of the most important steps is to regularly clean and inspect the door. This will help to remove any dirt and debris that could cause corrosion. Additionally, homeowners should apply a protective coating, such as a sealant or rust inhibitor, to the door. This will help to prevent moisture from reaching the metal and causing rust.

How to Repair Armored Garage Door Rust

When rust does occur, it is important to take action as soon as possible. The first step is to remove any loose rust. This can be done with a wire brush or sandpaper. Once the rust has been removed, the area should be cleaned with a damp cloth or mild cleaner. Then, the area should be treated with a rust converter or rust inhibitor. Finally, apply a protective coating, such as a sealant or rust inhibitor, to the door.

Important Considerations

When dealing with armored garage door rust, it is important to note that the damage can become worse if left untreated. Additionally, it is important to remember that rust can affect the structural integrity of a garage door. If rust has caused extensive damage, it may be necessary to replace the entire door. Finally, it is important to note that rust can spread quickly, so it is important to take action as soon as possible to prevent further damage.

Conclusion

Armored garage door rust is a common problem that can affect the structural integrity of a garage door. Fortunately, there are steps homeowners can take to protect their door from rust and repair any damage that does occur. Regular inspections, cleaning, and the application of a protective coating are all important steps in preventing rust and preserving the life of an armored garage door.
