What Are Garage Door Extension Springs?

Garage door extension springs are a vital part of the functionality of a garage door. These springs are responsible for counterbalancing the weight of the door. Without these springs, the door would not be able to open and close as it should, and in some cases, it may not open at all. Extension springs are usually attached to either side of the door and run parallel to the horizontal track. In some cases, however, the springs may be attached to the wall above the door.

Types of Extension Springs

Extension springs come in a variety of shapes and sizes, depending on the size and weight of the door in question. The most common types are side-mounted, torsion and tension. Side-mounted springs are usually the easiest to install, as they attach directly to the door on either side. Torsion springs are installed on the ceiling and are responsible for much of the lifting power of the door. Lastly, tension springs attach to the wall above the door and are responsible for counterbalancing the weight of the door.

How Do Extension Springs Work?

When the door is opened, the springs are stretched. As the door closes, the springs are released, creating a counterbalancing force. This force helps to ensure that the door doesnâ€™t open too quickly or too slowly, as it would if the springs were not in place. It also helps to reduce the amount of effort needed to open and close the door.

Safety Considerations

Garage door extension springs can be dangerous if not installed properly. Due to the amount of tension the springs are under, if they are not attached correctly, they can snap and cause serious injury. Additionally, if the door is opened too quickly, the springs can come loose, leading to the door falling rapidly. For these reasons, it is important to have a professional garage door technician install and service your garage door extension springs.

How Often Should Extension Springs Be Replaced?

It is recommended that garage door extension springs be replaced every seven to ten years. This is due to the amount of tension the springs are under, which can cause them to wear over time. If the springs have been damaged or have become too worn, it is important to replace them as soon as possible, as a broken spring could lead to serious injury. If the springs seem to be working correctly, but are more than ten years old, it is recommended that they be replaced in order to avoid any potential accidents.

Where Can You Find Garage Door Extension Springs?

Garage door extension springs can be found in most home improvement stores or online. It is important to ensure that you purchase the correct type and size of spring for your door, as incorrect sizes and types can lead to potential problems. Additionally, it is important to purchase springs from a reputable dealer in order to ensure that they are of the highest quality.

It is also recommended that they be replaced every seven to ten years in order to avoid any potential problems.
