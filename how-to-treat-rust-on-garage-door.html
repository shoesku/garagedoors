How To Treat Rust On Your Garage Door

What is Rust?

Rust is a type of corrosion that occurs when iron or metal alloys, such as steel, are exposed to oxygen and moisture. This exposure causes the metal to degrade and form iron oxide, which is what we see as rust. Rust can cause serious damage to your garage door, so it’s important to know how to treat it.

What Causes Rust?

Rust is caused by a combination of oxygen and water. This combination leads to oxidation, which is the process of rust forming. Rust can occur on metal surfaces that are exposed to the elements, such as your garage door. Other causes include saltwater, acid rain, and even fertilizers.

How to Treat Rust on Your Garage Door

The first step to treating rust on your garage door is to identify the source of the rust. If the rust is coming from outside, such as from acid rain or saltwater, you will need to take steps to protect your garage door from further exposure. If the rust is coming from inside, such as from moisture or lack of maintenance, you will need to take steps to address the problem.

1. Clean the Rust

The first step to treating rust is to clean the affected area with a wire brush or steel wool. This will remove any rust that has already formed. Make sure to wear protective gloves and safety glasses. You can also use a rust-removal product such as Naval Jelly to help remove the rust.

2. Seal the Rust

Once the rust has been removed or reduced, you will need to seal the area to prevent further rust from forming. You can use a rust-inhibiting primer, or you can use a rust-preventive paint. Make sure to follow the manufacturer’s instructions when applying the product to ensure the best results.

3. Check for Leaks

If the rust is coming from the inside of your garage, you may need to check for leaks. Make sure to check for cracks or gaps in your garage door and seal them if necessary. You can also use a weatherstripping sealant to help prevent further moisture from entering the area.

4. Protect the Area

Once the rust has been treated and the area has been sealed, you will need to take steps to protect the area from further rusting. You can apply a rust-inhibiting wax or a rust-preventive paint to the affected area. This will help protect the area from further corrosion.

5. Maintain the Area

Finally, you will need to make sure you maintain the area to prevent further rusting. Make sure to inspect the area regularly for signs of rust or corrosion. You can also apply a rust-inhibiting wax or paint every few years to help protect the area from further rusting.

Conclusion

Rust can be a serious problem for your garage door and can cause serious damage if it’s not treated. You should always take steps to identify and treat rust as soon as possible. Taking the steps outlined above can help you treat and prevent rust from forming on your garage door.
