How To Fix A Garage Door Handle Easily

Introduction

If you find yourself in the position of needing to fix a garage door handle, you may be feeling a bit overwhelmed. After all, this is not something that you do every day. Fortunately, fixing a garage door handle can be relatively simple, as long as you know what you are doing. In this article, we will provide you with step-by-step instructions on how to fix a garage door handle. By following these steps, you should be able to solve your dilemma and get your garage door back in working order in no time.

Tools You Will Need

Before you begin the process of fixing your garage door handle, there are a few tools you will need to have on hand. These tools include: a Phillips head screwdriver, a flat head screwdriver, a drill, a hammer, and a pair of pliers. You may also need a new handle, depending on the extent of the damage. If you do not have these tools, you can purchase them from your local hardware store.

Step 1: Disconnect the Power

The first step you need to take when fixing a garage door handle is to disconnect the power. This will ensure that you don’t get an electric shock while you are working. To do this, simply locate the power switch on the wall and turn it off. This switch is typically located near the garage door.

Step 2: Remove the Handle

Once the power is off, you can begin the process of removing the handle. To do this, you will need to use a Phillips head screwdriver to unscrew the handle from the door. Once you have done this, you can then remove the handle and set it aside.

Step 3: Replace the Handle

If the handle is damaged beyond repair, you may need to purchase a new one from your local hardware store. Once you have the new handle, you can then use a drill to drill holes for the screws. Once the holes have been drilled, you can then use a hammer and a pair of pliers to attach the new handle to the door.

Step 4: Reattach the Handle

Once the new handle is in place, you can then use a flat head screwdriver to reattach the handle to the door. Make sure that the screws are tight and that the handle is secure. Once the handle is reattached, you can then turn the power back on.

Step 5: Test the Handle

Once the power is back on, you can then test the handle to make sure it is working properly. To do this, open and close the door several times. If the handle works properly, you have successfully fixed your garage door handle.

Conclusion

Fixing a garage door handle can be a relatively simple task, as long as you have the right tools and know what you are doing. By following these steps, you should be able to have your garage door working again in no time. However, if you do not feel comfortable carrying out this task, it is always best to call in a professional to ensure that the job is done correctly.
