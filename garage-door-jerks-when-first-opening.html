How To Fix A Garage Door That Jerks When First Opening

Introduction

A garage door is an essential part of your home. It provides security and protection for your car, tools, and other valuable possessions. But when your garage door starts to jerk when it first opens, it can be a source of frustration and worry. If your garage door jerks when first opening, it could be a sign of an underlying problem. Fortunately, there are a few steps you can take to fix this issue, and we’re here to help.

Clean the Tracks

When your garage door jerks when first opening, it could be that the tracks need a good cleaning. Over time, dirt, debris, and other residue can build up on the tracks, causing the door to jerk when it’s opened. To clean the tracks, use a dry cloth and a mild household cleaner to remove any residue. If your tracks are still dirty after you clean them, you may need to use a wire brush to scrub away any remaining dirt.

Check the Springs

Another possible cause of jerking when opening your garage door is an issue with the springs. The springs are responsible for providing the power needed to open and close the door. If the springs are worn or damaged, they can cause the door to jerk when first opening. To check the springs, look for any signs of rust or wear-and-tear. If the springs are in good condition, you may need to adjust them to provide the proper tension.

Lubricate the Moving Parts

Lack of lubrication can also be a cause of jerking when opening your garage door. Over time, the moving parts can become dry and brittle, leading to jerking when the door is opened. To help reduce this, use a lubricant such as WD-40 on the tracks and other moving parts. This will help reduce friction and help keep your garage door running smoothly.

Check the Motor

If you have an electric garage door opener, the motor could be the cause of the jerking when opening. To check the motor, unplug the garage door opener and then plug it back in. If the motor doesn’t start, it could be the cause of the jerking. If this is the case, you may need to replace the motor.

Check the Balance

If your garage door jerks when first opening, it could be that the door is not properly balanced. If the door is off-balance, it can cause the door to jerk when opening. To check the balance, close the door halfway and then let go. If the door doesn’t stay in place, it’s likely that the door is off-balance and needs to be adjusted.

Check the Rollers

Another possible cause of jerking when opening your garage door is a problem with the rollers. The rollers are responsible for helping the door open and close smoothly. If the rollers are worn or damaged, they can cause the door to jerk when it’s opened. To check the rollers, look for any signs of wear-and-tear. If the rollers are in good condition, you may need to adjust them to provide the proper tension.

Conclusion

If your garage door jerks when first opening, it could be a sign of an underlying problem. Fortunately, there are a few steps you can take to fix this issue. Clean the tracks, check the springs, lubricate the moving parts, check the motor, check the balance, and check the rollers. Taking these steps can help you fix the issue and keep your garage door running smoothly.
