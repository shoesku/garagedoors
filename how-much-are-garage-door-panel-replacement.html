Everything You Need To Know About Garage Door Panel Replacement Costs

If you’ve been noticing your garage door isn’t operating as it should, it might be time to consider replacing the garage door panels. This can be a great way to give your garage door a facelift and improve its performance and security. However, it’s important to understand the costs associated with such a project before you begin. In this article, we’ll discuss what you need to know about garage door panel replacement costs.

Garage Door Panel Replacement Costs

Garage door panel replacement can be a great way to improve the look of your garage and increase its security. It can also help to improve the performance of your garage door, as well. However, it’s important to understand the costs associated with such a project before you begin.

The cost of a garage door panel replacement can vary depending on the type of door you have, the size of the panel, and the material used. Generally speaking, the cost for a single panel replacement can range from around $150 to $400, with most homeowners spending around $300 for a standard-sized panel.

In addition to the cost of the panel itself, you’ll also need to factor in the cost of labor. Depending on the complexity of the project, the cost of labor can range from around $100 to $300. Therefore, the total cost of a single garage door panel replacement can range from around $250 to $700.

Factors That Can Influence the Cost of a Garage Door Panel Replacement

The cost of a garage door panel replacement can vary depending on a few factors. Here are some of the most common factors that can influence the cost of the project:

Type of Door

The type of garage door you have can have a big impact on the cost of a panel replacement. If you have a standard roll-up door, the cost of a panel replacement will generally be much lower than if you have a more complex door, such as an overhead door.

Size of the Panel

The size of the panel you need to replace can also influence the cost of the project. Generally speaking, the larger the panel, the more expensive it will be.

Material Used

The material used for the panel can also have an impact on the cost. Generally speaking, steel panels tend to be the most affordable, while wood or composite panels tend to be more expensive.

Additional Costs to Consider

In addition to the cost of the panel itself and the cost of labor, there are a few other costs you’ll need to consider when planning your garage door panel replacement project. These additional costs include:

Hardware

You may need to purchase additional hardware to complete the project, such as hinges, handles, or locks. The cost of the hardware can vary depending on the type and quality of the hardware you choose.

Paint or Stain

If you’re replacing a wooden panel, you may need to purchase paint or stain to finish the panel. The cost of the paint or stain will depend on the type and quality you choose.

Hiring a Professional

If you’re considering a garage door panel replacement, it’s important to hire a professional. A professional can help ensure the project is completed correctly and safely. The cost of hiring a professional can range from around $100 to $300, depending on the complexity of the project.

Conclusion

Replacing a garage door panel can be a great way to improve the look of your garage and increase its security and performance. However, it’s important to understand the costs associated with such a project before you begin. The cost of a garage door panel replacement can vary depending on the type of door, the size of the panel, and the material used. In addition to the cost of the panel itself and the cost of labor, you’ll also need to factor in the cost of any additional hardware, paint, or stain you may need. Finally, it’s important to hire a professional to help ensure the project is completed correctly and safely.
