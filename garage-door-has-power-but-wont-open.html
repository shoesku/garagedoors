What To Do When Your Garage Door Has Power But Won't Open?

Best Garage doors and references for house or residential with custom material and more design. We Share more picture design with HD quality images.

Having a functional and reliable garage door is essential for the convenience of any homeowner. But when it fails to open properly, it can be a cause for frustration. Of course, when it has power but won't open, the problem can be even more perplexing. Fortunately, there are typically a few simple steps you can take to try to restore your garage door to working order.

Check the Track Alignment

One of the most common causes of a garage door becoming stuck is a misaligned track. When the track isn't aligned properly, the door may become stuck and unable to open or close. To check the alignment of the track, first inspect the rollers. If they have moved out of their original position, then the track is likely out of alignment. If this is the case, you'll need to open the door and adjust the track by loosening the bolts and brackets, and then tapping the track back into alignment. Once you've done this, you can then re-tighten the bolts and brackets.

Check the Limit Settings

The limit settings of a garage door are responsible for controlling the distance it travels when it is opening and closing. If the settings are incorrect, the door may open and close too far, or not far enough. This can cause the door to become stuck, as it will not be able to travel the full distance required to open or close completely. To check the limit settings, you'll need to locate the limit switch, which is typically located near the motor. You can then adjust the limit settings by turning the appropriate dial.

Check the Tension Spring

Another common cause of a garage door becoming stuck is a broken or damaged tension spring. This spring is responsible for applying tension to the garage door, and if it is damaged or broken, the door may not be able to open or close properly. To check the tension spring, you'll need to open the door and take a look at the spring. If it is damaged, you'll need to replace it. It is important to note that this is a job best left to a professional, as the tension spring can be dangerous to work with.

Clean the Tracks

When the tracks of a garage door become dirty or clogged, it can cause the door to become stuck. To prevent this from happening, it is important to keep the tracks clean. This can be done by regularly wiping down the tracks to remove any dirt, debris, or buildup. You can also use a lubricant to help reduce friction and improve the smoothness of the tracks.

Check the Rollers and Hinges

Another thing to check when your garage door has power but won't open is the rollers and hinges. If these components are loose, worn, or damaged, it can cause the door to become stuck. You'll need to check each roller and hinge to make sure they are in good working order. If they are not, you'll need to replace them.

Check the Motor

Finally, if none of the above steps have worked, you'll need to check the motor of your garage door. If it is not working properly, it can cause the door to become stuck. You'll need to check the power supply to the motor and make sure it is getting the correct voltage. You may also need to replace the motor if it is not working properly. Again, this is best left to a professional.

Conclusion

When your garage door has power but won't open, it can be a frustrating problem to have. Fortunately, there are a few steps you can take to try to resolve the issue. These include checking the track alignment, limit settings, tension spring, rollers and hinges, and motor. If you're still having trouble, it is best to call a professional for assistance.
