Keep The Warmth In With An Up And Over Garage Door Draught Excluder

In the winter months, it is important to keep the warmth in your home. This is especially true if you have a garage attached to your home. Too much cold air can seep through the gaps and make your home colder than you'd like. One of the best ways to keep the cold air out is with a draught excluder for your up and over garage door.

A draught excluder is a strip of material, usually foam or rubber, that you attach to the bottom of your garage door. This strip helps to prevent cold air from entering the garage and your home. It also helps to reduce energy costs as it prevents warm air from escaping. It is a great way to get the most out of your heating system.

How to Choose the Right Up and Over Garage Door Draught Excluder

When it comes to choosing the right draught excluder, there are a few things you should consider. First, you want to make sure that the material is durable and long-lasting. It should be able to withstand the elements and should be able to last for many years. You also want to make sure that the material is thick enough to do its job. A thin strip of material won't do much good.

The second thing to consider is the size of your door. You want to make sure that the draught excluder you choose is the right size for your door. For example, if you have a single garage door, you'll need a larger excluder than if you have a double door. Finally, you want to make sure the material is non-slip. This will ensure that it stays in place and does not move or shift when you open and close the door.

Installation of Your Up and Over Garage Door Draught Excluder

Once you've chosen the right draught excluder, you'll need to install it properly. The first step is to use a measuring tape to measure the perimeter of your garage door. Once you have these measurements, you can cut the excluder to size using a utility knife. Make sure to leave a few extra inches for trimming.

Next, attach the excluder to the bottom of the door. You can use adhesive tape or glue for this step. Make sure that the material is firmly attached and that there are no gaps or cracks between the excluder and the door. This will ensure that the material does its job and prevents cold air from entering the garage.

Benefits of an Up and Over Garage Door Draught Excluder

There are many benefits to having an up and over garage door draught excluder. First, as mentioned earlier, it helps to reduce energy costs by preventing warm air from escaping. It also helps to keep the garage and the rest of the house warmer, which can be especially important during the cold winter months. Additionally, it helps to reduce noise pollution, as it helps to dampen sounds coming from outside.

Lastly, an up and over garage door draught excluder can help to protect your garage door from damage. By preventing cold air from entering the garage, it helps to reduce the risk of damage caused by freezing temperatures. This can save you money in repairs and maintenance in the long run.

Conclusion

An up and over garage door draught excluder is a great way to keep the warmth in during the colder months. It helps to reduce energy costs, reduce noise pollution, and protect your garage door from damage. It is important to choose the right material, size, and installation method for your excluder in order to get the most out of it. With a bit of research and some careful consideration, you can find the perfect draught excluder for your garage door and maximize its benefits.
