Why You Should Lube Your Garage Door Regularly

Best Garage doors and references for house or residential with custom material and more design. We Share more picture design with HD quality images.

Your garage door may not get a lot of attention, but it is a crucial part of your home. It is the main access point from the outside world and it needs to be kept in good working order. To keep your garage door running smoothly, it is important to lubricate the moving parts regularly. This is especially true if you live in an area with cold weather, as the cold can cause the moving parts to seize up. Lubrication will keep your garage door in great shape, and it is a relatively easy job that you can do yourself.

What Parts of Your Garage Door Need Lubrication?

When you lubricate your garage door, there are several parts you should focus on. The first is the hinges, which are located along the sides of the garage door. These need to be lubricated with a silicone-based spray lubricant. You should also lubricate the rollers, springs, and tracks. These should be lubricated with a silicone-based lubricant as well. The last part you should lubricate is the opener chain or belt, which should be lubricated with a light-duty oil.

How Often Should You Lubricate Your Garage Door?

It is recommended that you lubricate your garage door at least twice a year. If you live in an area with extreme cold or hot temperatures, you may want to lubricate it more often. The best time to lubricate your garage door is in the spring and fall, when the temperature is more moderate. This will help ensure that your door is well lubricated and in top working condition.

What Type of Lubricant Should You Use?

When you lubricate your garage door, it is important to use the right type of lubricant. For the hinges, springs, and tracks, you should use a silicone-based lubricant spray. For the opener chain or belt, use a light-duty oil such as 3-in-1 or WD-40. You should also make sure to use a clean rag to wipe away any excess lubricant. This will help prevent dirt and debris from clogging up the moving parts.

How to Lubricate Your Garage Door

When you are ready to lubricate your garage door, first make sure you have all the necessary supplies. You will need a silicone-based lubricant spray, a light-duty oil such as 3-in-1 or WD-40, and a clean rag. Start by lubricating the hinges, then move onto the rollers, springs, and tracks. Finally, lubricate the opener chain or belt. Make sure to wipe away any excess lubricant with the clean rag.

Safety Tips

When you are lubricating your garage door, safety should always be your first priority. Make sure you are wearing protective goggles and gloves to protect yourself from any debris or lubricant that may be expelled. Do not attempt to lubricate your garage door in the dark or while alone. It is also a good idea to check the tension of the springs after lubricating to make sure they are not too tight or too loose.

Conclusion

Lubricating your garage door is an important part of keeping it in good working order. It is recommended that you lubricate your garage door at least twice a year, using a silicone-based lubricant for the hinges, rollers, springs, and tracks, and a light-duty oil such as 3-in-1 or WD-40 for the opener chain or belt. Make sure to wear protective goggles and gloves, and always check the tension of the springs after lubricating. Following these simple steps will keep your garage door running smoothly and efficiently.
