Fixing Your Garage Door Motor Not Working

Best Garage doors and references for house or residential with custom material and more design. We Share more picture design with HD quality images.

Having a garage door motor not working can be a real pain. It can be a hassle trying to figure out what is wrong and how to fix it. Luckily, there are some simple steps you can take to troubleshoot and fix your garage door motor. We’ll walk you through those steps in this article, so you can go from garage door not working to garage door working in no time!

Step 1: Check the Power Source

The first thing you’ll want to do is check the power source. Make sure the power cord is plugged in and the outlet is working. If you have an electric garage door opener, you’ll also want to check the fuse or circuit breaker to make sure it hasn’t been tripped. If it has been tripped, try resetting it and see if that fixes the problem.

Step 2: Check the Motor

Next, check the motor. Make sure it is securely attached to the garage door opener and that all of the wires are connected properly. If you see any loose wires, try reattaching them and see if that fixes the problem. If the motor looks okay, you’ll want to check the sensors. Make sure they are both clean and pointed in the right direction.

Step 3: Check the Remote Control

If the motor and sensors look okay, you’ll want to check the remote control. Make sure the batteries are fresh and that the remote is within range of the garage door opener. If it is, try pressing the button and see if the garage door responds. If it doesn’t, you’ll want to move onto the next step.

Step 4: Check the Safety Reversal System

The safety reversal system is designed to stop the garage door from closing if it senses something is blocking the path. This could be anything from a person to an animal or an object. Check to make sure the system is turned on and that the sensors are free from obstruction. If they are, try opening and closing the garage door and see if the safety reversal system is working.

Step 5: Check the Tracks

If the safety reversal system is working, you’ll want to check the tracks. Make sure they are clean and free from debris. If they are, try lubricating them with a light oil. This will help keep them running smoothly and reduce any friction that could cause the garage door to stick or not open or close properly.

Step 6: Replace the Motor

If you’ve checked all of the above and your garage door motor still isn’t working, it’s probably time to replace it. This can be a tricky process, so we recommend calling a professional to help. They will be able to find the right replacement motor for your garage door opener and install it quickly and safely.

Conclusion

The process of fixing your garage door motor not working can be tricky, but it’s not impossible. If you follow the steps outlined above, you should be able to get your garage door up and running in no time. Good luck and happy fixing!
