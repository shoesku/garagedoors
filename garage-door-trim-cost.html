What Determines The Cost Of Garage Door Trim?

Garage door trim is an important part of the overall aesthetic of your garage door. It can also be a great way to save money on your garage door installation, as it is relatively inexpensive to install. Trim is typically installed around the frame of the door, and it comes in a variety of materials and colors. The cost of trim will depend on the material and the complexity of the installation.

Types of Garage Door Trim

The most common type of trim used for garage doors is vinyl. Vinyl is relatively inexpensive, and it is easy to install. It also comes in a variety of colors, so you can find one that matches the color of your door. Other types of trim include wood, aluminum, and composite. Each type of trim has its own advantages and disadvantages, and the cost will depend on the material you choose.

Installation Costs

The cost of installing trim is also important to consider. If you are installing the trim yourself, it can be relatively inexpensive. However, if you are hiring a professional to install the trim for you, the cost will be much higher. The complexity of the installation will also have an effect on the cost, as more complex installations will require more time and materials.

Factors That Affect the Cost of Trim

The material you choose for your garage door trim will have a significant effect on the cost. Vinyl tends to be the least expensive option, while wood, aluminum, and composite will be more costly. The complexity of the installation will also affect the cost, as more complex installations will require more time and materials. Finally, the size of the trim will also affect the cost, as larger pieces will require more material.

Average Cost for Garage Door Trim

The cost of garage door trim can vary greatly depending on the type of material, the complexity of the installation, and the size of the trim. Generally speaking, vinyl trim can cost anywhere from $2 to $10 per linear foot, while wood, aluminum, and composite trim can cost anywhere from $15 to $50 per linear foot. The cost of installation will also vary depending on the complexity of the job.

Tips for Saving Money on Garage Door Trim

If you are interested in saving money on your garage door trim installation, there are a few tips that can help. First, consider installing the trim yourself if possible. This will save you money on labor costs. If you are hiring a professional, shop around to get the best price. Finally, consider buying the trim in bulk, as this can help you save money on materials.

Conclusion

Garage door trim is an important part of the overall look of your garage door, and it can also help you save money on your garage door installation. The cost of trim will depend on the material, the complexity of the installation, and the size of the trim. Vinyl tends to be the least expensive option, and it is easy to install. Shopping around for the best price and buying in bulk can also help you save money on trim installation.
