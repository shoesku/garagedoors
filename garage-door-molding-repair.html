How To Repair Garage Door Molding

Best Garage doors and references for house or residential with custom material and more design. We Share more picture design with HD quality images.

Garage door molding is a necessary part of your home's exterior. It helps protect your doors from the elements, adds an aesthetically pleasing look to your home, and helps keep your home secure. In the event that your garage door molding is damaged, however, it is important to repair it as soon as possible. Fortunately, the process of repairing garage door molding is relatively straightforward, and can be done by most homeowners with the right tools and materials.

Step 1: Remove the Damaged Molding

The first step is to remove any damaged molding. This should be done carefully, ensuring that no further damage is done to the surrounding area. If the damaged molding is connected to any other materials, such as siding or trim, be sure to remove it carefully so as not to damage those materials as well. Once the damaged molding is removed, the remaining area should be inspected to make sure that there are no further areas of damage.

Step 2: Measure and Cut the New Molding

Once the area of damage is inspected and the old molding is removed, the next step is to measure and cut the new molding. Make sure to measure accurately and double-check the measurements to ensure that the new molding fits properly. When cutting the new molding, use a miter saw to make sure that the angles are precise. If you do not have a miter saw, a handsaw or other types of saws can be used, though the results may not be as precise.

Step 3: Install the New Molding

Once the new molding is cut, it can be installed. To do this, use a caulking gun to apply a bead of exterior-grade caulk along the edge of the door jamb. Then, press the new molding firmly into place. Make sure to check that the molding is properly aligned and secure before proceeding. Once the new molding is in place, use a circular saw to trim any excess length or width.

Step 4: Secure the Molding

Once the new molding is installed and trimmed, the next step is to secure it in place. To do this, use a drill to predrill holes in the molding, making sure that the holes are aligned with the existing holes in the door jamb. Then, use exterior-grade screws to secure the molding in place. Make sure to countersink the screws so that they do not protrude from the surface of the molding.

Step 5: Finish the Job

After the new molding is secured in place, the final step is to finish the job. To do this, use a caulking gun to apply a generous bead of exterior-grade caulk along the edge of the new molding. Then, use a putty knife to smooth out the caulk and make sure that there are no gaps or voids. Once the caulk is dry, the job is done.

Conclusion

Repairing garage door molding is a relatively straightforward process that most homeowners can do themselves. By following the steps outlined above, you can quickly and easily repair any damaged molding, ensuring that your garage door is secure and that your home looks great.
