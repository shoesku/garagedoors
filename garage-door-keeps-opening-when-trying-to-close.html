How To Fix A Garage Door That Keeps Opening When Trying To Close

Best Garage doors and references for house or residential with custom material and more design. We Share more picture design with HD quality images.

A malfunctioning garage door can be a frustrating problem to deal with. When your garage door keeps opening when you try to close it, it’s usually because of a problem with the sensors, torsion springs, or the door opener. Fortunately, there are some easy steps you can take to fix the issue. In this guide, we’ll walk you through the steps to get your garage door working properly again.

Check the Sensors

Garage door openers are equipped with sensors to prevent the door from closing when something is blocking its path. If the sensors are out of alignment or dirty, this can cause the door to open when you try to close it. To check the sensors, look for two small boxes attached to the wall on either side of the garage door. Make sure that the boxes are facing each other, and that nothing is obstructing them. If the sensors are out of alignment, use a level to adjust them until they’re even. Then, use a soft cloth to clean off any dust or debris that’s built up on the sensors.

Check the Torsion Springs

Torsion springs are short metal springs that are responsible for lifting the garage door when you press the opener. If the torsion springs are out of alignment, the door won’t be able to open and close properly. To check the torsion springs, open the door manually and look for any signs of damage or wear and tear. If the springs are damaged, you’ll need to have them replaced by a professional. If the springs look fine, try tightening them with a wrench.

Check the Door Opener

If the sensors and torsion springs are in good condition, the issue might be with the door opener. If the opener is damaged or worn out, it won’t be able to open and close the door properly. To check the opener, press the remote control and listen for any strange noises coming from the motor. If you hear a grinding or clicking sound, it means the opener needs to be serviced or replaced. You can also check the chain or belt that connects the opener to the door. If it’s loose or broken, it will need to be replaced.

Make Adjustments to the Track

If the sensors, torsion springs, and opener are in good condition, the issue could be with the track. If the track is bent or misaligned, it will cause the door to open when you try to close it. To fix the issue, you’ll need to adjust the track using a wrench. First, loosen the bolts that hold the track in place. Then, use a pry bar to adjust the track until it’s properly aligned. Finally, use a wrench to tighten the bolts and secure the track.

Check the Rollers and Hinges

The rollers and hinges on the garage door can also cause it to open when you try to close it. If the rollers or hinges are worn out, they won’t be able to support the weight of the door. To check the rollers and hinges, open the door manually and look for any signs of wear and tear. If the rollers or hinges are damaged, you’ll need to have them replaced by a professional.

Clean the Tracks and Lubricate the Rollers

If the rollers and hinges are in good condition, the issue might be dirt or debris on the tracks. To fix this issue, use a soft cloth to wipe away any dirt or debris from the track. Then, use a lubricant to lubricate the rollers. You can also use a can of compressed air to blow away any dust or dirt that’s built up on the track.

Replace the Opener

If all of the above steps don’t fix the issue, it’s probably time to replace the opener. Garage door openers are designed to last for several years, but they can wear out over time. You can purchase a new opener from a local hardware store or online retailer. Be sure to purchase an opener that’s compatible with your garage door. Once you have the new opener, you can install it yourself or hire a professional to do the job.

Conclusion

If your garage door keeps opening when you try to close it, the issue could be with the sensors, torsion springs, door opener, track, rollers, or hinges. Fortunately, there are some easy steps you can take to fix the issue. First, check the sensors, torsion springs, and door opener for any signs of damage or wear and tear. Then, adjust the track and replace any damaged rollers or hinges. Finally, clean the track and lubricate the rollers. If all else fails, you may need to replace the opener.
