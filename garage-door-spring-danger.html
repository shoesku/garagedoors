Is Your Garage Door Spring Dangerous?

If you have a garage door in your house, you've likely heard about the dangers associated with garage door springs. For those who don't know, garage door springs are located near the top of the garage door, and when they break, they can cause serious injury or even death. It is important to understand the potential dangers of these springs and how to ensure your safety when using them.

Why Do Garage Door Springs Pose a Danger?

Garage door springs pose a danger because they are under extreme tension. When they break, they can release this tension, which can propel the spring at high speeds. If a person is nearby, they can be injured by the flying spring. Additionally, if a person is standing too close and the spring breaks, it can cause serious injury or even death. It is important to keep a minimum distance of 10 feet away from the spring when it is being used.

How to Prevent Injury from Garage Door Springs

The best way to prevent injury from garage door springs is to have them regularly inspected and serviced by a professional. An experienced technician will be able to identify any signs of wear and tear and replace the springs if necessary. Additionally, it is important to avoid attempting to repair the springs yourself. If a spring is broken, it should be replaced by a professional as attempting to repair it yourself could lead to further injury.

When to Call a Professional

If you are experiencing any issues with your garage door spring, it is important to call a professional as soon as possible. A professional will be able to quickly identify the issue and provide a solution to ensure your safety. Additionally, if the spring has become worn or is showing signs of wear, it should be replaced. It is important to note that springs should be replaced every five to seven years to ensure optimal performance.

How to Choose the Right Professional for the Job

When selecting a professional to inspect or replace your garage door spring, it is important to do your research. Look for a technician that specializes in garage door springs and has a proven track record of success. Additionally, make sure to read reviews from past customers and ask for references. By taking the time to vet a professional, you can ensure that your garage door spring is handled safely and efficiently.

Signs of a Faulty Garage Door Spring

It is important to be aware of the signs of a faulty garage door spring. These can include a slow opening or closing, an uneven opening or closing, or a loud noise when the door is being opened or closed. It is important to contact a professional if you notice any of these signs as they could indicate that the spring is beginning to fail. Additionally, if your door suddenly stops working, this could also be a sign that the spring is faulty and needs to be replaced.

Conclusion

Garage door springs can be dangerous if not handled correctly. It is important to keep a minimum distance of 10 feet away from a spring when it is being used, and to have it inspected and serviced by a professional at least every five to seven years. Additionally, it is important to be aware of the signs of a faulty spring and to contact a professional if any of these signs are present. By taking these simple precautions, you can ensure your safety when using your garage door spring.
