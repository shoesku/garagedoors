Garage Door Keeps Reversing When Closing: What To Do?

If your garage door keeps reversing when closing, it can be a real hassle. Not only can it be an inconvenience, it can also be a safety hazard. With the right knowledge, you can learn how to fix it and avoid it in the future.

Why Does My Garage Door Keep Reversing?

There are several possible causes for a garage door reversing when closing. The most common is a misalignment of the travel path of the door. This means that the door is not tracking correctly and is running into an obstruction or is out of alignment with the tracks. This misalignment can be caused by something as simple as a loose track or as complex as a bent track or a faulty motor. Other possible causes of a reversing garage door include a broken spring, a worn or broken chain, or a faulty opener.

Checking for Common Causes

If your garage door keeps reversing when closing, the first thing you should do is check for any of the common causes. Start by checking the tracks and making sure they are aligned properly. If they are not, you may be able to realign them yourself or you may need to call a professional. If the tracks are aligned properly, check for any obstructions in the way of the door. These could be anything from a misplaced item to a broken piece of the track. If you find an obstruction, remove it and see if the door is still reversing.

Check for Wear and Tear

If you can't find any obstructions, it may be time to check for wear and tear. Start by checking the springs and chains to make sure they are in good condition and not worn out. If you find any worn or broken parts, you will need to replace them. You should also check the opener to make sure it is working properly. If it is not, you may need to replace it or have it repaired.

Check the Sensors

If you have checked all the other possible causes and your garage door is still reversing when closing, it may be time to check the sensors. The sensors on a garage door are designed to detect when something is in the way of the door and will automatically reverse the door to prevent it from hitting whatever is in its path. If the sensors are not working properly, they may be detecting something that is not there, causing the door to reverse even when nothing is in its way.

Call a Professional

If you are unable to find the cause of your garage door reversing when closing, it may be time to call a professional. A professional technician can diagnose the problem and repair or replace any faulty parts. They can also provide you with advice on how to maintain your garage door in the future to avoid any similar issues.

Conclusion

If your garage door keeps reversing when closing, it can be a real hassle. Fortunately, with the right knowledge and tools, you can troubleshoot the problem and find the cause. If you are unable to find the cause, it may be time to call a professional. With the help of a professional, you can get your garage door working properly again and avoid any future issues.
