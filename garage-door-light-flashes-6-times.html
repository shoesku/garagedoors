What Does It Mean When Your Garage Door Light Flashes Six Times?

If you’ve noticed that your garage door light is flashing six times, you’re likely concerned about what it means. Fortunately, it isn’t anything too serious and can usually be fixed without having to call a professional. Before you start trying to fix it, though, you need to understand what the issue is and why your garage door light is flashing six times.

In most cases, when your garage door light flashes six times, it means that your garage door opener is not working correctly. It’s trying to tell you that it’s having trouble communicating with the remote control, which is why it’s flashing. If your remote control is not working, then it’s likely that the batteries are either dead or not properly connected.

What To Do When Your Garage Door Light Flashes Six Times

The first thing you should do when your garage door light flashes six times is to check the batteries in your remote control. If they’re dead, then you should replace them. If they’re not dead, then you should make sure they’re properly connected. If the batteries are fine and the remote control is still not working, then it’s likely that your garage door opener is not working correctly.

If your garage door opener is not working correctly, then you should check the wiring for any loose connections. If you find any, then you should tighten them. You should also check to make sure that all of the cables are properly connected. If any of the cables are loose or disconnected, then you should reconnect them.

Troubleshooting Your Garage Door Opener

If your garage door opener is still not working correctly after you’ve checked the wiring and the cables, then you should try to troubleshoot the issue. You can do this by unplugging the garage door opener and plugging it back in. This will reset the system and might help to resolve the issue.

If that doesn’t work, then you should try to reset the remote control. To do this, you should press and hold the “learn” button on the garage door opener until the light flashes. Then, press and hold the button on the remote control until the light on the garage door opener flashes. This should reset the remote control and should help to resolve the issue.

Replacing Your Garage Door Opener

If your garage door opener is still not working correctly after you’ve tried the troubleshooting steps, then it’s likely that your garage door opener is broken and needs to be replaced. Fortunately, replacing a garage door opener is a relatively easy process and can usually be done without having to hire a professional.

Before you replace your garage door opener, you should make sure that you’ve checked all of the wiring and cables for any loose connections. You should also make sure that the batteries in your remote control are working properly. Once you’ve done that, you can go ahead and purchase a new garage door opener and install it.

Conclusion

If your garage door light is flashing six times, then it’s likely that your garage door opener is not working correctly. In most cases, the issue can be fixed by checking the batteries in the remote control and making sure that all of the wiring and cables are properly connected. If that doesn’t work, then you should try to troubleshoot the issue by unplugging and plugging the garage door opener back in. If that still doesn’t work, then it’s likely that you need to replace the garage door opener.
