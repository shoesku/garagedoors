Why Is My Garage Door Not Closing Or Opening All The Way?

If you have taken a look at your garage door recently and noticed that it isn’t opening or closing all the way, you may be wondering what could be causing the issue. There are a few different reasons why this could be happening, and knowing what they are can help you to determine what needs to be done in order to fix the problem. Here are some of the most common causes of a garage door not opening or closing all the way.

Rollers

The rollers that help to open and close the garage door can become bent, worn down, or even broken, which can cause the door to not open or close all the way. If your garage door is not closing or opening all the way, it’s a good idea to check the rollers to see if they need to be replaced. If they are bent, you may be able to straighten them out, but if they are worn down or broken, you will need to replace them.

Tracks

The tracks that the rollers run along can also become bent, worn down, or broken, which can cause the door to not open or close all the way. You will need to check the tracks to see if they need to be replaced or repaired. In some cases, you may be able to straighten out the tracks, but if they are worn down or broken, you will need to replace them.

Springs

The springs that help to open and close the garage door can become worn out over time, which can cause the door to not open or close all the way. If your springs are worn out, you will need to replace them in order to fix the problem. Replacing the springs can be a difficult job and should only be done by a professional.

Opener

The opener can also cause the garage door to not open or close all the way. If the opener is not working properly, you will need to replace it in order to fix the problem. Replacing the opener can be a difficult job and should only be done by a professional.

Cables

The cables that help to open and close the garage door can become worn out over time, which can cause the door to not open or close all the way. If your cables are worn out, you will need to replace them in order to fix the problem. Replacing the cables can be a difficult job and should only be done by a professional.

Weather Stripping

The weather stripping around the garage door can become worn out over time, which can cause the door to not open or close all the way. If your weather stripping is worn out, you will need to replace it in order to fix the problem. Replacing the weather stripping can be a difficult job and should only be done by a professional.

Sensor

The sensor on the garage door can become misaligned over time, which can cause the door to not open or close all the way. If the sensor is misaligned, you will need to adjust it in order to fix the problem. Adjusting the sensor can be a difficult job and should only be done by a professional.

Conclusion

As you can see, there are a few different reasons why your garage door may not be opening or closing all the way. Knowing what these reasons are can help you to determine what needs to be done in order to fix the problem. If you are not sure how to fix the problem, it’s a good idea to contact a professional for assistance.
