How To Open An Automatic Garage Door Manually From The Outside

Introduction

In the 21st century, it’s hard to imagine a home without an automatic garage door. However, there are still moments when you may need to open the garage door manually from the outside, such as when the power is out, or the power is connected but the door is malfunctioning. Even if you don’t have an automatic garage door, you’ll need to know how to open the door manually from the outside. This article explains how to do this, so you can rest easy knowing you can always access your garage.

Steps to Open the Door Manually

The first step is to locate the emergency release cord. This is usually a red cord and it’s typically located near the top of the garage door. Pulling on the emergency release cord will disengage the motor, allowing you to manually open the garage door. You may need a ladder to reach it, depending on the height of your garage door.

Once you’ve located the emergency release cord, pull it down. The door should unlock and be able to be opened manually. However, it is important to note that the emergency release cord should only be used if the power is out, or if the garage door is malfunctioning. If the power is on, and the garage door is working but you just need to get into the garage, you should use the manual override instead.

The manual override is usually a small, round button located near the bottom of the garage door. It may be hidden behind a small panel or protected by a cover. Pressing the manual override will disengage the motor and allow you to manually open the garage door. It’s important to note that the manual override should only be used if the power is on, and the garage door is working. If the power is out or the garage door is malfunctioning, you should use the emergency release cord.

Safety Precautions

It is important to take safety precautions when attempting to open a garage door manually from the outside. If the power is out, make sure you are using a flashlight or other source of light when trying to locate the emergency release cord. This will help to ensure you don’t accidentally pull the wrong cord.

If the power is on, make sure you are using the manual override and not the emergency release cord. Pulling the emergency release cord with the power on could cause the garage door to malfunction. It could also cause the door to start closing on its own, which could be dangerous.

Finally, make sure you are standing well away from the garage door when opening it manually. This will ensure that you are not injured if the door starts to close on its own. It’s also a good idea to wear protective gloves to protect your hands from the sharp edges of the door.

Conclusion

Opening an automatic garage door manually from the outside is relatively easy. All you need to do is locate the emergency release cord or the manual override, depending on whether the power is out or on, and pull or press the button. However, it is important to take safety precautions to ensure you don’t get injured. With these tips, you should have no problem opening your automatic garage door manually from the outside.
