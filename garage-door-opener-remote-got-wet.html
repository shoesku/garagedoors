What To Do When Your Garage Door Opener Remote Got Wet

Introduction

If you’ve ever had the misfortune of having your garage door opener remote get wet, you know it can be a real nuisance. The device may not work properly, or it may stop working altogether. This can be especially annoying if the remote is necessary to open and close your garage door. Fortunately, there are a few steps you can take to repair your garage door opener remote if it has been affected by water.

Remove the Batteries

The first step is to remove the batteries from your remote. If the batteries remain in the remote while it is wet, they can corrode and short circuit. This can cause damage to both the batteries and the remote. Once you’ve removed the batteries, be sure to wipe down the inside of the remote with a dry cloth. This will help remove any excess water that may have gotten inside.

Remove the Circuit Board

The next step is to remove the circuit board from the remote. This is a delicate process, and should only be done by an experienced repair technician. Once the circuit board is removed, it should be thoroughly inspected for signs of water damage. If any water has gotten inside the board, it may need to be replaced.

Clean the Circuit Board

Once you’ve inspected the board, you should clean it with a soft, dry cloth. This will remove any dirt or debris that may have accumulated on the board. Be sure to avoid using any harsh cleaning chemicals, as these could damage the board further.

Check for Corrosion

After cleaning the board, you should check for any signs of corrosion. Corrosion can occur if water has been allowed to sit on the board for an extended period of time. If any corrosion is present, it should be removed with a soft cloth and a mild detergent.

Reassemble the Remote

Once you’ve inspected and cleaned the board, you can begin to reassemble the remote. Be sure to inspect each part of the remote for signs of water damage before reassembling it. If any parts appear to be damaged, you should replace them with new ones.

Test the Remote

Once the remote is reassembled, it’s time to test it. Place the batteries back in the remote and try to open and close your garage door. If the remote works, then you’ve successfully repaired it. If it still doesn’t work, then you may need to take it to a professional repair technician.

Preventing Future Damage

To prevent damage to your garage door opener remote in the future, it’s important to keep it dry. Store the remote in a dry place, such as a drawer or cabinet, to avoid any moisture buildup. Additionally, you should check the remote periodically for signs of water damage and replace any corroded parts as soon as possible.

Conclusion

If your garage door opener remote got wet, there are a few steps you can take to repair it. First, remove the batteries and wipe down the inside of the remote with a dry cloth. Next, remove the circuit board and inspect it for water damage. Clean the board with a soft, dry cloth and check for corrosion. Reassemble the remote and test it to make sure it’s working properly. Finally, store the remote in a dry place and check it periodically for any signs of water damage.
