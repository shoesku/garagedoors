Common Reasons Why Your Garage Door Is Not Closing But The Motor Is Running

Troubleshooting your garage door when it doesn't close properly can be a tricky endeavor. To make it easier, it's important to have a basic understanding of how a garage door works. Garage door openers are powered by a motor, which turns a chain or belt to lift the door. When the door is in the correct position, the opener reverses the motor to lower the door into place. The problem is, when the door is not closing properly and the motor is running but the door is stuck somewhere in between, it can be quite a challenge to figure out what is causing the problem.

1. Misaligned Garage Door Sensors

One of the most common issues that can cause your garage door to not close is misaligned sensors. The sensors are located at the bottom of the garage door and are responsible for detecting any obstructions. If the sensors become misaligned, they won't detect the obstruction and the door will not close. To fix this issue, you'll need to align the sensors and make sure they are both pointing in the same direction.

2. Broken Torsion Spring

The torsion spring is responsible for providing the power needed to lift and close the garage door. If the spring is broken, the door won't be able to close properly. Depending on the severity of the break, you may be able to repair it yourself or you may need to call a professional for assistance. Either way, it's important to get the spring fixed as soon as possible to avoid further damage.

3. Faulty Garage Door Opener

Another potential cause of a garage door not closing is a faulty garage door opener. If the opener is not working correctly, it won't be able to lift the door properly. The best way to fix this issue is to replace the opener with a new one. It's also important to make sure the opener is properly installed and that all the wiring is connected correctly. If you're not sure how to install the opener, it's best to call a professional for assistance.

4. Worn Out Garage Door Rollers

Garage door rollers play an important role in how the door operates. If the rollers become damaged or worn out, they won't be able to lift the door properly and the door won't be able to close. To fix this issue, you'll need to replace the worn out rollers with new ones. This is a relatively easy job that can be done with basic tools.

5. Obstruction in the Track

The track of your garage door is responsible for guiding the door up and down. If there is an obstruction in the track, it won't be able to lift the door properly. To fix this issue, you'll need to remove the obstruction from the track and make sure it is free of any debris. If the track is still not working properly, you may need to replace it with a new one.

6. Faulty Limit Switch

The limit switch is responsible for telling the motor when to stop running. If the limit switch is not working properly, the motor will keep running and the door won't close. To fix this issue, you'll need to replace the limit switch with a new one. This is a relatively easy job, but it's best to call a professional if you're not sure how to do it.

7. Damaged or Bent Tracks

If the tracks of your garage door are damaged or bent, the door won't be able to close properly. To fix this issue, you'll need to straighten out the tracks and make sure they are free of any obstructions. If the tracks are still not working properly, you may need to replace them with new ones.

8. Stuck Rollers

If the rollers of your garage door are stuck, the door won't be able to close properly. To fix this issue, you'll need to lubricate the rollers and make sure they are free of any debris. If the rollers are still not working properly, you may need to replace them with new ones.

9. Broken Cables

The cables of your garage door are responsible for providing the power needed to lift and close the door. If the cables become damaged or broken, the door won't be able to close properly. To fix this issue you'll need to replace the cables with new ones. This is a relatively simple job, but it's best to call a professional for assistance if you're not sure how to do it.

10. Loose Hardware

If the hardware on your garage door is loose, it won't be able to close properly. To fix this issue, you'll need to tighten the hardware and make sure it is secure. If the hardware is still not working properly, you may need to replace it with new one.

Conclusion

Troubleshooting your garage door when it won't close properly can be a tricky endeavor. By understanding how a garage door works and the common issues that can cause it to not close, you can make the process a lot easier. If you're still having trouble, it's best to call a professional for assistance. They will be able to diagnose the problem and make the necessary repairs to get your garage door working properly again.
