What Is Garage Door Edge Molding?

Garage door edge molding is a type of trim that is used to seal the edges of the door. It is applied along the door edges and can help to protect the door from water and air infiltration. It also helps to create a uniform look and can also be used to add some color to the door. Edge molding can be purchased in a variety of materials and colors, so it is easy to find something that will match the design of your home.

What Are the Benefits of Installing Garage Door Edge Molding?

Installing edge molding can help to provide protection to your garage door. It can help to seal the edges of the door so that water and air can't get in. This can help to prevent rust, corrosion, and other damage to the door. Edge molding can also help to create a uniform look to your garage door. It can be used to match the trim on the rest of your home and can also add some color to the door. Edge molding is an easy and inexpensive way to add some style and protection to your garage door.

How Do You Install Garage Door Edge Molding?

Installing garage door edge molding is a relatively simple process. First, measure the width and height of the door frame. Then, cut the molding to the appropriate size. Next, use nails or screws to attach the molding to the door frame. Be sure to use the appropriate type of fastener for the material you are using. Finally, apply a sealant to the edges of the molding to help protect the door from water and air infiltration.

What Types of Materials Are Used for Edge Molding?

Edge molding is typically made from wood, plastic, metal, or vinyl. Wood is a popular choice because it is relatively inexpensive and can be easily painted. Plastic is also a popular choice because it is durable and can be easily molded into different shapes. Metal is a great option for those looking for a more modern look. Vinyl is a great choice for those looking for a more durable and weather-resistant option.
