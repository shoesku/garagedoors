Garage Door Colors: How To Pick The Right Shade For Your Home

Best Garage doors and references for house or residential with custom material and more design. We Share more picture design with HD quality images.

When it comes to choosing the right shade for a garage door, homeowners have more options than ever before. From traditional colors like white and black, to modern colors like cobalt blue and olive green, there’s something for everyone. But if you don’t know where to begin, it can be overwhelming. Here’s a guide to help you choose the perfect color for your roll-up garage door.

Assess Your Home’s Exterior

Your garage door is an important part of your home’s exterior, so it’s important to choose a color that fits with the rest of your home’s look. If you have a traditional-style home with red brick siding, you might want to go with a classic white or black color. If you have a more modern home, you might consider a bolder color like navy blue or hunter green. Think about the style of your home and the colors you’ve used elsewhere, and try to find a garage door color that complements the rest of your design.

Choose the Right Finish

When choosing a color for your garage door, you’ll also want to consider the finish. If you want a more subtle look, you can go with a matte finish, which won’t reflect much light. If you want something with a bit more shine and sparkle, you can opt for a glossy finish. You’ll also want to consider the material of your garage door. Some materials, like wood, can be painted, while others, like metal, may require a different kind of finish.

Consider the Color of Your Trim

If you have trim around your garage door, you’ll want to consider the color when choosing a shade for your roll-up door. If your trim is a neutral shade like white or beige, you can go with a bold color like red or yellow. If you have a darker trim, like black or dark grey, you’ll want to go with a more subtle shade like light blue or green.

Think About Lighting

The lighting around your house can also affect the look of your garage door. If you have a lot of natural light, you can choose a color that takes advantage of the brightness, like yellow or light green. If you have a darker home, you might want to go with a darker color like navy blue or charcoal grey. Consider the amount of natural light in your home and choose a shade that will complement it.

Find Inspiration

If you’re having trouble deciding on a color, it can be helpful to look for inspiration. Look at garage door colors in your neighborhood, or search online for images of doors in different colors. You can also look at color swatches from paint stores or home improvement stores. Get an idea of the colors you like, and then narrow down your choices until you find the perfect hue.

Test the Color

Once you’ve chosen a color for your garage door, it’s a good idea to test it out before you commit. If you’re painting the door, you can buy a sample of the paint and apply it to a small section of the door. If you’re getting a pre-painted door, you can ask for a sample so you can see how the color looks in different lighting. This way, you can be sure you’re making the right decision.

Hire a Professional

Finally, it’s important to hire a professional garage door company to install your door. A professional can ensure that your door is installed correctly and will last for years to come. Plus, they can help you choose the right color and finish for your door, so you can get the perfect look for your home.

Choosing the right color for your roll-up garage door can be a daunting task. But with the right information and a little bit of inspiration, you can find the perfect hue for your home. Consider your home’s exterior, the color of your trim, and the amount of natural light in your home, and then choose a shade that fits your style. And don’t forget to hire a professional garage door company to install your door for the best results.
