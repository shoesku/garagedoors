How To Replace A Up And Over Garage Door Handle

If you have recently purchased a new home, you may be wondering how to replace a up and over garage door handle. Replacing a garage door handle is not a difficult process, but it does require some steps that should be followed to ensure proper installation. This article will provide detailed instructions on how to replace a up and over garage door handle, as well as provide information on the best materials to use and how to choose the right handle for your needs.

Steps to Replacing a Up and Over Garage Door Handle

The first step to replacing a up and over garage door handle is to remove the existing handle. Start by unscrewing the screws that hold the handle in place. Next, pull the handle out and set it aside. If the handle is held in place by a latch, you will need to use a flathead screwdriver to release the latch before removing the handle.

Once the handle has been removed, you can begin to install the new handle. Start by attaching the new handle to the door. Most up and over garage door handles will attach with two screws. Make sure the screws are secured and that the handle is firmly attached to the door.

Once the handle is attached, you can reinstall the screws that hold the handle in place. Make sure the screws are tightened properly and that the handle is secure. If the handle has a latch, make sure it is properly locked in place.

Once the handle is installed, you can test it to make sure it works correctly. Pull on the handle to open the garage door. If the door opens, the new handle is installed correctly. If the door does not open, you may need to adjust the handle or tighten the screws that hold it in place.

Materials Needed to Replace a Up and Over Garage Door Handle

Before replacing a up and over garage door handle, you will need to have the right materials. You will need a flathead screwdriver, a Phillips head screwdriver, and a drill with a drill bit the same size as the screws that hold the handle in place. You will also need the new handle and screws.

Choosing the Right Handle for Your Needs

When choosing a new up and over garage door handle, it is important to consider your needs. If your garage door is frequently used, you may want to choose a handle that is easy to grip and has a large, comfortable handle. If security is a concern, you may want to choose a handle that has a secure locking mechanism.

It is also important to consider the material of the handle. Metal handles are typically more durable than plastic handles, but they can be more expensive. Plastic handles are usually less expensive and may be a good choice if you are on a budget. Whichever material you choose, make sure it is made of a durable material that will last for years.

Conclusion

Replacing a up and over garage door handle is not a difficult process. With the right tools and materials, you can easily replace your old handle with a new one. Make sure to choose the right handle for your needs and follow the installation instructions carefully. With a few simple steps, you can have a new, secure handle on your garage door in no time.
