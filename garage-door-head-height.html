Installing a new garage door is an exciting project. It can add a great deal of curb appeal, increase your home’s security and make your life easier. However, many homeowners don’t realize the importance of head height when it comes to garage door installation. When garage doors are too low, they can cause a variety of problems.

The Importance of Head Height

When a garage door is installed, the head height is the most important factor. This is the distance from the floor of the garage to the top of the garage door when it is open. It is important to make sure that the head height is correct to ensure that the door is safe and secure.

Risks of Low Head Height

When the head height is too low, it can cause a variety of problems. This includes the door not being able to close properly, not being able to open properly, and not being able to stay open. Additionally, if the head height is too low, it can cause the door to be a safety hazard, as it can be easy to trip and fall. Low head height can also cause the door to be less secure, as it can be easier for intruders to open the door.

Minimum Head Height Requirements

The minimum head height requirement for garage doors is seven feet. This is to ensure that the door is safe, secure and able to open and close properly. It is important to make sure that the head height is correct before installing the door, as it can be difficult to adjust the head height after the door is installed.

Additional Considerations

When installing a garage door, it is important to make sure that the head height is correct. Additionally, it is important to make sure that the door is securely fastened to the wall and that the springs and other components are installed properly.
