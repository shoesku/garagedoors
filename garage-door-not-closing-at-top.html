Fixing A Garage Door That Won’t Close At The Top

Best Garage doors and references for house or residential with custom material and more design. We Share more picture design with HD quality images.

If you’re having trouble getting your garage door to close properly, you’re not alone. A garage door that won’t close at the top is a common problem that can be caused by a variety of issues. Fortunately, it’s usually not too difficult to fix and there’s no need to call a professional for help.

Step 1: Check the Track

The first thing you should check is the track of your garage door. This is the metal track that the rollers run along. If the track is bent or damaged in any way, it can prevent the door from closing properly. To check it, open the door and look for any signs of damage. If you see any, you’ll need to have the track repaired or replaced.

Step 2: Adjust the Limit Switch

Another common cause of a garage door that won’t close at the top is an improperly adjusted limit switch. The limit switch is a small device located on the motor that controls how far the door can travel before it stops. If the limit switch isn’t set correctly, it can prevent the door from closing properly. To adjust the limit switch, you’ll first need to locate it. It should be mounted on the side of the motor. Once you’ve located it, you can adjust it to the correct setting.

Step 3: Check the Springs

If the track and limit switch are in good working order, you may need to check the springs. The springs are responsible for lifting the door and allowing it to close. If the springs are loose or broken, it can cause the door to not close properly. If you suspect the springs are the cause, it’s best to call a professional to have them repaired or replaced.

Step 4: Check the Safety Sensors

If all of the above steps have been completed and the door still won’t close properly, then you may need to check the safety sensors. The safety sensors are located on either side of the door and are used to detect any obstructions in the path of the door. If the sensors are not aligned properly, the door won’t be able to close. To check the sensors, you’ll need to open the door and look for any obstructions in the path of the sensors. If you find any, you’ll need to remove them and then realign the sensors.

Step 5: Check the Motor

If all of the above steps have been completed and the door still won’t close properly, then you may need to check the motor. The motor is responsible for powering the door and if it’s faulty, it can prevent the door from closing properly. To check the motor, you’ll need to open the door and look for any signs of damage or wear and tear. If you find any, you’ll need to have the motor repaired or replaced.

Step 6: Lubricate the Moving Parts

If all of the above steps have been completed and the door still won’t close properly, then you may need to lubricate the moving parts of the door. Over time, the moving parts of the door can become dry and sticky, which can prevent the door from closing properly. To lubricate the moving parts, you’ll need to purchase a lubricant specifically designed for garage doors and then use it to lubricate all the moving parts of the door.

Step 7: Check the Balance of the Door

If all of the above steps have been completed and the door still won’t close properly, then you may need to check the balance of the door. The balance of the door is important to ensure that it operates smoothly and closes properly. To check the balance of the door, you’ll need to open the door halfway and then let go. If the door stays in the same position, then it is properly balanced. If the door moves up or down, then it needs to be balanced.

Step 8: Contact a Professional

If all of the above steps have been completed and the door still won’t close properly, then you may need to contact a professional. A professional garage door repair service can diagnose the issue and make the necessary repairs to get your door working properly again. They can also provide you with advice and tips on how to prevent similar problems in the future.

Conclusion

Garage doors that won’t close at the top is a common problem that can be caused by a variety of issues. Fortunately, it’s usually not too difficult to fix and there’s no need to call a professional for help. By following the steps outlined above, you should be able to identify and fix the problem quickly and easily.
