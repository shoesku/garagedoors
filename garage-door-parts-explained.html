Garage Door Parts Explained

Introduction

Your garage door is made up of a wide variety of parts, each of which serves an important purpose. In order to keep your garage door working smoothly and safely, itâ€™s important to understand the different components and how they work together. This article will explain the different parts of a garage door, so you can be better informed when making repairs or replacement decisions.

Garage Door Track

The garage door track is the metal rail system that the door rolls up and down on. It consists of two vertical tracks which are connected to the wall on either side of the door opening. The door is suspended from the tracks at either side of the opening, and it is guided along the tracks as it is opened and closed. The tracks must be aligned correctly in order for the door to open and close properly.

Garage Door Springs

Garage door springs are what give the door the power to open and close. There are two types of springs used in garage doors; torsion springs and extension springs. Torsion springs are located above the door opening, while extension springs are located on the sides of the door. The springs are wound tightly and are designed to help lift the weight of the door as it opens and closes.

Garage Door Rollers

Garage door rollers are small wheels which are attached to the bottom of the door. They are designed to help the door move up and down the track as it is opened and closed. The rollers must be in good condition in order for the door to open and close properly. Over time, the rollers can become worn down and need to be replaced.

Garage Door Hinges

Garage door hinges are the metal pieces which attach the door to the frame. They are located at the top and sides of the door, and are responsible for holding the door in place as it is opened and closed. The hinges must be in good condition in order for the door to open and close properly.

Garage Door Cables

Garage door cables are the metal wires which are attached to the bottom of the door and to the track. They are designed to help keep the door in place as it is opened and closed. The cables must be in good condition in order for the door to open and close properly. Over time, the cables can become worn down and need to be replaced.

Garage Door Opener

The garage door opener is the motorized unit which is responsible for opening and closing the door. The opener consists of a motor, a control panel, and a remote transmitter. The opener is programmed to respond to a signal from the transmitter, which allows you to open and close the door from anywhere.

Garage Door Weather Stripping

Garage door weather stripping is the foam or rubber strip which is attached to the bottom of the door. It is designed to help keep out drafts and moisture, and to keep the door insulated. Over time, the weather stripping can become worn down and need to be replaced.

Garage Door Locks

Garage door locks are the devices which are used to secure the door when it is closed. They are installed at the top and sides of the door and are designed to keep it from being opened from the outside. The locks must be in good condition in order for the door to be secure.

Conclusion

Understanding the different parts of a garage door can help you make informed decisions when it comes to repairs or replacements. Knowing the different components and how they work together can help you troubleshoot problems quickly and easily. With this knowledge, you can keep your garage door running smoothly and safely.
