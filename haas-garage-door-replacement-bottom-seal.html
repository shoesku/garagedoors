About Haas Garage Door Bottom Seals

You may not know it, but the bottom seal of your Haas garage door is more important than you think. This seal is designed to keep out weather, insects, and moisture, which can cause serious damage to your garage, as well as your stored items. Over time, the seal can wear out and need to be replaced. Luckily, replacing the bottom seal of your Haas garage door isn’t too difficult, and can be done in a few simple steps. In this article, we’ll discuss the basics of replacing your Haas garage door bottom seal in 2023.

Preparation for Replacing Your Bottom Seal

Before you begin any repair or replacement project, it’s important to make sure you have the necessary tools and materials to complete the job. For replacing your Haas garage door bottom seal, you’ll need the following: a Phillips head screwdriver, a utility knife, a measuring tape, a pair of safety glasses, and the new bottom seal. Make sure the seal you purchase is the correct size for your door, as this is essential for a successful installation.

Steps for Replacing Your Bottom Seal

Once you’ve gathered all the necessary materials, you can begin the process of replacing your bottom seal. The following steps will walk you through the process:

  1. Using a Phillips head screwdriver, remove the screws from the bottom of the garage door.
  2. Carefully remove the old seal from the garage door. If it’s stuck, use a utility knife to cut it away.
  3. Measure the width of the opening at the bottom of the door. This is important, as the new seal must fit into the opening.
  4. Put on your safety glasses and cut the new seal to the correct size with a utility knife.
  5. Slide the new seal into the opening of the garage door.
  6. Secure the new seal to the door with the screws you removed in step one.
  7. Check the seal to make sure it’s securely attached and in the right position.
  8. Test the seal by opening and closing the garage door.

Tips for a Successful Bottom Seal Replacement

When replacing your Haas garage door bottom seal, there are a few tips that can help ensure a successful job. First, make sure you’re using the right size seal for your door. If the seal is too small, it won’t provide an effective seal. Also, take your time when cutting the seal to ensure a precise fit. Finally, make sure the seal is securely attached before closing the garage door. If it’s not, it can get stuck in the middle of the opening and cause damage.

Conclusion

Replacing the bottom seal of your Haas garage door doesn’t have to be a daunting task. By following the steps outlined in this article, you can easily and quickly replace your bottom seal and ensure your garage is properly sealed against moisture, insects, and the elements. Remember to use the right size seal, cut it carefully, and make sure it’s securely attached before closing the door. Good luck!
