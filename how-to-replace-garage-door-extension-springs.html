What Are Garage Door Extension Springs?

Garage door extension springs are one of the most important components of a garage door. They are responsible for providing the tension necessary for the door to open and close safely. They are typically installed on the side of the garage door track, or along the header, and stretch along the length of the door when it is open. When the door is closed, the springs are wound up and provide tension to the door to open and close. Unfortunately, garage door springs are not designed to last forever and eventually need to be replaced.

Why Do Garage Door Extension Springs Need to Be Replaced?

Garage door springs are not designed to last forever, and it is important to replace them when they start to wear out. Worn out springs can cause a number of safety issues, such as the door not opening or closing properly. Additionally, worn out springs can cause the door to become unbalanced and cause it to be difficult to open and close. Replacing the springs is the best way to ensure the safety of your garage door.

What Are the Steps to Replacing Garage Door Extension Springs?

Replacing garage door extension springs is a relatively simple process, but it is important to follow the steps carefully to ensure the safety of your door and the person replacing the springs. The first step is to remove the existing springs. This is done by disconnecting the springs from the track and winding them down until all of the tension has been released. Once the springs have been removed, the new springs should be installed in the same place as the old ones.

Tools Needed to Replace the Springs

When replacing garage door extension springs, it is important to have the right tools on hand. The most important tool is a winding bar. This tool is used to wind the springs up and down, and it helps to ensure that the tension is correctly balanced. Additionally, a socket set and some pliers will also be needed. It is also important to have safety glasses and gloves to protect yourself from any potential hazards.

Installing the New Springs

Once the old springs have been removed, the next step is to install the new springs. The springs should be installed in the same place as the old ones, and the tension should be adjusted until the door is balanced and operates smoothly. The springs should be wound up until they are tight, but not so tight that the door is difficult to open or close. Once the tension has been adjusted, the springs should be secured to the track with the winding bars.

Testing the Door

Once the new springs have been installed, it is important to test the door to make sure that it is functioning properly. The door should open and close smoothly, and it should not be unbalanced or difficult to open or close. If the door is not functioning properly, the tension on the springs may need to be adjusted. Additionally, if the door is still unbalanced, the springs may need to be replaced again.

Conclusion

Replacing garage door extension springs is an important task that should be done on a regular basis. By following the steps outlined above, you can ensure that your garage door is operating safely and efficiently. However, it is important to remember that this is a relatively complicated process and it is best to leave it to a professional if you are not comfortable attempting it yourself.
