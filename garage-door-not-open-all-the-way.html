Common Causes for a Garage Door That Won’t Open All The Way

If your garage door won’t open all the way, it could be due to a variety of reasons. Most of the time, it’s due to a problem with the springs or track, but it could also be due to an obstruction or the remote malfunctioning. No matter the cause, it’s important to address the problem as soon as possible. Before you call a technician, however, you should attempt to troubleshoot the issue yourself. Here’s what you should do to troubleshoot a garage door that won’t open all the way.

Check for Obstructions

The first step is to check for any obstructions that might be preventing the door from fully opening. As you open the door, look at both sides of the track to see if there’s anything blocking it. This could be anything from leaves and dirt to a toy or toolbox. When you find the obstruction, remove it and try opening the door again. If the obstruction was the cause of the issue, the door should open as normal.

Inspect the Springs and Rollers

The next step is to take a closer look at the springs and rollers. If the springs are worn out or broken, they won’t be able to lift the door all the way. You should also check the rollers to make sure they’re in good condition and not stuck or stuck on something. Replacing the springs or rollers is something you should leave to a professional.

Check the Tracks

The tracks are an important part of the garage door system and can also cause problems, so it’s important to check them for any issues. Look for any signs of wear and tear, and make sure that the tracks are aligned properly. If the tracks are bent or out of alignment, you should call a technician to fix them.

Check the Remote

If you have a remote-controlled garage door, you should check the remote to make sure it’s working properly. If the remote isn’t working, it could be due to a weak battery. Replace the battery and try again. If the remote still isn’t working, you should contact the manufacturer for a new remote.

Check the Limit Settings

Finally, you should check the limit settings of your garage door opener. If the limit settings are set too low, the door won’t open all the way. You can adjust the limit settings by following the instructions in your owner’s manual or by calling a technician.

Conclusion

If your garage door won’t open all the way, it could be due to a variety of reasons. The most common causes are a problem with the springs or track, an obstruction, or a remote malfunction. Before you call a technician, you should attempt to troubleshoot the issue yourself by checking for any obstructions, inspecting the springs and rollers, checking the tracks, checking the remote, and checking the limit settings. If you can’t figure out the cause of the problem, you should contact a professional for help.
