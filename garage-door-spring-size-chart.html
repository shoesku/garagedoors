Garage Door Spring Size Chart: The Definitive Guide For 2023

Do you know the size of your garage door spring? If you don’t, don’t worry. You’re not alone. In fact, it’s quite common for homeowners to not be aware of the size of their springs. But if you’re planning on replacing your garage door springs, it’s important to know the size. In this article, we’ll help you understand the different types of garage door springs, how to measure them, and where to find a garage door spring size chart.

Types of Garage Door Springs

There are two main types of garage door springs – torsion springs and extension springs. Torsion springs are mounted on a metal shaft above the garage door and are wound up to provide lift. Extension springs are located above the upper tracks on either side of the door and extend as the door closes. Both types of springs last for an average of 10,000 cycles, or about seven to nine years.

Measuring Your Garage Door Springs

To determine the size of your garage door springs, you’ll need to measure them. To measure a torsion spring, you’ll need to measure the inside diameter, wire size, and length. To measure an extension spring, you’ll need to measure the inside diameter and length. Make sure to measure all the springs, as there are usually two, one on either side of the door.

Finding a Garage Door Spring Size Chart

Once you’ve got all the measurements, you can refer to a garage door spring size chart to find the right size spring for your door. Most garage door spring suppliers will have a size chart on their website. Simply enter the measurements into the chart and it will give you the size of the spring you need. It’s important to make sure you get the right size, as a spring that’s too strong may cause damage to the door, while a spring that’s too weak won’t be able to lift the door.

Conclusion

Knowing the size of your garage door springs is essential if you’re planning on replacing them. Thankfully, measuring the springs and finding a size chart is a fairly easy process. With the right size springs, you’ll be able to get your garage door back in working order in no time.
