Troubleshooting Liftmaster Garage Door Flashing Arrows

It's frustrating when your Liftmaster garage door opener starts flashing arrows. It can be difficult to determine why the lights are flashing and what you need to do to make them stop. Fortunately, there are a few troubleshooting steps you can take to get your garage door opener working again.

Common Causes of Garage Door Flashing Arrows

If the arrows on your Liftmaster garage door opener are flashing, it could be caused by several different things. Here are some of the most common causes of flashing arrows on a garage door opener:

  • The garage door opener has been unplugged.
  • The wall switch for the garage door opener has been switched off.
  • The safety sensors at the base of the garage door are not aligned properly.
  • The opener has been overloaded or has experienced a power surge.
  • The door is stuck in the closed position.
  • The remote control battery is dead.
  • The wall switch is not working properly.

Troubleshooting Steps to Fix the Flashing Arrows

Once you have identified the cause of the flashing arrows, you can take the following steps to fix the problem:

  • Check the power cord of the garage door opener and make sure it is plugged in.
  • Check the wall switch and make sure it is switched on.
  • Check the safety sensors at the base of the door and make sure they are properly aligned.
  • Unplug the garage door opener and plug it back in after a few minutes.
  • Try to manually open the door and make sure it is not stuck in the closed position.
  • Replace the battery in the remote control.
  • Replace the wall switch if it is not working properly.

Conclusion

If the arrows on your Liftmaster garage door opener are flashing, don't panic. There are a few simple troubleshooting steps you can take to get your garage door opener working again. Check the power cord, wall switch, safety sensors, and remote control to make sure everything is in working order. If all else fails, contact a professional garage door repair technician for help.
