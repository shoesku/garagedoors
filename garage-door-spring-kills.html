Garage Door Spring Kills - A Serious Home Safety Hazard

Garage door springs are a common sight in homes all around the world. They are used to open and close the door to your garage and provide a convenient way to get in and out of the garage without having to manually lift the door. However, these springs can be dangerous when they are not properly installed or maintained. If a garage door spring is not handled correctly, it can cause serious injury or even death.

Garage door springs are typically made of steel or aluminum and can be quite powerful. The average garage door spring can exert up to 200 pounds of force, which is enough to cause serious injury or even death if someone were to be struck by it. In addition, the springs can also cause property damage if they should break while the door is in motion. It is important to be aware of the potential dangers posed by the garage door spring when it is in use and to take the necessary safety precautions.

Here Are Five Tips to Avoid Garage Door Spring Kills

1. Make sure to inspect the springs regularly - The best way to ensure the safety of your garage door springs is to inspect them regularly. Look for signs of wear and tear, as well as any loose or broken components. If you find any of these issues, it is important to have the springs replaced as soon as possible.

2. Have a professional install the springs - It is best to have a professional install the garage door springs for you. This will ensure that the springs are properly installed and that there are no safety issues that could arise from an improper installation.

3. Make sure to lubricate the springs regularly - Garage door springs should be lubricated at least once a year. This will help to reduce the risk of the springs breaking due to wear and tear. It is also important to make sure that the springs are properly lubricated to prevent them from becoming too stiff and causing the door to malfunction.

4. Do not attempt to repair the springs yourself - If you find any issues with the garage door springs, it is important to have a professional repair them. Attempting to repair the springs yourself can be dangerous and may even cause further damage to the springs or the door.

5. Stay away from the springs when the door is in motion - It is important to stay away from the springs when the door is in motion. This is especially important for children and pets, as they are more likely to be injured if they are struck by the springs.

Conclusion

Garage door springs can be a serious safety hazard if they are not properly maintained and handled. It is important to be aware of the potential dangers posed by the springs and to take the necessary precautions to ensure the safety of your home and family. Following these tips, you can help to avoid garage door spring kills and keep your family safe.
