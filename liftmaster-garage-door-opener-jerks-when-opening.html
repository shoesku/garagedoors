What Causes a Liftmaster Garage Door Opener to Jerk When Opening?

If your Liftmaster garage door opener is jerking when opening, this is a sign of a problem. In most cases, the problem is caused by a worn-out garage door opener chain, worn-out gears, or a malfunctioning motor. In some cases, the issue is caused by an obstruction in the track or an issue with the wall switch. Whatever the cause, it's important to have the issue addressed quickly before more serious damage is done to the opener.

How to Troubleshoot a Liftmaster Garage Door Opener That Jerks When Opening

If you have a Liftmaster garage door opener that is jerking when opening, follow these steps to troubleshoot the issue:

  • Check the chain. If the chain is worn-out or stretched, it can cause the door to jerk when opening. To check the chain, open the door manually and inspect the chain for signs of wear. If the chain is worn-out, replace it.
  • Check the gears. If the gears are worn-out, they can cause the door to jerk when opening. To check the gears, open the door manually and inspect the gears for signs of wear. If the gears are worn-out, replace them.
  • Check the motor. If the motor is malfunctioning, it can cause the door to jerk when opening. To check the motor, open the door manually and inspect the motor for signs of wear. If the motor is malfunctioning, replace it.
  • Check the track. If there is an obstruction in the track, it can cause the door to jerk when opening. To check the track, open the door manually and inspect the track for signs of an obstruction. If there is an obstruction, remove it.
  • Check the wall switch. If the wall switch is malfunctioning, it can cause the door to jerk when opening. To check the wall switch, open the door manually and inspect the wall switch for signs of wear. If the wall switch is malfunctioning, replace it.

What to Do If the Problem Persists

If you have tried all of the troubleshooting steps listed above and the problem is still occurring, it is time to contact a professional garage door repair technician. A professional technician can diagnose the problem and make the necessary repairs to get your Liftmaster garage door opener functioning properly.

Preventative Maintenance Tips

To help prevent your Liftmaster garage door opener from jerking when opening, there are a few preventative maintenance tips that you can follow. First, inspect the chain, gears, and motor every six months and replace them as needed. Second, inspect the track every six months and remove any obstructions. Finally, inspect the wall switch every six months and replace it as needed.

How to Find a Professional Garage Door Repair Technician

If you are having problems with your Liftmaster garage door opener, it is important to find a professional garage door repair technician to diagnose and fix the issue. To find a qualified technician, start by asking friends, family, and neighbors for recommendations. You can also search online for local technicians in your area. Make sure to check out the technician's website, read reviews, and compare prices before making a decision.

Conclusion

If your Liftmaster garage door opener is jerking when opening, it is important to troubleshoot the issue and make the necessary repairs to get it functioning properly. Follow the steps outlined above to troubleshoot and repair your Liftmaster garage door opener. And if the problem persists, contact a professional garage door repair technician for assistance. Finally, follow the preventative maintenance tips listed above to help prevent future problems with your Liftmaster garage door opener.
