What To Do When Chamberlain Garage Door Opener Closes And Opens Again?

If you’ve been dealing with your Chamberlain garage door opener closing and opening again on its own, you’re not alone. This issue is common among garage door owners and can be a result of a few different things. To help you troubleshoot the problem and gain control of your garage door again, we’ve put together a guide on what to do when your Chamberlain garage door opener closes and opens again.

Checking the Safety Sensor

The first step to take when dealing with a Chamberlain garage door opener that keeps opening and closing is to check the safety sensors. Most Chamberlain garage door openers come with a set of photoelectric sensors that are designed to detect when something is in the way of the garage door. If the sensors are blocked, then the door will close and then open again, as if it’s stuck in a loop.

To check the sensors, look at the LED lights on the sensors. If they’re both lit up, then the sensors are in good working condition. If only one of the lights is lit, then the sensor is blocked and you’ll need to move whatever is blocking it in order for your garage door to open and close properly.

Checking the Track Alignment

Another possible issue that could be causing your Chamberlain garage door opener to open and close again is a misaligned track. If the track is misaligned, then it can cause the garage door to jam or stick, which can cause the door to open and close again. To check the alignment of your track, look for any visible gaps in the track.

If you see a gap, then you’ll need to fix it. The best way to do this is to use a level and a hammer to gently tap the track back into place. If the track is severely misaligned, then you may need to call a professional to come and realign the track for you.

Checking the Motor

If the safety sensor and track are both in good working condition, then the issue may lie within the motor. If your Chamberlain garage door opener is old or has been used heavily, then the motor may be worn out and need to be replaced. You can check the motor by listening for any odd sounds or vibrations when the garage door is in operation.

If you hear any strange noises or vibrations, then the motor may need to be replaced. You can either do this yourself or have a professional come and do it for you. Whichever option you choose, make sure that you purchase a motor that is compatible with your Chamberlain garage door opener.

Checking the Remote Control

Another possible issue that could be causing your Chamberlain garage door opener to open and close again is a faulty remote control. If the remote control is not working properly, then the door may not be able to open and close correctly. To check the remote control, make sure that the batteries are still good and that the remote control is not too far away from the garage door.

You can also try resetting the remote control. To do this, press and hold the ‘learn’ button on the motor unit for a few seconds. This should reset the remote control and allow it to work correctly. If the remote control is still not working, then you may need to purchase a new one.

Checking the Spring Tension

If none of the above steps have worked, then the issue may be due to the tension in the springs. If the springs are too tight or too loose, then the door may not be able to open and close properly. To check the tension of the springs, you’ll need to use a torque wrench. First, loosen the tension of the springs, then measure the torque with the torque wrench.

If the torque is too high, then you’ll need to loosen the tension of the springs. If the torque is too low, then you’ll need to tighten the tension of the springs. Once the torque is at the correct level, the garage door should be able to open and close properly.

Conclusion

Dealing with a Chamberlain garage door opener that keeps opening and closing can be frustrating, but it doesn’t have to be. By following the steps outlined above, you should be able to troubleshoot the issue and gain control of your garage door again. If you’re still having trouble, then you may need to call a professional to come and take a look at your Chamberlain garage door opener.
