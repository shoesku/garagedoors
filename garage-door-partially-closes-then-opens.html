When You Have an Automatic Garage Door

If your garage door only partially closes then opens again, it can be a frustrating experience. Fortunately, the problem can usually be resolved with a few simple troubleshooting steps. If you have an automatic garage door, the first step is to check the settings on your door’s motor. Many automatic garage doors have a setting that allows the door to only partially close, and then open again if it detects an obstacle. If this is the case, you’ll need to adjust the setting on your door’s motor so that it will close all the way.

Next, check to see if there’s something blocking the door from closing. Look for items that are obstructing the sensors or the tracks. If you find something, remove it and try closing the door again. If the door still only partially closes, it may be due to a misalignment of the sensors or the tracks.

If the sensors and tracks are properly aligned, the problem could be due to a broken spring or cable. When these components break, the door won’t be able to open and close properly. If you suspect that this is the case, it’s best to call a professional to come and inspect the door.

When You Have a Manual Garage Door

If you have a manual garage door, the first step is to check the door’s track and make sure that it’s free of any obstructions. If the track is clear, then the next step is to check the door’s balance. If the door is out of balance, it won’t be able to close properly.

If the door is in balance, the problem could be due to a worn-out roller or hinge. When these components become worn, the door won’t be able to close properly. If you suspect that this is the case, it’s best to call a professional to come and inspect the door.

Finally, check to see if the door is closing evenly. If the door is closing unevenly, it could be due to a problem with the springs or cables. If you suspect that this is the case, it’s best to call a professional to come and inspect the door.

Preventative Maintenance for Your Garage Door

If you find yourself dealing with a garage door that won’t close properly, it’s important to take the time to do some preventative maintenance on the door. This will help to ensure that the problem doesn’t happen again in the future.

First, check to make sure that the sensors and tracks are properly aligned. If they’re not, they should be fixed. Next, check to make sure that the door’s springs and cables are in good condition. If they’re worn out, they should be replaced. Finally, check to make sure that the door’s rollers and hinges are in good condition. If they’re worn out, they should be replaced.

By taking the time to do some basic preventative maintenance on your garage door, you’ll help to ensure that it operates properly for years to come. If you find that you’re still having trouble with your door, it’s best to call a professional to come and inspect the door.
