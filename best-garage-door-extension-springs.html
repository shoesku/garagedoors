What are Garage Door Extension Springs?

Garage door extension springs are a specific type of spring that is designed to assist in the opening and closing of the garage door. They are typically made of a strong, durable material such as steel, and are designed to be able to withstand a significant amount of stress and strain. They are attached to the door itself and are used to help the door open and close smoothly and efficiently. They are also known as extension torsion springs and are often used in conjunction with an opener or motor to provide extra assistance when opening and closing the door.

Types of Garage Door Extension Springs

There are two main types of garage door extension springs: torsion springs and side-mounted springs. Torsion springs are designed to be wound and unwound in order to provide the necessary force to open and close the door, while side-mounted springs are mounted on the side of the door and are designed to provide a more steady force for the door. Both types of springs are designed to be able to withstand a significant amount of wear and tear, and should be replaced regularly in order to ensure that the garage door system is functioning properly.

How to Choose the Best Garage Door Extension Springs

When selecting the best garage door extension springs, it is important to consider the size and weight of the door, as well as the type of opening system you have in place. Different types of springs are designed to handle different types of doors, and selecting the wrong type of spring can lead to significant problems with the garage door system. Additionally, it is important to select a spring that is able to handle the weight and force of the door, as well as the frequency of opening and closing. Finally, it is important to ensure that the spring is made of a durable material that is able to withstand the elements and provide long-lasting performance.
