The Dangers Of Garage Door Springs: What You Need To Know

Garage door springs are a necessary component to any functioning garage door, but they can also be incredibly dangerous. In fact, garage door springs are responsible for a startling number of deaths and injuries each year. It’s important to understand the risks associated with garage door springs and how to avoid them. Here’s what you need to know.

How Garage Door Springs Work

Garage door springs are usually made of steel and are designed to counterbalance the weight of the door. They’re wound tight, so when the door is opened, they’re under tremendous pressure. The tightness of the springs is what allows the door to open and close smoothly.

When garage doors are opened manually, the door is lifted by the operator, who is usually able to control the speed at which the door is opened. However, when a garage door is opened with an opener, the springs are wound tight, and the opener releases the tension in a split second. This sudden release of tension causes the door to quickly snap open.

Common Causes of Garage Door Spring Deaths

Garage door spring deaths can be caused by several different factors. One of the most common causes is when the springs are not properly maintained. If the springs are not regularly checked and lubricated, they can become worn and weaken over time. This can cause the springs to break or snap, resulting in a deadly situation.

Another common cause of death is when the springs are tampered with. Many people try to repair or adjust their own garage door springs, which can be incredibly dangerous. Springs are under immense pressure and can cause serious injury or death if handled improperly. It’s best to leave any repairs or adjustments to a professional.

Preventing Garage Door Spring Deaths

The best way to prevent a garage door spring death is to make sure the springs are regularly inspected and maintained. This can be done by a professional who is experienced in dealing with garage door springs. They will be able to check the springs for any signs of wear and tear and replace them if necessary.

In addition, it’s important to make sure that the springs are not tampered with. If you need to make any repairs or adjustments, it’s best to leave it to a professional. This will help to ensure that the springs are not damaged or weakened, which can lead to a deadly situation.

Conclusion

Garage door springs can be incredibly dangerous and are responsible for a significant number of deaths and injuries each year. It’s important to make sure that the springs are regularly inspected and maintained, and that any repairs or adjustments are left to a professional. By taking these steps, you can help to ensure that your garage is as safe as possible.
