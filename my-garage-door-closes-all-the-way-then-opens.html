How To Fix A Garage Door That Closes All The Way Then Reopens

Best Garage doors and references for house or residential with custom material and more design. We Share more picture design with HD quality images.

A malfunctioning garage door can be a major inconvenience, preventing you from getting your car out when you need it and leaving your home vulnerable to intruders. One common issue is when the door closes all the way then reopens. This is often caused by a problem with the limit switch, which is responsible for telling the motor when to stop. Fortunately, this is something that you can fix yourself, without having to call a professional.

Check the limit switch

The limit switch is a small box located on the side of the motor and it tells the motor when to stop. If the switch is misaligned, the motor will not know when to stop, resulting in the door closing all the way then reopening. To fix this, you will need to adjust the limit switch.

Adjust the limit switch

To adjust the limit switch, first locate the screws that are used to hold it in place. You can usually find these on the side of the motor. Use a screwdriver to loosen the screws, then gently slide the switch up or down. You may need to try a few different positions before you find the one that works. Once you have found the correct position, tighten the screws and test the door. If it still does not work, you may need to adjust the switch again.

Check the sensors

The sensors are located near the bottom of the garage door and they are responsible for detecting any obstructions in the way of the door. If the sensors are not working properly, the door may close all the way and then reopen. To fix this, you will need to check the sensors and make sure they are correctly aligned. If they are not, you can adjust them by loosening the screws on the side and then moving them up or down.

Check the track

The track is the metal rail that the door rolls up and down on. If the track is bent or misaligned, the door may not be able to close properly. To fix this, you will need to check the track and make sure it is straight. If it is not, you can use a hammer to gently bend it back into place. You may also need to use a file to remove any burrs or sharp edges that could be preventing the door from closing properly.

Check the springs

The springs are responsible for providing the power to open and close the door. If the springs are worn or damaged, they may not be providing enough power. To check the springs, you will need to look for any signs of wear or corrosion. If you find any, you will need to replace them. Replacing the springs can be a difficult job, so it is best to call a professional for help.

Check the motor

The motor is the device that powers the door. If the motor is not working properly, it may be the cause of the problem. To check the motor, you will need to disconnect the power and then open the cover. Check the wires and make sure they are all connected properly. If they are not, you may need to replace them. If the wires are connected properly, you can test the motor by connecting it to a power source and seeing if it works.

Conclusion

If your garage door closes all the way then reopens, there are several things you can check to try and fix the problem. You can start by checking the limit switch, sensors, track, springs, and motor. If these do not fix the issue, you may need to call a professional for help.
