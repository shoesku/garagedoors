How To Use An Overhead Garage Door Emergency Release

Focused keyword is "overhead garage door emergency release"

Your overhead garage door is one of the most important parts of your home. Not only does it provide you with easy access to your garage, but it also serves as an extra layer of security for your home. When there is a malfunction with your overhead garage door, you may find yourself in an emergency situation where you need to use the emergency release. It's important to know how to properly use the emergency release in order to ensure the safety of your home and those inside.

What is an Overhead Garage Door Emergency Release?

An overhead garage door emergency release is a mechanism that allows you to manually open the door when the power is out or the garage door opener is not functioning. The emergency release is usually located near the top of the door and consists of a handle or lever and a cable that runs from the handle to the motor. It is designed to be used as a last resort in the event of a power outage or malfunctioning garage door opener.

How Does the Overhead Garage Door Emergency Release Work?

The overhead garage door emergency release works by disengaging the motor from the garage door. When the handle or lever is pulled, the cable connected to the motor is disengaged, allowing you to open and close the door manually. Depending on the model of your garage door, there may be a series of steps to follow in order to properly disengage the motor.

Steps for Properly Using an Overhead Garage Door Emergency Release

Step 1: Locate the Emergency Release Handle

The first step to using an overhead garage door emergency release is to locate the handle or lever. Depending on the model of your garage door, the handle or lever may be located near the bottom, middle, or top of the door. If you cannot locate it, look for a small, metal handle or lever near the top of the door.

Step 2: Pull the Emergency Release Handle

Once you have located the emergency release handle, you will need to pull it. Pull the handle or lever until you hear it click and the garage door is disconnected from the motor. Depending on the model of your garage door, you may need to pull the handle or lever several times in order to properly disengage the motor.

Step 3: Open the Garage Door Manually

Once you have successfully disengaged the motor, you can open the garage door manually. You will need to lift the door up and then swing it open. Depending on the size and weight of the door, you may need help in order to open it properly. Make sure to open the door slowly and carefully to avoid any damage to the door or the motor.

Step 4: Close the Garage Door Manually

When you are finished, you will need to close the door manually. Make sure to close the door slowly and carefully to avoid any damage to the door or the motor. Once the door is closed, you can re-engage the motor by pushing the emergency release handle or lever back in until it clicks.

Conclusion

Knowing how to properly use an overhead garage door emergency release is essential for any homeowner. It is important to remember that the emergency release should only be used as a last resort, as it can cause damage to the garage door and motor if used improperly. By following the steps outlined above, you can safely and effectively use the emergency release to open and close your overhead garage door.
