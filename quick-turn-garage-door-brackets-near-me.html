Types Of Garage Door Brackets

When it comes to garage door brackets, there are a few different types to choose from. The most common type is the standard screw-in bracket. This style requires you to screw the bracket into the wall and then attach it to the door. It’s typically the easiest style to install, since all you have to do is drill the holes, insert the screws, and then attach the bracket.

Another popular type of garage door bracket is the quick turn bracket. This style uses a bracket with a built-in spring system, which allows you to attach the bracket to the wall and then quickly turn it into place. This style is great for those who are short on time or those who want a quick installation.

The last type of bracket is the overhead spring bracket. This style uses a bracket that is attached to the garage door’s header and then connected to the spring system. This type of bracket is great for those who need extra support for their garage door.

The Installation Process

The installation process for each type of bracket is fairly similar. The first step is to measure the door and the opening to ensure that the bracket will fit properly. Once you’ve determined the proper size, you can then mark the spot where you need to drill the holes.

Next, you’ll need to drill the holes for the screws. Depending on the type of bracket you’re installing, you may need to use a special type of screw. Once the holes are drilled, you can then insert the screws and attach the bracket to the wall.

Finally, you’ll need to attach the bracket to the door. Depending on the type of bracket you have, you may need to use a special tool or a wrench to do this. Once the bracket is attached, you can then install the springs or other components as needed.
