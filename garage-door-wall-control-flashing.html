Troubleshooting Garage Door Wall Control Flashing

Having a garage door wall control flashing can be a real nuisance, especially if you’re not sure what’s causing it. It’s important to know why your wall control is flashing so that you can get it fixed before it becomes a larger problem.

In this article, we’ll discuss some of the potential causes of garage door wall control flashing and how to troubleshoot the issue. Read on to learn more about why your wall control might be flashing and the best ways to fix it.

What Causes Garage Door Wall Control Flashing?

There are several potential causes of garage door wall control flashing. Some of the most common causes include:

  • The wall control is not properly connected to the power source.
  • The garage door opener has an obstruction in its path.
  • The wall control is malfunctioning.
  • The wall control is not properly programmed.

How To Troubleshoot Garage Door Wall Control Flashing

If your garage door wall control is flashing, there are a few steps you can take to troubleshoot the issue and get it fixed. Here are some of the most effective troubleshooting methods for garage door wall control flashing:

Check The Power Source

The first thing you should do is check the power source to make sure it is properly connected to the wall control. If the power source is disconnected or not properly connected, this could be causing the wall control to flash. Make sure the power source is connected securely and that the wall control is receiving power.

Check For Obstructions

If the power source is properly connected, then you should check for any obstructions that might be blocking the garage door opener’s path. Objects such as toys, tools, and furniture can all obstruct the path and cause the wall control to flash. Make sure to clear away any obstructions in the garage door opener’s path before continuing.

Check The Wall Control

If the power source and path are both clear, then you should check the wall control itself. The wall control may be malfunctioning or not properly programmed. To check the wall control, you should first check the programming instructions to make sure it is properly programmed. If the wall control is still flashing after you’ve checked the programming, then it’s likely that the wall control itself is malfunctioning and needs to be replaced.

Conclusion

Garage door wall control flashing can be a real nuisance, but it’s important to know why your wall control is flashing so that you can get it fixed before it becomes a larger problem. By following the troubleshooting tips in this article, you can quickly identify the cause of the issue and get it fixed. We hope this article has been helpful in troubleshooting your garage door wall control flashing.
