Troubleshooting Your Garage Door Spring To Avoid Constant Breakage

If you have recently experienced a garage door spring breaking, you know the frustration that comes along with it. When your garage door won’t open or close, it’s an inconvenience to you and your family. Not to mention, the repair cost can be a burden. Thankfully, there are some things you can do to help prevent the spring from breaking in the future.

Why Garage Door Springs Break

Garage door springs are responsible for lifting and lowering the garage door. As the springs are used, they naturally become worn and weakened over time. When this happens, they are more susceptible to breaking. There are a few common causes of garage door spring breakage:

  • Bad Installation – If the spring wasn’t installed correctly, it can cause it to break sooner than expected.
  • Rust – Exposure to moisture and other elements can cause the springs to rust and eventually break.
  • Over-Extension – If the springs are extended too far, they can become damaged and eventually break.
  • Age – The average lifespan of a garage door spring is 10,000 cycles. After this, it’s likely to break.

How to Avoid Constant Breakage

To help prevent garage door spring breakage, there are a few steps you can take. Some preventative measures you can take are:

  • Lubrication – Lubricate the springs monthly with a light oil. This will help reduce friction and keep the springs in good working order.
  • Inspection – Inspect the springs monthly to ensure they are in good condition. If you notice any cracks or rust, contact a professional for repair.
  • Maintenance – Have a professional inspect and maintain your garage door system on an annual basis. This will help detect any problems and extend the life of your garage door.
  • Replace – If the springs are more than 10 years old, it’s best to replace them. This will help ensure your safety and reduce the risk of breakage.

Repair or Replace?

If your garage door spring is already damaged, you may be wondering if you should repair or replace it. Generally, if the spring is more than 10 years old, you should replace it. This will help ensure your safety and prevent any further breakage. If the spring is less than 10 years old, you may be able to repair it. However, it’s best to have a professional inspect it first to determine the best course of action.

When to Call a Professional

If you’ve experienced multiple breakages, it’s best to call a professional. They can inspect the entire system and determine what’s causing the breakage. It’s important to have a professional repair or replace the springs to ensure your safety. Plus, they have the right tools and knowledge to get the job done right.

Conclusion

Garage door spring breakage can be a nuisance, but there are some steps you can take to help prevent it. Make sure to lubricate and inspect the springs monthly and have a professional inspect and maintain the system on an annual basis. If your garage door spring is already damaged, it’s best to have a professional repair or replace it. Following these steps can help you avoid constant breakage and keep your garage door working smoothly for years to come.
