Replacing Your Genie Garage Door Opener Parts

If you own an old Genie garage door opener, you may be looking to replace some of the parts. Genie garage door openers are known for their durability and longevity, but like any piece of machinery, they need maintenance and sometimes parts replacements. While Genie garage door openers come with a warranty, it’s always a good idea to know how to replace parts yourself, in case you ever need to. Here are some of the common parts that you may need to replace for your Genie garage door opener.

Replacing the Drive Belt in the Genie Garage Door Opener

The drive belt in a Genie garage door opener is what connects the motor to the drive shaft. It’s responsible for driving the motor and opening and closing the door. If you notice that your Genie garage door opener is making a lot of noise, it could be an indication that the belt is worn or damaged. Replacing the belt is fairly simple and only requires a few tools.

First, you’ll need to disconnect the power to the opener. Then, you’ll want to loosen the tension on the belt by unscrewing the tensioning bolt. Next, you’ll need to remove the old belt and replace it with the new one. Finally, you’ll need to re-tension the belt and reconnect the power.

Replacing the Drive Chain in the Genie Garage Door Opener

The drive chain in a Genie garage door opener is what connects the motor to the trolley. It’s responsible for translating the motor’s power into the force needed to open and close the door. If you notice that your Genie garage door opener is making a lot of noise, it could be an indication that the chain is worn or damaged. Replacing the chain is fairly simple and only requires a few tools.

First, you’ll need to disconnect the power to the opener. Next, you’ll need to remove the old chain and replace it with the new one. Finally, you’ll need to reconnect the power and test the opener to make sure it’s working properly.

Replacing the Limit Switches in the Genie Garage Door Opener

The limit switches in a Genie garage door opener are responsible for stopping the door when it reaches the end of its travel. If you notice that your Genie garage door opener is not opening or closing all the way, it could be an indication that the limit switches need to be adjusted or replaced. Replacing the limit switches is fairly simple and only requires a few tools.

First, you’ll need to disconnect the power to the opener. Next, you’ll need to locate the limit switches and remove them. Then, you’ll need to replace them with the new switches. Finally, you’ll need to reconnect the power and test the opener to make sure it’s working properly.

Replacing the Safety Sensor in the Genie Garage Door Opener

The safety sensor in a Genie garage door opener is responsible for stopping the door if it senses something in the way. If you notice that your Genie garage door opener is not opening or closing all the way, it could be an indication that the safety sensor needs to be replaced. Replacing the safety sensor is fairly simple and only requires a few tools.

First, you’ll need to disconnect the power to the opener. Next, you’ll need to locate the safety sensor and remove it. Then, you’ll need to replace it with the new sensor. Finally, you’ll need to reconnect the power and test the opener to make sure it’s working properly.

Replacing the Wall Button in the Genie Garage Door Opener

The wall button in a Genie garage door opener is responsible for initiating the opening and closing of the door. If you notice that your Genie garage door opener is not opening or closing when you press the button, it could be an indication that the wall button needs to be replaced. Replacing the wall button is fairly simple and only requires a few tools.

First, you’ll need to disconnect the power to the opener. Next, you’ll need to locate the wall button and remove it. Then, you’ll need to replace it with the new button. Finally, you’ll need to reconnect the power and test the opener to make sure it’s working properly.

Conclusion

Replacing Genie garage door opener parts is a relatively straightforward process that can be done by most DIYers. While it may seem intimidating at first, it’s actually quite simple once you understand the basics. And with a little patience and the right tools, you can replace any of the parts in your Genie garage door opener and get your door back in working order.
