How To Fix A Garage Door That Won't Fully Close

Best Garage doors and references for house or residential with custom material and more design. We Share more picture design with HD quality images.

Is your garage door not closing all the way down? It may be due to a variety of common factors, such as a worn-out motor, a misaligned track, or a faulty remote control. Whatever the reason, it's important to act quickly to fix the issue and ensure your home remains secure. In this article, we'll show you how to troubleshoot and fix a garage door that won't fully close.

Step 1: Check the Motor

The most common cause of a garage door not fully closing is a worn-out motor. Over time, the motor can become weak or burned out, resulting in the door not closing as it should. To check if the motor is the culprit, open and close your garage door manually. If the process is difficult, it's likely the motor is the issue.

If the motor is the problem, the best solution is to replace it. This can be done by consulting a professional garage door repair service. They will be able to assess the situation and provide you with an accurate quote for the repair.

Step 2: Check the Track

Another common cause of a garage door not fully closing is a misaligned track. If the track is bent or damaged, it can cause the door to become off-balance and not close properly. To inspect the track, open the garage door and look for any obvious signs of damage, such as dents or bends.

If you do find any bent or damaged sections, the best solution is to consult a professional. They will be able to accurately assess the situation and provide you with an accurate quote for the repair. If the track is beyond repair, it may need to be replaced.

Step 3: Check the Remote Control

If the motor and track are in good condition, the issue may be with the remote control. Over time, the remote control can become weak or worn out, resulting in the door not opening or closing as it should. To check if this is the issue, try using a different remote control, such as a universal remote. If the door opens and closes properly, then the remote control is likely the problem.

If the remote control is the issue, the best solution is to replace it. This can be done by consulting a professional garage door repair service. They will be able to accurately assess the situation and provide you with an accurate quote for the repair.

Step 4: Check the Springs

Finally, the issue may be with the springs. Over time, the springs can become weak or damaged, resulting in the door not opening or closing as it should. To check if this is the issue, open and close the garage door manually. If the process is difficult, it's likely the springs are the issue.

If the springs are the problem, the best solution is to replace them. This can be done by consulting a professional garage door repair service. They will be able to accurately assess the situation and provide you with an accurate quote for the repair.

Conclusion

In this article, we've shown you how to troubleshoot and fix a garage door that won't fully close. The most common causes are a worn-out motor, a misaligned track, a faulty remote control, or weak springs. If you do find any of these issues, the best solution is to consult a professional garage door repair service. They will be able to accurately assess the situation and provide you with an accurate quote for the repair.
