The Basics of Garage Door Lubricant

Many people have heard of lubricant, but many don’t know the specifics of it. Lubricants are substances that reduce friction between two surfaces and can be used in a variety of applications. In the case of garage doors, lubricant is used to reduce the amount of friction between the door and its mounting hardware, such as the track, springs, or rollers. This reduces stress on the components, increasing lifespan and reducing noise.

What Type of Lubricant Is Best For Garage Doors?

When it comes to lubricants, not all products are created equal. The best lubricants for garage doors are typically either silicone-based or white lithium-based. Silicone-based lubricants are good for both metal and plastic parts and won’t corrode or rust. White lithium-based lubricants are good for metal components, as they won’t corrode or rust, but should not be used on plastic parts.

Using Lubricant On Garage Doors

Once you’ve chosen the right type of lubricant for your garage door, it’s time to apply it. The first step is to make sure the door is in the closed position. Second, open the door and clean the springs, track, and rollers with a damp cloth. This will remove any dirt or debris that could prevent the lubricant from sticking.

How to Apply Lubricant

Once the surface is clean, it’s time to apply the lubricant. To do this, you will need a lubricant spray or a lubricant brush. If using a spray, point the nozzle at the springs, track, and rollers and spray in a sweeping motion. If using a brush, dip it in the lubricant and apply it to the springs, track, and rollers.

How Often Should I Lubricate My Garage Door?

It’s recommended that you lubricate your garage door at least once a year. This will ensure that the door continues to run smoothly and reduce noise. If you live in a particularly wet or humid climate, you may want to apply lubricant more frequently.

Benefits of Using Lubricant on Garage Doors

There are numerous benefits to using lubricant on your garage door. The most obvious benefit is that it reduces friction, which in turn reduces stress on the door’s components and increases its lifespan. Lubricant also reduces noise, making your garage a quieter place. Finally, lubricant makes your door easier to open and close.

Final Thoughts

Lubricating your garage door is a simple and effective way to reduce noise and make your door run more smoothly. It’s important to choose the right type of lubricant and apply it properly. With the right lubricant and a little bit of maintenance, your garage door can last for years to come.
