How Much Does A Sectional Garage Door Cost?

Introduction

If you’re in the market for a new garage door, you’ve probably already heard of sectional garage doors. Sectional garage doors are becoming increasingly popular due to their improved security, insulation and overall look. But one of the biggest questions people have when considering a sectional garage door is: “how much does a sectional garage door cost?”

The truth is, the cost of sectional garage doors varies widely, depending on the size, material, and features of the door. In this article, we’ll break down the factors that impact the cost of sectional garage doors, so you can make an informed decision when it comes to choosing the right door for your home.

Size Matters

The size of your garage door will play a large role in the overall cost of the door. Generally speaking, the larger the door, the more expensive it will be. This is because larger doors require larger springs and other components to support their weight. The more components a door requires, the more it will cost.

In addition to the size of your garage door, the size of the opening will also play a role in the cost. If the opening of your garage door is not perfectly square, or if the structure of the opening is not perfectly aligned, you may need to hire a professional to modify the opening to accommodate a sectional garage door. This can add to the overall cost of the door.

Material Matters

The material of your sectional garage door will also affect the cost. Generally, the more durable the material, the more expensive the door will be. For example, steel sectional garage doors are usually more expensive than aluminum or wood doors. Steel is more durable and requires less maintenance, making it a popular choice for many homeowners.

In addition to the material, the type of insulation the door uses will also affect the cost. Insulated sectional garage doors are more expensive than non-insulated doors, but often offer better energy efficiency and noise reduction. If you’re looking for a more energy-efficient door, an insulated sectional garage door may be the right choice for you.

Features Matter

The features of your sectional garage door will also impact the cost of the door. For example, doors with added security features, like reinforced locks and deadbolts, will cost more than those without. If you’re looking for extra security, you may want to consider a door with these features.

Another feature that can add to the cost of the door is automation. Automated sectional garage doors are more expensive than manual doors, but they offer the convenience of being able to open and close the door from a remote location. If you’re looking for added convenience, an automated sectional garage door may be the right choice for you.

Cost Estimates

Now that you know the factors that affect the cost of a sectional garage door, you’re probably wondering what the cost estimates are. The cost of a sectional garage door can range from as little as $500 for a basic, non-insulated door, to as much as $2,500 or more for a fully-automated, insulated door.

The cost of a sectional garage door will depend on the size, material, and features of the door, so it’s important to do your research before making a purchase. By taking the time to consider the factors that affect the cost of the door, you can ensure that you make the right choice for your home.

Conclusion

Sectional garage doors are becoming increasingly popular due to their improved security, insulation and overall look. But one of the biggest questions people have when considering a sectional garage door is: “how much does a sectional garage door cost?” The truth is, the cost of sectional garage doors varies widely, depending on the size, material, and features of the door. The cost of a sectional garage door can range from as little as $500 for a basic, non-insulated door, to as much as $2,500 or more for a fully-automated, insulated door. By taking the time to consider the factors that affect the cost of the door, you can ensure that you make the right choice for your home.
