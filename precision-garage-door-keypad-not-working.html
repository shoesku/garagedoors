Precision Garage Door Keypad Not Working? Here’s What You Can Do!

For many homeowners, a garage door keypad is a great way to grant access to the garage without having to use a physical key or a smart phone app. But what do you do when the keypad isn't working? Here are some tips on how to troubleshoot a malfunctioning door keypad, and what to do if the problem persists.

Check the Batteries

The first step to troubleshooting any malfunctioning door keypad should always be to check the batteries. Most garage door keypads use AA or AAA batteries, and these batteries will need to be replaced periodically. If the batteries are not the issue, the next step is to check the wiring.

Check the Wiring

Most garage door keypads are wired directly into the door itself, which means that any wires that are frayed or damaged could be the cause of the keypad not working. If the wiring looks fine, then the next step is to check the receiver.

Check the Receiver

The receiver is the device that receives the signal from the keypad and then tells the door what to do. If the receiver is malfunctioning, it could be preventing the door from responding to the keypad. If the receiver appears to be working properly, then the problem could be a faulty keypad.

Replace the Keypad

If the batteries, wiring, and receiver are all functioning properly, then the keypad itself may be the culprit. Replacing the keypad should solve the problem, but it is important to make sure that the new keypad is compatible with the existing system.

Check the Sensors

Most modern garage door systems have safety sensors that detect when something is obstructing the door. If these sensors are not working properly, it could cause the door to malfunction and not respond to the keypad. Checking the sensors and making sure they are properly aligned is essential for a functioning door.

Check the Alignment

The alignment of the door itself can also affect the functioning of the keypad. If the door is off-kilter, it can cause the keypad to malfunction. Checking the alignment of the door and making sure that it is properly aligned is a good first step in troubleshooting a malfunctioning door keypad.

Check the Programming

Many garage door keypads are programmed to respond to user-defined codes. If the programming of the keypad has been changed, it could cause it to malfunction. Checking the programming and making sure that it is properly configured is essential for a functioning keypad.

Check the Frequency

Finally, if the batteries, wiring, receiver, keypad, sensors, and programming are all functioning properly, then the problem may be with the frequency of the keypad. If the keypad is sending a signal that is not compatible with the receiver, it could cause the door to malfunction. Checking the frequency and making sure that it is compatible with the receiver is essential for a functioning door.

Contact a Professional

If all else fails, it may be time to contact a professional. A qualified technician can diagnose and repair any malfunctioning keypad, no matter the cause. It is important to make sure that the technician is experienced and trained in garage door keypad repair. Precision garage door keypad not working can be a frustrating problem. But with a little troubleshooting and the help of a qualified technician, the problem can be solved quickly and easily. With the right tools and knowledge, you can get your garage door keypad functioning properly in no time.
