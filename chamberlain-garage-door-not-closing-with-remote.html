Troubleshooting A Chamberlain Garage Door That Won't Close With The Remote

Best Garage doors and references for house or residential with custom material and more design. We Share more picture design with HD quality images.

Having a garage door that won't close with the remote can be a major inconvenience. It can be especially annoying if the door suddenly stops working after it had been working fine for a long time. Fortunately, the issue can usually be resolved quickly and easily. In this article, we'll look at the most common causes of a Chamberlain garage door not closing with the remote, and how you can fix the problem.

Check the Batteries

The most likely culprit is a dead or dying battery. Many people forget to check the batteries in their remote, and they can become drained without you even noticing. Take out the batteries from your Chamberlain garage door remote and check to see if they need to be replaced. If the batteries are dead, replace them with new ones and see if that resolves the issue. If the issue persists, then continue with the troubleshooting steps.

Check the Range

Another potential cause of the issue is that the remote is out of range of the receiver. Chamberlain garage door openers come with a remote range of up to 100 feet, so make sure you're within that range when attempting to open or close the door. If you're too far away, the signal may not be strong enough to reach the receiver.

Check for Obstructions

Another common issue is that the signal from the remote is being blocked or obstructed by something. This could be anything from furniture to walls to other electronics. Try moving the remote around to different locations and see if the issue is resolved. If you're still having trouble, then it's time to move on to the next step.

Check the Remote Frequency

If the issue is still persisting, then it's possible that the remote is not on the same frequency as the receiver. Chamberlain garage door openers come with a default frequency of 315 MHz, but it's possible that the frequency has been changed. You can check the frequency of your remote by opening the battery compartment and looking for the frequency printed on the inside. If the frequency is different than the default 315 MHz, then you'll need to reprogram the remote.

Reprogram the Remote

Reprogramming the remote is relatively simple and can be done in just a few steps. First, you'll need to locate the "Learn" button on your Chamberlain garage door opener. This is typically located near the antenna. Once you've found the button, press it and hold it for about 6 seconds. This will put the receiver into "Learn" mode. Next, press the button on your remote and hold it for about 6 seconds. This will sync the remote to the receiver. Once the remote has been programmed, you should be able to open and close your garage door with the remote.

Check the Wall Switch

If you're still having trouble opening or closing your garage door with the remote, then it's time to check the wall switch. The wall switch is the button that is mounted on the wall inside the garage. Make sure the wall switch is in the "on" position. If it's not, then turn it on and see if the issue is resolved. If it is on, then it's time to move on to the next step.

Check the Opener

If the wall switch is on, then it's possible that the opener itself is not working properly. This can be due to a number of different issues, including a faulty circuit board or a worn-out motor. If this is the case, then it's best to contact a professional to take a look at the opener and determine what needs to be done to fix it.

Conclusion

If your Chamberlain garage door won't close with the remote, then there are a few potential causes that you can check. Start by checking the batteries in the remote and replacing them if necessary. You should also check the range, look for obstructions, and check the frequency of the remote. If all else fails, then you may need to reprogram the remote or even replace the opener itself. Hopefully, this article has been helpful in troubleshooting your Chamberlain garage door issue. Good luck!
