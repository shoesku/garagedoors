How To Fix A Jammed Garage Door That Won't Open

Best Garage doors and references for house or residential with custom material and more design. We Share more picture design with HD quality images.

When you are trying to open your garage door but it won't budge, it can be an incredibly frustrating experience. Whether you're late for work or trying to get the car out to go on a trip, the last thing you want to deal with is a jammed garage door. Luckily, there are some easy steps you can take to get your garage door back in working order.

Step 1: Check the Track and Rollers

The first thing you should do when trying to fix a jammed garage door is to check the track and rollers. The track is what the rollers sit in and is responsible for guiding the door up and down. The rollers are the small wheels that roll along the track. If either the track or rollers are damaged or bent, this could prevent the door from moving. Take a look at the track and rollers and make sure they are in good condition. If they look okay, move on to the next step.

Step 2: Lubricate the Track and Rollers

If everything looks okay with the track and rollers, the next step is to lubricate them. Lubricating the track and rollers can help reduce friction, allowing the door to move more freely. You should use a lubricant specifically designed for garage doors, as it will provide the best protection. Spray the lubricant onto the track and rollers and then use a rag to wipe away any excess. Once you are done, the garage door should move more smoothly.

Step 3: Check the Motor and Drive Chain

If lubricating the track and rollers didn't work, the next step is to check the motor and drive chain. The motor and drive chain are responsible for lifting and lowering the garage door. If either is damaged or malfunctioning, it can cause the door to jam. Check the motor and drive chain for any signs of damage and make sure they are properly lubricated. If everything looks okay, move on to the next step.

Step 4: Check the Springs

The springs are responsible for providing the power to the motor and drive chain and if they are not functioning properly, they can cause the garage door to jam. Check the springs for any signs of wear and tear and make sure they are properly lubricated. If everything looks okay, move on to the next step.

Step 5: Check the Cables

The cables are responsible for connecting the motor and drive chain to the door and if they are damaged, it can cause the door to jam. Check the cables for any signs of wear and tear and make sure they are properly lubricated. If everything looks okay, move on to the next step.

Step 6: Check the Limit Switch

The limit switch is responsible for controlling the motor and if it is damaged, it can cause the garage door to jam. Check the limit switch for any signs of wear and tear and make sure it is properly lubricated. If everything looks okay, move on to the next step.

Step 7: Check the Door Sensors

The door sensors are responsible for detecting any obstructions in the path of the door and if they are damaged, it can cause the garage door to jam. Check the sensors for any signs of wear and tear and make sure they are properly lubricated. If everything looks okay, move on to the next step.

Step 8: Call a Professional

If none of the above steps have worked and your garage door is still jammed, it may be time to call a professional. A professional will be able to diagnose the problem and make the necessary repairs. They will also be able to advise you on any preventative maintenance that needs to be done to help ensure your garage door is running smoothly.

Conclusion

If your garage door is jammed and won't open, don't panic. There are several steps you can take to try and fix the problem yourself. Start by checking the track and rollers, lubricating them, and then checking the motor and drive chain, springs, cables, limit switch, and door sensors. If none of these steps work, it may be time to call a professional for help.
