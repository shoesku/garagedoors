How To Lift A Garage Door Without Power

Introduction

Garage doors can be a great convenience for any homeowner, allowing them to access their garage without having to manually lift the door. However, what happens when the power goes out and suddenly you can’t open your garage door? In this article, we’ll discuss how to lift a garage door without power and provide some tips for ensuring you can still access your garage in a power outage.

Step 1: Check the Lock Mechanism

Before you attempt to lift the garage door, you should check to make sure it is not locked. If the door is locked, you will not be able to open it manually. You can usually check the lock mechanism by pulling on the release handle, which is typically located near the top of the door. If the door is unlocked, you should be able to pull the release handle and then lift the door.

Step 2: Gather Assistance

Next, it’s important to gather some assistance before attempting to lift the garage door. This is especially true if the door is large or heavy. Having another person to help you lift the door will make the process much easier and safer. If you can’t find someone to help you, you can use a broomstick or other long pole to help lift the door.

Step 3: Locate the Lifting Points

Once you have gathered some assistance, you can then locate the lifting points on the garage door. These are typically located near the top corners of the garage door and are used to lift the door. If you can’t find the lifting points, you can also use a broomstick or other long pole to help lift the door.

Step 4: Lift the Door

Now that you’ve located the lifting points and gathered some assistance, you can begin to lift the garage door. Make sure you are lifting slowly and evenly to avoid damaging the door or injuring yourself. Once the door is lifted, you can then pull it away from the garage and open it manually. It may take some effort to open the door, so be sure to take your time and take frequent breaks if needed.

Step 5: Secure the Door

Once you’ve opened the garage door, you should then secure it in place. This can be done by using a broomstick or other long pole to wedge the door open. You should also use a rope or chain to tie the door open in case the pole slips or the door begins to close.

Conclusion

Lifting a garage door without power can be a daunting task, but it can be done with the right preparation and assistance. Just remember to check the lock mechanism, gather assistance, locate the lifting points, lift the door, and secure the door in place. With these tips, you should be able to open your garage door in no time!
