Garage door springs are one of the most important parts of a garage door system. Without them, the garage door will not open or close properly. Garage door spring parts can be a tricky thing to understand, so we’ve put together this guide to help you understand the different types of springs and how they work.

Types of Garage Door Springs

There are two main types of garage door springs: torsion springs and extension springs. Torsion springs are the most common type of garage door spring. They are mounted on a metal shaft above the garage door opening and use a twisting force to lift the garage door. Extension springs are mounted on either side of the garage door and use tension to open and close the door.

How Garage Door Springs Work

Garage door springs are what make it possible for a garage door to open and close. When the door is closed, the springs are under tension, and when the door is open, the springs are relaxed. When the door is opened, the springs are released and the door is lifted by the tension. When the door is closed, the springs are re-tensioned and the door is lowered.

Installation of Garage Door Springs

Installing garage door springs is a job best left to professionals. Garage door springs are under a great deal of tension and can be dangerous to work with if you don’t know what you’re doing. It is important that the springs are installed correctly to ensure that the garage door operates properly and safely.

Maintenance of Garage Door Springs

Regular maintenance and inspection of your garage door springs is essential to ensure that they remain in good working order. It is important to check the tension of the springs regularly and to make sure that they are not worn or damaged. If you notice any signs of wear or damage, it is important to replace the springs as soon as possible.

Benefits of Having Garage Door Springs

Having garage door springs is beneficial for both safety and convenience. The springs are what make it possible for the garage door to open and close smoothly and safely. They also reduce the amount of effort required to open and close the door, making it easier and more convenient for you.

Common Issues with Garage Door Springs

The most common issue with garage door springs is that they can become worn or damaged over time. This can happen due to a number of factors, such as weather, wear and tear, or improper installation. It is important to inspect the springs regularly and replace them if necessary to ensure that the garage door continues to operate safely and properly.

Conclusion

Garage door springs are an essential part of a garage door system, and understanding how they work is key to keeping your garage door in good working order. It is important to inspect and maintain your garage door springs regularly and to replace them as necessary. If you need help with installation or maintenance, it is best to contact a professional.
