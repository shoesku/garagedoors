How To Remove A Garage Door Spring In 2023

Introduction

Garage doors can be a source of great convenience and comfort, but they can also present a challenge if something goes wrong. One of the most common issues that homeowners run into with their garage door is a broken or malfunctioning spring, which can cause the door to be stuck in either the open or closed position. If you think you may have a broken spring, it's important to take steps to repair it as soon as possible. In this article, we'll take a look at how to remove a garage door spring in 2023.

What You'll Need

Before you begin the removal process, it's important to make sure you have the right tools for the job. You'll need a pair of pliers, a pair of locking pliers, a screwdriver, and a set of sockets. You should also have a ladder on hand in case you need to reach higher, as well as safety glasses and gloves to protect your eyes and hands. Of course, you'll also need a new garage door spring.

Step One: Turning Off the Power

The very first step in removing a garage door spring is to turn off the power. If your garage door is connected to an opener, you should unplug the opener before you begin. If your opener doesn't have a plug, you'll need to disconnect the power at the circuit breaker. This will ensure that you won't accidentally get shocked if you come into contact with any wires.

Step Two: Removing the Spring

Once the power is off, you can begin the process of removing the spring. The first step is to use the pliers to loosen the nuts and bolts that are holding the spring in place. As you loosen them, keep track of which nut and bolt came from which side, as this will make it easier for you to install the new spring. Make sure to wear your gloves as you do this, as the springs can be very sharp.

Step Three: Installing the New Spring

Once the old spring has been removed, you can begin the process of installing the new one. Start by placing the new spring in the same position as the old one and then reattach the nuts and bolts. Make sure to tighten them securely but not too tightly, as doing so can cause damage to the spring. Once all of the nuts and bolts are in place, you can move on to the next step.

Step Four: Lubricating the Spring

The next step is to lubricate the spring. This will help to ensure that it will work properly and last for many years. Use a lubricant that is specifically designed for garage door springs and apply it liberally to all of the moving parts. This will help to ensure that the spring won't become stuck or stick together when it's in use.

Step Five: Reattaching the Spring

Once the spring is lubricated, it's time to reattach it to the garage door. Start by using the pliers to tighten the nuts and bolts that secure the spring to the door. Make sure to tighten them securely but not too tightly, as this can cause damage to the spring. Once all of the nuts and bolts are in place, you can move on to the next step.

Step Six: Testing the Spring

Now that the spring is installed, it's time to test it. Start by plugging in the opener and then using the remote or switch to open and close the door. If the spring is working properly, the door should open and close without issue. If the door doesn't open or close properly, you may need to adjust the tension of the spring. This can be done by using the locking pliers to loosen or tighten the nuts and bolts on the spring.

Conclusion

Removing a garage door spring can be a tricky task, but with the right tools and a bit of patience, anyone can do it. Start by turning off the power and then use the pliers to loosen the nuts and bolts that hold the spring in place. Once the old spring is removed, install the new one and then lubricate it. Finally, reattach the spring and test it to make sure it's working properly. Following these steps will help to ensure that your garage door is functioning properly and safely.
