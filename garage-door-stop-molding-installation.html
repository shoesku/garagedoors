How To Install Garage Door Stop Molding

Best Garage doors and references for house or residential with custom material and more design. We Share more picture design with HD quality images.

Installing garage door stop molding can be a relatively easy job for a do-it-yourselfer. Not only will it give your garage a finished look, but it will also add a layer of protection to your door. In this article, we will discuss the steps you need to take in order to install garage door stop molding correctly.

Step 1: Gather the Necessary Materials

Before you start your project, make sure you have all of the necessary materials. You will need a miter saw, a drill, wood screws, a hammer, a pry bar, a measuring tape, and the stop molding itself. You may also need shims, depending on the type of door you are installing the molding on.

Step 2: Measure and Cut the Molding

Once you have all of the necessary materials, you need to measure and cut the molding. Measure the opening of your garage door and use the miter saw to cut the molding to the correct size. It is important to make sure the pieces are cut accurately, as this will ensure the molding fits properly.

Step 3: Attach the Molding

Once the molding is cut, you can begin to attach it to the door. Start by holding the molding in place and using a hammer and wood screws to secure it. If the molding is not fitting tightly against the door, you may need to use shims to make it fit properly. Make sure you do not over-tighten the screws, as this may damage the door.

Step 4: Caulk the Molding

Once the molding is attached, you need to caulk it. This will help create a seal between the door and the molding, which will help keep out moisture and pests. Use a good quality silicone caulk and apply it to the edges of the molding. Let the caulk dry before you move on to the next step.

Step 5: Paint the Molding

Once the caulk is dry, you can paint the molding. Choose a paint that is suitable for your door, as some paints may not adhere to the surface properly. Once the paint is dry, you can admire your handiwork and enjoy the finished look of your garage door.

Conclusion

Installing garage door stop molding can be a great way to give your garage a finished look and protect your door from the elements. By following these steps, you can easily install the molding yourself and get the job done quickly and correctly. Make sure you have the necessary materials, measure and cut the molding correctly, attach it properly, caulk it, and paint it to get the best results.
