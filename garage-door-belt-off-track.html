How To Put A Garage Door Belt Back On Track

If you use a belt-driven garage door opener, it may occasionally slip off track. This is a common problem, but luckily it can be easily fixed. Here’s how to fix a garage door belt off track in just a few simple steps.

Step 1: Secure the Opener and Door

Before you begin, make sure to unplug the garage door opener and secure the door. If you don’t, the door may come crashing down while you’re trying to fix the belt. To secure the door, you can either use a locking bar or a vice-grip pliers. If you use a locking bar, make sure to insert it in the track between the door and the roller bracket.

Step 2: Take Note of the Position

Once the door is secured, take note of the position of the belt before you take it off. This will make it much easier to put it back on the track. Make sure to also check the tension spring and the roller bracket. This will ensure that the belt is properly aligned when you put it back on track.

Step 3: Take Off the Belt

Now it’s time to take off the belt. To do this, use a wrench to loosen the bolts that hold the belt in place. Once the bolts are loose, you can easily slide the belt off the track. If you’re having trouble, you can also use a hammer to tap the belt off the track.

Step 4: Check for Damage

Now that the belt is off the track, it’s time to check for any damage. Look for any frays, tears, or other signs of wear and tear. If there is any damage, it’s best to replace the belt. This will ensure that the belt doesn’t slip off track again.

Step 5: Put the Belt Back on Track

Once you’ve checked for damage, it’s time to put the belt back on the track. Make sure to align the belt with the tension spring and the roller bracket. Then, use a wrench to tighten the bolts that hold the belt in place. Make sure to not overtighten the bolts, as this could damage the belt.

Step 6: Test the Door

Now, it’s time to test the door. Plug in the garage door opener and press the “open” button. The door should open and close without any issues. If the belt slips off track again, you may need to adjust the tension spring or the roller bracket.

Step 7: Clean Up

Once you’ve tested the door and it’s working properly, make sure to clean up any debris or tools you used during the repair. This will ensure that the garage door opener continues to work properly.

Conclusion

Fixing a garage door belt off track doesn’t have to be a difficult task. With just a few simple steps, you can easily get your garage door working again. Just remember to take note of the belt’s position before you take it off, check for any damage, and clean up after you’re done. With the right tools and a few minutes of your time, you can easily put a garage door belt back on track.
