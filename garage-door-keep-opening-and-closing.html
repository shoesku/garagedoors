How To Fix An Automated Garage Door That Keeps Opening And Closing

Best Garage doors and references for house or residential with custom material and more design. We Share more picture design with HD quality images.

It's a common problem: you press the button to open or close your automated garage door, and it keeps opening and closing without stopping. This can be incredibly frustrating and can keep you from being able to get your car in or out of the garage. Fortunately, there are some fixes for this problem that you can do yourself. Read on to learn how to fix an automated garage door that keeps opening and closing.

Check the Sensors

The first thing to do when you have this problem is to check the sensors. These are the small boxes with red and green lights that are mounted on both sides of the garage door. They are designed to detect any objects that are in the way of the door, so if something is blocking the sensors, it can cause the door to keep opening and closing. Check to make sure that nothing is in the way of the sensors, and clean them off if they are dirty.

Check the Tracks

If the sensors are clear, then you should check the tracks. The garage door runs on tracks, and if the tracks are bent or damaged in any way, it can cause the door to keep opening and closing. Check the tracks for any signs of damage, and if you find any, you'll need to have a professional come and repair them.

Check the Springs

The springs are also an important part of the garage door system, and if they are worn out or damaged, it can cause the door to keep opening and closing. Check the springs for any signs of wear and tear, and if they need to be replaced, you'll need to call a professional.

Check the Remote

If the sensors, tracks, and springs all appear to be in good condition, then you should check the remote. The remote is the device that you use to open and close the garage door, and if the batteries are low or if there is a problem with the connection, then it can cause the door to keep opening and closing. Replace the batteries in the remote and make sure that the connection is working properly.

Check the Opener

Finally, if all of the above steps have been checked and the problem still persists, then it may be time to check the opener. The opener is the motor that powers the garage door, and if it is not working properly, it can cause the door to keep opening and closing. Check the opener for any signs of damage or wear and tear, and if it needs to be replaced, you'll need to call a professional to come and do the job.

Conclusion

An automated garage door that keeps opening and closing can be incredibly frustrating, but fortunately, there are some steps that you can take to fix the problem. Start by checking the sensors, tracks, springs, remote, and opener to make sure that everything is working properly. If any of these components are damaged or worn out, then you'll need to call a professional to come and repair them. With a little bit of effort, you should be able to get your garage door working properly again in no time.
