Common Reasons Why Your Garage Door Opener Stopped Working

It’s never fun when you press the button to open your garage door, and nothing happens. Unfortunately, this is an all-too-common occurrence with garage door openers. If your garage door opener stopped working and you’re not sure why, you’re in the right place. We’ll discuss some of the common reasons why garage door openers stop working and what you can do to fix them.

Motor Failure

The motor is the engine of your garage door opener, so it’s no surprise that a motor failure is the most common reason why garage door openers stop working. When the motor fails, it usually produces a clicking noise as it tries to open the door. If this is the case, you’ll need to replace the motor.

Power Supply Issues

If your garage door opener is plugged into an electrical outlet, you may have a power supply issue. The outlet may not be providing enough power to the opener or it may be faulty. To test this, plug something else into the outlet and see if it works. If it does, you may need to upgrade the outlet or use a surge protector.

Faulty Wiring

Faulty wiring is another common issue that can cause your garage door opener to stop working. If the wiring is worn or damaged, it can prevent the opener from functioning properly. If you suspect this is the problem, it’s best to have an electrician look at it.

Remote Control Issues

If your remote control isn’t working, it may be due to a dead battery or a faulty connection. To check the battery, remove it and test it with a multimeter. If it’s dead, replace it with a new one. If the connection is faulty, you may need to replace the remote control.

Limit Switch Issues

The limit switch is responsible for determining when the door should open and close. If the limit switch is not set correctly, it can cause your garage door opener to stop working. To fix this, you’ll need to adjust the limit switch.

Obstructions

If your garage door opener is having trouble opening or closing, it could be due to an obstruction. This could be something as simple as a broom or a toy that is blocking the door. If this is the case, you’ll need to remove the obstruction.

Broken Gears

The gears in your garage door opener can wear out over time. If this happens, the opener may not be able to open or close the door. To fix this, you’ll need to replace the gears.

Faulty Photo Eye Sensor

The photo eye sensor is responsible for detecting objects in the path of the door. If the sensor is not working properly, it can prevent the door from opening or closing. If this is the case, you’ll need to replace the sensor.

Worn Out Rollers

The rollers on the garage door can become worn out over time. If this happens, the door may not be able to open or close properly. To fix this, you’ll need to replace the rollers.

Conclusion

If your garage door opener stopped working, it could be due to a number of different issues. Common issues include motor failure, power supply issues, faulty wiring, remote control issues, limit switch issues, obstructions, broken gears, faulty photo eye sensor, and worn out rollers. To determine which issue is causing the problem, you’ll need to do some troubleshooting. If you’re not sure how to do this, it’s best to call a professional.
