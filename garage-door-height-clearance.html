Understanding Garage Door Height Clearance

When building a new garage, it's important to consider the height of the door. The height of the door affects the amount of clearance needed for vehicles to enter and exit the garage safely. The height of the door is measured from the bottom of the door to the top of the door frame. Typically, standard garage door heights range from 7 to 8 feet. However, depending on the size of the vehicle, the height of the door may need to be adjusted to provide enough room for clearance.

The amount of clearance needed is determined by the type of vehicle and the size of the garage. The size of the vehicle will determine the amount of space needed to safely drive into and out of the garage. For example, a sedan may require 6 to 7 feet of clearance, while an SUV may require 8 to 10 feet of clearance. The size of the garage is also important, as a smaller garage may not be able to accommodate a larger vehicle.

Determining Garage Door Heights
