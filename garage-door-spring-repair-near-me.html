Garage door springs are essential components of your garage door system. They provide the tension and counterbalance necessary to lift the heavy weight of the door. What are Garage Door Springs?

Garage door springs are the components of your garage door system that provide tension and counterbalance necessary to lift the heavy weight of the door. The most common type of garage door spring is the torsion spring. These springs are located above the door, and when activated, they twist and create tension to lift the weight of the door. Garage door springs are usually rated for 10,000 cycles of operation before they need to be replaced. This means that if you open and close your garage door 10,000 times, your springs may need to be replaced.

Symptoms of a Faulty Garage Door Spring

If your garage door springs are worn or damaged, you will likely notice the following symptoms: The door is difficult to open and close, the door won't stay open when you let it go, it takes more effort to open and close the door, and you may even hear a loud noise when operating your door. If you notice any of these symptoms, you should consider having your garage door springs repaired near you.

Common Causes of Garage Door Spring Problems

Garage door springs can become worn or damaged for a variety of reasons. They can become corroded due to exposure to the elements, which can cause them to become weakened and brittle. They may also become warped or stretched due to heavy use, which can cause them to lose their tension and become less reliable. Additionally, if your garage door is not properly balanced, it can cause undue stress on the springs and cause them to fail prematurely.

When to Replace Garage Door Springs
