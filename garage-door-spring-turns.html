How To Change Your Garage Door Spring Turns

Are you having trouble with your garage door? If you’ve noticed that your garage door isn’t opening or closing properly, it may be due to a worn-out garage door spring. The spring is responsible for counterbalancing the weight of your garage door, and when it wears out, it can cause your garage door to become stuck or unbalanced. Fortunately, changing your garage door spring turns is a relatively easy task that can be done by anyone with basic DIY skills. In this article, we’ll show you exactly how to change your garage door spring turns.

What Are Garage Door Spring Turns?

Garage door springs are responsible for counterbalancing the weight of your garage door. Each garage door spring typically has a certain number of “turns”, or coils, which determine how much weight the spring can support. Over time, the number of turns can start to decrease due to the daily wear and tear of the spring. When this happens, the spring can become unbalanced and cause your garage door to become stuck or uneven.

What Tools Do I Need?

Fortunately, changing your garage door spring turns doesn’t require a lot of tools or knowledge. The most important tool you’ll need is a set of winding bars. These are long metal bars that are used to manually wind the spring. You’ll also need a few basic tools, such as a socket wrench, a hammer, and a set of pliers. Additionally, you’ll need a ladder and a pair of safety glasses.

How Do I Change My Garage Door Spring Turns?

Before you start, it’s important to disconnect the power to your garage door opener. This will ensure that your garage door doesn’t accidentally open or close while you’re working on it. Once you’ve disconnected the power, you can start the process of changing your garage door spring turns.

Step 1: Unwind the Existing Spring

The first step is to unwind the existing spring. To do this, you’ll need to use your winding bars to manually unwind the spring. Make sure to keep a close eye on the number of turns, as this will determine how much weight the spring can support. Once you’ve unwound the spring, you can move on to the next step.

Step 2: Measure the New Spring

Once you’ve unwound the existing spring, you’ll need to measure the new spring. This is important, as it will determine how much weight the new spring can support. To measure the spring, you’ll need to use a ruler to measure the length of the spring from end to end. Once you’ve measured the new spring, you can move on to the next step.

Step 3: Wind the New Spring

Once you’ve measured the new spring, it’s time to wind it. To do this, you’ll need to use your winding bars to manually wind the spring. Make sure to keep a close eye on the number of turns, as this will determine how much weight the spring can support. Once you’ve wound the new spring, you can move on to the next step.

Step 4: Test the Spring

Once you’ve wound the new spring, it’s time to test it. To do this, you’ll need to open and close the garage door a few times. This will help ensure that the spring is properly balanced and that it can support the weight of the garage door. If everything seems to be working properly, you can move on to the final step.

Step 5: Reattach the Power

Once you’ve tested the spring, it’s time to reattach the power to your garage door opener. This will ensure that the garage door operates properly when the power is turned on. Once you’ve reattached the power, you’re all done!

Conclusion

Changing your garage door spring turns is a relatively easy task that can be done by anyone with basic DIY skills. With the right tools and a little bit of patience, you can easily change your garage door spring turns in no time. Just remember to always be careful when working with garage door springs, as they are under a lot of tension and can be dangerous if not handled properly. Good luck!
