What Are Garage Door Springs and What Do They Do?

Garage door springs are components of your garage door that provide counterbalance to the weight of the door. They are located on either side of the door and move together when the door is opened and closed. The two springs work in unison to ensure that the door is balanced evenly and can be opened and closed with ease. When one of the springs breaks, the door becomes unbalanced and can be difficult to open, leading to potential damage.

What Are the Signs of a Broken Garage Door Spring?

There are a few tell-tale signs that your garage door springs may be broken. First, you’ll notice that the door is heavier than usual and takes more effort to open and close. If the door is opened manually, it may be difficult to keep it open and it may even shut on its own. You may also hear a loud bang when the door is opened or closed, which is a sign that the spring has snapped.

What Are the Different Types of Garage Door Springs?

Garage door springs come in two different types: torsion and extension. Torsion springs are located above the door and provide tension to the door when it is opened and closed. Extension springs are located on the sides of the door and provide lift assistance when the door is opened. Both types of springs are under heavy tension and can be dangerous to replace or repair, so it’s important to seek the help of a professional garage door technician if you suspect a broken spring.

What Are The Steps Involved in Replacing a Broken Garage Door Spring?

Replacing a broken garage door spring is a job best left to the professionals, as it can be dangerous to do it yourself. The technician will first need to identify which type of spring is broken and then determine the size and weight of the door in order to properly replace the spring. The technician will then release the tension on the broken spring and remove it from the door. The new spring will then be installed and the tension will be adjusted to the correct level.

What Are Some Tips for Preventing Garage Door Spring Damage?

One of the best ways to prevent garage door spring damage is to regularly inspect the springs to ensure they are in good condition. You should also inspect the door periodically to make sure the springs are still properly balanced and that the tension is properly adjusted. If you hear any strange noises coming from your garage door, it’s important to have it inspected right away, as this could be a sign of a broken spring. Additionally, you should never try to repair or replace a broken spring yourself, as this can be dangerous.

What Is the Average Cost of Replacing a Broken Garage Door Spring?

The cost of replacing a broken garage door spring will depend on the type of spring that needs to be replaced and the size and weight of the door. Generally, the cost of replacing a broken spring can range from $150 to $400. It’s important to note that if the spring is not properly installed, it can cause further damage to the door and the opener, so it’s important to hire a professional to ensure the job is done correctly.

Conclusion

Garage door springs are an essential part of the door’s operation and play an important role in providing the necessary counterbalance to the weight of the door. If one of the springs breaks, it can lead to a heavy and difficult-to-open door, so it’s important to have it repaired as soon as possible. The repair process can be dangerous, so it’s best to hire a professional for the job. Additionally, it’s important to inspect the springs regularly in order to prevent any potential damage.
