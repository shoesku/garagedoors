Troubleshooting A Noisy Garage Door Motor

Garage door motors are not designed to be overly complex pieces of machinery, but they can wear down over time and start to make strange noises. If your garage door motor is making strange noises, it is important to get it looked at right away. If left unchecked, the motor may start to malfunction and create a safety hazard. This article will explain the common causes of a noisy garage door motor and what to do to help fix it.

Common Causes of Noisy Garage Door Motors

There are a few common causes of noisy garage door motors. The first is that the motor may need to be lubricated. Over time, the moving parts of the motor can dry out and cause friction. This can create a loud grinding noise. Additionally, the motor may need to be adjusted. If the motor is not properly adjusted, it can create a loud clanking noise. Lastly, the motor may be malfunctioning. If the motor is malfunctioning, it can create a loud humming noise that can be heard from outside the garage.

How to Fix a Noisy Garage Door Motor

If your garage door motor is making noise, there are a few things you can do to try and fix it. First, it is important to lubricate the moving parts of the motor. This can help reduce friction and reduce the noise. Additionally, it is important to check the alignment of the motor and make sure it is properly adjusted. Lastly, it is important to check the motor for signs of wear and tear and replace any parts that are malfunctioning.

When to Call a Professional

If the above steps do not fix the problem, it is important to call a professional. A professional can help diagnose the problem and suggest the best course of action. Additionally, they can help with more complex repairs such as replacing parts or adjusting the motor. It is important to remember that a garage door motor is a complex piece of machinery and should not be attempted to be repaired without the help of a professional.

Tips to Keep Your Garage Door Motor Running Smoothly

In order to keep your garage door motor running smoothly, it is important to regularly inspect it for signs of wear and tear. Additionally, it is important to lubricate the moving parts of the motor on a regular basis. This can help reduce friction and extend the life of the motor. Lastly, it is important to call a professional if you notice any strange noises or if the motor is malfunctioning in any way.

Conclusion

Garage door motors can start to make strange noises over time due to wear and tear. It is important to inspect the motor regularly and lubricate the moving parts on a regular basis. If you notice any strange noises coming from the motor, it is important to call a professional right away. A professional can help diagnose the problem and suggest the best course of action.
