What To Do When Your Craftsman Garage Door Opener Won't Close All The Way

Best Garage doors and references for house or residential with custom material and more design. We Share more picture design with HD quality images.

If you have a Craftsman garage door opener, then you know that this brand is known for its reliability and ease of use. But like any other mechanical device, your Craftsman garage door opener may occasionally have issues. One of the most common issues is when your Craftsman garage door opener won't close all the way. This can be an annoying and potentially dangerous problem, as it leaves your garage vulnerable to intruders. Fortunately, there are a few things you can do to troubleshoot and fix this issue.

Check the Power Source

The first thing you should do when you notice that your Craftsman garage door opener won't close all the way is to check the power source. If the power source is not functioning properly, then this could be the cause of the issue. Make sure that the power source is plugged in correctly and that there is no damage to the cord or outlet. Also, check to make sure that the circuit breaker has not been tripped. If the power source is working correctly, then you can move on to the next step.

Check the Limit Switches

The next thing you should do is to check the limit switches. The limit switches are responsible for telling the garage door opener when to stop. If the limit switches are not properly adjusted, then the garage door opener will not close all the way. To adjust the limit switches, you should refer to the user manual for your particular model of Craftsman garage door opener. Typically, you will need to adjust the switch that is located near the bottom of the garage door.

Check the Tracks

Another possible cause of your Craftsman garage door opener not closing all the way is a problem with the tracks. The tracks are the metal rails that the garage door opener rides on. If the tracks are bent, warped, or out of alignment, then this can prevent the garage door opener from functioning properly. To check the tracks, you should visually inspect them for any signs of damage. If you notice any damage, you should call a professional to repair the tracks.

Check the Springs

Another possible cause of your Craftsman garage door opener not closing all the way is a problem with the springs. The springs are responsible for providing the tension that allows the garage door opener to open and close. If the springs are worn or broken, then this can prevent the garage door opener from functioning properly. To check the springs, you should visually inspect them for any signs of damage. If you notice any damage, you should call a professional to repair or replace the springs.

Check the Motor

The motor is the component of your Craftsman garage door opener that actually moves the door. If the motor is not functioning properly, then this can prevent the garage door opener from closing all the way. To check the motor, you should visually inspect it for any signs of damage. If you notice any damage, you should call a professional to repair or replace the motor.

Check the Remote

The remote is the component of your Craftsman garage door opener that allows you to open and close the garage door from a distance. If the remote is not functioning properly, then this can prevent the garage door opener from closing all the way. To check the remote, you should visually inspect it for any signs of damage. If you notice any damage, you should call a professional to repair or replace the remote.

Conclusion

If your Craftsman garage door opener is not closing all the way, then there may be a problem with the power source, limit switches, tracks, springs, motor, or remote. To fix the issue, you should check each of these components and make any necessary repairs or replacements. If you are unsure of how to do this, then you should call a professional for assistance.
