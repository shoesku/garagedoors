How To Troubleshoot Garage Door Opener Not Closing With Remote?

Nothing can be more frustrating than coming home after a long day and finding out that your garage door won't close with your remote. You might find yourself wondering what went wrong and how you can fix it. Unfortunately, troubleshooting garage door opener not closing with remote is not as easy as it sounds.

Common Causes of Garage Door Opener Not Closing with Remote

Before you start troubleshooting your garage door opener, it is important to understand the common causes of this issue. One of the most common causes of garage door opener not closing with remote is a broken or malfunctioning remote control. This could be due to a number of factors, such as a worn-out battery, a faulty connection, or a damaged circuit board.

Another common cause of garage door opener not closing with remote is an obstruction in the door's track, such as dirt, debris, or a broken part. This can prevent the opener from properly engaging the door and prevent it from closing.

Steps to Troubleshoot Garage Door Opener Not Closing with Remote

Now that you know the common causes of a garage door opener not closing with remote, here are some steps you can take to troubleshoot the issue:

1. Check the Remote Batteries

The first thing you should do is check the batteries in your remote. Over time, batteries can start to wear out and lose their charge, which can prevent your remote from functioning properly. To check the batteries, open the remote and take out the old batteries. If they are worn out or corroded, replace them with new ones.

2. Check the Remote Antenna

The next step is to check the antenna on your remote. This is the small wire that sticks out from the back of the remote. If the antenna is damaged or bent, it can affect the signal transmission between the remote and the opener. To check the antenna, simply replace it with a new one.

3. Check the Door Track

The next step is to check the door track for any obstructions that may be preventing the opener from properly engaging the door. This could be anything from dirt and debris to a broken part. To check the door track, open the door manually and use a flashlight to look for any obstructions that may be preventing the opener from engaging the door properly.

4. Check the Wall Switch

The next step is to check the wall switch. This is the switch located on the wall near the garage door. If the switch is not working properly, it can prevent the opener from engaging the door. To check the switch, make sure it is turned on and that the wires are securely connected.

5. Check the Garage Door Opener

The last step is to check the garage door opener itself. Over time, the opener can start to malfunction due to wear and tear or a faulty circuit board. To check the opener, open the door manually and listen for any strange noises or odd smells. If you hear or smell something out of the ordinary, it may be time to replace the opener.

Conclusion

Troubleshooting a garage door opener not closing with remote can be a tricky process. However, by following the steps outlined above, you should be able to identify and fix the issue quickly and easily. Remember to always turn off the power to the garage door before attempting any repairs or adjustments. If the issue persists, it may be time to contact a professional.
