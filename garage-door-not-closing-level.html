What Is The Garage Door Not Closing Problem?

If you have a garage door that is not closing properly, it can be a major source of frustration. The garage door is an important part of your home and its security, so it’s important to make sure that it’s working properly. Fortunately, there are a few simple tips and tricks that you can use to get your garage door back to its proper level quickly and easily.

Tools Needed to Fix Your Garage Door

Before you start trying to fix your garage door, you should make sure that you have all the tools that you need. Depending on the type of garage door you have, you may need a few different tools, such as a level, a screwdriver, a wrench, pliers, and an adjustable wrench. You should also have a rag and some lubricant handy so that you can lubricate the parts of your garage door.

Step 1: Check the Balance and Level of the Door

The first step in fixing your garage door is to check the balance and level of the door. You should start by checking the level of the door by using a level. If the door is not level, you should adjust the hinges to try to level it out. This may require some trial and error, but it is important to make sure that the door is balanced properly before you start to work on it.

Step 2: Check for Loose or Damaged Parts

Once the door is balanced, you should check for any loose or damaged parts. This includes checking the hinges, the rollers, the tracks, and the springs. If any of these parts are loose or damaged, they should be replaced or tightened. If the parts are too damaged, they may need to be replaced entirely.

Step 3: Check the Springs and Cables

Once you have checked and replaced any loose or damaged parts, you should then check the springs and cables. These are two important components of the door and need to be in good working condition in order for it to close properly. You should inspect the cables for any frays or breaks, and check the springs to make sure they are properly tensioned. If they are not, you should adjust them as necessary.

Step 4: Lubricate the Moving Parts

Once the door is balanced, the parts are in good condition, and the springs and cables are properly adjusted, you should then lubricate the moving parts. This will help to ensure that the door operates smoothly and efficiently. You should use a lubricant that is specifically designed for garage doors, as regular lubricants can actually damage parts of the door.

Step 5: Test the Door

Once you have finished all of the above steps, you should then test the door to see if it is closing properly. You should open and close the door a few times to make sure that it is operating correctly. If everything appears to be in working order, you should be able to close the door without any issues.

Conclusion

Fixing your garage door not closing level can be a relatively easy task if you have the right tools and knowledge. By following the steps outlined above, you should be able to get your garage door back in working order in no time. Once it is fixed, you can rest easy knowing that your home is safe and secure.
