Why Is My Garage Door Spring Not Working?

If you’ve been spending more time with your garage door this year, you may have noticed that your garage door spring is not working as it should. This is a common problem that can be caused by a variety of issues. In this article, we’ll discuss the most common causes of a malfunctioning garage door spring and some tips for getting it fixed.

What is a Garage Door Spring?

A garage door spring is an important component of your garage door system. It is responsible for lifting and lowering the door when it is opened and closed. The spring is under tension and can become worn out over time, leading to a malfunction.

Common Causes of Garage Door Spring Malfunction

There are a few common causes of a malfunctioning garage door spring. The most common is that the tension of the spring has become too weak and it is unable to lift the door. Other causes can include corrosion on the spring, a broken spring, or a broken cable.

How to Fix a Garage Door Spring

In most cases, it’s best to hire a professional to fix your garage door spring. A professional will have the tools and knowledge necessary to diagnose the issue and make the necessary repairs. If you’re handy and want to attempt a repair yourself, there are a few things you should keep in mind.

Safety First

First and foremost, it is important to take safety precautions when attempting to fix a garage door spring. Wear safety glasses and gloves when handling the spring or any other part of the garage door system. It is also important to disconnect the power source before attempting any repairs.

Check the Spring

Once you’ve taken the necessary safety precautions, it’s time to check the spring. Visually inspect the spring for any signs of corrosion or damage. If the spring is broken or shows signs of corrosion, it should be replaced. If the spring looks okay, you can try to adjust the tension.

Adjust the Tension

Once you’ve checked the spring, you can attempt to adjust the tension. This should be done cautiously and with the help of a professional if possible. Adjusting the tension too much can cause further damage to the spring or the entire garage door system.

Call a Professional

If you’re unable to diagnose or fix the problem yourself, it’s best to call a professional. A professional can diagnose the issue and make any necessary repairs. They can also provide advice on how to prevent future problems with your garage door spring.

Conclusion

A malfunctioning garage door spring can be a troublesome issue. The most common causes are a broken or corroded spring, or a broken cable. It’s best to hire a professional to make any necessary repairs. If you choose to attempt a repair yourself, make sure to take safety precautions and follow the advice of a professional if possible.
