Solving Garage Door Electric Eye Problems Caused By Sun

Is your electric eye having trouble sensing your car when you pull up to your garage door? This is a common problem caused by direct sunlight. Sunlight can interfere with the sensors, causing them to malfunction and not open or close the door properly. Fortunately, there are a few quick and easy solutions to this problem.

What are Electric Eye Sensors?

Electric eye sensors are a type of photoelectric device that is used to detect the presence of an object near the door. The sensor emits a beam of light that is reflected off of the object. When the beam of light is interrupted, the sensor will detect it and send a signal to the control unit, which will then open or close the door.

The problem with this system is that direct sunlight can interfere with the sensors, causing them to malfunction. This can be a major inconvenience, especially if your car is stuck outside or if you're trying to get into the house.

How to Fix Electric Eye Problems Caused by Sunlight

Reposition the Sensors

The first thing to try is to reposition the sensors. If the sensors are placed too close to each other or too close to the sun, they may not be able to detect the beam of light. Try to position the sensors in a way that does not interfere with the sun's rays.

Install a Sun Shield

If repositioning the sensors does not work, you can try installing a sun shield. This will block the sun's rays from directly hitting the sensors, which should help to reduce interference. You can purchase sun shields from most home improvement stores.

Replace the Sensors

If all else fails, you may need to replace the sensors. This is an easy fix, but it can be costly depending on the type of sensors you have. Be sure to do your research and purchase the best quality sensors you can afford.

Conclusion

If your garage door electric eye is having trouble sensing your car due to direct sunlight, there are a few solutions you can try. First, try repositioning the sensors. If that does not work, you can install a sun shield or replace the sensors. With a little bit of effort, you should be able to solve the problem and get your garage door working again.
