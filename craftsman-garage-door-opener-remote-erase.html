If you have a Craftsman garage door opener remote that is not responding to your commands, you may need to erase it. Doing so will reset the remote, which can help solve connection issues. This article will provide a step-by-step guide to erasing your Craftsman garage door opener remote.

Step 1: Prepare the Remote

Before you begin, you will need to make sure that the remote is prepared for erasing. To do this, you will need to press and hold the “Smart” button on the remote. This will put the remote into a special mode that will allow it to be reset. Once the remote is in this special mode, it will be ready to be erased.

Step 2: Locate the Erase Button

Now that the remote is in the special mode, you will need to locate the “Erase” button. The Erase button is located on the back of the remote, and is marked with a small “E” icon. Once you have located the Erase button, you can proceed to the next step.

Step 3: Press the Erase Button

Now that you have located the Erase button, you can press it. Be sure to press and hold the button for at least five seconds. This will initiate the erasing process, and the remote should be reset within a few seconds.

Step 4: Test the Remote

Once the remote has been erased, it should be ready to use. To test it, you should press the “Open” or “Close” button on the remote. If the garage door responds to your commands, then the remote has been successfully erased.

Step 5: Repeat the Process if Necessary

If the remote is still not responding to your commands, you may need to repeat the erasing process. You should repeat steps one through four until the remote is working correctly. If the remote still does not respond, you may need to replace it with a new one.

Conclusion

Erasing a Craftsman garage door opener remote is a fairly simple process. It should only take a few minutes to complete, and can help to resolve connection issues with the remote. If you follow the steps outlined in this article, you should have no trouble erasing your remote and getting it working again.
