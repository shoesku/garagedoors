What To Do When Your Garage Door Lock Won't Unlock

Best Garage doors and references for house or residential with custom material and more design. We Share more picture design with HD quality images.

It can be a frustrating experience when you go to unlock your garage door, only to find that the lock won't budge. It can be even more frustrating if you don't know what to do or why it's happening. Fortunately, there are a few likely causes and solutions for a garage door lock that won't unlock.

Check the Lock

The first step is to check the lock itself. Make sure the key is properly inserted and that you have the correct key. If you don't have the correct key, you may be able to get a new one from the manufacturer. It's also possible that the key is worn out or damaged. If this is the case, you'll need to get a new key cut.

Check the Door

Once you've checked the lock, you should turn your attention to the door itself. Make sure the door is properly aligned with the frame and that there is no debris or other obstructions in the way. If the door is not properly aligned, try adjusting it with a screwdriver or wrench. If there is debris in the way, try to remove it, or you can use a lubricant to help break it up.

Check the Hardware

If the lock and door seem to be working properly, you should check the hardware connecting the door to the frame. Make sure that all the screws and bolts are tight and that there is no rust or corrosion. If there is rust or corrosion, you can try cleaning it with a wire brush. If the hardware is too damaged, you may need to replace it.

Check the Lock Cylinder

If all else fails, you should check the lock cylinder. This is the part of the lock that actually secures the door. Make sure the cylinder is properly lubricated and that the pins are in good condition. If the pins are worn or broken, you may need to replace them. If the cylinder is too damaged, you may need to replace the entire lock.

Call a Professional

If you're still having trouble getting your garage door lock to unlock, you may need to call a professional. A locksmith or garage door repair company can help you diagnose the problem and get your garage door lock working again. They can also provide you with advice on how to prevent future lock issues.

Conclusion

If your garage door lock won't unlock, there are a few likely causes and solutions. Start by checking the lock, the door, the hardware, and the lock cylinder. If all else fails, you may need to call a professional. With the right diagnosis and repair, you can get your garage door lock working again in no time.
