Why Does My Garage Door Not Close All The Time?

Best Garage doors and references for house or residential with custom material and more design. We Share more picture design with HD quality images.

Having a garage door that doesn't close all the time can be a real annoyance. You may have to get up and close it multiple times before it finally stays shut. Before you call an expert to fix it or buy a new one, there are a few things you can check and possibly fix yourself. In this article, we will discuss the possible causes for why your garage door isn't closing all the time and how you can fix them.

Inspect The Safety Sensors

Most modern garage doors have sensors that detect when something is in the path of the door. These sensors will prevent the door from closing if it detects something blocking its path. Inspect the sensors to make sure they are aligned correctly and are not blocked by dirt, debris, or anything else. If they are blocked, clean them off and align them properly. If this doesn't work, you may need to replace them.

Check The Motor

The motor is what powers the door to open and close. If the motor is malfunctioning, it could be the cause of why your door isn't closing all the time. To check the motor, you will need to have a multi-meter. With the multi-meter, you can test the motor's power levels to see if it is working properly. If the power levels are too low or too high, you may need to replace the motor.

Check The Tracks

The tracks are what the garage door runs on. If the tracks are bent, rusty, or misaligned, the door can become stuck and won't close all the way. If the tracks are in bad condition, you will need to replace them. You can even replace them yourself if you have the right tools and know-how.

Check The Springs

The springs are what provides the tension needed to open and close the door. If the springs are worn or broken, the door may not close all the way. To check the springs, you will need to disconnect the power to the garage door and manually open and close it. If the door is hard to open or close, it is likely the springs need to be replaced.

Check The Lock

If your garage door has a manual lock, it could be the cause of why the door isn't closing all the way. Check the lock to make sure it is in the unlocked position. If it is locked, unlock it and then try to close the door. If the door still won't close all the way, you may need to replace the lock.

Check The Limit Switch

The limit switch tells the motor when to stop and reverse the direction of the door. If the limit switch is malfunctioning, the door may not close all the way. To check the limit switch, you will need to access the motor and test it with a multi-meter. If the switch is not working, you will need to replace it.

Check The Cables

The cables are what connect the motor to the door. If the cables are frayed or broken, the door may not close all the way. To check the cables, you will need to disconnect the power and manually open and close the door. If the door is hard to open or close, it is likely the cables are worn or broken and need to be replaced.

Check The Roller

The rollers are what the door runs on. If the rollers are worn or broken, the door may not close all the way. To check the rollers, you will need to open the door and inspect them. If they are worn or broken, you will need to replace them. This is a job best left to a professional.

Conclusion

If your garage door isn't closing all the time, there are a few things you can check and possibly fix yourself. Inspect the safety sensors, check the motor, tracks, springs, lock, limit switch, cables, and rollers. If any of these parts are malfunctioning or worn, you will need to replace them. If you don't feel comfortable doing it yourself, you should call a professional. They will be able to properly diagnose and fix the problem.
