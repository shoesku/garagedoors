How To Troubleshoot When Your Garage Door Motor Only Turns One Way

Best Garage doors and references for house or residential with custom material and more design. We Share more picture design with HD quality images.

It can be a frustrating experience when your garage door motor only turns one way. You may find yourself constantly having to manually open your garage door, or worse yet, not being able to open it at all. Thankfully, there are a few steps you can take to troubleshoot the issue and get your garage door running smoothly again.

Step 1: Check the Power Supply

The first step in troubleshooting a garage door motor that only turns one way is to check the power supply. If your garage door motor is connected to a wall outlet, make sure the outlet is receiving power. You can do this by plugging a lamp or other device into the outlet and confirming that it turns on. If the outlet is not receiving power, you will need to make sure the circuit breaker for that outlet is switched on. If it is already on, then the next step is to inspect the wiring.

Step 2: Inspect the Wiring

Once you have confirmed that power is being supplied to the outlet, you can move on to inspecting the wiring. Start by unplugging the power cord from the wall outlet and then remove the cover plate from the motor. You will want to look for any signs of wear or damage to the wiring. If you see any frayed or damaged wires, you will need to replace them. You should also check to make sure the connections are secure.

Step 3: Check the Motor

Once you have inspected the wiring, you can move on to checking the motor. Start by disconnecting the power cord from the wall outlet and then remove the cover plate from the motor. You will want to check for signs of wear or damage to the motor. If you see any broken wires or other signs of damage, you will need to replace the motor.

Step 4: Check the Gears

If everything else checks out, the next step is to check the gears. Start by removing the cover plate from the motor and then inspect the gears for any signs of wear or damage. If you see any broken or worn-out gears, you will need to replace them. If the gears appear to be in good condition, you can move on to the next step.

Step 5: Replace the Chain

The next step is to check the chain. Start by removing the cover plate from the motor and then inspect the chain for any signs of wear or damage. If you see any broken or worn-out links, you will need to replace the chain. If the chain appears to be in good condition, you can move on to the next step.

Step 6: Adjust the Limit Switch

The limit switch is what tells the motor when to stop and start. If the limit switch is not properly adjusted, it can cause the motor to only turn one way. To adjust the limit switch, start by removing the cover plate from the motor and then locate the limit switch. Once you have located the limit switch, you can adjust it by turning the screw clockwise or counterclockwise until it is properly adjusted.

Step 7: Check the Spring Balance

The spring balance is what helps to ensure the door opens and closes properly. If the spring balance is off, it can cause the motor to only turn one way. To check the spring balance, start by removing the cover plate from the motor and then locate the spring balance. Once you have located the spring balance, you can adjust it by turning the screw clockwise or counterclockwise until it is properly adjusted.

Step 8: Test the Motor

The last step is to test the motor. Start by reconnecting the power cord to the wall outlet and then press the button on the motor. If the motor is working properly, it should turn both ways. If the motor is still only turning one way, you will need to repeat the steps above until the issue is resolved.

Conclusion

Troubleshooting a garage door motor that only turns one way can be a difficult task, but with these steps, you should be able to get your garage door running smoothly again. If you are still having problems, it is best to contact a professional to inspect the motor and ensure it is functioning properly.
