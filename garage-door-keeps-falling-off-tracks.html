How To Fix A Garage Door That Keeps Falling Off Tracks

Introduction

If your garage door is falling off the tracks, it’s not only a nuisance, it can also be a safety hazard. Fortunately, with some common tools and a bit of patience, you can fix the problem yourself. In this article, we’ll explain the most common causes of a garage door falling off its tracks and how to repair it. Read on to learn more about this issue.

Common Causes of a Garage Door Falling Off Its Tracks

There are several reasons why your garage door might be falling off its tracks. The most common causes are loose hardware, misaligned tracks, or broken springs. Let’s take a closer look at each of these issues.

Loose Hardware

The most common cause of a garage door falling off its tracks is loose hardware. It’s important to inspect the hardware on a regular basis to make sure it is secure. The most common hardware that can become loose are the hinges and rollers. If these become loose, they can cause the door to lose alignment and fall off its tracks.

Misaligned Tracks

Your garage door tracks are responsible for guiding the door up and down. If the tracks become misaligned, it can cause the door to come off its tracks. This can be caused by wear and tear, or if the tracks were not installed properly. In either case, you will need to realign the tracks in order to fix the problem.

Broken Springs

The springs on your garage door are responsible for helping to lift the weight of the door. If the springs become damaged or broken, it can cause the door to become unbalanced, causing it to fall off its tracks. If this is the case, you will need to replace the springs in order to repair the problem.

Repairing a Garage Door That Keeps Falling Off Tracks

Now that we’ve discussed the most common causes of a garage door falling off its tracks, let’s talk about how to fix the problem. Depending on the cause, the repair process may be quite simple, or it may require a bit more effort. Here are a few tips for how to repair a garage door that keeps falling off its tracks.

Tighten or Replace the Hardware

If the problem is loose hardware, you can try tightening it first. If that doesn’t work, then you will need to replace the hardware. This is a relatively simple task that you can do yourself if you have the right tools.

Realign the Tracks

If the problem is misaligned tracks, you can try to realign them yourself. This can be done by adjusting the screws or bolts on the track. However, if the tracks are severely misaligned, you may need to hire a professional to do the job.

Replace the Springs

If the problem is broken springs, you’ll need to replace them. This is a more complex task and should be done by a professional. Springs are under a great deal of tension, so attempting to replace them yourself can be dangerous.

Conclusion

A garage door that keeps falling off its tracks is a common problem that can be fixed with some common tools and a bit of patience. The most common causes are loose hardware, misaligned tracks, or broken springs. If you’ve identified the cause of the problem, you can use the tips in this article to repair it yourself. If the repair is more complex, you may need to hire a professional.
