Tips For Servicing A Merlin Garage Door In 2023

If you're the proud owner of a Merlin Garage Door, you'll want to make sure it's serviced regularly to keep it working efficiently and safely. Thankfully, this is a relatively simple process, and one that you can do yourself. With a little bit of know-how and the right tools, you can ensure that your Merlin Garage Door is running smoothly for many years to come.

Gather the Right Tools

Before you begin servicing your Merlin Garage Door, it's important to make sure that you have all the right tools. To start, you'll need a lubricant specifically designed for use on garage doors. You'll also need a pair of pliers, a screwdriver, and a wrench. Make sure all of the tools are in good working order before you start.

Check the Rollers

One of the most important parts of a Merlin Garage Door is the rollers. These are attached to the door and help it move up and down. To check the rollers, open the garage door and move it up and down manually. If it moves smoothly and without any loud noises, then the rollers are likely in good condition. If the door is difficult to move or makes loud noises, then you'll need to replace the rollers.

Examine the Bottom Brackets

The bottom brackets are the pieces of hardware that attach the door to the tracks. Over time, these brackets can become loose or corroded, which can cause the door to become misaligned and difficult to operate. To check the brackets, open the garage door and look for any signs of rust or corrosion. If you see any, then you'll need to replace the brackets.

Tighten the Hardware

It's also important to check all of the hardware on the Merlin Garage Door for any signs of wear or loose connections. Use the screwdriver and wrench to tighten any screws and bolts that are loose. If you find any worn out pieces of hardware, then you'll need to replace them.

Lubricate the Moving Parts

Once you've checked and tightened all of the hardware, it's time to lubricate the moving parts. This includes the rollers, hinges, tracks, and springs. Use the lubricant specifically designed for garage doors and apply it to all of the moving parts. This will help ensure that your Merlin Garage Door operates smoothly and without any squeaking or sticking.

Check the Balance of the Door

The last step in servicing your Merlin Garage Door is to check the balance of the door. To do this, open the door about halfway and let go. The door should remain in the same place without moving. If the door does move, then you'll need to adjust the springs and rollers to balance the door.

Conclusion

Servicing a Merlin Garage Door is a relatively simple process that can help ensure that your door continues to operate smoothly and safely. With the right tools and a little bit of know-how, you can easily do this yourself. Just remember to check the rollers, bottom brackets, hardware, and balance of the door, and to lubricate the moving parts.
