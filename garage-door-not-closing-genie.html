Troubleshooting A Genie Garage Door Not Closing

Best Garage doors and references for house or residential with custom material and more design. We Share more picture design with HD quality images.

It's a common problem for Genie garage door owners: your door won't close. This can be a major inconvenience for anyone, especially if you're trying to keep your car and other items safe from the elements. Fortunately, there are some steps you can take to troubleshoot and fix the problem.

Check the Door Sensors

The first thing you should do when troubleshooting a Genie garage door not closing is to check the door sensors. These sensors detect any obstacles in the way of the door and will prevent it from closing if something is blocking the path. If the sensors are blocked by debris, dirt, or other objects, the door won't close. To fix this issue, simply remove the obstruction and try closing the door again.

Check the Remote Control

If the door sensors are not the issue, then the next step is to check your Genie garage door remote control. If the batteries are dead or the signal is not reaching the door, then the door will not close. Try replacing the batteries and then attempting to close the door again. If that doesn't work, then you may need to replace the remote control. Alternatively, you could also try resetting the garage door opener.

Check the Tracks

If the door still won't close, then the next step is to check the tracks. Over time, the tracks that the door runs on can become warped and misaligned, which can prevent the door from closing properly. To fix this issue, you will need to straighten out the tracks and then adjust the screws that hold them in place. Once the tracks are aligned correctly, try closing the door again.

Check the Springs

The next step is to check the springs. If the springs are loose or worn out, they may be preventing the door from closing. To fix this problem, you will need to adjust or replace the springs. This should be done by a qualified professional as improper adjustment or replacement can cause serious injury.

Check the Cables

The last step is to check the cables. Over time, cables can become frayed and break, preventing the door from closing. To fix this issue, you will need to replace the cables. Again, this should be done by a qualified professional to ensure that the job is done correctly and safely.

Conclusion

If your Genie garage door is not closing, then there are a few steps you can take to troubleshoot and fix the problem. Check the door sensors, the remote control, the tracks, the springs, and the cables. If any of these components are damaged or malfunctioning, then they may be preventing the door from closing. In that case, you will need to repair or replace them. If you are not comfortable doing this yourself, then it is best to contact a qualified professional.
