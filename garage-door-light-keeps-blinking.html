What Does a Blinking Garage Door Light Mean?

The first step in understanding why your garage door light is blinking is to determine the type of garage door opener you have. If you have a newer model, your garage door light will likely be a single LED light or a series of small LED lights. This type of garage door light should blink when the door is opening or closing. This is a normal function of the opener and should not be cause for concern.

On older models, however, the garage door light may be a larger bulb or even a series of bulbs. If you have this type of opener and the light is blinking, it usually means that there is something wrong with the opener. The most common cause of a blinking garage door light on an older model is a failed capacitor.

How to Diagnose a Blinking Garage Door Light

If you have a newer opener with a single LED light or a series of small LED lights, the first thing to do is to check the power supply to make sure that it is connected and working properly. If the power supply is connected but not working, then you may need to replace the power supply.

If you have an older opener with a larger bulb or a series of bulbs, then you'll need to check the capacitor to see if it is working properly. The capacitor is an electrical component that stores energy and helps to keep the opener running smoothly. If the capacitor is not functioning properly, then it can cause the opener to malfunction or the garage door light to blink.

How to Fix a Blinking Garage Door Light

If you have a newer opener with a single LED light or a series of small LED lights, then you should check the power supply and replace it if necessary. If the power supply is working properly, then you should try resetting the opener. This can usually be done by pressing and holding the reset button on the back of the opener for a few seconds.

If you have an older opener with a larger bulb or a series of bulbs, then you'll need to replace the capacitor. This is a fairly simple task that can usually be done with a few basic tools. You'll need to locate the capacitor and then remove it from the opener and replace it with a new one.
