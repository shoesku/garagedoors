Why Is My Garage Door Flashing Orange?

Best Garage doors and references for house or residential with custom material and more design. We Share more picture design with HD quality images.

If you’ve ever had your garage door start flashing orange, you’ve probably been quite confused. After all, it’s not every day you come home and find your garage door flashing an unusual color.

Most garage doors come with a safety feature that will flash an orange light when something is wrong. This is often the case when the door is stuck, or if there is something blocking the door from closing. That’s why the orange light comes on—it’s a warning to you that something needs to be fixed.

Why an Orange Light?

Garage door manufacturers have opted to use orange as the color of warning because it’s easily recognizable and stands out from other colors. The orange light is designed to draw your attention to the fact that the garage door isn’t working properly. It’s also designed to be bright enough to be seen, but not so bright that it’s blinding or annoying.

The orange light will usually stay on until the problem is fixed or the door is reset. If the problem persists, you’ll need to contact a garage door repair specialist to diagnose the issue and make any necessary repairs.

Common Reasons for an Orange Light

If your garage door is flashing an orange light, there are a few common causes for this issue. Here are some of the most common reasons for a orange light warning:

  • The garage door is blocked from closing.
  • The garage door opener is malfunctioning.
  • The door is stuck in the open or closed position.
  • The door sensors are misaligned or malfunctioning.
  • The door springs are worn or broken.
  • The opener’s batteries are low.

How to Fix an Orange Light

If your garage door is flashing an orange light, you’ll need to diagnose and fix the issue as soon as possible. The first step is to check if the door is blocked or stuck in the open or closed position, as this is the most common cause of an orange light warning.

If the door is blocked or stuck, you can usually free it by removing the obstruction or manually pushing or pulling the door. If the door is still stuck, you may need to call a garage door repair specialist to diagnose and fix the issue.

If the door isn’t blocked or stuck, then the issue is likely related to the opener or sensors. You may need to check the batteries, alignment, and wiring of the opener and sensors, or you may need to replace the opener or sensors if they’re malfunctioning.

Conclusion

If your garage door is flashing orange, you’ll need to diagnose and fix the issue as soon as possible. The orange light is a warning that something is wrong with the door, and it needs to be addressed before it can be used safely again. Check for obstructions or stuck doors, and if the issue persists, contact a garage door repair specialist for help.
