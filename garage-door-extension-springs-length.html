Garage door extension springs are a common component of most garage doors. These springs are designed to help the door open and close with ease. Most homeowners don't realize that there are various lengths to choose from when it comes to garage door extension springs. In this article, we'll explain the different lengths available, and how to choose the right size for your garage door.

What is the Standard Length for Garage Door Extension Springs?

The standard length for garage door extension springs is 7 feet. This length is ideal for single-car garage doors. For double-car garage doors, the best choice is a 10-foot extension spring. The length of the garage door extension spring will affect the tension, so it's important to choose the right size.

What Are the Benefits of Longer Springs?

Longer garage door extension springs are beneficial in a few different ways. First, they provide more tension when the door is opened and closed. This means the door will open and close more smoothly. Additionally, longer springs provide more support and stability to the door, which can help prevent it from sagging or warping over time. Finally, longer springs can last longer than shorter springs.

What Are the Benefits of Shorter Springs?

Shorter garage door extension springs are beneficial in a few different ways as well. For one, they don't require as much tension to open and close the door. This can be helpful for homeowners who don't want to use too much force when opening and closing their garage doors. Additionally, shorter springs are less likely to break due to excessive tension or wear. Finally, shorter springs are usually less expensive and easier to install.

What Should I Know Before Installing a Garage Door Extension Spring?

There are a few things you should know before installing a garage door extension spring. First, make sure you're using the right size for your garage door. Second, measure the door's existing spring to make sure you're replacing it with the same size. Third, make sure the spring is properly tensioned. Finally, be aware of the weight of the garage door and make sure the spring can handle it.

How Can I Tell If the Spring Is Properly Tensioned?

To check if the spring is properly tensioned, open the garage door about halfway and then let go. The door should stay in place for a few seconds before slowly closing. If the door falls quickly, then the spring is not properly tensioned. In this case, you'll need to adjust the tension on the spring.

How Long Do Garage Door Extension Springs Last?

Most garage door extension springs will last between 5 and 8 years. However, this can vary depending on the quality of the spring, how often the door is used, and the weight of the door. It's important to inspect the springs regularly to make sure they're in good condition.

Where Can I Buy Garage Door Extension Springs?

Garage door extension springs are available at most hardware stores and home improvement stores. You can also buy them online from a variety of retailers. Be sure to read the product description to make sure you're buying the right size for your garage door.

Conclusion

Garage door extension springs come in a variety of lengths, so it's important to choose the right size for your door. Longer springs provide more tension and support, but shorter springs are easier to install and require less tension. Make sure to measure your door's existing spring and check the tension before installing a new spring. Finally, be sure to inspect the springs regularly to make sure they're in good condition.
