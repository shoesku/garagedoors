How To Get The Right Spare Key For Your Garage Door

Best Garage doors and references for house or residential with custom material and more design. We Share more picture design with HD quality images.

Having a spare key for your garage door is a great idea, especially if you ever find yourself locked out of your garage. It can save you from having to call a locksmith and pay their expensive fees, or from having to break in to your own home. It can also help you if you ever lose or forget your original key, so it's always a good idea to have a spare key ready.

However, it's important to make sure that you get the right spare key for your garage door. Different garage door models and brands may have different requirements for the spare key, so it's important to get the right one for your door. Here's what you need to know about getting the right spare key for your garage door.

Choose the Right Brand

The first step in getting the right spare key is to make sure that you get the one that's made for your particular brand of garage door. Different brands may have different types of locks, and so their keys may be different as well. For example, some of the more popular brands like Chamberlain and LiftMaster use different keys than other brands, so it's important to make sure that you get the right one for your door.

If you don't know which brand of garage door you have, you can usually find out by looking at the manual that came with your door. The manual should have the brand name and model number of your door, and from there you can find out which type of key you need.

Choose the Right Size

Once you know which brand of key you need, the next step is to make sure that you get the right size. Different brands may have different sizes of keys, so it's important to make sure that you get the correct size for your door. You can usually find the size of your key in the manual for your garage door, but if you can't find it there you can also measure the key that you already have.

When measuring the key, make sure to measure the length of the blade and not the entire key, as this will give you the most accurate measurement. Once you know the size of the key you need, you can then purchase a spare key that is the same size.

Choose the Right Material

The last step in choosing the right spare key is to make sure that you get the right material. Different garage door brands may have different materials for their keys, so it's important to make sure that you get the right one for your door. The most common materials for garage door keys are metal and plastic, so make sure to get the one that is made for your particular brand of door.

If you're not sure which material is best for your door, it's always a good idea to check with the manufacturer. They should have information about the type of key that works best for your particular model of door, and they may even be able to provide you with a spare key in the material that you need.

Keep Your Spare Key Safe

Once you have the right spare key for your garage door, it's important to make sure that you keep it in a safe place. You don't want to risk losing or misplacing your key, as this could leave your garage door vulnerable to theft or other forms of damage. Instead, it's best to keep your key in a secure place where it won't be easily found, such as in a locked drawer or a safe.

It's also a good idea to keep a copy of your spare key in a different location. This way, if you ever do lose your main key, you'll still have a spare that you can use to get back into your garage. This can be especially helpful if you ever find yourself locked out of your garage in an emergency.

Get Professional Help

If you're ever unsure about which type of spare key you need for your garage door, it's always a good idea to consult a professional. A locksmith or garage door technician can provide you with the right key for your door and can also help you with any other questions that you may have. This can save you time, money, and hassle, and can help you get the right spare key for your garage door.

Conclusion

Having a spare key for your garage door is a great idea, but it's important to make sure that you get the right one. Different garage door brands and models may have different requirements for their spare keys, so it's important to make sure that you get the right one for your door. Be sure to choose the right brand, size, and material for your key, and always keep it in a secure place. If you ever have any questions or concerns, it's always a good idea to consult a professional.
