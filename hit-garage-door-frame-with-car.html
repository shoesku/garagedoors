How To Fix A Garage Door Frame After You Hit It With Your Car

Introduction

Accidents happen. And sometimes, if you’re not careful, you accidentally hit your garage door frame with your car. Whether you’re in a rush to get somewhere or you’re just being careless, hitting your garage door frame with your car can cause serious damage. Not to worry though, because this article will provide you with all the information you need to repair your garage door and frame after you’ve hit it.

Steps to Take When You Hit Your Garage Door Frame

The first step to take when you hit your garage door frame with your car is to assess the damage. Take a look at the frame and the door to see how much damage has been done. Keep in mind that you may need to replace the frame if it has been bent or otherwise damaged. It’s also important to make sure that the door is still properly functioning. If the door isn’t opening and closing properly, then you’ll need to repair or replace it as well.

Remove Debris

Once you’ve assessed the damage, it’s time to remove any debris that may have been left behind. This includes any broken pieces of wood, metal, or plastic that may have been knocked loose from the frame or the door. Make sure to use gloves when handling any sharp or jagged pieces of debris.

Repair the Frame

Once you’ve cleared the debris, it’s time to repair the frame. If the frame is bent or otherwise damaged, it may need to be replaced. If the frame is still in good shape, then you can use a hammer and nails to straighten it out. If the frame has been cracked or split, then you may need to use glue or a reinforcing bar to fix it.

Repair the Door

If your garage door isn’t functioning properly, then it may need to be repaired or replaced. If the door is bent or otherwise damaged, it may need to be replaced. If the door is still in good shape, then you can use a screwdriver and some WD-40 to tighten the hinges and rollers. If the door has been split or cracked, then you may need to use some wood glue to repair it.

Reinforce the Frame

Once you’ve repaired the frame and door, it’s time to reinforce them. This can be done by using a reinforcing bar or steel plate to strengthen the frame and door. This will help prevent further damage in the future.

Inspect the Door

After you’ve reinforced the door and frame, it’s time to inspect them. Make sure that the door is still opening and closing properly, and that the frame is still securely attached to the wall. If anything isn’t functioning properly, then you may need to make further repairs.

Conclusion

Hitting your garage door frame with your car can be a scary experience. But don’t worry, because with a bit of patience and the right tools, you can repair your garage door and frame. Just remember to take your time, assess the damage, remove any debris, repair or replace the frame and door, reinforce the frame, and inspect the door. Then you’ll be back to using your garage in no time!
