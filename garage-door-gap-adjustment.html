Garage Door Gap Adjustment: How To Make Sure Your Garage Door Is Secure

When it comes to security, one of the most important aspects of your home is the garage door. Not only does it protect your property from outside intruders, but it also keeps the home safe from the elements. However, if you don’t properly maintain your garage door, it could be vulnerable to break-ins. One of the simplest ways to make sure your garage door is secure is to adjust the gap between the door and the frame. Here is a comprehensive guide on how to make sure your garage door gap is properly adjusted.

Why is a Proper Gap Important?

Having a proper gap between the door and the frame is essential for home security. If the gap is too small, it can allow for outside intruders to easily pry the door open. Additionally, a too-small gap may also make it easier for wind or water to get inside, which can cause damage to your home. On the other hand, having a gap that is too large can also be a security issue. If the gap is too large, it can allow someone to easily slip their hand in and unlock the door. It can also make it much easier for someone to lift the door off of its track.

How to Adjust the Gap

To make sure your garage door gap is properly adjusted, you will need to do the following:

  • Inspect the gap between the door and the frame. It should be between 1/4” and 3/4”.
  • If the gap is too large, you can use a screwdriver to adjust the hinges. Tighten the screws slightly to reduce the gap.
  • If the gap is too small, loosen the screws slightly to increase the gap.
  • If the gap is still not right, you may need to adjust the door itself. This can be done by adjusting the bolts on the bottom of the door.

If you are not comfortable with making the adjustments yourself, it is best to contact a professional. They will be able to ensure that the gap is properly adjusted, as well as inspect the rest of the door for any other potential security issues.

Materials Needed for Adjustment

To make the adjustments to your garage door gap, you will need the following materials:

  • Phillips-head screwdriver
  • Adjustable wrench
  • Cordless drill (optional)

It is also important to make sure you have the right tools for the job. If you don’t have the right tools, you may end up damaging the door or the frame. Additionally, if you are not comfortable with making the adjustments yourself, it is best to contact a professional.

Additional Security Measures

Adjusting the gap between the door and the frame is one of the simplest ways to improve the security of your home. However, there are many other security measures you can take to make sure your home is as safe as possible. Some of these include installing a security camera system, adding a deadbolt to the door, and installing motion-sensor lights. Additionally, you should also make sure to regularly inspect your garage door for any signs of wear or damage.

Final Thoughts

It is essential to make sure your garage door gap is properly adjusted in order to keep your home safe and secure. Adjusting the gap is a simple and straightforward process, and with the right tools, you can do it yourself in a few minutes. However, if you are not comfortable making the adjustments yourself, you should contact a professional.
