How To Replace Your Garage Door Weather Seal In 2023

Garage door weather seals are an essential component of a well-functioning garage door. They help to keep out moisture, pests, and help to reduce energy costs by keeping cold air out of the home in the winter. Unfortunately, these seals don't last forever and are prone to wear and tear over time. If you notice that your garage door isn't sealing well anymore, it may be time to replace the weather seal.

Tools Needed for Replacing the Weather Seal

You don't need a lot of specialized tools for replacing the weather seal on your garage door. The most important tool you will need is a good ladder, as you will need to be able to reach the top of the garage door to be able to replace the seal. You will also need a flathead screwdriver, a pair of scissors, and a utility knife. You may also want to wear work gloves, as the seal is likely to be dirty and may contain debris.

Steps for Replacing the Weather Seal

The first step to replacing the weather seal is to purchase the right kind of seal. There are many types of weather seal available, so it's important to make sure you purchase the correct type. Once you have the correct seal, you are ready to begin the process. Begin by using the flathead screwdriver to remove the old seal. You may need to use the utility knife to cut away any pieces of the old seal that have become stuck or have been damaged.

Once the old seal is removed, you can begin to install the new seal. Begin by taking the new seal and laying it down along the top of the garage door. Make sure that the seal fits all the way around the door and that it is lined up correctly on all sides. Once you have the correct placement, use the scissors to cut the seal to size. Be sure to leave a few extra inches at the end, as this will be necessary for later steps.

When you have the seal cut to the proper size, you can begin to install it. Start by peeling back the adhesive backing of the seal and pressing it down firmly against the garage door. Make sure that it is pressed down firmly and that there are no gaps or air bubbles. Once the seal is in place, you can use the screws to secure it to the frame of the garage door. Make sure that the screws are tight and that the seal is securely attached to the door.

When the seal is installed, you can use the extra inches at the end of the seal to attach it to the door frame. Use the scissors to cut the extra material off and then use the screws to attach the seal to the frame. Once the seal is attached, you can use the utility knife to trim any excess material. Be sure to leave a few extra inches at the end to allow for future expansion.

Conclusion

Replacing the weather seal on your garage door can be a fairly simple process. With the right materials and tools, you can easily complete the job in a few hours. Make sure that you purchase the correct type of seal and that you securely attach it to the frame of the garage door. With regular maintenance, you can ensure that your garage door weather seal remains in good condition for years to come!
