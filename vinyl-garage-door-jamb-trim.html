Vinyl Garage Door Jamb Trim: Your Guide For 2023

Are you looking for an easy way to spruce up your garage? Installing vinyl garage door jamb trim is a great solution. This type of trim is easy to install and provides a clean, professional look that will make your garage look like new. Plus, vinyl is a durable material that can withstand the wear and tear of everyday use.

Installing vinyl garage door jamb trim is a relatively straightforward task. While the exact installation process may vary depending on the type of garage and the type of trim you choose, the basics are the same. Here’s what you need to know to get started:

Step 1: Measure the Door Jamb

Before you purchase any trim, you’ll need to measure the jamb of your garage door. You’ll want to measure the width and height of the jamb to ensure that you purchase the correct size trim. Most vinyl garage door jamb trim is available in a variety of sizes, so you should be able to find the perfect size for your needs.

Step 2: Choose the Right Trim

Once you’ve measured the jamb of your garage door, it’s time to choose the right trim for your needs. Vinyl garage door jamb trim is available in a variety of colors, styles, and textures, so you’re sure to find something that matches the look of your home. You can also purchase custom-made trim if you’d like.

Step 3: Prepare the Door Jamb

Before you install the trim, you’ll need to prepare the door jamb. Start by cleaning the jamb with a damp cloth to remove any dirt or debris. Once the jamb is clean, use a jigsaw to cut out any sections of the jamb that will be covered by the trim. This will ensure that the trim fits snugly against the jamb.

Step 4: Install the Trim

When you’re ready to install the trim, start by measuring and cutting the trim to fit the jamb. Make sure to leave a small gap between the trim and the jamb to allow for expansion and contraction. Use a nail gun to secure the trim in place, making sure to keep the nails at least one inch away from the edge of the trim.

Step 5: Seal the Trim

Once the trim is in place, you’ll need to seal it to protect it from the elements. Use a silicone sealant to seal the edges of the trim, making sure to cover all the seams. Allow the sealant to dry before you use your garage door.

Conclusion

Installing vinyl garage door jamb trim is a great way to give your garage a fresh, new look. It’s a relatively easy job that can be completed in a short amount of time. With the right tools and a little bit of patience, you can have a garage that looks like new in no time.
