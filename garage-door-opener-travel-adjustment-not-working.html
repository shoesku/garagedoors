Troubleshooting When Your Garage Door Opener Travel Adjustment Is Not Working

Overview

Having a garage door opener travel adjustment that is not working can be incredibly frustrating. You can’t open and close the door properly and that can cause a huge inconvenience. Thankfully, there are a few steps you can take to troubleshoot the problem and hopefully get the garage door opener travel adjustment back in working order. In this article, we’ll discuss the steps you should take if you have a garage door opener travel adjustment that is not working.

Check the Settings

The first thing you should do when troubleshooting a garage door opener travel adjustment that is not working is to check the settings. It may be a simple issue like the travel limit settings needing to be adjusted. Depending on your model of garage door opener, you may need to use a manual or remote control to adjust the settings. Be sure to consult your user manual to determine how to adjust the settings.

Inspect the Motor

The next step you should take when troubleshooting a garage door opener travel adjustment that is not working is to inspect the motor. Make sure that it is properly connected to the power source and that there are no loose wires. If the motor is not functioning properly, you may need to replace it. It’s also a good idea to inspect the gear system to make sure that the gears are properly lubricated and in good condition.

Clean the Tracks

If the problem is not with the motor or gear system, then you should check the tracks. If the tracks are dirty or blocked, it can cause the garage door to not open or close properly. Be sure to check the tracks for dirt, debris, and obstructions. If you find any, be sure to clean them and remove any obstructions.

Check the Wiring

If the tracks are clean and the motor and gear system are functioning properly, then you should check the wiring. Make sure that all the wires are properly connected and that there are no loose or broken wires. You may need to use a multimeter to test the wiring to make sure that it is functioning properly.

Replace the Sensors

If none of the above solutions work, then you should check the sensors. The sensors are responsible for detecting if the garage door is open or closed and they can malfunction if they become dirty or damaged. Be sure to clean the sensors and check for any damage. If the sensors are damaged, you may need to replace them.

Replace the Entire Unit

If the above solutions do not work, then it may be time to replace the entire garage door opener unit. This may seem like a daunting task, but it’s actually relatively simple. Be sure to consult your user manual for instructions on how to replace the unit. Also, be sure to purchase a unit that is compatible with your garage door.

Conclusion

If you have a garage door opener travel adjustment that is not working, then there are a few steps you can take to troubleshoot the problem. Be sure to check the settings, inspect the motor and gear system, clean the tracks, check the wiring, and replace the sensors. If none of these solutions work, then you may need to replace the entire unit. Be sure to consult your user manual for instructions on how to replace the unit.
