How To Troubleshoot Gliderol Garage Door Not Closing

Best Garage doors and references for house or residential with custom material and more design. We Share more picture design with HD quality images.

Are you a homeowner who has a Gliderol garage door and it doesn't seem to close all the way? Many homeowners experience this issue, but with a few simple troubleshooting steps, you can get your Gliderol garage door to close properly. Here are some tips and tricks to help you get your Gliderol garage door to close all the way.

Check the Sensors

The first thing you should do when your Gliderol garage door won't close all the way is to check the sensors. Gliderol garage doors have sensors that detect whether something is in the way of the closing door. If the sensors detect something, the door will stop closing and will reverse back up. Check the sensors to make sure they are properly aligned and nothing is blocking them. If the sensors are not aligned properly or something is blocking them, the door will not close all the way.

Check the Tracks

The next thing you should check when your Gliderol garage door won't close all the way is the tracks. Over time, the tracks can become blocked with dirt, leaves, and other debris. This can cause the door to not close all the way. Use a broom to sweep away any dirt or debris that may be blocking the tracks. You can also lubricate the tracks with a garage door lubricant to help the door close more smoothly.

Adjust the Spring Tension

You may also need to adjust the spring tension on your Gliderol garage door. Over time, the springs can become loose and need to be tightened. If the springs are too loose, the door will not close all the way. To adjust the spring tension, you will need to use a special tool. You can purchase the tool from a hardware store or your local Gliderol dealer.

Check the Limit Switch

If you still can't get your Gliderol garage door to close all the way, you may need to check the limit switch. The limit switch is responsible for telling the motor when the door has reached its full opening and closing position. If the limit switch is not set correctly, the door will not close all the way. To adjust the limit switch, you will need to access the control panel on the motor. You can find instructions for adjusting the limit switch in your Gliderol garage door manual.

Check the Motor

If you have tried all of the above troubleshooting steps and your Gliderol garage door still won't close all the way, you may need to check the motor. The motor could be the culprit if the door won't close all the way. Check the motor to make sure it is working properly. You may need to replace the motor if it is not working properly.

Replace the Springs

If the motor is working properly and you have adjusted the tension on the springs, you may need to replace the springs. Over time, the springs can become worn out and need to be replaced. You can purchase new springs from your local Gliderol dealer or online. Be sure to follow the instructions carefully when replacing the springs.

Call a Professional

If you have tried all of the troubleshooting steps and your Gliderol garage door still won't close all the way, you may need to call a professional. A professional technician can come to your home and inspect the door. They can determine what is causing the problem and make the necessary repairs. Repairs can range from simple adjustments to more complex repairs, such as replacing the motor or replacing the springs.

Conclusion

If your Gliderol garage door won't close all the way, don't panic. With a few simple troubleshooting steps, you can get your Gliderol garage door to close properly. Check the sensors, the tracks, the spring tension, the limit switch, and the motor. If all else fails, you may need to call a professional technician to inspect the door and make the necessary repairs.
