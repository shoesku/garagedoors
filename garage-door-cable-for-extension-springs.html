What Are Extension Springs?

Extension springs are an important part of your garage door system. These are the springs that are responsible for lifting and lowering the door when it is opened or closed. They are typically made of metal and are very strong, but if they are not installed correctly or if they are not maintained properly, they can become damaged or broken. This is why it is important to make sure that your extension springs are in good condition and that you have the right cables for them.

What Are Garage Door Cables For Extension Springs?

Garage door cables for extension springs are the cables that connect the springs to the door itself. They are made of either steel or galvanized steel and are designed to be able to withstand the strain of lifting and lowering the garage door. The cables are also designed to be able to handle the weight of the door, as well as any other external forces that may be applied to the door. The cables are typically connected to the extension springs by either a screw or a nut, and they can be adjusted to fit the specific needs of the garage door.
