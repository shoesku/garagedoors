How To Fix A Garage Door That Won't Close With Nothing In The Way

Overview

If you're having trouble with a garage door that won't close, it can be a frustrating experience. It's important to remember that the cause of the problem is likely not something serious, and it can usually be fixed with a few simple steps. In this article, we'll discuss how to fix a garage door that won't close when there is nothing in the way.

Check the Motor

The first thing you should do when your garage door won't close is to check the motor. If the motor is running, but the door isn't closing, there may be an issue with the power supply. Check the power cords, and make sure they are properly connected and in good condition. If the power supply is not the issue, then it could be a problem with the motor itself. You may need to replace the motor or have it serviced.

Check the Springs

If the motor is not the issue, then you should check the garage door springs. The springs are what lift the door and help to close it. If the springs are worn out or broken, they may not be able to lift the door properly, which could cause it to stay open. If this is the case, then you will need to replace the springs in order to get the door to close.

Check the Tracks

The tracks are what guide the door as it opens and closes. If the tracks are bent, misaligned, or damaged, then the door may not be able to close properly. Check the tracks for any signs of damage or misalignment, and use a level to make sure the tracks are properly aligned. If the tracks are not the issue, then you can move on to the next step.

Check the Rollers

The rollers are what allow the door to roll up and down. If the rollers are worn out, then they may not be able to support the weight of the door. Check the rollers for any signs of wear and tear, and make sure they are properly lubricated. If they are not the issue, then you can move on to the next step.

Check the Limit Switch

The limit switch is what tells the motor when to stop lifting or lowering the door. If the limit switch is not properly adjusted, then it may not be able to tell the motor when to stop, which could cause the door to stay open. Check the limit switch for any signs of damage or wear and tear, and make sure it is properly adjusted.

Check the Safety Sensors

The safety sensors are what prevent the door from closing if there is something in the way. If the sensors are not properly aligned or are obstructed, then they may not be able to detect if there is something in the way. Check the sensors for any signs of damage or misalignment, and make sure they are properly aligned and unobstructed.

Check the Control Panel

The control panel is what allows you to control the door from a remote location. If the control panel is not working properly, then it may not be able to send the signal to the motor to close the door. Check the control panel for any signs of damage or wear and tear, and make sure it is properly connected and in good condition.

Check the Hinges

The hinges are what connect the door to the track. If the hinges are worn out or broken, then the door may not be able to close properly. Check the hinges for any signs of damage or wear and tear, and make sure they are properly lubricated. If the hinges are not the issue, then you can move on to the next step.

Conclusion

When your garage door won't close with nothing in the way, it can be a frustrating experience. Fortunately, the cause of the problem is usually not something serious, and it can usually be fixed with a few simple steps. Make sure to check the motor, springs, tracks, rollers, limit switch, safety sensors, control panel, and hinges for any signs of damage or wear and tear. If all else fails, you may need to call a professional to help you with the issue.
