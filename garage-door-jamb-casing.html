Tips For Installing Garage Door Jamb Casing

Best Garage doors and references for house or residential with custom material and more design. We Share more picture design with HD quality images.

If you’re looking to upgrade the look of your garage, one great way to do it is by installing a garage door jamb casing. This is a simple and cost-effective way to give your garage a more professional, put-together look. It’s also a great way to protect your garage’s interior walls from wear and tear. Here are a few tips to help you install your jamb casing with ease.

1. Measure and Cut the Wood

Before you begin, you’ll need to measure the width of your garage door opening and cut the wood for your casing. Generally, the wood should overlap the opening by around 3/4” on each side. You’ll also need to cut the head jamb and side jambs for the opening. When measuring, be sure to measure twice and cut once to ensure that you get the perfect fit.

2. Install the Head Jamb and Casing

The first step is to install the head jamb and the casing. To do this, you’ll need to use a level to make sure the jamb is perfectly straight. You can use a nail gun to secure the jamb into place, but be sure to use enough nails to ensure it’s secure. Once the jamb is in place, use a miter saw to cut the casing to size. Place the casing onto the head jamb with a level and use a nail gun to secure it.

3. Install the Side Jambs

Next, you’ll need to install the side jambs. Start by placing the side jamb onto the side wall and using a level to make sure it’s straight. Secure the jamb into place using a nail gun. Once it’s secure, use a miter saw to cut the casing to size, then place it onto the side jamb and secure it using a nail gun.

4. Install the Casing Around the Door Opening

Now it’s time to install the casing around the door opening. Start by measuring the width of the opening and cutting the casing accordingly. Place the casing onto the wall and use a level to make sure it’s straight. Secure it into place using a nail gun. Cut the casing around the door opening to the desired size and secure it into place.

5. Finish the Casing

Once all of the casing is in place, you’ll need to finish it off. Start by filling the nail holes with wood putty or caulk. Once the putty or caulk is dry, sand down the casing until it’s smooth. Lastly, you can use a paint or stain of your choice to finish the look.

Conclusion

Installing a garage door jamb casing is a great way to give your garage a more professional, put-together look. It’s a fairly straightforward process, but it’s important to take the necessary steps to ensure that your casing is properly installed. Follow the tips above and you’ll be able to enjoy your new, improved garage in no time.
