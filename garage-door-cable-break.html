How To Deal With A Broken Garage Door Cable

Best Garage doors and references for house or residential with custom material and more design. We Share more picture design with HD quality images.

Garage door cables are one of the most important components of a functioning garage door. The cables are responsible for lifting and lower the garage door. When the cables break, it can be a major nuisance and a safety hazard. In this article, we will go over how to deal with a broken garage door cable.

Step 1: Identify the Problem

The first step in dealing with a broken garage door cable is to identify the problem. If you look closely, you should be able to see the broken cable. It is important to note that the cable may be frayed, cracked, or have a hole in it. Once you have identified the problem, you should move on to step two.

Step 2: Remove the Cable

The next step is to remove the broken cable. In most cases, the cable is attached to the garage door using screws. If you have the right tools, you should be able to remove the screws and the cable. However, if you don’t have the right tools, you may need to call a professional to help you with the removal process.

Step 3: Replace the Cable

Once the broken cable has been removed, you can then replace it with a new one. Most home improvement stores carry garage door cables, so you should be able to find one that fits. When replacing the cable, make sure to use the same type of screws that were used to attach the original cable. This will ensure that the cable is securely attached.

Step 4: Test the Cable

Once the cable has been replaced, it’s time to test it out. Open the garage door and make sure that the cable is working properly. If it is not, then you may need to adjust the tension of the cable. This can be done by loosening the screws that attach the cable to the door, and then tightening them back up.

Step 5: Secure the Cable

Once the cable is working properly, you should secure it. To do this, you can use zip ties or wire ties to hold the cable in place. This will ensure that the cable does not become loose or damaged in the future.

Step 6: Replace Any Damaged Parts

If the cable has damaged other parts of the garage door, then you should replace those parts as well. Make sure to replace any worn or damaged rollers, hinges, or springs. If any of these parts are not working properly, then the garage door may not function properly.

Step 7: Test the Garage Door

Once the cable is replaced and any other parts are fixed, it’s time to test the garage door. Open and close the door several times to make sure that it is working properly. If it is not, then you may need to adjust the tension of the cable again.

Conclusion

In conclusion, dealing with a broken garage door cable can be a hassle, but it’s not impossible. By following the steps outlined in this article, you should be able to replace the cable and get your garage door working properly again. Just remember to always be cautious when working with garage door cables, as they can be very dangerous if not handled correctly.
