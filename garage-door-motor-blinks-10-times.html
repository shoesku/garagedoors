Troubleshooting When Your Garage Door Motor Blinks 10 Times

It is quite common for garage door motors to blink 10 times when something is wrong. In most cases, this is a sign that something is wrong with the safety sensors, but it could also be a sign of a more serious issue. In this article, we will discuss what causes a garage door motor to blink 10 times, and how you can troubleshoot and fix the issue.

What Causes a Garage Door Motor to Blink 10 Times?

Garage door motors are designed to sense when something is wrong. When something is wrong, the motor will send out a signal that it is not working properly. In most cases, this signal will be a blinking of the motor 10 times. This blinking is a sign that something is wrong with the safety sensors, such as a misalignment or a blockage.

In some cases, the blinking could also be caused by a power surge or a power outage. If this is the case, then you will need to reset the garage door motor, and then try again. If the motor still blinks 10 times, then the issue is likely with the safety sensors.

How to Troubleshoot a Garage Door Motor that Blinks 10 Times

The first step in troubleshooting a garage door motor that blinks 10 times is to check the safety sensors. The sensors are located near the bottom of the garage door, and they should be aligned and free of any blockages. If the sensors are not properly aligned, then you can adjust them to make sure they are in line with each other.

If the sensors are blocked or misaligned, then you will need to clear the blockage or adjust the alignment. In some cases, you may need to replace the sensors if they are damaged or malfunctioning. Once the sensors are aligned and free of any blockages, then you should try to open the door again.

Other Causes of Blinking

In some cases, the blinking could be caused by a power surge or a power outage. If this is the case, then you will need to reset the garage door motor, and then try again. If the motor still blinks 10 times, then the issue is likely with the safety sensors.

Another cause of blinking could be a malfunctioning remote. If you have a remote, then you should check to make sure it is working properly. If it is not working properly, then you should try replacing the batteries or replacing the remote.

What to Do if You Cannot Fix the Issue

If you are unable to fix the issue yourself, then you may need to call a professional. A professional will be able to determine the exact cause of the issue and provide you with a solution. It is important to remember that some issues may require the assistance of a professional in order to be fixed.

Conclusion

If your garage door motor is blinking 10 times, then it is likely a sign that something is wrong with the safety sensors. You can try to troubleshoot the issue yourself, but if you are unable to fix it, then you may need to call a professional. It is important to remember that some issues may require the assistance of a professional in order to be fixed.
