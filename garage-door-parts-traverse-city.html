Types of Springs

When it comes to replacing garage door springs, you have two main options: torsion springs or extension springs. Torsion springs are the most common type of garage door springs and are typically used in homes with a single panel garage door. These springs are mounted above the garage door and are designed to be wound and unwound when the door opens and closes. Extension springs are mounted on either side of the garage door and are designed to stretch and contract as the door moves.

Types of Cables

Garage door cables are a crucial part of the door’s opening and closing process. Without them, the door would not be able to lift or lower. There are two main types of garage door cables: torsion cables and extension cables. Torsion cables are used in conjunction with torsion springs to provide the force needed to lift and lower the door. Extension cables are used with extension springs and are designed to provide the tension needed to move the door.

Types of Openers

Garage door openers are the motors used to open and close the door. There are three main types of openers: screw drive, belt drive, and chain drive. Screw drive openers use a threaded rod to open and close the door. They’re relatively quiet and require minimal maintenance. Belt drive openers use a rubber belt to open and close the door. They’re typically the quietest option but can be more expensive than other types of openers. Chain drive openers use a metal chain to open and close the door. They’re the least expensive option but tend to be the loudest.

Types of Materials

Garage doors come in a variety of materials, including wood, steel, aluminum, and fiberglass. Wood is the most traditional material used in garage doors and can be stained or painted to match the color of the home. Steel is the most common material used in garage doors. It’s strong, durable, and relatively inexpensive. Aluminum is lightweight and less expensive than steel, but it is also less durable. Fiberglass is lightweight and durable, but it is more expensive than other materials.

Maintenance and Repair

Once you’ve installed your new garage door, it’s important to keep up with regular maintenance and repairs.
