How To Fix A Chamberlain Garage Door Opener Jerking When Opening

Do you have a Chamberlain garage door opener that jerks when opening? If so, you’re not alone! It’s a common issue that can be rectified with a few simple steps. This article will walk you through the process of diagnosing and fixing the problem. Read on to learn more.

Step 1: Check the Tracks

The first thing to do is check the tracks for any debris or obstructions that can cause the jerking. Look for anything that might be blocking the path of the garage door. If you see something, remove it. Then, check the tracks for any signs of damage or wear. Replace the tracks if necessary.

Step 2: Check the Garage Door Spring

The next step is to check the garage door spring. It should be in good condition and free of any rust or damage. If it’s not, then it’s likely the source of the jerking. You’ll need to replace the spring in order to fix the problem.

Step 3: Check the Motor

The motor of the Chamberlain garage door opener may also be the source of the jerking. Look for any signs of damage or wear and if you find any, replace the motor. If the motor is in good condition, then the source of the jerking is likely something else.

Step 4: Check the Circuit Board

The circuit board is the brain of the Chamberlain garage door opener and it can be the cause of the jerking. Check the circuit board for any signs of damage or wear. If you find any, replace the circuit board. If it’s in good condition, then the source of the jerking is likely something else.

Step 5: Check the Limit Switches

The limit switches of the Chamberlain garage door opener can also be the source of the jerking. Check the limit switches for any signs of damage or wear. If you find any, replace the limit switches. If they are in good condition, then the source of the jerking is likely something else.

Step 6: Check the Drive Chain

The drive chain of the Chamberlain garage door opener can also be the source of the jerking. Check the drive chain for any signs of damage or wear. If you find any, replace the drive chain. If it’s in good condition, then the source of the jerking is likely something else.

Step 7: Check the Remote

The remote of the Chamberlain garage door opener can also be the source of the jerking. Check the remote for any signs of damage or wear. If you find any, replace the remote. If it’s in good condition, then the source of the jerking is likely something else.

Step 8: Check the Wiring

The wiring of the Chamberlain garage door opener can also be the source of the jerking. Check the wiring for any signs of damage or wear. If you find any, replace the wiring. If it’s in good condition, then the source of the jerking is likely something else.

Step 9: Check the Sensors

The sensors of the Chamberlain garage door opener can also be the source of the jerking. Check the sensors for any signs of damage or wear. If you find any, replace the sensors. If they are in good condition, then the source of the jerking is likely something else.

Step 10: Contact a Professional

If you’ve checked all of the above and still can’t find the source of the jerking, then it’s time to contact a professional. A professional can help diagnose the problem and can make any necessary repairs. It’s important to make sure you get the problem fixed as soon as possible in order to avoid any further damage.

Fixing a Chamberlain garage door opener that jerks when opening doesn’t have to be a difficult process. With a few simple steps, you can easily diagnose and fix the problem. Start by checking the tracks, garage door spring, motor, circuit board, limit switches, drive chain, remote, wiring, and sensors. If you can’t find the source of the jerking, then contact a professional for help. With the right help, you can get your Chamberlain garage door opener back in working order in no time!
