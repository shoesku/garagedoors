Painting Your Garage Door Jamb? Here's What You Should Know

Painting your garage door jamb is an easy way to update the look of your home. A fresh coat of paint can make your home look more inviting and even increase its value. But before you get started, there are a few things you should know. Read on to learn more about painting your garage door jamb.

Choosing the Right Paint

When it comes to painting your garage door jamb, you want to make sure you choose the right paint. You'll want to pick a paint that is designed to withstand the elements, such as rain, snow, and extreme temperatures. A high-quality exterior paint will hold up better and last longer than a lower-quality one. Additionally, you'll want to make sure you choose a paint that is specifically designed for metal surfaces. If you're unsure which paint to choose, consult a professional painter for advice.

Prepping the Surface

Before you start painting, you'll need to prep the surface. Begin by cleaning the door jamb with a degreaser, dish soap, and water. This will help remove any dirt, dust, and grime that may be present. Once the surface is clean, use a wire brush to remove any rust or flaking paint. Once the surface is clean and rust-free, you'll be ready to start painting.

Priming the Door Jamb

Once the surface is prepped, you'll need to apply a primer to the door jamb. Primer helps the paint adhere better and provides an extra layer of protection against the elements. You'll want to use a primer designed specifically for metal surfaces. Allow the primer to dry completely before moving on to the next step.

Applying the Paint

Once the primer is dry, you'll be ready to apply the paint. Start by using a paintbrush to apply the paint to the edges and corners of the door jamb. Once these areas are covered, use a roller to apply the paint to the flat surfaces. Make sure each coat of paint is completely dry before applying the next one. Depending on the type of paint you use, you may need to apply two to three coats for even coverage.

Finishing Touches

Once you've finished painting the door jamb, you'll want to finish it off with a few finishing touches. Start by using a damp cloth to remove any paint drips or smudges. You'll also want to use a brush or roller to smooth out any brush marks or uneven areas. Finally, use a sealant to help protect the paint and make it last longer.

DIY or Professional?

Painting your garage door jamb can be a DIY project or you can hire a professional. If you're comfortable with painting and have the right supplies, it can be a relatively easy job. But if you're not confident in your painting skills, it's best to hire a professional. They'll be able to do a better job and the results will be worth the extra cost.

Caring for Your Door Jamb

Once you've finished painting your door jamb, you'll want to make sure you take care of it. Start by checking the paint periodically for any signs of wear or damage. You'll also want to make sure you clean the door jamb regularly to help prevent dirt and grime from building up. Finally, if you notice any chips or peeling paint, you'll want to touch up the area as soon as possible to help prevent further damage.

Conclusion

Painting your garage door jamb is an easy way to update the look of your home. But before you get started, make sure you choose the right paint, prep the surface, and apply the paint correctly. You can do the job yourself or hire a professional for a better result. And remember to take care of the door jamb once it's painted to help keep it looking its best.
