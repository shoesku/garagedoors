What Causes A Garage Door Not To Close All The Way

Best Garage doors and references for house or residential with custom material and more design. We Share more picture design with HD quality images.

Introduction

Are you frustrated with a garage door that won't close all the way? You're not alone. It's a common problem with garage doors that can be caused by a variety of causes. Whether it's a broken torsion spring, a misaligned track, or a worn out opener, there are a few things you can do to get your garage door closing properly again. In this article, we'll look at some of the most common causes of garage door problems and how to fix them.

Broken Torsion Spring

One of the most common causes of garage door problems is a broken torsion spring. The torsion spring is what allows the garage door to open and close smoothly, and if it's broken, the door won't close all the way. If your garage door is stuck in the open position, it's likely that the torsion spring needs to be replaced. Replacing the torsion spring is a job best left to a professional, so it's best to call a garage door repair service to have it fixed.

Misaligned Tracks

If your garage door is still closing, but it's not closing all the way, it could be due to misaligned tracks. The tracks guide the garage door as it opens and closes, and if they are out of alignment, the door won't be able to close properly. To check the alignment of the tracks, you'll need to open the door and look at the tracks from the inside. The tracks should be aligned perfectly with each other, and if they are not, you'll need to adjust them.

Worn Out Opener

Another common cause of garage door problems is a worn out opener. The opener is responsible for powering the door and telling it when to open and close. If the opener is worn out, the door won't be able to close all the way. If you think your opener might be the problem, you can try replacing it. There are many different types of openers, so make sure to choose one that is compatible with your garage door.

Obstructions

Another possible cause of a garage door not closing all the way is an obstruction. If there is something blocking the door from closing, such as a toolbox or a toy, it won't be able to close properly. Check the area around the door for any obstructions, and if you find any, remove them before attempting to close the door again.

Faulty Sensors

The last possible cause of a garage door not closing all the way is faulty sensors. The sensors are responsible for detecting when something is in the way of the door, and if they are not working properly, the door won't close all the way. If you think your sensors might be the problem, you can try cleaning them with a damp cloth. If that doesn't work, you may need to replace them.

Conclusion

Garage doors that won't close all the way can be a major hassle. Luckily, there are a few things you can do to try and fix the problem. The most common causes of garage door problems are broken torsion springs, misaligned tracks, worn out openers, obstructions, and faulty sensors. If you think one of these is causing your garage door to not close all the way, try fixing it before calling a professional.
