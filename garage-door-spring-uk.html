The Basics Of Garage Door Spring In The Uk

The UK has a wide variety of garage door springs designed to serve different purposes. You can find several different types of garage door springs available in the market, including torsion springs, extension springs, and tension springs. It is important to understand the differences between these types of springs before you choose one for your garage door.

Torsion Springs

Torsion springs are the most common type of garage door springs in the UK. They are used to counterbalance the weight of the garage door, enabling it to open and close with ease. Torsion springs are usually mounted on the garage door frame and are secured with a metal bracket. They are usually made of steel and can be used for both single and double garage doors.

Torsion springs are usually rated for a certain number of cycles. This means that they will be able to open and close the door a certain number of times before they wear out or break. Torsion springs are usually rated for 10,000 to 25,000 cycles, depending on the size and type of garage door.

Extension Springs

Extension springs are also commonly used for garage doors in the UK. These springs are mounted on the sides of the garage door and are used to counterbalance the weight of the door. They are usually made of steel and are available in different sizes and lengths. Extension springs are usually rated for up to 10,000 cycles before they wear out or break.

The advantage of using extension springs is that they are usually less expensive than torsion springs. However, they are not as durable as torsion springs and may require replacement sooner.

Tension Springs

Tension springs are also used for garage doors in the UK. These springs are usually mounted on the sides of the garage door and are used to counterbalance the weight of the door. They are usually made of steel and are available in different sizes and lengths.

The advantage of using tension springs is that they are usually more durable than extension springs and require less maintenance. However, they are more expensive than extension springs and may require replacement sooner.

Selecting the Right Spring for Your Garage Door

When selecting a garage door spring for your UK garage door, it is important to consider the size of the garage door and the weight of the door. The spring must be able to counterbalance the weight of the door and provide enough force to open and close the door with ease.

It is also important to consider the type of material the spring is made of. Steel springs are usually the most durable and require less maintenance, but they may require replacement sooner than other types of springs.

Finally, it is important to select a spring that is rated for the number of cycles you need. Torsion springs are usually rated for 10,000 to 25,000 cycles, while extension and tension springs are usually rated for up to 10,000 cycles.

Torsion springs are the most common type of springs used for garage doors in the UK. They are usually rated for 10,000 to 25,000 cycles, depending on the size and type of garage door. Extension and tension springs are also commonly used and are usually rated for up to 10,000 cycles.

When selecting a garage door spring, it is important to consider the size of the garage door, the weight of the door, the type of material the spring is made of, and the number of cycles the spring is rated for. This will help you choose the right spring for your garage door.
