Fixing A Garage Door Arm That Came Off

Understanding the Basics

The garage door arm is a crucial component of any garage door. It’s the part that connects the door to the opener, allowing it to open and close. If it comes off, the door won’t be able to move, so it’s important to fix it as soon as possible. To do so, it’s important to understand the basics of how the arm works and what you’ll need to do to get it back in place. In this article, we’ll discuss how to fix a garage door arm that came off.

What You’ll Need

Before you get started, you’ll need a few tools and supplies. You’ll need a screwdriver, a wrench, a pair of pliers, and some lubricant. You may also need a replacement arm if the one that came off is damaged beyond repair. You’ll also need a ladder if you’re not tall enough to reach the arm. Once you have everything you need, you’re ready to get started.

Step One: Locate the Arm

The first step is to locate the arm. It’s usually located near the top of the garage door, just below the opener. If you can’t find it, check the manual for your garage door opener to see where it’s located. Once you’ve located the arm, you’re ready to move on to the next step.

Step Two: Disconnect the Arm

Once you’ve located the arm, the next step is to disconnect it. You’ll need to use your wrench or pliers to loosen the bolts that are holding the arm in place. Once the bolts are loose, you can carefully remove the arm. Be sure to keep track of the bolts and any other parts that come off with the arm.

Step Three: Clean and Lubricate

Once the arm has been removed, it’s time to clean and lubricate it. Use a rag and a little bit of lubricant to clean off any dirt or debris. Then, apply some lubricant to the moving parts of the arm. This will help to ensure that it moves smoothly and doesn’t get stuck.

Step Four: Reattach the Arm

Once the arm is clean and lubricated, it’s time to reattach it. Use your screwdriver to tighten the bolts that were holding the arm in place. Be sure to tighten them securely, as they’ll be under a lot of strain when the door is in use. Once the bolts are tight, you can move on to the next step.

Step Five: Test the Door

Once the arm has been reattached, it’s time to test the door. Open and close the door several times to make sure that it’s operating correctly. If it’s not, then you’ll need to go back and check the bolts and make sure they’re tight. If the door is still not operating correctly, then you may need to replace the arm.

Conclusion

Fixing a garage door arm that came off is a relatively easy task, but it’s important to do it correctly. Be sure to follow these steps and use the right tools and supplies to ensure that the job is done right. If you’re still having trouble, then you should contact a professional garage door repair company to help you get your door back in working order.
