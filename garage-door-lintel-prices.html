Garage Door Lintel Prices - What You Need To Know In 2023

Garage door lintels are an essential part of the structure of your garage. They provide support for the garage door and can be a major factor in determining the overall strength and stability of the entire garage. As such, it is important to choose the right lintel for your garage. But the most important factor when choosing a lintel is the cost. Here is what you need to know about garage door lintel prices in 2023.

Factors Affecting Garage Door Lintel Prices

The cost of garage door lintels is affected by a number of factors, including the size, material, and installation of the lintel. When considering the size, it is important to note that larger lintels will require more material and labor to install, thus resulting in higher costs. The material used to construct the lintel is also a major factor in determining the cost. Steel lintels are the most common, but they can also be made from aluminum, wood, and other materials. Finally, the cost of installation is also a major factor, as it can vary greatly depending on the complexity of the job.

Average Prices for Steel Garage Door Lintels

Steel is the most common material used for garage door lintels, and it is also the most affordable. The average cost for a steel lintel is typically around $50 to $100. This cost can vary based on the size of the lintel and the complexity of the installation. It is important to note that steel lintels may require additional reinforcement, such as steel reinforcement bars, which can add to the overall cost.

Average Prices for Aluminum Garage Door Lintels

Aluminum is a more expensive option than steel, but it is also more durable and can provide a more attractive look. The average cost for an aluminum lintel is typically around $100 to $200. Again, this cost can vary based on the size and complexity of the installation. Additionally, aluminum lintels may require additional reinforcement, such as aluminum reinforcement bars, which can add to the overall cost.

Average Prices for Wood Garage Door Lintels

Wood is the least common material used for garage door lintels, but it can provide a unique and attractive look. The average cost for a wood lintel is typically around $150 to $300. This cost can vary based on the size and complexity of the installation. Additionally, wood lintels may require additional reinforcement, such as wood reinforcement bars, which can add to the overall cost.

Average Prices for Installation

In addition to the cost of the lintel itself, it is important to factor in the cost of installation. The average cost of installation for a garage door lintel is typically around $100 to $200. This cost can vary based on the complexity of the job and the materials used. It is important to note that the cost of installation can be significantly higher for more complex jobs.

Where to Find the Best Prices

The best way to find the best prices for garage door lintels is to shop around. There are a number of retailers that sell garage door lintels, both online and in-store. It is important to compare prices from multiple retailers to find the best deal. Additionally, it is important to factor in the cost of installation when comparing prices, as this can add significantly to the overall cost.

Conclusion

Garage door lintels are an essential part of the structure of your garage. They provide support for the garage door and can be a major factor in determining the overall strength and stability of the entire garage. As such, it is important to choose the right lintel for your garage. When considering the cost, it is important to factor in the size, material, and installation of the lintel. The average cost for a steel lintel is typically around $50 to $100, while aluminum and wood lintels are more expensive. The cost of installation can also vary greatly depending on the complexity of the job. The best way to find the best prices for garage door lintels is to shop around and compare prices from multiple retailers. With the right knowledge, you can find the best deals on garage door lintels in 2023.
