The Step-By-Step Guide To Replacing Your Garage Door Bottom Seal

Whether you want to keep dirt and debris out of your garage, or you’re trying to improve the energy efficiency of your home, replacing the bottom seal on your garage door is a relatively easy DIY task. The bottom seal is often overlooked, but a properly sealed door can help keep your garage clean and comfortable, and reduce your energy bills.

What You'll Need

Before you begin the project, you will need to gather the materials and tools necessary to complete the job. Most importantly, you will need a replacement bottom seal for your garage door. Consult your owner’s manual, or the manufacturer’s website for the correct size and type of seal for your door.

You will also need a few basic tools, such as a screwdriver, a drill, a utility knife, and a level. A few other helpful items include gloves, safety glasses, a tape measure, and a ladder. Once you’ve gathered your materials, you’re ready to begin.

Step 1: Prepare the Area

The first step is to prepare the area around your garage door. If you have a tiled floor, you should cover it with a drop cloth or plastic to protect it from any messes you might make. It’s also a good idea to clear any furniture or other items away from the door, so that you have plenty of room to work.

Step 2: Remove the Old Seal

Once the area is prepared, you can begin to remove the old seal. Start by unscrewing the screws that hold the seal in place. Once the screws are removed, you can pull the seal away from the door. Use a utility knife to cut away any remaining adhesive that may be stuck to the door.

Step 3: Measure and Cut the New Seal

Once you’ve removed the old seal, you can measure and cut the new seal. Measure the length of the door, from the top to the bottom. Cut the new seal to the correct size, using a utility knife. Make sure you leave enough room for the seal to overlap the door slightly.

Step 4: Install the New Seal

Once you’ve cut the new seal to the correct size, you can begin to install it. Start by cleaning the door frame with a cloth or a vacuum. This will ensure that the seal sticks properly. Next, use a level to make sure the seal is straight before attaching it to the door.

Use a drill to attach the seal to the door. Make sure the screws are secure, but not too tight. Finally, use a utility knife to trim off any excess seal.

Step 5: Test the Seal

Once the seal is installed, it’s important to test it to make sure it’s working properly. Open and close the door a few times to make sure the seal isn’t sticking or binding. If the seal is working properly, you’re finished.

Conclusion

Replacing the bottom seal on your garage door is a relatively easy DIY task that can help keep dirt and debris out of your garage, and reduce your energy bills. With a few basic tools and materials, you can easily complete the job in just a few hours. Once the job is done, you can enjoy the benefits of a properly sealed garage door for years to come.
