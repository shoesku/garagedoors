Troubleshooting When A Garage Door Light Flashes And Won't Close

Best Garage doors and references for house or residential with custom material and more design. We Share more picture design with HD quality images.

If you notice the light on your garage door flashing, it’s a sign of a malfunction. Luckily, this problem is typically easy to diagnose and fix. In this article, we’ll show you how to troubleshoot your garage door when the light is flashing and the door won’t close. Read on to learn more.

Check the Garage Door Sensors

The first thing you should check when you notice a flashing garage door light is the sensors. The sensors are located on either side of the bottom of the door and are responsible for sensing when something is in the way while the door is closing, stopping the door from closing and causing the light to flash. To check the sensors, you should:

  • Remove any debris that is blocking the sensors.
  • Check the wires that connect the sensors to the opener.
  • Check that the sensors are pointing in the same direction and that they are properly aligned.
  • Make sure the LED lights on the sensors are on.
  • Make sure the sensors are plugged in.

If the sensors are not the issue, the next step is to check the garage door opener.

Check the Garage Door Opener

If the sensors are functioning correctly, the next step is to check the garage door opener itself. To do this, you should:

  • Check that the power cord is plugged into an outlet.
  • Check the circuit breaker to make sure it has not been tripped.
  • Make sure the opener is properly connected to the wall control.
  • Check the wall control to make sure it is properly connected.
  • Check the wall control to make sure the batteries are still good.

If the opener is functioning properly, but the light is still flashing and the door won't close, the issue is likely with the door itself.

Check the Garage Door Tracks

If the opener is working properly, the next step is to check the garage door tracks. This is the most common cause of a flashing light and a door that won't close. To check the tracks, you should:

  • Check that the tracks are properly aligned.
  • Check that the tracks are not bent or damaged in any way.
  • Check that the tracks are securely attached to the wall.
  • Check that the tracks are clear of any debris.
  • Check that the hardware that holds the tracks in place is tight and secure.

If the tracks are not the issue, the next step is to check the springs.

Check the Springs

The next step is to check the springs. The springs are responsible for counterbalancing the weight of the door, allowing it to open and close with ease. To check the springs, you should:

  • Check that the springs are properly aligned.
  • Check that the springs are not worn or damaged.
  • Check that the springs are securely attached to the door.
  • Check that the springs are clear of any debris.
  • Check that the hardware that holds the springs in place is tight and secure.

If the springs are not the issue, the next step is to check the cables.

Check the Cables

The cables are responsible for providing the tension needed to lift and lower the door. To check the cables, you should:

  • Check that the cables are properly aligned.
  • Check that the cables are not worn or damaged.
  • Check that the cables are securely attached to the door.
  • Check that the cables are clear of any debris.
  • Check that the hardware that holds the cables in place is tight and secure.

If the cables are not the issue, the next step is to check the rollers.

Check the Rollers

The rollers are responsible for providing the smooth, even motion of the door as it opens and closes. To check the rollers, you should:

  • Check that the rollers are properly aligned.
  • Check that the rollers are not worn or damaged.
  • Check that the rollers are securely attached to the door.
  • Check that the rollers are free of any debris.
  • Check that the hardware that holds the rollers in place is tight and secure.

If the rollers are not the issue, the next step is to check the hinges.

Check the Hinges

The hinges are responsible for providing the smooth, even motion of the door as it opens and closes. To check the hinges, you should:

  • Check that the hinges are properly aligned.
  • Check that the hinges are not worn or damaged.
  • Check that the hinges are securely attached to the door.
  • Check that the hinges are free of any debris.
  • Check that the hardware that holds the hinges in place is tight and secure.

If the hinges are not the issue, the last step is to check the balance of the door.

Check the Balance of the Door

The balance of the door is important for it to open and close properly. To check the balance of the door, you should:

  • Check that the door is properly balanced.
  • Check that the door is not too heavy.
  • Check that the door is not binding on the tracks.
  • Check that the door is not too far off the tracks.
  • Check that the door is not too tight against the weatherstripping.

If the balance of the door is not the issue, the last step is to check the tracks one last time.

Check the Tracks One Last Time

The tracks are the most important part of the system, as they guide the door along its path. To check the tracks one last time, you should:

  • Check that the tracks are properly aligned.
  • Check that the tracks are not bent or damaged in any way.
  • Check that the tracks are securely attached to the wall.
  • Check that the tracks are clear of any debris.
  • Check that the hardware that holds the tracks in place is tight and secure.

If all of these checks have proven to be successful, then it may be time to call a professional garage door repair technician to come and take a look.
