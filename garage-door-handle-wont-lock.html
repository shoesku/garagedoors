What To Do When Your Garage Door Handle Won't Lock

In today's world, it's more important than ever to make sure that your garage door is secure and functioning properly. Unfortunately, it's not uncommon for the handle of a garage door to become loose and difficult to lock. If you're having trouble getting your garage door handle to lock, there are a few things you can do to help get it working properly again.

Check the Latch

The first thing you should do is check the latch itself. If the latch is loose or has become misaligned, it can be difficult to lock the door. You can usually adjust the latch by loosening the screws and then repositioning it. Make sure to tighten the screws securely when you're done.

Check the Strike Plate

The strike plate is the part of the door that the latch engages with when you close the door. If the strike plate is loose or misaligned, it can make it difficult to get the door to lock. You can usually adjust the strike plate by loosening the screws and then repositioning it. Make sure to tighten the screws securely when you're done.

Check the Lock and Handle

If the latch and strike plate are in good working order, you should then check the lock and handle. If either of these components are broken or malfunctioning, they may need to be replaced. You can usually find replacement parts online or at your local home improvement store.

Replace the Lock and Handle

If the lock and handle need to be replaced, you'll want to make sure you buy the right parts for your door. Measure the size and type of lock and handle you need before you buy so you don't end up with the wrong size or type. Once you have the right parts, you'll need to remove the old lock and handle and then install the new ones. This is usually a straightforward process, but it's always best to consult with a professional locksmith if you're unsure.

Lubricate the Lock and Handle

If the latch, strike plate, and lock and handle are all in good working order but the door still won't lock, you may need to lubricate the lock and handle. You can usually buy a lubricant specifically designed for door locks at your local home improvement store. Apply the lubricant to the lock and handle, then use a cloth to wipe away any excess. This should help get the door locking properly again.

Replace the Entire Lock Assembly

If none of the above steps have solved the problem, you may need to replace the entire lock assembly. This is a more complicated job, and you should always consult with a professional locksmith before attempting it. It's also important to make sure you buy the right parts for your door so you don't end up with the wrong size or type.

Conclusion

If your garage door handle won't lock, there are a few steps you can take to try and get it working properly again. If none of these steps work, you may need to replace the entire lock assembly. It's always best to consult with a professional locksmith before attempting any repairs or replacements. With the right knowledge and tools, you should be able to get your garage door handle functioning properly again.
