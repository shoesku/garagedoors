Why You Should Grease Your Garage Door Rails

Greasing your garage door is an important part of maintaining it. The rails that the door runs on are what allows it to open and close smoothly and quietly. Without proper lubrication, the rails can become dry, causing the door to make squeaking noises, stick, and eventually, not open or close at all. This is why it's important to keep your rails lubricated with grease.

When Should You Grease Your Garage Door Rails?

Ideally, you should grease your garage door rails every three months. This will ensure that the rails are properly lubricated and functioning properly. If it has been longer than three months since you last greased your garage door rails, you should do so as soon as possible.

What Type of Grease Should You Use?

The best kind of grease to use on your garage door rails is a lubricant designed specifically for garage doors. This type of grease is designed to last longer and provide better lubrication than regular grease. You can find this type of grease at most hardware stores. It is important to note, however, that you should not use motor oil, WD-40, or any other type of lubricant that is not specifically designed for garage door rails.

How to Grease Your Garage Door Rails

The first step in greasing your garage door rails is to open the door fully. This will make it easier to access the rail. Once the door is open, you should clean off any dirt or debris that is on the rails. This will ensure that the grease is applied to a clean surface.

Once the rails are clean, you can apply the grease. You should apply the grease along the full length of the rail. Use enough grease so that it forms a light film over the rail. Once the grease is applied, you should move the door up and down a few times to ensure that the grease is evenly distributed. Once the door is moving freely, you can close it.

What If My Garage Door Rails Are Still Squeaking?

If your garage door rails are still squeaking after you have greased them, there may be another problem. The most likely culprit is worn-out rollers or hinges. If this is the case, you should contact a professional to replace the parts.

Conclusion

Greasing your garage door rails is an important part of maintaining your garage door. Greasing the rails every three months will ensure that your door is functioning properly and without any squeaking noises. It is important to use the right type of grease and to make sure that the rails are clean before applying the grease. If your garage door rails are still squeaking after greasing them, you may need to replace the rollers or hinges. Taking care of your garage door rails will ensure that your door is functioning properly for years to come.
