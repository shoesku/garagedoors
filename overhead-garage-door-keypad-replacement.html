Replacing Your Overhead Garage Door Keypad In 2023: A Step-By-Step Guide

Best Garage doors and references for house or residential with custom material and more design. We Share more picture design with HD quality images.

Having an overhead garage door keypad is an essential part of any homeowner’s security system. Not only do they provide an extra layer of security when entering or leaving your home, but they also allow you to access your garage without needing to carry a key or use a remote. However, as with any technology, keypads can eventually wear out and need to be replaced.

If your overhead garage door keypad has seen better days, here is a handy guide to help you replace it with ease. Before you begin, make sure you have the correct keypad and power source for your garage door.

Step 1: Remove the Old Keypad

The first thing you need to do is remove the old keypad. If your keypad is mounted to the wall, first use a screwdriver to unscrew the screws securing it. Then, carefully pull the keypad away from the wall. If your keypad is not wall-mounted, remove any screws that are securing it to the garage door.

Once the screws are removed, carefully pull the keypad away from the garage door. You may need to use a pair of needle-nose pliers to gently remove the wires from the keypad. Make sure to note the order of the wires before removing them, as you will need to reattach them in the same order.

Step 2: Install the New Keypad

Now it’s time to install the new keypad. Start by connecting the wires to the new keypad in the same order that they were connected to the old keypad. Once all the wires are connected, insert the keypad into the wall or garage door, depending on the type of installation. Then, use a screwdriver to screw the keypad into place.

If your keypad requires a power source, connect the power supply to the keypad. Most overhead garage door keypads require a 9-volt battery, so make sure to have one on hand.

Step 3: Program the Keypad

Once the keypad is installed, it’s time to program it. This process varies from model to model, so consult the instruction manual for your specific keypad. Generally, you will need to enter a code that is either provided with the keypad or that you create yourself.

Once you have programmed the keypad, test it out to make sure it is working properly. Then, test it again with a few different codes, just to make sure that it is working properly.

Step 4: Secure the Keypad

Finally, it’s important to make sure that your keypad is secure. If your keypad is wall-mounted, make sure that the screws are secure and that the keypad is not loose. If it is not wall-mounted, make sure that the keypad is securely attached to the garage door.

It’s also important to make sure that the power supply for the keypad is secure. If your keypad requires a 9-volt battery, make sure that it is firmly inserted into the keypad. If your keypad requires a different type of power supply, make sure that the power supply is securely connected to the keypad.

Conclusion

Replacing your overhead garage door keypad can be a daunting task, but it doesn’t have to be. With the right tools and a bit of patience, you can easily replace your keypad and get back to enjoying the security it provides. Just remember to follow the steps outlined in this guide and take your time, and you’ll have your keypad replaced in no time.
