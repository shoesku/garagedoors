Types of Garage Door Hinges

When it comes to garage door hinges, you have several options to choose from. Depending on your door’s style and material, some types of hinges may be more suitable than others. Take a look at the following types of hinges to find the one that works best for you.

Strap Hinges

Strap hinges are one of the most common types of hinges used for garage doors. They’re made from steel, and feature a long, flat plate with two screws at either end. Strap hinges are strong and durable, making them a great choice for high-traffic doors. They’re also relatively easy to install, so you won’t need to hire a professional to do the job.

Butt Hinges

Butt hinges are an old-fashioned type of hinge that is still popular for garage doors. They’re made from steel and feature two plates that are connected by a pin. They’re easy to install and provide a great aesthetic to your door. However, they’re not as strong as strap hinges, so they’re best used on lighter doors.

Barrel Hinges

Barrel hinges are a popular choice for heavier garage doors. They’re made from steel and feature two plates that are connected by a barrel-shaped pin. They’re strong and durable, and can handle heavier doors with ease. Plus, they’re easy to install and provide a classic look to your door.

Flush Hinges

Flush hinges are a great choice for lighter garage doors. They’re made from steel and feature two plates that are connected by a pin. They’re easy to install and provide a great aesthetic to your door. Plus, they’re relatively inexpensive, so they won’t break the bank.

Pivot Hinges

Pivot hinges are a great choice for heavier doors. They’re made from steel and feature two plates that are connected by a pin. They’re strong and durable, and can handle heavier doors with ease. Plus, they’re easy to install and provide a great look to your door.
