How Much Does It Cost To Fit A New Garage Door?

Best Garage doors and references for house or residential with custom material and more design. We Share more picture design with HD quality images.

Introduction

If you’re considering replacing your old garage door, you may be wondering how much it’s going to cost to get a new one fitted. A new garage door can be a great way to give your home a facelift and add some extra security, but it’s important to consider the cost before making any decisions. In this article, we’ll look at the average cost of fitting a new garage door, as well as some of the factors that can affect the price.

Average Cost of Fitting a New Garage Door

The cost of fitting a new garage door can vary depending on a number of factors. The type of garage door you choose, the size of the door, and the labour costs can all have an effect on the overall cost. Generally speaking, you can expect to pay anywhere from £400 to £1500 for a new garage door, depending on the factors listed above.

Types of Garage Doors

The type of garage door you choose will have a big effect on the price. There are several different types of garage doors available on the market, from traditional up and over doors to sectional roller doors. The more secure and durable types of garage doors, such as roller doors, will typically cost more than the more basic up and over doors.

Size of the Garage Door

The size of the garage door is another factor that will have an effect on the price. Larger garage doors will cost more than smaller doors, as they require more materials and labour to fit. If you’re replacing an old door, the size of the new door should be the same as the old one.

Labour Costs

The labour costs associated with fitting a new garage door can vary depending on the complexity of the job. A simple up and over door may only take an hour or two to fit, whereas a more complex roller door may take several hours. Labour costs can range from £50 to £200, depending on the type of door and the complexity of the job.

Additional Costs

In addition to the cost of the door and the labour costs, there may be additional costs involved. You may need to hire a skip to dispose of the old door, as well as buy any additional materials that may be required, such as screws and hinges. You may also need to pay for electricity if the door is to be fitted with an electric opener.

Conclusion

As you can see, the cost of fitting a new garage door can vary depending on a number of factors. The type of door, the size, and the labour costs can all have an effect on the overall cost. Generally speaking, you can expect to pay anywhere from £400 to £1500 for a new garage door, depending on the factors listed above.
