Types Of Garage Door Extension Springs

Garage door extension springs are an essential part of the operation of your garage door. These springs are placed along the sides of the garage door and help to balance the weight of the door and provide the power needed to lift and lower it. These springs are typically made of a high-quality steel, which makes them durable and reliable. There are several types of garage door extension springs available, and each type has unique features that make it best suited for different applications. Let's take a look at the different types of garage door extension springs and how they can help you.

Single-Torsion Springs

The most common type of garage door extension springs is the single-torsion spring. This type of spring is made up of a single coiled steel spring that is securely attached at one end to the door's track. Single-torsion springs are designed to provide the door with the power it needs to lift and lower it. They are also designed to be very durable and long-lasting. If you have a single-torsion spring, it is important to check it regularly for signs of wear and tear, and replace it if necessary.

Double-Torsion Springs

Double-torsion springs are a more advanced type of garage door extension springs. These springs are made up of two coiled steel springs that are attached at both ends of the door's track. The double-torsion springs are designed to provide the door with the power it needs to lift and lower it. They are also designed to be very durable and long-lasting. If you have a double-torsion spring, it is important to check it regularly for signs of wear and tear, and replace it if necessary.

Oil-Tempered Springs

Oil-tempered springs are a type of garage door extension springs that are made up of a coiled steel spring that is oil-tempered to make it extremely durable and long-lasting. These springs are designed to provide the door with the power it needs to lift and lower it. They are also designed to be very durable and long-lasting. If you have an oil-tempered spring, it is important to check it regularly for signs of wear and tear, and replace it if necessary.

Galvanized Springs

Galvanized springs are a type of garage door extension springs that are made up of a coiled steel spring that is galvanized to make it corrosion-resistant. These springs are designed to provide the door with the power it needs to lift and lower it. They are also designed to be very durable and long-lasting. If you have a galvanized spring, it is important to check it regularly for signs of wear and tear, and replace it if necessary.

Polyurethane Springs

Polyurethane springs are a type of garage door extension springs that are made up of a coiled steel spring that is coated with a polyurethane material. These springs are designed to provide the door with the power it needs to lift and lower it. They are also designed to be very durable and long-lasting. If you have a polyurethane spring, it is important to check it regularly for signs of wear and tear, and replace it if necessary.

Extension Cable Springs

Extension cable springs are a type of garage door extension springs that are made up of a coiled steel spring that is attached to a cable. The cable is then attached to the door's track. These springs are designed to provide the door with the power it needs to lift and lower it. They are also designed to be very durable and long-lasting. If you have an extension cable spring, it is important to check it regularly for signs of wear and tear, and replace it if necessary.

Safety Cables

Safety cables are a type of garage door extension springs that are designed to provide the door with an additional safety feature. These cables are attached to the spring and run along the sides of the door's track. They are designed to prevent the spring from snapping if it becomes overstretched or breaks. Safety cables are a great way to ensure the safety of your family and property.

Conclusion

Garage door extension springs are an essential part of the operation of your garage door. There are several different types of garage door extension springs available, each of which can provide the door with the power it needs to lift and lower it. It is important to check your garage door extension springs regularly for signs of wear and tear, and replace them if necessary. With the right type of garage door extension springs, you can ensure the safety of your family and property.
