Why Is Garage Door Maintenance So Important?

Garage doors are complex pieces of machinery, and they require regular maintenance to keep them running smoothly and safely. Regular maintenance can help to prevent expensive repairs down the line and allow you to spot any potential issues before they become serious. A garage door maintenance checklist can help you keep your garage door in tip-top shape.

Garage Door Maintenance Checklist

As part of your regular maintenance routine, you should inspect your garage door for signs of wear and tear. The following checklist can help you ensure your door is properly serviced and maintained.

1. Check the Tracks

Make sure that the tracks are clean and free of debris. Check for any signs of rust or corrosion. If the tracks are bent or damaged, you may need to have them replaced. Additionally, make sure that the tracks are properly aligned and that the screws are securely tightened.

2. Inspect the Rollers and Springs

Check the rollers and springs for signs of wear and tear. Make sure that the rollers are properly lubricated. If they are worn or corroded, they should be replaced. Additionally, check the springs for signs of wear. If the springs are worn, they should be replaced as soon as possible.

3. Check the Cables

Inspect the cables for signs of fraying or wear. If the cables are frayed or damaged, they should be replaced. Additionally, make sure that the cables are properly secured to the door.

4. Test the Auto-Reverse Feature

Check that the auto-reverse feature is working properly. This feature should stop the door from closing if it detects an obstruction in its path. If the feature is not working properly, you may need to have it repaired or replaced.

5. Inspect the Weatherstripping

Check the weatherstripping for signs of wear and tear. If the weatherstripping is worn, it should be replaced. Additionally, make sure that the weatherstripping is properly secured to the door.

6. Replace the Batteries in the Garage Door Opener

Replace the batteries in the garage door opener every year or two. This will help to ensure that the opener is functioning properly. Additionally, this will help to prevent the opener from failing due to a dead battery.

7. Test the Balance of the Door

Make sure that the door is properly balanced. If the door is not balanced, it may not open or close properly. Additionally, it could put an unnecessary strain on the opener.

8. Clean and Lubricate the Moving Parts

Clean and lubricate the moving parts of the door, such as the rollers, hinges, and springs. This will help to ensure that the door is operating smoothly and efficiently. Additionally, it will help to reduce wear and tear on the door.

9. Test the Safety Features

Make sure that the safety features of the door are working properly. This includes the auto-reverse feature, emergency release, and the safety sensors. Additionally, check the emergency release to make sure it is in good working order.

10. Inspect the Door for Damage

Finally, inspect the door for signs of damage. Check for dents, cracks, or other signs of wear and tear. If you find any damage, it is important to have it repaired as soon as possible.

Conclusion

Following the above garage door maintenance checklist can help you keep your garage door in excellent condition and prevent expensive repairs in the future. Additionally, it will help to keep your family safe by ensuring that the door is functioning properly. If you need help with any of the items on the checklist, contact a professional garage door repair service for assistance.
