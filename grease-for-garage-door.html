Why Should You Grease Your Garage Door?

It's no secret that garage doors are a huge convenience, allowing us to quickly and easily access our homes, garages, and other buildings. However, not many people think about the importance of greasing their garage doors. Greasing the door can ensure that it runs smoothly and efficiently, and that it lasts for many years. It can also prevent major repairs and even lower your energy bill. But what type of grease should you use? That's the question we'll be answering in this article.

Types of Grease

There are a few different types of grease that can be used on garage doors. The first type is mineral-based grease. This is the most common type and it's usually used on older garage doors. This type of grease is fairly affordable and easy to find. The downside is that it doesn't last as long as other types of grease, so you'll need to reapply it more often. The second type of grease is synthetic-based grease. This type of grease is more expensive but it lasts longer and is better for newer garage doors. It also provides a better seal around the door and helps keep it from rusting.

Which Grease is Best?

The best type of grease for your garage door really depends on the age and condition of the door. For older doors, you'll want to use mineral-based grease. It's cheaper and easier to find, and it will do the job. However, for newer doors, you should opt for synthetic-based grease. It's more expensive but it will last longer, provide a better seal, and help keep the door from rusting. Ultimately, the choice is yours.

How to Apply Grease to Your Garage Door

Now that you know the type of grease you should use, let's talk about how to apply it. First, you'll want to make sure you have the right tools. You'll need a grease gun, a rag, and some grease. Start by cleaning the area around the door and then use the grease gun to apply the grease. Make sure to get into all the nooks and crannies and be sure to wipe off any excess grease. You may need to repeat this process a few times to make sure the door is properly greased.

How Often Should You Grease Your Garage Door?

Once you've applied the grease, you'll need to keep an eye on it and reapply it periodically. How often depends on the type of grease you're using and the condition of the door. For mineral-based grease, you should apply it every 3-6 months. For synthetic-based grease, you should apply it every 6-12 months. You'll also want to keep an eye on the door and look for any signs of wear and tear. If you notice any problems, you should apply more grease and have the door inspected by a professional.

Conclusion

Greasing your garage door is an important part of maintaining it. It helps ensure that the door runs smoothly and efficiently, and it can also help prevent major repairs and lower your energy bill. Different types of grease can be used, and the best type for your door depends on its age and condition. Once you know the type of grease to use, you can apply it properly and make sure to reapply it as needed. Doing this will help keep your garage door in great shape for many years to come.
