Are Garage Door Extension Springs Dangerous?

Garage doors are a common fixture in most homes and businesses these days. They provide a secure entry point, and can help keep intruders out of your property. But despite their popularity, many people are unaware that garage doors can be dangerous. This is especially true when it comes to the extension springs that are used to balance the weight of the door. Extension springs are under a lot of tension, and can snap if handled incorrectly. In this article, we’ll take a look at the risks associated with garage door extension springs, and discuss the best safety practices for their installation and maintenance.

What Are Garage Door Extension Springs?

Garage door extension springs are used to help counterbalance the weight of the door. They’re typically made of steel and are mounted on either side of the door. As the door is opened and closed, the springs stretch and contract, helping to reduce the strain on the opener motor, and making the door easier to lift. Extension springs come in a variety of sizes to accommodate different weight doors.

The Dangers of Garage Door Extension Springs

Garage door extension springs are under a lot of tension, and can be dangerous if not handled properly. If the spring is not properly installed, it can snap and cause serious injury. In addition, if the spring is not regularly maintained and inspected, it can wear out and break, which could cause serious damage to the door and any property in its path.

Preventing Injury from Extension Springs

The best way to prevent injury from a garage door extension spring is to ensure that it is properly installed and maintained. Extension springs should only be installed by a trained professional, as they require special tools and knowledge to do so safely. Most manufacturers also recommend that the springs be inspected regularly, and replaced when necessary. If the springs are more than 5 years old, it is recommended that they be replaced.

Signs of a Faulty Spring

It is important to be aware of the signs of a faulty spring, as this can help identify a problem before it becomes dangerous. If the spring is not balanced correctly, the door may be difficult to open or close. In addition, if the spring is not adjusted properly, it may make a loud noise when the door is opened or closed. If you notice any of these signs, it is important to have the spring inspected as soon as possible.

Steps to Take if a Spring Breaks

If a spring does break, it is important to take the proper safety precautions. First, turn off the power to the opener so the door cannot be opened or closed. Then, use a pair of pliers to carefully remove the broken pieces of the spring. Do not attempt to repair or replace the spring yourself, as this can be dangerous. Instead, contact a professional garage door repair service to have the spring replaced.

Conclusion

Garage door extension springs can be dangerous, but taking the proper safety precautions can help prevent injury. Be sure to have the springs installed and inspected by a professional, and regularly inspect them yourself. If you notice any signs of wear or damage, have the springs replaced. Following these steps can help you safely enjoy your garage door for years to come.
