How To Install Garage Door Extension Springs 110 Lbs

Best Garage doors and references for house or residential with custom material and more design. We Share more picture design with HD quality images.

Installing a garage door extension springs 110 lbs may sound intimidating, but it's actually a pretty simple process that can be completed in just a few hours. Extension springs are a great way to increase the life span of your garage door, as they are designed to help reduce the strain on the door's system and make opening and closing the door much easier. With the right tools and some basic knowledge, you can easily install your own extension springs and enjoy the benefits they offer.

Things You'll Need

Before you can begin your installation, you'll need to gather a few items. You'll need a ladder, a pair of vice grips, a wrench, a pair of pliers, and a drill. Additionally, you'll need a measuring tape, a pencil, and the extension springs themselves. You'll also need a friend to help you with the installation, as the springs can be quite heavy and difficult to maneuver alone. Once you have all the necessary items, you can begin your installation.

Steps to Install

Step 1: Measure the Door

The first step in installing your garage door extension springs 110 lbs is to measure the door. Start by measuring the width and height of the door. You'll need to have the measurements handy when you go to buy the springs, so be sure to write them down. Once you have the measurements, you can purchase the correct size springs for your door.

Step 2: Secure the Springs

Once you have the correct size springs, it's time to secure them in place. Begin by using the drill to make two holes in the door, one near the top and one near the bottom. The holes should be spaced evenly apart and should be large enough for the springs to fit through. Once the holes are drilled, you can insert the springs into the holes and use the vice grips to secure them in place.

Step 3: Attach the Cable

Next, you'll need to attach the cable to the springs. This is done by first looping the cable around the top of the spring and then looping it around the bottom. Once the cable is secured, use the pliers to tighten the cable so it won't slip from the spring. Then, use the wrench to tighten the cable further, and use the measuring tape to make sure the cable is evenly tightened.

Step 4: Install the Brackets

The next step is to install the brackets. This is done by using the drill to make two holes in the door frame, one on either side of the door. Once the holes have been made, use the screws to attach the brackets to the frame. Make sure the brackets are securely attached, as the entire weight of the door will be supported by them.

Step 5: Adjust the Springs

Once the brackets are installed, it's time to adjust the springs. To do this, use the wrench to loosen the tension on the springs. This will allow you to adjust the tension to the desired level. Be sure to check the tension regularly to make sure it is even on both sides of the door. Once the tension is adjusted to the right level, use the wrench to tighten the springs and secure them in place.

Step 6: Test the Door

Once you have completed the installation, it's time to test the door. Push the door up and down a few times to ensure that it is operating correctly. If the door is not operating correctly, you may need to adjust the tension of the springs again. Once you are satisfied that the door is operating correctly, you can enjoy the increased life span of your garage door.

Conclusion

Installing garage door extension springs 110 lbs is a relatively simple process that can be completed in just a few hours with the right tools and some basic knowledge. With the right set up, you can enjoy the benefits that extension springs offer and increase the life span of your garage door. So if you want to install extension springs in your garage door, now you know how.
