How To Fix A Garage Door Motor Jammed

Garage door motor jams are a common problem that can cause serious damage to your property if not addressed in a timely manner. Unfortunately, many homeowners are unaware of this issue and they often wait until it’s too late to call a professional. If you’ve noticed that your garage door motor has jammed, then it’s important to take action right away. In this article, we’ll discuss the steps you need to take in order to fix your jammed garage door motor.

What Causes a Garage Door Motor Jam?

A garage door motor jam can be caused by a variety of factors. One of the most common causes is a lack of lubrication. If your garage door isn’t properly lubricated, then it can cause the motor to jam. Additionally, if the motor is too old or worn out, then it can also jam. Other causes of a garage door motor jam include faulty wiring, a clogged chain, or an obstructed track.

Check the Motor

The first step in fixing a garage door motor jam is to check the motor itself. Make sure that it’s properly lubricated and that all of the wiring is intact. If you notice any problems, then you should replace the motor as soon as possible. You should also check the chain and track to make sure that they’re free of debris and obstruction. If they’re clogged or blocked, then you should clear them out.

Check the Connections

If the motor is working properly, then it’s time to check the connections. Make sure that the power source is connected and that the wiring is secure. If you’re still having problems, then you should check the connections between the motor and the door. Make sure that all of the screws are tight and that the hinges are aligned correctly. If you’re still having trouble, then you should replace the connecting parts.

Check the Springs

The next step is to check the springs. If the springs are damaged or worn out, then it can cause the motor to jam. Check the springs for any signs of wear and tear. If you notice any damage, then you should replace them as soon as possible. You should also check the tension of the springs. If they’re too tight, then it can cause the motor to jam.

Check the Track

The last step is to check the track. Make sure that the track is free of debris and obstruction. If the track is blocked, then it can cause the motor to jam. Additionally, make sure that the track is aligned correctly. If the track is off, then it can cause the motor to jam.

Conclusion

Garage door motor jams can be a serious issue if not addressed in a timely manner. If you’ve noticed that your garage door motor has jammed, then it’s important to take action right away. Make sure that the motor is properly lubricated and that all of the wiring is intact. Additionally, check the connections and the track for any signs of wear and tear. If you follow these steps, then you should be able to fix your jammed garage door motor.
