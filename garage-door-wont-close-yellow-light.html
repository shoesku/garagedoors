What To Do When Your Garage Door Won't Close With Yellow Light?

Garage Door Opener Signal

A garage door opener signal is a flag that is sent from the garage door opener to the garage door, indicating that the door should open, close, or stay in its current position. The signal is usually sent via a remote control or a wall switch. Depending on the type of garage door opener, the signal can usually be seen as a blinking LED light, often in yellow color. A yellow LED light indicates a warning signal that the door should not be operated. This is usually caused by an obstruction or a problem with the opener itself.

What Causes Garage Door Won't Close with Yellow Light?

When your garage door won’t close with a yellow light, it’s usually because of an obstruction or a problem with the garage door opener’s safety sensors. The safety sensors are located at the bottom of the garage door, and they are designed to prevent the door from closing if something is blocking its path. If the sensors are blocked or damaged, the yellow light will come on to indicate that the door should not be operated. Additionally, if the garage door opener is malfunctioning, it may also cause the yellow light to come on.

Troubleshooting Yellow Light on Garage Door Opener

If you have a yellow light on your garage door opener, it’s important to troubleshoot the issue as soon as possible. The first step is to check the safety sensors. Make sure that nothing is blocking the sensors, and that they are properly aligned. If the sensors are clear and properly aligned, the next step is to check the garage door opener itself. Make sure that all of the connections are secure, and that the power is on.

Checking the Garage Door Springs

If the safety sensors and garage door opener are both functioning properly, the next step is to check the garage door springs. If the springs are worn or damaged, they may not be able to lift the weight of the door, which can cause the door to stay in the open position. If the springs are worn or damaged, they should be replaced as soon as possible.

Checking the Garage Door Tracks

The next step is to check the garage door tracks for any obstructions or damage. Make sure that the tracks are free of debris, and that they are properly aligned. If the tracks are damaged, they should be repaired or replaced. Additionally, if the tracks are not properly aligned, they should be adjusted to ensure that the door is able to move freely.

Checking the Garage Door Rollers

Finally, you should check the garage door rollers for any obstructions or damage. Make sure that the rollers are free of debris, and that they are able to move freely. If the rollers are worn, they should be replaced as soon as possible. Additionally, if the rollers are not properly aligned, they should be adjusted to ensure that the door is able to move freely.

Conclusion

If your garage door won’t close with a yellow light, it’s important to troubleshoot the issue as soon as possible. Start by checking the safety sensors, and then move on to checking the garage door opener, springs, tracks, and rollers. If any of these components are damaged or not functioning properly, they should be repaired or replaced as soon as possible. Doing so will help ensure that your garage door is able to close safely and properly.
