Garage door jambs, also known as jamb liners, are designed to protect the walls of a garage from the elements and wear-and-tear. They are usually made of metal or wood, but plastic garage door jambs have recently become popular due to their durability and affordability. Installing a plastic garage door jamb can be relatively simple and straightforward, but you should still take some time to research and compare different options before making your purchase.

To begin, you should determine the size of the garage door jamb you need for your garage. Measure the width and height of the garage door and then use these measurements to find a jamb that is the correct size. The jamb should be slightly larger than the door itself to ensure that it is strong and secure. You should also consider the thickness of the jamb and make sure that it is thick enough to provide adequate protection and insulation.

When it comes to the materials used to make plastic garage door jambs, you should look for a product that is made of durable polyethylene or polypropylene. These materials are both durable and resistant to heat, moisture, and corrosion. Additionally, polyethylene and polypropylene are lightweight, making them easier to install. You should also look for a product that has UV protection to prevent fading and discoloration.

Next, you should look for a product that is designed to be resistant to scratches, dents, and other damages. Many plastic jamb liners come with a protective coating to prevent these types of damage.
