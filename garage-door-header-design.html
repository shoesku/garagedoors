Your garage door header is the main structural support for your garage door. It is the most critical element in making sure your garage door is secure and operates properly. As such, it is important to make sure you choose the right type of header for your home and garage door. In this article, we will discuss the different types of garage door headers and how to choose the right one for your home.

Types of Garage Door Headers

The type of garage door header you choose will depend on the type of garage door you have. There are two types of headers commonly used on garage doors: the flat header and the curved header.

Flat Header

The flat header is the most commonly used type of header. It is a flat steel beam that spans the width of the garage door opening. This type of header is typically used on single-panel garage doors and is the least expensive type of header.

Curved Header

The curved header is the second most common type of header. It is a curved steel beam that spans the width of the garage door opening. This type of header is typically used on double-panel garage doors and is more expensive than the flat header.

Choosing the Right Garage Door Header

When choosing the right type of header for your garage door, there are a few things to consider. First, you should consider the size of your garage door opening and the weight of the door. The heavier the door, the stronger the header needs to be.

You should also consider the type of garage door you have. If you have a single-panel garage door, you should use a flat header. If you have a double-panel garage door, you should use a curved header.

Finally, you should consider the type of material you want to use for the header. Steel is the most common material used for garage door headers, but there are also other materials available, such as aluminum and wood.

Installing Your Garage Door Header

Once you have chosen the right type of header for your garage door, it is time to install it. Installing a garage door header can be a difficult process, so it is important to follow the instructions carefully.

The first step is to measure the width of the garage door opening and the weight of the door. This will help you determine the size and strength of the header you need to use.

Next, you will need to mark the location of the header on the wall above the garage door opening. You will also need to mark the location of the header support brackets. The brackets should be placed on the wall studs and secured with screws.

Finally, you will need to attach the header to the wall and the support brackets. You should use lag screws to secure the header in place.
