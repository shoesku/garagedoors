Fixing Your Garage Door Wall Button When There's No Power

Best Garage doors and references for house or residential with custom material and more design. We Share more picture design with HD quality images.

Introduction

When you need to open your garage door, you typically rely on the wall button to get the job done. But what happens when that wall button no longer has any power? It can be a frustrating experience, and you may not know where to turn for help. Fortunately, there are several things you can do to get your wall button working again – even if it has no power.

Check the Circuit Breaker

The first thing you should do when your wall button has no power is to check the circuit breaker. If you have an older garage door opener, it may not be on its own dedicated circuit. This can cause the power to be cut off when other appliances in the home are running. If this is the case, simply reset the breaker and your wall button should be back up and running.

Check the Wall Button Wiring

If the circuit breaker is not the issue, the next step is to check the wiring behind the wall button. Garage door openers typically have two wires – one red and one white. Make sure these wires are firmly attached to the wall button, and that they are not frayed or damaged in any way. If the wires seem to be okay, move on to the next step.

Check the Garage Door Opener

The next step is to check the garage door opener itself. If the opener is not functioning properly, it can cause the wall button to lose power. If the opener is not responding to your commands, you may need to replace it. If the opener is functioning properly, you can move on to the next step.

Check the Wall Button Battery

If you have a newer model garage door opener, it may have a backup battery in the wall button. This battery can be used to power the wall button in the event of a power outage. If this is the case, simply replace the battery and your wall button should be back up and running.

Check the Wall Button Fuse

If the battery is not the issue, the next step is to check the wall button fuse. This fuse can become damaged or blown, which can cause the wall button to lose power. To check the fuse, simply remove the cover and inspect it. If the fuse is blown, simply replace it and your wall button should be back up and running.

Replace the Wall Button

If all of the above steps fail to get your wall button working again, you may need to replace it. Wall buttons are relatively inexpensive, and you can purchase them from most hardware stores. Before installing the new wall button, make sure to double-check the wiring and make sure everything is connected properly.

Conclusion

If your garage door wall button has no power, don't panic. There are several things you can do to get it back up and running. Start by checking the circuit breaker, then move on to the wall button wiring, the garage door opener, the wall button battery, and finally the wall button fuse. If all else fails, you may need to replace the wall button. With a little bit of patience and some basic troubleshooting, you can get your wall button working again in no time.
