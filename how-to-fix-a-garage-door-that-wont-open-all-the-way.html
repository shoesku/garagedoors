Common Causes of Garage Door Malfunctions

A garage door that won't open all the way can be a serious inconvenience and can make it difficult to get your car in and out of the garage. Fortunately, there are many common causes of garage door malfunctions and many of them can be fixed easily and without the help of a professional. Here are some of the most common reasons why a garage door won't open all the way.

Obstruction on the Track

One of the most common causes of a garage door not opening all the way is an obstruction on the track. Over time, dirt and debris can build up on the track, preventing the door from opening and closing properly. It's important to check the track periodically to make sure there is no debris and to clean it off if there is.

Broken or Worn Springs

Another common cause of a garage door not opening all the way is broken or worn springs. The springs are responsible for helping the door open and close and if they are worn out or broken, the door won't open properly. If you suspect this is the cause, it's important to contact a professional as replacing springs can be dangerous and should only be done by someone with experience.

Misaligned Tracks

Misaligned tracks can also cause a garage door not to open all the way. Over time, the tracks can become bent or misaligned, preventing the door from opening and closing properly. It's important to check the tracks periodically to make sure they are aligned and to adjust them if they are not.

Faulty Wiring

Faulty wiring can also cause a garage door not to open all the way. Over time, the wires can become worn or damaged, preventing the door from opening or closing properly. If you suspect this is the cause, it's important to contact a professional as repairing or replacing faulty wiring can be dangerous and should only be done by someone with experience.

Broken or Damaged Rollers

Broken or damaged rollers can also be the cause of a garage door not opening all the way. The rollers are responsible for helping the door open and close and if they are worn out or broken, the door won't open properly. If you suspect this is the cause, it's important to contact a professional as replacing rollers can be dangerous and should only be done by someone with experience.

Incorrectly Installed Openers

Incorrectly installed openers can also be the cause of a garage door not opening all the way. If the opener was not installed correctly, it can cause the door to open and close improperly, resulting in the door not opening all the way. It's important to make sure the opener is installed correctly if you suspect this is the cause.

Faulty Sensors

Faulty sensors can also cause a garage door not to open all the way. The sensors are responsible for detecting when an object is in the way and will stop the door from opening or closing if an object is detected. If the sensors are not working properly, the door may not open all the way. It's important to check the sensors periodically to make sure they are working properly and to replace them if they are not.

Incorrectly Adjusted Limit Switch

Incorrectly adjusted limit switch can also be the cause of a garage door not opening all the way. The limit switch is responsible for telling the door when to stop opening and closing and if it is not adjusted correctly, it can cause the door not to open all the way. It's important to check the limit switch periodically to make sure it is adjusted correctly and to adjust it if it is not.

Conclusion

A garage door that won't open all the way can be a serious inconvenience and can make it difficult to get your car in and out of the garage. Fortunately, there are many common causes of garage door malfunctions and many of them can be fixed easily and without the help of a professional. Obstruction on the track, broken or worn springs, misaligned tracks, faulty wiring, broken or damaged rollers, incorrectly installed openers, faulty sensors, and an incorrectly adjusted limit switch are all common causes of a garage door not opening all the way. It's important to check these components periodically to make sure they are in good working condition and to replace or adjust them if they are not.
