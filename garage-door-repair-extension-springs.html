Your garage door is an essential part of your home, providing security and convenience. Unfortunately, when something goes wrong with the garage door, such as a broken extension spring, it can be both costly and inconvenient. It's important to have a basic understanding of the different types of garage door repair extension springs and how to conduct repairs yourself.

How Do Garage Door Extension Springs Work?

The basic function of a garage door extension spring is to counterbalance the weight of the door. This allows the door to open and close with minimal effort. Extension springs are typically found on one-piece or sectional garage doors, which have panels that roll up or down when the door is opened or closed. Extension springs are positioned on either side of the door, stretching along the length of the track.

The springs are connected to the cable drums on either side of the door, which are attached to a torsion bar. As the door opens, the extension springs are stretched, and as the door closes, the springs are released, helping to counterbalance the weight of the door. Extension springs can eventually wear out due to the constant stretching and release of tension.

Signs of a Broken Extension Spring

If you notice any of the following signs, it's likely that your extension springs need to be repaired or replaced:

  • The garage door is difficult to open or close, or it doesn't open or close at all.
  • The door appears to be lopsided or off-balance.
  • There are visible signs of damage or wear on the extension springs.
  • When the door is open, the springs are no longer parallel.

DIY Extension Spring Repair

If you have experience in basic home repairs and are comfortable working with tools, you may be able to repair or replace your extension springs yourself. Before you begin, make sure to disconnect the power to the garage door opener, and make sure the door is in the down position.

Replacing broken or worn extension springs is a fairly straightforward job, and the required parts can be purchased from your local hardware store. You'll need to measure the length and diameter of the spring, as well as the width of the coils. Make sure to buy two springs of the same size, as they are designed to work in tandem.

Once you have the new springs, you'll need to remove the old ones. This will require the use of a pair of pliers and a wrench. Once the old springs are removed, you'll need to attach the new ones, making sure to use the same number of coils as the old springs. Then, you'll need to attach the springs to the cable drums and the door, following the manufacturer's instructions.

The Benefits of Professional Repairs

If you're not comfortable conducting DIY repairs, it's best to call a professional. Not only will a professional be able to conduct repairs quickly and efficiently, but they will also be able to spot any other potential issues that may be present, such as worn-out cables or a door that is off-balance. Professional repairs can also help to extend the life of your garage door.

Conclusion

Garage door repair extension springs can be a tricky job, but with a little knowledge, it is possible to do the job yourself. However, if you're not confident in your abilities, it's best to call a professional for help. Professional repairs will ensure the job is done right, and can help to extend the life of your garage door.
