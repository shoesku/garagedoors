Identifying Problems with Your Garage Door

If your garage door begins to shake when it closes, you may be concerned that something has gone wrong with the door. Fortunately, this is usually nothing more than a minor issue that can be addressed with a few simple steps. To determine what may be causing the shaking, first you'll need to take a look at the door. Check to see if the door appears to be balanced properly, with no sagging sections or loose parts. If you can easily identify the problem, you may be able to fix it yourself. Otherwise, it may be time to call in a professional to take a look.

Common Reasons for Garage Door Shaking

In most cases, shaking is caused by a loose part or imbalance in the door. If the door is out of balance, it may be due to a broken spring or worn out rollers. A loose part, such as a roller or a track, can also cause the door to shake. In some cases, the shaking can be caused by a worn out motor or opener, which should be checked by a professional. Finally, a lack of lubrication on the door's parts can also cause the door to shake when it closes.

Checking the Springs and Rollers

If you suspect that a broken spring or worn out rollers are causing the shaking, you should start by checking these components. Broken springs can be easily identified by looking for signs of rust or damage. If the springs are in good condition, then you can move on to checking the rollers. Rollers should be checked for signs of wear and tear, such as cracks or chips. If the rollers are in good condition, then you can move on to checking the other components.

Inspecting the Tracks

The next step is to inspect the garage door tracks. If there is any debris or dirt in the tracks, it should be removed. Additionally, you should check for any loose parts or bent tracks. If there is any damage to the tracks, they should be replaced as soon as possible. This is especially important if the tracks are worn out or bent, as this can cause the door to shake when it closes.

Checking the Motor and Opener

If the springs, rollers, and tracks all appear to be in good condition, then you should move on to checking the motor and opener. If the motor or opener is not functioning properly, then you may need to call in a professional to inspect it. Additionally, you should check for any signs of wear and tear, such as rust or cracked parts. If the motor or opener is in good condition, then you should move on to lubricating the parts.

Lubricating the Garage Door Parts

If all other components appear to be in working order, you should move on to lubricating the parts. This is an important step, as lack of lubrication can cause the door to shake when it closes. Start by lubricating the springs, rollers, and tracks. You can use a lubricant specifically designed for garage doors, or you can use a lubricant such as WD-40. Once the parts are lubricated, you should check to make sure that the door is closing smoothly. If the door is still shaking, then you may need to call in a professional.

Conclusion

If your garage door is shaking when it closes, then you may be concerned that something has gone wrong with the door. Fortunately, this is usually nothing more than a minor issue that can be addressed with a few simple steps. Start by checking the door for any loose parts or imbalances. If you can identify the problem, you may be able to fix it yourself. Otherwise, it may be time to call in a professional. Finally, remember to lubricate the parts to ensure that the door is working smoothly.
