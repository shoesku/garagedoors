How To Fix A Garage Door That Keeps Popping Off Track

If you have a garage door that keeps popping off the track, you know it can be very frustrating. You may have tried to fix it yourself, but it’s difficult to get the door back on the track without damaging it. Fortunately, there are a few steps you can follow to get your garage door back on track without having to call in a professional.

Step 1: Assess the Damage

The first step in fixing a garage door that keeps popping off the track is to assess the damage. If the garage door is completely off the track, you'll need to determine what caused it in the first place. If one of the rollers is simply off the track, you'll need to determine why it shifted. It’s also a good idea to inspect the other rollers to make sure they’re in good condition. If the rollers are cracked or worn, they may need to be replaced.

Step 2: Make Sure the Tracks are Aligned

The tracks that the garage door runs on should be aligned properly. If they’re not, the door won’t be able to move properly. To check the alignment, use a level to see if the tracks are level. If they’re not, you’ll need to adjust them so they’re level. You may need to use a wrench or screwdriver to make the adjustments.

Step 3: Tighten the Track Bolts

If the tracks on your garage door are loose, it could cause the door to pop off the track. To check, use a wrench to tighten the bolts that hold the tracks in place. Make sure the bolts are tight, but don’t over-tighten them. If the bolts are too tight, it could cause the tracks to become warped, which could make the problem worse.

Step 4: Adjust the Rollers

The rollers are what allow the garage door to move up and down the tracks. If they’re not adjusted properly, it could cause the door to pop off the track. To adjust the rollers, use a wrench to loosen the nuts and bolts on the roller brackets. Then, adjust the rollers so they’re level with the tracks. Once they’re adjusted, tighten the nuts and bolts to hold them in place.

Step 5: Lubricate the Tracks and Rollers

If the tracks and rollers are dry and rusty, they won’t move properly. To lubricate them, use a lubricant designed specifically for garage doors. Apply the lubricant to the tracks and rollers and then use a cloth to wipe away any excess. This will help the door move more freely and reduce the chances of it popping off the track.

Step 6: Check the Springs

If the garage door springs are weak or broken, it could cause the door to pop off the track. To check the springs, look for signs of wear and tear. If the springs are weak or broken, they should be replaced. However, this is a job best left to a professional since the springs can be very dangerous if not handled correctly.

Step 7: Test the Door

Once you’ve completed the above steps, it’s time to test the door. Open and close the garage door several times to make sure it’s working properly. If it still pops off the track, you may need to adjust the rollers or replace the rollers. If the door still won’t stay on the track, it may be time to call in a professional.

Conclusion

If your garage door keeps popping off the track, it can be very frustrating. Fortunately, there are a few steps you can take to get it back on track. Start by assessing the damage, then make sure the tracks are aligned, tighten the track bolts, adjust the rollers, lubricate the tracks and rollers, check the springs, and test the door. If the door still won’t stay on the track, it may be time to call in a professional.
