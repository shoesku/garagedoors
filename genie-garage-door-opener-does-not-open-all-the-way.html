Troubleshooting Genie Garage Door Opener Does Not Open All The Way

Introduction

If your Genie garage door opener does not open all the way, it can be a very frustrating experience. Whether you are trying to get into your garage in the morning or you are trying to leave in the evening, it can be very inconvenient. Fortunately, there are a few common causes that can be easily fixed. In this article, we will discuss the steps you can take to troubleshoot and fix your Genie garage door opener that is not opening all the way.

Check the Photo Eye Sensors

The first step you should take is to check the photo eye sensors. These sensors are located on either side of the garage door. They are designed to detect any objects, such as a person or a pet, that are in the way of the garage door when it is closing. If these sensors are blocked or out of alignment, the garage door will not open all the way. To check if the photo eye sensors are blocked or out of alignment, simply look at the red LED light that is located on each sensor. If the light is lit, then the sensors are working properly. If the light is not lit, then the sensors are either blocked or out of alignment. If this is the case, you will need to adjust the sensors to get them back in alignment.

Check the Limit Settings

The next step you should take is to check the limit settings on your Genie garage door opener. The limit settings control how far the garage door will open and close. If the limit settings are not set correctly, then the garage door will not open all the way. To check the limit settings, look for the limit screws located on the motor unit of your Genie garage door opener. There should be two limit screws, one for the up limit and one for the down limit. Adjust these screws until the garage door opens and closes as far as you need it to.

Check the Force Settings

The force settings on your Genie garage door opener control how hard the motor unit works to open and close the garage door. If the force settings are too low, then the motor unit will not be able to open the garage door all the way. To check the force settings, look for the force adjustment screws located on the motor unit. There should be two force adjustment screws, one for the up force and one for the down force. Adjust these screws until the garage door opens and closes as far as you need it to.

Check the Wall Control Unit

The wall control unit is the device that is used to open and close the garage door. If the wall control unit is not working properly, then the garage door will not open all the way. To check if the wall control unit is working properly, press the open and close buttons on the unit. If the garage door does not respond, then the wall control unit is not working properly and will need to be replaced.

Check the Track and Rollers

The track and rollers on your Genie garage door opener can also cause the garage door to not open all the way. To check the track and rollers, look for any obstructions or damages. If the track is blocked, then the garage door will not be able to open all the way. If the rollers are damaged, then the garage door will not be able to move smoothly. If either of these issues are present, then you will need to replace the track and/or rollers in order to get the garage door opening all the way again.

Check the Springs

The springs on your Genie garage door opener can also cause the garage door to not open all the way. If the springs are worn or broken, then they will not be able to provide the necessary tension to open the garage door all the way. To check the springs, look for any signs of damage or wear. If the springs are damaged, then you will need to replace them in order to get the garage door opening all the way again.

Conclusion

If your Genie garage door opener does not open all the way, then it can be a very frustrating experience. Fortunately, there are a few common causes that can be easily fixed. By following the steps outlined in this article, you should be able to troubleshoot and fix your Genie garage door opener that is not opening all the way.
