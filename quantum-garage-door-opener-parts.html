Garage Door Opener Motor

The motor is perhaps the most important part of the Quantum garage door opener. It is responsible for powering the system and lifting the door. Most Quantum garage door openers feature a powerful, yet quiet, DC motor. This type of motor is designed to last for years and can handle even the heaviest of garage doors. The motor also features a built-in safety system that will stop the motor if it senses an obstruction in the path of the door.

Remote Control and Wall Console

The Quantum garage door opener system features a remote control for easy access to your garage. The remote control sends a signal to the garage door opener motor when you press the button. This signal then opens or closes the garage door. The Quantum system also includes a wall console which allows you to control the garage door from inside the home. The wall console also features a keypad for entering a PIN code to open the garage door.

Sensors and Safety Features

The Quantum garage door opener features a pair of sensors that detect any obstacles in the path of the garage door. If an object is detected, the motor will automatically stop and reverse the door. This is an important safety feature that prevents damage to the garage door or any objects that are in the way. The Quantum system also features an auto-lock feature that locks the garage door once it is closed.

Power Supply and Battery Backup

The Quantum garage door opener system is powered by a 120V AC power supply. This power supply is used to power the motor, sensors, and other components. The Quantum system also features a rechargeable battery backup which allows the opener to operate even during a power outage. The battery backup is designed to last for several years before needing to be replaced.

Accessories

In addition to the main parts of the Quantum garage door opener, there are a few accessories that can be added to the system. These accessories can add convenience and security to your home.
