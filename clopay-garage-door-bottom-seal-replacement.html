Replacing Clopay Garage Door Bottom Seal

Step 1: Preparing the New Garage Door Bottom Seal

The first step to replacing your Clopay garage door bottom seal is to prepare the new seal. If you have purchased a pre-made seal, you’ll need to cut it to size. Measure the width of the garage door opening and cut the seal to that width using a hacksaw or a sharp utility knife. If you’ve purchased a roll of seal material, you’ll need to cut a length that’s long enough to fit the width of your garage door opening. Make sure you measure twice and cut once!

Step 2: Removing the Old Garage Door Bottom Seal

Next, you’ll need to remove the existing garage door bottom seal. Start by unscrewing the screws that secure the old seal to the bottom of the door. Once the screws are removed, pull the seal away from the door. Take your time and be careful not to damage the door. If the seal is stuck to the door, use a putty knife or a flat-head screwdriver to pry it off.

Step 3: Installing the New Garage Door Bottom Seal

Once you’ve removed the old seal, you can begin installing the new one. Start by placing the new seal onto the door. Make sure the seal is flush against the bottom of the door. Secure the seal to the door using the screws you removed in step two. Once the seal is in place, test it by opening and closing the garage door. If the seal is not tight against the ground, you may need to adjust the screws.

Step 4: Finishing the Installation

The last step is to finish the installation. Start by trimming any excess seal material. You can use a utility knife or a pair of scissors to do this. Once you’ve trimmed the excess material, use a caulk gun to apply a thin bead of caulk along the edges of the seal. This will help to keep the seal in place and reduce the chances of it becoming loose over time. Finally, use a damp cloth to wipe away any excess caulk.

Conclusion

Replacing the bottom seal on your Clopay garage door is a simple job that anyone can do with a few basic tools. Make sure you take your time and measure twice before cutting the seal. Once the seal is installed, you can enjoy the improved air-tightness of your garage and the peace of mind that comes with knowing that your belongings are secure.
