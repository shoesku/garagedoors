What To Do When Your Garage Door Has No Power

Best Garage doors and references for house or residential with custom material and more design. We Share more picture design with HD quality images.

Check the Electrical Supply

When your garage door suddenly stops working, the first thing you need to do is check the electrical supply. Most garage doors run on electricity, so if the power is out, the door won’t open. If the power is out, check the circuit breaker and reset it if necessary. If you still don’t have power, you may need to call an electrician to take a look. If the power is on, but your door still won’t open, it’s time to move onto the next step.

Check the Safety Sensors

If your door won’t open despite having power, the next thing you’ll need to check is the safety sensors. These are usually located at the bottom of the door and help prevent the door from closing on people or objects. If the sensors are covered in dirt or debris, or if the lenses are cracked, they won’t be able to detect anything in the way and will prevent the door from opening. Start by cleaning the sensors with a soft cloth and then check to see if the lenses are cracked. If they are, you may need to replace them.

Check the Wall Button

If the safety sensors are working properly, the next thing you should check is the wall button. This is the button located inside the garage and is what you press to open the door. If the button isn’t working, it could be a sign of a bigger problem. To check it, press the button and listen for a click. If you hear a click, the button is working and the problem is likely elsewhere. If you don’t hear a click, you may need to replace the button.

Check the Motor

If the wall button is working and the safety sensors are working properly, the next thing you’ll need to check is the motor. This is the biggest component of the garage door and is responsible for lifting and lowering the door. If the motor isn’t working, the door won’t open or close. The easiest way to check the motor is to listen for it. If you don’t hear anything, the motor is likely the problem. You may need to call a garage door repair technician to take a look.

Check the Cables and Springs

If the motor is working, the next thing you’ll need to check is the cables and springs. These are responsible for lifting and lowering the door and if they’re damaged, the door won’t open. Start by checking the cables for any signs of damage or wear and tear. If they look worn, you’ll need to replace them. You’ll also need to check the springs for any signs of damage or wear and tear. If the springs are worn, you’ll need to replace them as well.

Check the Tracks

The last thing you’ll need to check is the tracks. The tracks are responsible for guiding the door up and down and if they’re damaged or blocked, the door won’t open. Start by checking the tracks for any signs of damage such as dents or rust. If the tracks are damaged, you’ll need to have them repaired or replaced. You’ll also need to check the tracks for any signs of blockage such as dirt, debris, or leaves. If the tracks are blocked, you’ll need to clean them out.

Call a Professional

If you’ve checked all of the above and your garage door still won’t open, it’s time to call a professional. A professional garage door technician will be able to diagnose and repair the problem quickly and efficiently. They’ll also be able to give you advice on how to keep your garage door in good working order in the future.

Conclusion

When your garage door has no power, it can be a frustrating experience. The good news is that there are a few simple steps you can take to try and fix the problem yourself. Start by checking the electrical supply, then the safety sensors, wall button, motor, cables and springs, and tracks. If you’ve checked all of these and your door still won’t open, it’s time to call a professional. With their help, you’ll be able to get your garage door working again in no time.
