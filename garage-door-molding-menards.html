How To Install Garage Door Molding At Menards

If you’re looking to improve the look of your garage, you should consider installing garage door molding. It’s a relatively inexpensive and easy way to give your garage a modern, finished look. Garage door molding can also come in handy if your garage door has uneven gaps or if the weatherstripping around the door is no longer effective. Installing garage door molding is a great way to add a decorative touch to any garage.

When you’re ready to install your garage door molding, you’ll need to head to your local Menards store. Menards carries a wide range of garage door molding in various styles and colors. You can also find trim and accessories to complete the look of your garage. When you’re shopping for your garage door molding, make sure to measure the size of your door and the height of the wall where you’ll be installing it. This will make sure that your molding fits perfectly.

Tools and Materials Needed

Once you’ve gathered your garage door molding from Menards, you’ll need to gather the tools and materials you’ll need to complete the installation. These include a tape measure, a level, a drill, a saw, a hammer, and nails. You’ll also want to make sure you have a few sandpaper sheets and a caulking gun. You may also need a jigsaw if your molding won’t fit the size of your door.

Preparing the Area

Before you begin the installation process, you’ll need to prepare the area. This means cleaning the area around the door and removing any debris or dust. If you’re replacing existing molding, you’ll need to remove it first. You’ll also need to make sure the area is level and that the walls are in good condition.

Installing the Molding

Once the area is prepped, you’re ready to begin installing the molding. Start by measuring the area where you’ll be installing the molding. Once you have the measurements, you can mark the area with a pencil or marker. You’ll also want to make sure the area is level before you begin. Once you’ve marked the area, you can begin to install the molding.

Start by setting the molding into the area and making sure it fits properly. If it doesn’t fit, you’ll need to trim it to size using a saw or jigsaw. Once the molding is the right size, you can nail it into place. Make sure to use galvanized nails that are the same size as the molding. If you’re using trim, you can nail it into place after the molding is installed.

Finishing Touches

Once the molding is installed, you’ll want to give it a few finishing touches to make sure it’s secure and looks great. Start by sanding the edges of the molding, and then fill any holes or gaps with caulk. This will help to seal the molding and make it look more finished. Once the caulk is dry, you can paint the molding to match the color of your garage.

Benefits of Garage Door Molding

Installing garage door molding is a great way to add a decorative touch to your garage. Not only will it improve the look of your garage, but it can also be used to help seal in the cold air during the winter and keep out pests. It can also be used to cover up any gaps or unevenness in your garage door, making your garage look more professional.

Conclusion

Installing garage door molding is a relatively easy and inexpensive way to improve the look of your garage. By shopping at Menards, you can find the perfect molding to match your style and budget. With a few tools and supplies, you can install your own garage door molding and give your garage a finished and modern look.
