How To Fix Your Shaky Garage Door

Do your garage doors shake and shudder when going down? Are you worried about the safety of your family and your home? Don’t worry, this is something that can easily be fixed. Here’s what you need to know to get your garage door back in top shape.

1. Check the Tracks

The most common cause of a shaky garage door is the tracks. The tracks are the metal rails that your garage door runs along. Over time, these tracks can become bent and worn, leading to a shaky garage door. If you have bent tracks, you’ll need to replace them.

To check for bent tracks, open your garage door and look for any signs of damage. If you notice any dents or warps in the tracks, you’ll need to replace them. You can also check for debris that may be lodged in the tracks. If you find any debris, you’ll need to remove it.

2. Clean and Lubricate the Tracks

Even if you don’t find any damage to the tracks, you should still clean and lubricate them. This will help ensure that your garage door is running smoothly. Start by using a clean cloth to remove any dirt or debris. Then, use a lubricant to coat the tracks and help reduce friction.

3. Check the Rollers

The rollers are the small wheels that attach to the bottom of your garage door. Over time, these rollers can wear out and become damaged, leading to a shaky garage door. If you notice any cracks or damage to the rollers, you’ll need to replace them.

To check for damage, open your garage door and look for any signs of wear. If you notice any cracks or chips, you’ll need to replace the rollers. You can also check for debris that may be stuck in the rollers. If you find any debris, you’ll need to remove it.

4. Lubricate the Rollers

Even if you don’t find any damage to the rollers, you should still lubricate them. This will help ensure that your garage door is running smoothly. Start by using a lubricant to coat the rollers and help reduce friction.

5. Check the Springs

The springs are the metal coils that help your garage door open and close. Over time, these springs can become worn and damaged, leading to a shaky garage door. If you notice any signs of damage to the springs, you’ll need to replace them.

To check for damage, open your garage door and look for any signs of wear. If you notice any cracks or chips, you’ll need to replace the springs. You can also check for debris that may be stuck in the springs. If you find any debris, you’ll need to remove it.

6. Check the Cables

The cables are the metal wires that help your garage door open and close. Over time, these cables can become frayed and damaged, leading to a shaky garage door. If you notice any signs of damage to the cables, you’ll need to replace them.

To check for damage, open your garage door and look for any signs of wear. If you notice any fraying or breaks, you’ll need to replace the cables. You can also check for debris that may be stuck in the cables. If you find any debris, you’ll need to remove it.

7. Check the Pulleys

The pulleys are the small wheels that help your garage door open and close. Over time, these pulleys can become worn and damaged, leading to a shaky garage door. If you notice any signs of damage to the pulleys, you’ll need to replace them.

To check for damage, open your garage door and look for any signs of wear. If you notice any cracks or chips, you’ll need to replace the pulleys. You can also check for debris that may be stuck in the pulleys. If you find any debris, you’ll need to remove it.

8. Tighten the Hardware

If all else fails, you may need to tighten the hardware on your garage door. Over time, the screws and bolts on your garage door can become loose, leading to a shaky garage door. To tighten the hardware, you’ll need a screwdriver and a wrench.

Start by tightening all of the screws and bolts on your garage door. Be sure to check the tracks, rollers, springs, cables, and pulleys. Once you’ve tightened all of the hardware, your garage door should be running smoothly.

9. Call a Professional

If you’re still having trouble with your garage door, you may need to call a professional. A professional can diagnose the problem and make the necessary repairs to get your garage door back in top shape. They can also provide you with tips and advice for keeping your garage door running smoothly for years to come.

Conclusion

Shaky garage doors can be a real nuisance. But with a little bit of patience and know-how, you can get your garage door back in top shape. Start by checking the tracks, rollers, springs, cables, and pulleys. Then, clean and lubricate the parts. If all else fails, you may need to call a professional. With a little bit of effort, you’ll have your garage door running smoothly again in no time.
