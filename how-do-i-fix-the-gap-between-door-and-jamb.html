How To Fix The Gap Between Door And Jamb

Are you looking for the answer to the age-old question, "How do I fix the gap between door and jamb?" If so, you've come to the right place. This article will provide easy-to-follow instructions on how to fix this common problem.

What Causes the Gap Between Door and Jamb?

When installing a door, a gap often appears between the door and the jamb. This is usually caused by a misalignment of the door jamb and the door frame, or by the door frame being out of square. In some cases, the gap can also be caused by the door settling over time.

Tools and Materials Needed

To fix the gap between door and jamb, you will need the following tools and materials: a drill, a drill bit, a screwdriver, a hammer, a level, shims, adhesive or caulk, and wood screws.

Steps to Fix the Gap

Here are the steps to follow to fix the gap between door and jamb:

Step 1: Remove the Door

The first step is to remove the door from its hinges and lay it flat on the ground. This will give you easier access to the frame and jamb.

Step 2: Drill the Holes

Using a drill and a drill bit, drill two holes on the jamb and two holes on the door frame. These holes should be directly opposite one another, with each hole being aligned with the other.

Step 3: Level the Door

Using a level, make sure that the door is level and square. If it is not, you will need to adjust it with a hammer and a screwdriver.

Step 4: Insert Shims

Once the door is level, you can insert the shims into the holes you drilled. Make sure that the shims are flush with the door and jamb. This will help to fill the gap between the two.

Step 5: Secure the Door

Using wood screws, secure the door to the jamb. Make sure that the screws are tight, but not so tight that they begin to strip the wood.

Step 6: Apply Adhesive or Caulk

Finally, apply adhesive or caulk to the edges of the door and jamb. This will help to create a tight seal between the two and prevent air infiltration.

Conclusion

Fixing the gap between door and jamb is a relatively easy task that can be done by anyone with a basic set of tools. By following the steps outlined in this article, you should be able to easily fix this common problem.
