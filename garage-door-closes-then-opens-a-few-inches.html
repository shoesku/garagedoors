What Causes the Garage Door to Close and Open a Few Inches?

The most common cause for a garage door to close and open a few inches is usually due to a misalignment of the limit switch. The limit switch is a part of the lifting mechanism that tells the opener when to stop and start. When the switch is misaligned, it can cause the door to stop and start at different points, resulting in it opening and closing a few inches. Other causes can include a malfunctioning opener, a broken roller, a broken spring, or a problem with the tracks.

How Can You Fix the Problem?

The first step in fixing the problem is to identify the source of the issue. If the limit switch is misaligned, you will need to adjust it to the correct setting. To do this, you will need to locate the switch and make sure it is in line with the opening and closing points of the door. If the opener is malfunctioning, you will need to replace it or have it professionally repaired. If a roller is broken, you will need to replace it. A broken spring will also need to be replaced. Lastly, if the problem is with the tracks, you will need to have them realigned.

Safety Tips for Working on a Garage Door

When you are working on a garage door, there are a few safety tips that you should keep in mind. First, always make sure to disconnect the power source to the opener before working on any part of the system. This will help to prevent any accidental injuries or damage to the components. Second, always make sure to use the right tools for the job. The wrong tools can cause damage to the parts and the door itself. Lastly, always make sure to wear the proper safety equipment, such as gloves and safety glasses.

Conclusion

If your garage door closes and opens a few inches, the most likely cause is a misalignment of the limit switch. You can fix the problem by adjusting the switch or by replacing any malfunctioning or broken parts. Make sure to always take the proper safety precautions when working on the door, such as disconnecting the power source and wearing the right safety equipment. If the problem persists, you should call a professional for help.
