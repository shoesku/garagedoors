The Troubles Of Losing Your Garage Door Key

Losing your garage door key can be a major inconvenience. Not only can it be difficult to enter your own home through the garage, but it can also be a major security risk. If you've lost your key, it's important to know what to do to rectify the situation and keep your home safe. Here are some tips to help you get back into your garage and keep your home secure.

Change Your Locks

The first thing you should do when you lose your garage door key is to change the locks. If you don't, anyone who finds the missing key will have access to your home. This makes it incredibly important to get the locks changed as soon as you can. But, if you plan on replacing the locks yourself, it's important to make sure you know what you're doing. If you make a mistake, it could make it even easier for someone to access your home. To be sure that your locks are changed correctly, you should hire a professional locksmith.

Rekey Your Existing Locks

If you don't want to completely replace your locks, you may want to consider rekeying them. Rekeying is the process of changing the pins inside the lock so that the old key will no longer work. Rekeying is less expensive than replacing the locks, but it still gives you peace of mind that your home is secure. The process is still complex, though, so it's best to hire a professional locksmith to ensure that it's done correctly.

Get A New Key

If you've lost your garage door key, you'll need to get a new one made. Depending on the type of lock you have, you may be able to get a new key cut from a hardware store. However, many garage door locks require a special key that can only be made by a professional locksmith. If you have this type of lock, you'll need to contact a locksmith to get a replacement key made.

Consider Upgrading Your Security

Losing your garage door key is an ideal time to consider upgrading your security. There are many different security options available that can help you keep your home safe from intruders. For example, you can install a keypad entry system or a smart lock that allows you to control your locks from your smartphone. You can also install a camera system to help you keep an eye on your property.

Keep Track Of Your Keys

Once you've taken the steps to secure your home, it's important to remember to keep track of your keys. If you have multiple people who need access to the garage, it might be a good idea to get a key box or key safe. This will ensure that everyone can access the garage without having to keep track of multiple keys.

Create A Spare Key

No matter how careful you are, there's always a chance that you'll lose your garage door key at some point. To make sure that you're prepared for this possibility, you should create a spare key. This spare key can be kept with a trusted friend or family member so that you can access your garage if you ever lose your key.

Contact Your Local Locksmith

If you find yourself in a situation where you've lost your garage door key, it's important to contact your local locksmith right away. A professional locksmith can help you assess the situation and determine the best course of action. They can also provide you with replacement keys, change your locks, or provide you with other security solutions.

Conclusion

Losing your garage door key can be a major inconvenience, but it doesn't have to be a major security risk. By taking the proper steps, you can ensure that your home stays secure and that you can access your garage when you need to. If you ever find yourself in a situation where you've lost your key, contact your local locksmith right away for help.
