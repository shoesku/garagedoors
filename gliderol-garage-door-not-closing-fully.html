Troubleshooting Your Gliderol Garage Door Not Closing Fully

Introduction

Are you having trouble with your Gliderol garage door not closing fully? Does it open and close, but not quite all the way? Don't worry, this is a common problem and it can be easily fixed. In this article, we'll discuss the different reasons why your Gliderol garage door might not be closing fully and how to troubleshoot the problem. Read on to learn more.

Causes of Gliderol Garage Door Not Closing Fully

There can be several different causes of your Gliderol garage door not closing fully. One of the most common causes is a misaligned track. When the track is not aligned properly, it can cause the door to not close all the way. Another cause can be a worn out roller, spring, or cable. A worn out roller can cause the door to not close all the way. Finally, the door may not be closing all the way due to a lack of lubrication. If the hinges and tracks are not properly lubricated, then the door will not open and close smoothly.

Troubleshooting the Problem

The first step in troubleshooting your Gliderol garage door not closing fully is to check and make sure that the track is properly aligned. If it is not, then you should use a level to adjust it until it is properly aligned. If the track is properly aligned and the door is still not closing fully, then you should check the rollers, springs, and cables to make sure they are in good working condition. If any of them are worn out, then you should replace them.

Lubrication

Once you have checked the rollers, springs, and cables, the next step is to make sure that the door is properly lubricated. You will want to use a lubricant specifically designed for garage doors, such as WD-40. Apply the lubricant to all of the moving parts and then open and close the door to ensure that it is moving smoothly. If the door is still not closing all the way, then you may need to adjust the tension in the springs.

Springs

If the springs need to be adjusted, then you will want to use a winding bar to adjust them. A winding bar is a tool specifically designed to adjust garage door springs. It is important to be careful when adjusting the tension in the springs as too much tension can cause the door to not close all the way. You should only adjust the springs a small amount at a time until the door is closing properly.

Conclusion

If your Gliderol garage door is not closing fully, then there are several steps that you can take to troubleshoot the problem. The first step is to make sure that the track is properly aligned. Then, check the rollers, springs, and cables to make sure they are in good working condition. Finally, properly lubricate the door and adjust the tension in the springs if necessary. With these steps, you should be able to solve the problem and get your Gliderol garage door closing properly.
