What Does It Mean When Your Garage Door Motor Light Is Flashing?

Best Garage doors and references for house or residential with custom material and more design. We Share more picture design with HD quality images.

If you have a garage door motor, you may have seen it flashing. This can be a bit disconcerting and can cause you to wonder what is going on. Luckily, there are several common reasons for flashing lights, and they’re all relatively easy to fix. In the following article, we’ll discuss what it means when your garage door motor light is flashing, how to troubleshoot the problem, and how to fix it.

Why Is the Garage Door Motor Light Flashing?

The most common reason for a flashing light on your garage door motor is that the power supply has been interrupted or the circuit breaker has been tripped. This is especially common if you’ve recently installed a new garage door or motor, as the circuit breaker may need to be reset. Another reason for a flashing light is that the safety sensors have been blocked or moved, so the motor can’t detect that the door is open.

If the motor light is flashing and the power supply has not been interrupted, then it’s possible that the motor is malfunctioning and needs to be serviced. A flashing light on the motor can indicate that the motor is overheating, that the gears are worn, or that the motor is not receiving enough power. In any of these cases, it’s best to contact a professional garage door technician to inspect and repair the motor.

Troubleshooting the Problem

The first step in troubleshooting a flashing light on your garage door motor is to check the power supply. Make sure that the power is on and that the circuit breaker has not been tripped. If the power is on and the circuit breaker is not tripped, then you can check the sensors. The sensors are located at the bottom of the door, and if they’ve been blocked or moved, then the motor won’t be able to detect that the door is open.

Next, check the motor itself. Make sure that the motor is securely connected to the power source and that it is receiving enough power. If the motor is not receiving enough power, then you may need to increase the power supply. If the motor is receiving enough power, then it may be malfunctioning. In this case, it’s best to contact a professional garage door technician to inspect and repair the motor.

How to Fix the Problem

If the power supply has been interrupted or the circuit breaker has been tripped, then you can simply reset the circuit breaker and the motor should be working again. If the safety sensors have been blocked or moved, then you can move them back into place and the motor should be working again. If the motor is not receiving enough power, then you can increase the power supply, and the motor should be working again.

If the motor is malfunctioning, then you will need to contact a professional garage door technician to inspect and repair the motor. The technician will be able to diagnose the problem and provide a solution. Depending on the severity of the problem, the technician may need to replace the motor or make repairs to the motor.

Conclusion

It’s important to be aware of what it means when your garage door motor light is flashing. It could be due to a power supply interruption, a tripped circuit breaker, or blocked safety sensors. It could also be due to a malfunctioning motor. In any case, it’s best to contact a professional garage door technician to inspect and repair the motor. With the right knowledge and the right tools, you can get your garage door working again in no time.
