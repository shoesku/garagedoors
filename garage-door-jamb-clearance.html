What Is A Garage Door Jamb Clearance?

Garage door jamb clearance, also known as the "headroom" of the garage door, is the amount of space above the top of the garage door when it is opened. When the garage door is closed, the garage door jamb clearance is the same as the height of the garage door. This is an important measurement to consider when installing a new garage door as it will determine how much space is available for the door to fit when it is open. The jamb clearance of a garage door will also affect the amount of insulation needed to keep the garage warm in cold climates.

In order to properly measure your garage door jamb clearance, you will need a tape measure and a level. Start by measuring the height of the garage door from the garage floor to the top of the door. Then, measure the height of the door frame (or jamb) from the floor to the top of the frame. Subtract the door height from the frame height to get the jamb clearance. This should be between 6 and 12 inches.

Why Is Garage Door Jamb Clearance Important?

Garage door jamb clearance is important for several reasons. First, it ensures that there is enough space for the garage door to open and close without hitting the frame. If the clearance is too small, the door may not be able to open or close properly, which can be a major inconvenience. Additionally, the jamb clearance affects the amount of insulation needed for the garage. If the clearance is too small, it can cause a draft in the garage and make it difficult to keep the space warm in cold climates.

In addition to the insulation issue, having too little jamb clearance can also cause damage to the door itself. If the clearance is too small, the door may rub against the frame when it is opened or closed, causing it to become damaged over time. This can lead to costly repairs or even the need for a new garage door. Therefore, it is important to make sure that the jamb clearance of your garage door is sufficient.

How to Increase Garage Door Jamb Clearance

If your garage door jamb clearance is too small, there are a few things that you can do to increase it. The first option is to adjust the height of the door frame. This is typically done by removing the door frame and then installing a new one that is higher. The second option is to add insulation to the garage space. This is done by applying an insulation material to the walls and ceiling of the garage. Finally, you can install a different type of door that requires less jamb clearance, such as a roll-up door.

Garage Door Jamb Clearance Tips

When installing a new garage door, it is important to measure the jamb clearance of the door before purchasing it. This will ensure that there is enough space for the door to open and close without rubbing against the frame. Additionally, it is important to make sure that the door is properly insulated to keep the garage warm in cold climates. Finally, if your jamb clearance is too small, there are ways to increase it, such as adjusting the height of the door frame or adding insulation.

Conclusion

Garage door jamb clearance is an important measurement to consider when installing a new garage door. It is important to measure the jamb clearance of the door before purchasing it to ensure that there is enough space for the door to open and close properly. Additionally, having sufficient jamb clearance is important for insulation purposes as it can help keep the garage warm in cold climates. If your jamb clearance is too small, there are ways to increase it, such as adjusting the height of the door frame or adding insulation. With the right amount of jamb clearance, your garage door will be more efficient and last longer.
