Tips For Replacing Panel Lift Garage Door Springs

Best Garage doors and references for house or residential with custom material and more design. We Share more picture design with HD quality images.

Garage door springs are an integral part of any panel lift garage door, and they can wear out over time. This is why it's important to replace them regularly, to ensure the door functions properly. Spring replacement can be a tricky process, but with the right tools and know-how, you can do it yourself. Here are some tips for replacing panel lift garage door springs.

Safety First

Before beginning any work on your garage door, it's important to take safety precautions. Make sure the door is disconnected from its power source and the opener is disengaged. If the door is attached to a torsion spring, be sure to secure the door in place with a clamp. This will prevent it from crashing down if the spring breaks suddenly. You should also wear safety glasses and gloves to protect your eyes and hands while working.

Assemble Your Tools

You'll need a few basic tools to replace your garage door springs. You'll need a wrench set, a socket set, a drill, a pair of pliers, and a level. You'll also need a ladder to reach the springs. Don't forget to check the tension of the springs before beginning the replacement process.

Identify the Springs

There are two types of springs used on panel lift garage doors: torsion springs and extension springs. Torsion springs are located above the door, while extension springs are located on the sides. It's important to identify the type of springs on your door before purchasing new ones.

Order Replacement Parts

Once you've identified the type of springs on your door, you can order new ones. Be sure to order the correct size, as springs come in a variety of sizes. It's also important to choose springs made of high-quality materials, such as steel, as they last longer and are more reliable.

Install the Springs

Once your replacement parts have arrived, you can begin installing them. If you are replacing torsion springs, start by disconnecting the cables from the springs. Then, remove the screws that hold the springs in place and remove the old springs. To install the new springs, follow the instructions provided with the springs. For extension springs, begin by disconnecting the spring from the wall and removing the old springs. Then, attach the new springs and secure them in place with screws.

Test the Door

Once the springs have been installed, it's important to test the door to ensure it's working properly. Open and close the door several times to make sure the springs are functioning properly. If the door is not opening and closing properly, you may need to adjust the tension of the springs. If the springs are still not working properly, you should consider hiring a professional to adjust them.

Conclusion

Replacing panel lift garage door springs can be a tricky process, but with the right tools and know-how, you can do it yourself. Be sure to take safety precautions, assemble the proper tools, and identify the type of springs before beginning the replacement process. Once the springs have been installed, test the door to ensure it's working properly. With these tips, you'll be able to replace your panel lift garage door springs in no time!
