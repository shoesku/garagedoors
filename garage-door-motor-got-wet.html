Don't Panic: What To Do When Your Garage Door Motor Gets Wet

Nobody wants to deal with a wet garage door motor. Not only is it inconvenient, but it can also be potentially dangerous. If your garage door motor gets wet, it is important to take the proper steps to ensure its safety and the safety of your family and property. Here is an in-depth guide on what to do when your garage door motor gets wet in 2023.

Step One: Disconnect the Power to the Motor

The first step to take when your garage door motor gets wet is to disconnect the power to it immediately. Even if the motor is not visibly wet, it is important to do this to prevent further damage. Unplug the motor from the power source and turn off any circuit breakers that may be connected to the motor.

Step Two: Take the Motor Apart

The next step is to take the motor apart and inspect the parts. Carefully remove the motor from its housing and take out any components that are wet. If any of the parts or wiring are visibly damaged, it is important to replace them. If the motor is not visibly wet, you may want to leave it in its housing.

Step Three: Clean the Parts

Once you have removed the wet parts, it is important to clean them. Use a soft cloth and mild soap to gently clean the parts. Be sure to avoid using harsh chemicals or abrasive materials as this could cause further damage. Once the parts are clean, you can use a towel to dry them off.

Step Four: Reassemble the Motor

Once you have cleaned and dried the parts, it is time to reassemble the motor. Carefully put the parts back together in the same way that they were before. Make sure that all of the connections are secure and that the wiring is in good condition. Once the motor is reassembled, you can reconnect it to the power source.

Step Five: Test the Motor

Once the motor is reassembled, it is important to test it to make sure that it is functioning properly. Connect the motor to its power source and press the button to open the door. If the door opens, then the motor is working. If the door does not open, then you may need to consult a professional.

Step Six: Contact a Professional

If the motor is not working properly after you have reassembled it, then it is time to contact a professional. A professional can inspect the motor and advise you on what needs to be done. They may need to replace the motor or certain parts, or they may be able to repair the motor.

Step Seven: Perform Regular Maintenance

The best way to avoid a wet garage door motor is to perform regular maintenance on the motor. Make sure to check the motor and its components on a regular basis and to clean off any dirt or debris. If you notice any signs of wear or damage, then it is important to replace the parts or contact a professional.

Conclusion:

If your garage door motor gets wet, it is important to take the proper steps to ensure its safety and the safety of your family and property. Disconnect the power to the motor immediately and take it apart to inspect the parts. Clean the parts and reassemble the motor. Test the motor and, if it is not working properly, contact a professional. Finally, perform regular maintenance on the motor to help prevent further issues.
