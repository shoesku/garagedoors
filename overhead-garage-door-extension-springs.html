Everything You Need To Know About Overhead Garage Door Extension Springs

When it comes to keeping your overhead garage door in working order, extension springs are an essential component. But, with so many different types of springs out there, it can be hard to know which one you should get for your door. In this article, we’ll break down everything you need to know about overhead garage door extension springs so you can make an informed decision.

What Are Overhead Garage Door Extension Springs?

Overhead garage door extension springs are part of the garage door’s counterbalance system. They’re typically made from galvanized steel and are attached to the door’s tracks. When the door is opened, the springs stretch and help lift the door. When the door is closed, the springs contract and keep the door securely closed.

Types of Overhead Garage Door Extension Springs

There are two main types of overhead garage door extension springs: standard torsion springs and side-mounted extension springs. Standard torsion springs are mounted directly above the door and are usually one-piece units. Side-mounted extension springs are usually two-piece units and are mounted on either side of the door. Both types of springs can be adjusted to increase or decrease the tension, depending on how heavy the door is.

How to Install Overhead Garage Door Extension Springs

Installing overhead garage door extension springs can be a tricky process, and it’s important that you follow the instructions carefully. Make sure to read the instructions on the package before you start the installation process. It’s also a good idea to have a few tools handy, such as a drill, a level, and a wrench. If you’re not comfortable with the installation process, it’s best to call a professional for help.

How to Maintain Overhead Garage Door Extension Springs

Once you’ve installed your overhead garage door extension springs, it’s important to maintain them to ensure they are in good condition. Check the springs regularly for any signs of wear and tear, and make sure to lubricate them regularly. You should also check the tension of the springs every few months, and adjust them as needed. If you notice any signs of wear or damage, it’s best to replace the springs as soon as possible.

How to Replace Overhead Garage Door Extension Springs

If your overhead garage door extension springs need to be replaced, it’s important that you follow the instructions carefully. Make sure to read the instructions on the package before you start the installation process. It’s also a good idea to have a few tools handy, such as a drill, a level, and a wrench. If you’re not comfortable with the installation process, it’s best to call a professional for help.

How to Tell if Your Overhead Garage Door Extension Springs Are Worn Out

If your overhead garage door extension springs are worn out, there are a few signs to look for. One of the most common signs is that the door doesn’t move as smoothly as it used to. You may also notice that the springs are sagging or that they’re not lifting the door as high as they used to. If you notice any of these signs, it’s best to replace the springs as soon as possible.

Get the Right Overhead Garage Door Extension Springs for Your Door

When it comes to buying the right overhead garage door extension springs for your door, it’s important to get the right size. Measure the length of your door and take into account the weight of the door when selecting the right springs. If you’re not sure which springs are right for your door, it’s best to consult a professional.

Conclusion

Overhead garage door extension springs are an essential component of any garage door. They help lift and lower the door, and they also keep it securely closed. It’s important to make sure that you get the right size and type of springs for your door, and to make sure that you maintain and replace them as needed. If you have any questions about overhead garage door extension springs, it’s always best to consult a professional for help.
