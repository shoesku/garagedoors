Garage Door Not Even? Here's How To Fix It In A Few Simple Steps

What's the Problem?

Have you ever noticed that your garage door isn't opening and closing as smoothly as it should? Does it seem like it's not level? If so, the problem is likely that your garage door isn't even. This means that it has become misaligned and is no longer able to open and close properly. Fortunately, this issue is relatively easy to fix and can be done in just a few simple steps.

Why Is It Important to Fix?

If your garage door isn't even, it can cause a number of issues. For starters, it may not open and close properly, which can be a safety issue. Additionally, it can cause problems with the operation of the door, such as causing it to open and close at an angle or not close all the way. Finally, it can cause excessive wear and tear on the door, leading to more frequent repairs and replacements.

What Causes It?

There are a variety of reasons why your garage door may become uneven. The most common cause is that the door may have been hit by a car or other object. Additionally, it could be due to the age of the door, as older doors may become less even over time due to wear and tear. Finally, it could be due to the installation of the door, as improper installation can lead to unevenness.

How to Fix It

The first step in fixing an uneven garage door is to check the rollers. The rollers are the pieces of metal that the door rides along when it opens and closes. If they are worn or damaged, they may need to be replaced. Additionally, check the hinges and make sure they are secure and in good condition. If they are worn or damaged, they should also be replaced.

Next, check the track. If the track is bent or damaged, it may need to be replaced. Additionally, make sure the track is level and aligned properly. If it is not, you'll need to adjust it until it is level.

Finally, check the springs. If the springs are worn or damaged, they will need to be replaced. Additionally, if the springs are too tight or too loose, they will need to be adjusted.

How to Avoid It

There are a few steps you can take to avoid your garage door becoming uneven. First, be sure to check the rollers and hinges regularly and replace them if they are worn or damaged. Additionally, make sure the track is straight and level and properly aligned. Finally, check the springs regularly and make sure they are not too tight or too loose.

Conclusion

If your garage door is not even, don't panic! It is a relatively easy fix and can be done in just a few simple steps. First, check the rollers, hinges, and track and make sure they are in good condition. Next, adjust the track until it is level and aligned properly. Finally, check the springs and make sure they are not too tight or too loose. By following these steps, you can easily fix an uneven garage door and ensure that it operates properly.
