Signs That Your Garage Door is Jerking

Have you ever heard your garage door jerk when it begins to open or close? If so, then you’re not alone. Garage door jerking is a common problem that many homeowners experience. But what causes it and how do you fix it? In this article, we’ll discuss the signs of garage door jerking and provide some tips to help you get your door running smoothly again.

One common sign of garage door jerking is an uneven opening or closing. If your garage door starts to move slowly and then suddenly jerks or accelerates, this could be a sign that there is a problem with your door’s balance. Another sign of jerking is if your door stops suddenly while it’s opening or closing. This could be a sign of a problem with the door’s sensors.

What Causes Garage Door Jerking?

There are several common causes of garage door jerking. One of the most common causes is an imbalance in the door’s weight. If the door is too heavy or too light, it can cause the door to jerk when it starts to open or close. Another common cause of jerking is a problem with the door’s sensors. If the sensors are not working properly, the door may not open or close smoothly.

Another cause of jerking is dirt and debris build up on the tracks. If the tracks become clogged with dirt and debris, it can cause the door to jerk when it starts to move. Finally, if the springs on your door become worn or broken, it can cause the door to jerk when it starts to move.

How to Fix Garage Door Jerking

Fortunately, there are several steps you can take to fix garage door jerking. The first step is to inspect the door and check for any signs of wear and tear. If you find any damaged parts, replace them as soon as possible. You should also check the door’s balance and make sure it is properly adjusted.

Next, you should inspect the door’s tracks for any signs of dirt and debris. If you find any, use a vacuum or brush to clean it out. Finally, check the door’s springs and make sure they are in good condition. If they are worn or broken, you should replace them as soon as possible.

Conclusion

If your garage door is jerking when starting to open, there are several steps you can take to fix the problem. Start by inspecting the door for any signs of wear and tear, and make sure the door’s balance is properly adjusted. Next, check the door’s tracks for any dirt and debris build up, and finally, inspect the door’s springs and make sure they are in good condition. With a little bit of inspection and maintenance, you can get your garage door running smoothly again in no time.
