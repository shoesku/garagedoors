What Do The Blinking Lights On Your Garage Door Opener Mean?

If you're like most people, you probably don't pay much attention to the blinking lights on your garage door opener. However, these lights can provide important information about the status of your garage door, and can help you troubleshoot any issues you might be having. In this article, we'll discuss the various blinking lights you might see on your garage door opener, and what they mean.

Green Blinking Lights

The most common blinking light on a garage door opener is a green light. This light indicates that the opener is ready to be used. If the green light is blinking, it means that the opener is in standby mode and is waiting for you to press the remote control button or the wall button inside the garage. If the green light is on and not blinking, it means that the opener is currently operating.

Red Blinking Lights

A red blinking light typically indicates that there is an issue with the opener, such as a malfunctioning sensor. If the red light is blinking, it is likely that the sensors are not properly aligned or that the safety beam has been interrupted. To fix this issue, check the alignment of the sensors, and make sure there are no obstructions blocking the safety beam. If these measures do not resolve the issue, then you may need to replace the sensors.

Orange Blinking Lights

An orange blinking light typically indicates that the opener is in the process of learning a new remote control. If you recently purchased a new remote control, then the orange light should be blinking while the opener learns the new remote control code. Once the opener has successfully learned the code, the light will stop blinking and remain in a steady state.

Slow or Fast Blinking Lights

The blinking speed of the lights on your garage door opener can also provide valuable information. If the light is blinking very quickly, then this typically indicates a short circuit or that there is an issue with the wiring in the opener. If the light is blinking slowly, then this usually means that the opener is not receiving enough power. To fix these issues, you may need to reset the circuit breaker, or contact an electrician for further assistance.

Conclusion

As you can see, the blinking lights on your garage door opener can provide important information about the status of the opener. If you're ever experiencing issues with your garage door opener, make sure to check the lights and see if they can provide any clues. If the blinking lights don't provide any clues, then you may need to contact a professional for further assistance.
