How To Uncoil A Garage Door Spring

Best Garage doors and references for house or residential with custom material and more design. We Share more picture design with HD quality images.

Garage door springs are an essential part of a functioning garage door. It’s what makes the door open and close with ease. If a garage door spring is damaged or broken, the door may not open or close as it should, or it may not open or close at all. Replacing a garage door spring isn’t a difficult process, but it can be dangerous. Uncoiling a garage door spring can be a tricky task, especially if you don’t know what you’re doing. Here are some tips for uncoiling a garage door spring safely and correctly.

Step 1: Wear Protective Gear

Before you begin uncoiling a garage door spring, you should wear proper protective gear. This includes safety glasses, a face mask, and heavy-duty gloves. Garage door springs can be dangerous because they are under a lot of tension and can snap or fly back at you when they’re released. Wearing protective gear will help keep you safe in the event that something goes wrong.

Step 2: Unhook the Spring

Once you’ve got your protective gear on, it’s time to start the uncoiling process. The first step is to unhook the spring from the garage door. It’s important to be careful here, as the tension from the spring can cause it to come loose suddenly. Use a pair of pliers to unhook the spring from the garage door. Once it’s unhooked, you can move on to the next step.

Step 3: Remove the Spring Cover

Next, you’ll need to remove the spring cover. This is the metal cover that is located on the side of the garage door. You’ll need to use a wrench to unscrew the cover and remove it. Once the cover is off, you’ll be able to access the spring. Be careful here, as the spring may be under a lot of tension, so be prepared for it to come loose suddenly.

Step 4: Uncoil the Spring

Now that the spring is exposed, you can start to uncoil it. Be sure to wear your protective gear and keep your hands away from the spring. Use a pair of pliers to carefully uncoil the spring. Start at the top of the spring and work your way down until it’s completely uncoiled. You may need to use a screwdriver to gently pry the spring open. Be sure to use caution here, as the spring may be under a lot of tension.

Step 5: Measure the Spring

Once the spring is uncoiled, you’ll need to measure it. Measure the length and diameter of the spring and make sure to jot down the measurements. This will help you determine the right size spring for your garage door. If you don’t have the correct size spring, it won’t work correctly and could be dangerous.

Step 6: Re-Coil the Spring

Once you’ve measured the spring, it’s time to re-coil it. This is the same process as uncoiling the spring, except you’ll be doing it in reverse. Start at the bottom of the spring and carefully wind it up. Be sure to use caution here, as the spring may be under a lot of tension. Once the spring is re-coiled, you can move on to the next step.

Step 7: Re-Attach the Spring

Now that the spring is re-coiled, you can re-attach it to the garage door. Use a pair of pliers to re-attach the spring to the door. Make sure the spring is securely attached so that it won’t come loose when the door is opened or closed. Once the spring is re-attached, you can move on to the next step.

Step 8: Test the Spring

The next step is to test the spring to make sure it’s working properly. Open and close the garage door a few times to make sure the spring is working correctly. If the door opens and closes smoothly, then the spring is working properly. If the door is jerky or doesn’t open or close all the way, then the spring may need to be adjusted or replaced.

Step 9: Replace the Spring Cover

Once you’ve tested the spring and it’s working properly, you can replace the spring cover. Use a wrench to screw the cover back on the garage door. Be sure the cover is securely attached so that the spring won’t come loose when the door is opened or closed.

Step 10: Enjoy the Results

Finally, you’re done! You’ve successfully uncoiled and re-attached the garage door spring. Now you can enjoy the results of your hard work. You’ve just saved yourself a lot of money by doing the job yourself! Congratulations!
