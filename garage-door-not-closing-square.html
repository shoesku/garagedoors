How To Fix A Garage Door Not Closing Square

Best Garage doors and references for house or residential with custom material and more design. We Share more picture design with HD quality images.

Introduction

Having a garage door that doesn't close square can be a real source of frustration. You might find it difficult to close and open, and it can even be a safety hazard if it isn't secure. If your garage door isn't closing square, it's a good idea to find out why and fix it. Read on to find out the most common causes of a garage door not closing square, and what you can do to fix it.

Check The Door Sensors

The first thing you should do when you find that your garage door isn't closing square is to check the door sensors. The door sensors are responsible for detecting when something is blocking the door from closing. If they are not working properly, then the door may not close properly. To check the door sensors, open the door and then look at the two sensors on either side. If they are not aligned correctly, or if there is something blocking them, then the door may not be able to detect when something is blocking it.

Check The Door Track

The next thing you should check is the door track. If the door track is bent or misaligned, then the door may not be able to close properly. To check the door track, open the door and look at the track that the door runs on. If you see any bends or misalignments, then you will need to have it fixed. Another thing to check is the rollers, as they may be worn or damaged, which could also cause the door to not close properly.

Check The Springs

The springs are responsible for raising and lowering the door, so if they are not functioning properly, then the door may not close square. To check the springs, open the door and look at the springs on the sides of the door. If the springs are worn or damaged, then you will need to have them replaced. If the springs are in good condition, then you can adjust them to get the door to close properly.

Check The Door Balance

The door balance is another important factor when it comes to getting the door to close square. If the door is not balanced properly, then it may not close properly. To check the door balance, open the door and lift it up. If the door is not balanced, then you will need to adjust the springs or replace the springs to get the door to balance properly.

Check The Opener

The opener is responsible for controlling the door when it is opened or closed, so if it is not working properly, then the door may not close square. To check the opener, open the door and press the open button. If the opener is not working properly, then you will need to have it repaired or replaced.

Check The Weather Stripping

Weather stripping is important for keeping the door sealed from the outside elements. If the weather stripping is worn or damaged, then the door may not close properly. To check the weather stripping, open the door and look at the weather stripping around the edges. If it is worn or damaged, then you will need to have it replaced.

Check The Hinges

The hinges are responsible for connecting the door to the track, so if they are worn or damaged, then the door may not close properly. To check the hinges, open the door and look at the hinges on the sides of the door. If they are worn or damaged, then you will need to have them replaced.

Conclusion

As you can see, there are a few different things that can cause a garage door not closing square. If you find that your garage door isn't closing properly, then it's a good idea to check the door sensors, the door track, the springs, the door balance, the opener, the weather stripping, and the hinges. If any of these components are worn or damaged, then you will need to have them repaired or replaced in order to get the door to close properly.
