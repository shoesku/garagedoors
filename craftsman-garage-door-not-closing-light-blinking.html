Troubleshooting A Craftsman Garage Door Not Closing

It’s always unnerving when a garage door won’t close – especially if you’re leaving for work or a family vacation. If you own a Craftsman garage door, there are a few things you can check to find out why it isn’t working properly. Before calling a professional garage door repair service, try the following troubleshooting steps to repair the issue yourself.

Check the Safety Reversal System

Your Craftsman garage door should be equipped with an infrared safety system that immediately reverses the door when it senses an obstruction on the ground. If the door doesn’t close, it may be because there is something in the way. Check the garage door track for any debris, toys, tools, or other items that may be blocking the door.

If the track is free of anything blocking the door, it may be caused by a defective safety reversal system. This is a common problem with garage doors and can be fixed with a few simple steps. First, locate the safety sensors near the bottom of the frame, which are connected to the electric opener. Make sure the sensors are connected on both sides of the door, and that there isn’t any debris in the way. If everything looks fine, then you can check the wiring to make sure the sensors are getting power.

Check the Photo Eyes

The photo eyes are the two infrared sensors located at the bottom of the frame on either side of the door. They send an infrared beam across the doorway and when the beam is broken, the door reverses. If the door is not closing, the photo eyes may be misaligned or dirty. To check the alignment, place a piece of paper in the path of the beam and make sure the door doesn’t close. If it does, then the photo eyes are misaligned and need to be adjusted.

If the alignment is correct, then the photo eyes may be dirty or blocked. Clean them with a soft cloth or a damp rag. If that doesn’t work, try replacing the photo eyes. You can purchase replacement photo eyes from a local home improvement store or online.

Check the Limit Settings

The limit settings tell the motor when to stop moving the door. The settings can become misadjusted over time, which can cause the door to either open too far or not close all the way. The limit settings are usually located on the motor and can be adjusted with a screwdriver.

Check the Motor

If the motors seem to be working properly, then you may have a problem with the battery. If the battery is dead or dying, it won’t be able to provide enough power to the motor to operate the door. Replace the battery with a new one and see if that solves the problem. If not, then the motor may need to be replaced.

Check the Remote Control

If you’re using a remote control to open and close the door, the problem may be with the remote itself. Make sure the batteries are fresh and that you’re pressing the right button. If the remote is working properly, make sure there are no obstructions blocking the signal.

Check the Lock

If the door won’t close, make sure the lock is not engaged. If the door has a manual lock, make sure it is not engaged. If the door has an automatic lock, make sure the power is on and that the lock is not jammed.

Check the Rollers

If the door seems to be stuck, then it may be due to a problem with the rollers. Rollers can become worn over time, which can cause them to jam. If the rollers are jammed, replace them with new ones.

Call a Professional

If none of these troubleshooting steps have worked, then it may be time to call a professional garage door repair service. They have the tools and experience necessary to quickly diagnose and repair any problems you may be having with your Craftsman garage door not closing.
