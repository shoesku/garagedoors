Troubleshooting A Garage Door Motor That Keeps Clicking

It can be really annoying when your garage door motor keeps clicking and it won't open. You know that you need to get it fixed, but you might not know where to start. Don't worry, because this guide will help you understand what is causing the clicking sound and how to fix it.

What Causes A Garage Door Motor To Click?

There are several possible causes for a garage door motor to start clicking. It could be due to a malfunctioning door opener, a faulty circuit board, a worn-out motor, or even a bad connection between the door and the motor. Depending on the issue, you may need to replace certain parts, or you may just need to make some minor adjustments.

How To Troubleshoot A Garage Door Motor That Keeps Clicking

The first step in troubleshooting a garage door motor that keeps clicking is to check the power source. Make sure that the power is turned on and that the outlet is not blocked. If the power is on, then you can move on to inspect the motor itself. Check the wiring for any loose connections or signs of wear and tear. If there are any visible problems, then you may need to replace the motor.

Check The Circuit Board

If the motor is in good condition, then you should check the circuit board. This is the part that sends signals to the motor to open and close the door. If the circuit board is malfunctioning, then it could be sending incorrect signals or not sending any at all. You can test the circuit board with a multimeter to see if it is working properly. If it is not, then you will need to replace it.

Check The Remote Control

If the motor is still clicking, then you should check the remote control. If it is not working properly, then it could be sending the wrong signal to the motor. Check the batteries in the remote and make sure that they are not dead. If they are, then replace them with fresh batteries. You can also try resetting the remote control to see if that fixes the issue.

Check The Door Sensors

The next step is to check the door sensors. These sensors are responsible for detecting any objects that may be blocking the door's path. If there is an object in the way, then the motor will not be able to open the door and will start clicking. Make sure that the sensors are free of any obstructions and that they are working properly.

Check The Lubrication

The last step is to check the lubrication of the motor. If the motor is not properly lubricated, then it can cause it to click. Make sure to use a lubricant that is specifically designed for garage door motors. Apply the lubricant to the motor and see if that solves the problem.

Conclusion

If your garage door motor keeps clicking and it won't open, then it is important to troubleshoot the issue. Check the power source, the motor, the circuit board, the remote control, the door sensors, and the lubrication. Once you have identified the problem, you can make the necessary repairs or adjustments to get your garage door working again.
