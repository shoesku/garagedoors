How To Troubleshoot A Liftmaster Garage Door Not Closing With Flashing Light

When your Liftmaster garage door opener is not closing with a flashing light, it can be frustrating. But don’t worry – troubleshooting these issues can be quick and relatively simple. To begin, you’ll need to determine the source of the issue and then take the appropriate steps to resolve it. This guide will take you through the troubleshooting process so you can get your garage door back up and running.

Step 1: Check the Door Sensors and Wires

The first thing you should do is check the door sensors. Most Liftmaster garage doors have two sensors – one on either side of the door. They need to be aligned properly, and they need to be free of any obstructions. If either one of the sensors is blocked, then the door opener won’t close properly. Additionally, make sure the wires connecting the sensors to the door opener are secure and in good condition.

Step 2: Check the Wall Station and Remote

The wall station and remote are both vital components of the garage door opener. If either one is malfunctioning, then it can cause the light to flash. Check that the wall station is properly wired, and that the remote is in good condition. You may need to replace the battery in the remote if it’s not working properly.

Step 3: Check the Drive Belt and Chain

If the door is still not closing properly, then you should check the drive belt and chain. The drive belt is a rubber belt that connects the motor to the door opener. Over time, it can become worn or stretched. If it’s damaged, then it can cause the door to not close properly. If the drive belt is fine, then you should check the chain. It should be free of any knots or kinks.

Step 4: Check the Motor

If all other components are in working order, then the issue may be with the motor. You can test the motor by removing the cover and listening for any strange noises. If you hear any loud noises or grinding, then it’s likely that the motor is damaged and needs to be replaced.

Step 5: Test the Door

Once you’ve completed all of the above steps, it’s time to test the door. Open the door manually and then close it. If the door closes properly, then the problem is resolved. If not, then you’ll need to perform a few more tests to determine the source of the issue.

Step 6: Adjust the Force Settings

The force settings on your Liftmaster garage door opener can be adjusted. To adjust the force settings, locate the force adjustment knob or lever. Turn the knob or lever clockwise to increase the force, or counterclockwise to decrease the force. Test the door again after adjusting the force settings.

Step 7: Check the Limit Settings

The limit settings determine when the door will stop opening and closing. To adjust the limit settings, locate the limit switch. Turn the knob or lever clockwise to increase the limit, or counterclockwise to decrease the limit. Test the door again after adjusting the limit settings.

Step 8: Call A Professional

If the door still isn’t closing properly after adjusting the force and limit settings, then it’s time to call a professional. A professional can inspect the door and identify any underlying issues. They can also help you with installation if needed. If you’re having trouble finding a professional in your area, then you can use a service like Liftmaster Pros to help you find an experienced technician.

Conclusion

If your Liftmaster garage door opener is not closing with a flashing light, then don’t panic. Troubleshooting these issues can be relatively simple if you know what to look for. Start by checking the door sensors and wires, and then move on to the wall station and remote. After that, check the drive belt and chain and test the motor. Finally, adjust the force and limit settings and test the door. If the issue persists, then it’s time to call a professional for help.
