Replacing Your Genie Garage Door Remote Easily

Introduction

Having a garage door that is automated is a great convenience. It's easy to open and close the door from the comfort of your car, and it’s also a security feature. At some point, though, you may find that you need to replace the remote for your Genie garage door opener. Doing this is a fairly straightforward process, and you can have it done quickly and easily with the right tools and know-how.

Things You'll Need

To replace your Genie garage door remote, you will need a few items. First, you’ll need the remote itself. You can purchase a compatible remote either online or at a local hardware store. You’ll also need a ladder, as you will need to climb up to the Genie garage door opener motor to program the remote. Lastly, you’ll need a screwdriver to remove a few screws from the Genie garage door opener.

Programming the Remote

Once you have all the items you need, it’s time to program the remote. Begin by removing the screws from the Genie garage door opener motor with a screwdriver. Once the screws are removed, you should be able to open the motor and access the circuit board. Look for the learn button, which is usually red, and press it. This will start the programming process. Next, you will need to climb up the ladder and press the button on the new remote. This will tell the Genie garage door opener that a new remote is being added. If everything is done correctly, the light on the Genie garage door opener will flash to indicate that the remote has been programmed correctly.

Testing the Remote

Once you have programmed the remote, it’s time to test it out. Stand near the Genie garage door opener motor and press the button on the new remote. You should hear the motor running and the Genie garage door should open. If it opens, the programming process was successful. If not, you may need to repeat the process and make sure the remote is compatible with your Genie garage door opener.

Replacing the Batteries

Your new remote should come with batteries, but it’s always a good idea to replace them as soon as possible. To do this, remove the cover from the remote and locate the battery compartment. Remove the old batteries and insert the new ones, making sure they are installed correctly. Once the batteries have been replaced, put the cover back on the remote and test it out.

Troubleshooting

If you are having trouble programming the remote, there are a few things you can do. First, make sure the remote you purchased is compatible with your Genie garage door opener. If you are unsure, you can consult the manual for your Genie garage door opener or contact the company for assistance. If the remote is compatible, you may need to try the programming process again.

Conclusion

Replacing a Genie garage door remote is a fairly straightforward process. With the right tools and know-how, you should be able to have it done in no time. Be sure to test the remote after you replace the batteries and after you have programmed it to make sure it is working correctly. If you have any problems, consult the manual for your Genie garage door opener or contact the company for assistance.
