What Causes My Garage Door to Close and Open Again?

You may have noticed that your garage door closes and then reopens shortly after. This is a common issue experienced by many homeowners and can be caused by a variety of things. It is important to figure out what is causing the issue so you can fix it and prevent it from happening again. In this article, we will discuss the most common causes of a garage door that closes and then reopens.

Faulty Safety Sensor

One of the most common causes of a garage door closing and then reopening is a faulty safety sensor. Your garage door has two safety sensors located at the bottom of the door. These sensors are designed to detect any objects that may be in the path of the door. If the sensors detect an object, the door will stop and then reverse. This is a safety feature to prevent any damage or injury that may occur from the door closing.

Broken Garage Door Opener

Another common cause of a garage door closing and then reopening is a broken garage door opener. Your garage door opener is responsible for controlling the door’s movement. If the opener is not working properly, it can cause the door to close and then reopen. This can be caused by a variety of things, including a faulty motor, bad wiring, or a broken circuit board.

Dirty or Misaligned Sensors

If your safety sensors are dirty or misaligned, it can cause the door to close and then reopen. The sensors need to be clean and in the proper position for the door to function properly. If the sensors are dirty, it can interfere with their ability to detect objects in the path of the door. If the sensors are misaligned, it can cause the door to think that something is in the way when there is not.

Improperly Adjusted Force and Limit Settings

The force and limit settings of your garage door can also cause the door to close and then reopen. These settings control how much power is used to open and close the door. If the force and limit settings are too low, it can cause the door to close and then reopen. It is important to make sure that these settings are properly adjusted to ensure that the door operates correctly.

Worn Out Springs

Worn out springs can also cause your garage door to close and then reopen. Springs are responsible for providing the necessary tension to open and close the door. If the springs are worn out, it can cause the door to close and then reopen. This can also be caused by springs that are too weak or too strong.

Faulty Circuit Board

The circuit board of your garage door opener can also cause the door to close and then reopen. This can be caused by a faulty circuit board, bad wiring, or a broken motor. It is important to have the circuit board checked by a professional to ensure that it is working properly.

How To Fix My Garage Door?

If you are experiencing a garage door that closes and then reopens, there are a few things you can do to fix the issue. First, check the safety sensors to make sure they are clean and working properly. If they are dirty or misaligned, you can clean them or adjust them so they are in the proper position. You can also check the force and limit settings to make sure they are properly adjusted. Finally, you can check the springs to make sure they are not worn out or too weak or too strong. If any of these issues are present, you should call a professional to come and make the necessary repairs.

Conclusion

If your garage door is closing and then reopening, it can be a frustrating experience. Thankfully, there are a few things you can do to fix the issue. First, check the safety sensors to make sure they are clean and working properly. You can also check the force and limit settings to make sure they are properly adjusted. Finally, you can check the springs to make sure they are not worn out or too weak or too strong. If any of these issues are present, you should call a professional to come and make the necessary repairs.
