Everything You Need To Know About Garage Door Track Specifications

A garage door is an essential part of your home. It provides security, it helps regulate the temperature inside, and it's an important part of your overall curb appeal. But before you can begin to enjoy the benefits of your new garage door, you need to make sure that you have everything you need to install it correctly. This includes understanding garage door track specifications.

Garage door tracks are the metal rails that guide the movement of the door along its frame. These tracks are an integral part of the door’s operation, so it’s important that you understand the different types of tracks and the specifications associated with them. Knowing what to look for can help you choose the right track for your garage door.

Types of Garage Door Tracks

The two main types of garage door tracks are curved and straight. Curved tracks are typically used for heavier and larger doors. This type of track is designed to provide added support for the door, making it more stable and secure.

Straight tracks are used for lighter and smaller doors. This type of track is simpler and less expensive than curved track, but it is not as durable and secure.

Garage Door Track Specifications

When selecting a track, there are a few key specifications that you should be aware of. These include the track length, width, and gauge. The length and width of the track should match the size of your garage door, while the gauge will determine the strength and durability of the track.

Track length is measured in feet and should be slightly longer than the width of your garage door. Track width is measured in inches and should match the width of your door. Track gauge is measured in millimeters and will determine the track’s strength and durability. The higher the gauge, the stronger the track.

Additional Considerations

In addition to the track length, width, and gauge, there are a few other factors to consider when choosing a track. These include the door weight, environment, and type of opener. It is important to ensure that the track is rated for the weight of your door, as well as the environment in which it will be installed. Finally, make sure that the track is compatible with the type of opener you are using.

Choosing the right track for your garage door is an important part of the installation process. Understanding the different types of tracks and the specifications associated with them will help you make the right choice. Knowing what to look for can help you ensure that your garage door operates safely and securely.
