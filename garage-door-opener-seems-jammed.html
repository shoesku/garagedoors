Troubleshooting When Your Garage Door Opener Seems Jammed

Introduction

Garage doors are extremely useful. They provide an extra layer of security to your home, and they keep your car safe from the elements. However, when your garage door opener seems jammed, it can be incredibly frustrating. Not only does it make it difficult to open and close the door, but it can also be a safety hazard. Fortunately, there are some simple steps you can take to troubleshoot and fix the problem.

Inspect the Tracks

The first thing you should do is inspect the tracks. If the tracks are bent, cracked, or otherwise damaged, this can cause the door to jam. You may be able to fix the problem yourself by straightening or replacing the tracks. However, if you're not sure how to do this, it's best to call a professional.

Check the Springs

The next step is to check the springs. Garage door springs are under a lot of tension, and if one of them breaks, it can cause the door to jam. If you suspect a broken spring, it's best to call a professional to repair or replace it.

Check the Motors

If the tracks and springs appear to be in good condition, the next step is to check the motors. The motor is responsible for lifting and lowering the door, and if it's not functioning correctly, it can cause the door to jam. If you suspect a problem with the motor, it's best to call a professional for repair or replacement.

Inspect the Rollers

The rollers are responsible for allowing the door to move smoothly along the tracks. If the rollers are worn out or damaged, this can cause the door to jam. You may be able to replace the rollers yourself, but it's best to call a professional if you're not sure how to do it.

Check the Lubrication

The next step is to check the lubrication. The moving parts of the garage door need to be properly lubricated in order to function correctly. If the lubricant has worn out or dried up, this can cause the door to jam. You can buy lubricant at any hardware store, and it's easy to apply.

Check the Balance

The last step is to check the balance. If the door is unbalanced, this can cause the motor to work harder than it should, which can cause the door to jam. To check the balance, you'll need to open the door manually and observe how it moves. If it moves unevenly, you'll need to adjust the tension.

Conclusion

If your garage door opener seems jammed, there are several possible causes. By following the steps outlined above, you should be able to identify and fix the problem. However, if you're not sure how to do this, it's best to call a professional for help.
