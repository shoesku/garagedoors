Opening and Closing an Electric Garage Door

In the modern world, electric garage doors are a convenient and secure way to access your garage. However, if it isn't working correctly, it can be a hassle. Before you call a professional, there are a few things you can try to fix your electric garage door that isn't closing. In this article, we'll walk you through some of the steps you can take to troubleshoot your electric garage door.

Check the Power Source

The first thing you should do is check the power source. If the power source is not working, the garage door won't be able to close. Make sure the power outlet is properly connected to the wall and that the power switch is turned on. If the power source is working, you will need to check the other components of the garage door to make sure it is functioning correctly.

Check the Safety Sensors

The safety sensors are one of the most important components of an electric garage door. The safety sensors are located at the bottom of the door and are designed to detect any obstructions in the doorway. If the safety sensors are not aligned correctly, the door will not close. To check the alignment of the safety sensors, place a piece of cardboard in the path of the sensors. If the door does not stop when the cardboard is in the path of the sensors, you will need to adjust the alignment.

Inspect the Rollers

Next, you should inspect the rollers. The rollers are located at the bottom of the door and are responsible for moving the door up and down. If the rollers are worn out or damaged, the door may not be able to close. To check the condition of the rollers, take a look at them to see if they are cracked or worn. If they are, you will need to replace them.

Check the Tracks

The tracks are responsible for guiding the door up and down. If the tracks are bent or damaged, the door may not be able to close. To check the tracks, take a look at them to see if they are bent or damaged. If they are, you will need to repair or replace them.

Check the Springs

The springs are responsible for providing tension to the door. If the springs are worn out or damaged, the door may not be able to close. To check the springs, take a look at them to see if they are cracked or worn. If they are, you will need to replace them.

Check the Motors

The motors are responsible for opening and closing the door. If the motors are not working correctly, the door may not be able to close. To check the motors, take a look at them to see if they are running correctly. If they are not, you will need to replace them.

Check the Remote Control

If you are using a remote control to open and close your garage door, you should make sure it is working correctly. If the remote control is not working, the door may not be able to close. To check the remote control, make sure the batteries are working and that the signal is strong. If the remote control is not working, you will need to replace it.

Call a Professional

If you have tried all of the above steps and the door still won't close, it may be time to call a professional. A professional can inspect all of the components of the garage door and make sure they are working correctly. They can also help you find the source of the problem and make any necessary repairs. Troubleshooting your electric garage door that isn't closing can be a hassle. However, if you follow the steps outlined above, you should be able to find the source of the problem and make any necessary repairs. If you are still having trouble, it may be time to call a professional for assistance.
