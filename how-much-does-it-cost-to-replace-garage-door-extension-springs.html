Replacing Garage Door Extension Springs – A Costly Necessity

Best Garage doors and references for house or residential with custom material and more design. We Share more picture design with HD quality images.

Garage doors are essential components of a home, providing security and protection from the elements. As such, it is always important to ensure that they are in good working order. If a malfunction or breakdown occurs, it is important to act quickly to repair or replace the damaged parts. One of the most common issues that homeowners face is the need to replace their garage door extension springs. This article will discuss the cost of replacing these springs and the importance of doing so promptly.

What are Garage Door Extension Springs?

Garage door extension springs are an integral part of the door’s operation. They are used to lift the door when it is in the open position and to provide counterbalance when the door is closed. Extension springs are typically located on either side of the door and are connected to the garage door track. They are usually made of metal and may be painted to match the color of the door.

Why is it Necessary to Replace Extension Springs?

Extension springs have a limited lifespan and will eventually wear out. As they age, the springs may become weak, causing the door to be unbalanced and difficult to open and close. In addition, if the springs become damaged or broken, the door may be at risk of falling, posing a safety hazard.

How Much Does it Cost to Replace Extension Springs?

The cost of replacing garage door extension springs can vary depending on the type and size of the springs, as well as the door’s make and model. Generally, the cost of replacing two springs can range from $150 to $300. It is important to note that replacing both springs is usually necessary to ensure the door operates properly and safely.

Who Can Replace Extension Springs?

Replacing garage door extension springs is a task that should only be performed by a professional. It is not advisable to attempt to replace the springs yourself as they are under high tension and can cause serious injury if handled incorrectly. It is best to contact a reputable garage door repair service to ensure that the job is done correctly and safely.

What Else Should I Know?

It is important to note that replacing garage door extension springs is not a one-time job. Over time, the springs may need to be replaced again due to normal wear and tear. As such, it is important to inspect the springs regularly and replace them when necessary to ensure the door is operating properly and safely.

Conclusion

Replacing garage door extension springs can be a costly but necessary job. It is important to ensure the springs are replaced promptly to avoid any potential safety issues. The cost of replacing the springs can vary depending on the type and size of the springs, as well as the door’s make and model. It is best to contact a professional to ensure the job is done correctly and safely.
