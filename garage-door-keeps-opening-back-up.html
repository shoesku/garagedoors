How To Fix A Garage Door That Keeps Opening Back Up

Best Garage doors and references for house or residential with custom material and more design. We Share more picture design with HD quality images.

Are you having trouble with your garage door, as it keeps opening back up after you close it? A malfunctioning garage door can be a huge inconvenience, and can even be dangerous if it doesn't close when you want it to. Don't worry - there are a few steps you can take to fix this issue and get your garage door working properly again.

Step 1: Make Sure That the Safety Sensor is Clean and Working Properly

The safety sensor is the part of your garage door opener system that keeps it from closing when something is in its path. If the safety sensor is not working properly, your garage door will not close, even if you press the wall switch or remote control. To check if the safety sensor is working, look for the two small lights on either side of the garage door. They should be illuminated when the door is closed. If they are not illuminated, the safety sensor is not working properly and needs to be cleaned or adjusted.

Step 2: Tighten the Garage Door Springs

If the safety sensor is working properly but the garage door still won't stay closed, you may need to tighten the springs. Garage door springs are what hold the door up when it is open, and when they become loose, the door won't stay closed. To tighten the springs, you will need to remove the old springs and install new ones. This is a very dangerous job and should only be done by a professional.

Step 3: Adjust the Track and Rollers of the Garage Door

If the springs have been tightened and the safety sensor is working properly, but the garage door still won't stay closed, you may need to adjust the track and rollers. Over time, the track and rollers can become loose due to wear and tear, and this can cause the garage door to open back up after you close it. To adjust the track and rollers, you will need to loosen the bolts that hold them in place and then adjust them so that they are properly aligned.

Step 4: Replace the Garage Door Opener Remote Control

If all of the above steps have been taken and the garage door still will not stay closed, you may need to replace the remote control. The remote control is the part of the garage door opener system that sends a signal to the motor to open or close the door. If the remote control is not working properly, it can cause the door to open back up after you close it. Replacing the remote control is relatively easy, and can be done with just a few tools.

Step 5: Call a Professional

If none of the above steps have been successful in fixing your garage door, you may need to call a professional. A professional garage door technician will be able to diagnose the problem and provide a solution. They will also be able to provide advice on how to prevent the problem from happening again in the future.

Conclusion

If your garage door keeps opening back up after you close it, don't panic - there are a few steps you can take to fix the problem. Start by checking the safety sensor and making sure it is working properly, then tighten the springs if necessary. If that doesn't work, you may need to adjust the track and rollers, replace the remote control, or call a professional. With a little bit of patience and some basic troubleshooting, you should be able to get your garage door working properly again in no time.
