Types of Garage Door Springs

There are two main types of garage door springs, torsion springs and extension springs. Torsion springs are the most common type and are used on single and double-wide garage doors. They are attached to two drums which are connected to the door. As the door is opened and closed, the drums rotate, creating tension in the spring and allowing it to open and close the door more smoothly. Extension springs are typically used on heavier, wider doors and are mounted on the sides of the door. They are connected to a pulley system, allowing them to stretch and retract as the door is opened and closed. Both types of springs are available in a variety of sizes, shapes, and designs to suit any garage door.
