Why Should You Remove Rust From Your Garage Door?

Rust can be an eyesore and detract from the overall look of your home exterior, but there are more important reasons to remove rust from your garage door. Rust can be a sign that your garage door is deteriorating, weakening the structural integrity of the door. If left untreated, rust can cause the structure of your garage door to weaken and eventually fail. This can cause injury to you and your family if the door should fail while in use.

Rust can also be an indicator of a build-up in your garage door’s tracks and hardware. If your garage door has been exposed to moisture, rust can quickly form and start to corrode the track, rollers, and other hardware components. This corrosion can cause the track to become misaligned or jammed, making it difficult to open or close your garage door.

How to Remove Rust From Your Garage Door

Step 1: Clean the Surface

The first step in removing rust from your garage door is to clean the affected area. Use a cloth and mild soap to wipe down the surface of the door, removing any dirt, debris, or cobwebs. This will help ensure that the cleaning products you use have direct contact with the rust and are not blocked by dirt.

Step 2: Use a Rust Remover

Once the surface is clean, you can apply a rust remover to the door. There are a variety of products available on the market, from sprays to gels, to tackle the problem. Be sure to read and follow the manufacturer’s directions for the best results. After the product has been applied, let it sit for the recommended amount of time before wiping off the residue.

Step 3: Use Steel Wool

For stubborn rust spots, use steel wool to remove the rust. Gently rub the steel wool over the rust spot until the rust is removed. Once the rust has been removed, use a cloth and mild soap to wipe away any residue.

Step 4: Use a Rust Inhibitor

Once the rust has been removed, use a rust inhibitor to help prevent the rust from returning. Rust inhibitors are available in spray form and can be applied directly to the garage door. Be sure to read and follow the manufacturer’s instructions for the best results.

Using Steam to Remove Rust

If the rust on your garage door is particularly stubborn, you can use steam to remove it. You can purchase a steam cleaner specifically designed for cleaning rust from metal surfaces. Be sure to read and follow the manufacturer’s instructions for the best results.

Steam cleaning is a great way to remove rust without using harsh chemicals. The steam will also help remove dirt and debris, leaving your garage door looking like new. After the steam cleaning is complete, use a rust inhibitor to help prevent the rust from returning.

Conclusion

Rust can be an unsightly nuisance and detract from the look of your home exterior, but it can also be an indication of a larger problem. If left untreated, rust can weaken the structure of your garage door and cause it to fail. It can also cause a build-up in your garage door’s track, making it difficult to open or close your garage door.

Removing rust from your garage door is important for the safety of you and your family. You can use a variety of products to remove rust, from sprays to gels. If the rust is particularly stubborn, you can use steam to remove it. Just be sure to use a rust inhibitor after the rust is removed to help prevent it from returning.
