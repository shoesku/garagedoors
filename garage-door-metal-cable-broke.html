What To Do When Your Garage Door Metal Cable Breaks

What is a Garage Door Metal Cable?

A garage door metal cable is a tension cable that is responsible for the movement of your garage door. It helps to keep the door open, close, and in place while it is in use, as well as when it is not. The cable is made up of several strands of metal wire that are tightly wound together. It is usually very strong and durable, and can last for many years with proper maintenance and care. Unfortunately, like any other part of your garage door, it can break after some time.

What Causes a Garage Door Metal Cable to Break?

A garage door metal cable can break for a variety of reasons. One of the most common causes is due to wear and tear over time. As the cables are constantly in motion, they can become worn down, resulting in them breaking. Another cause of a broken cable is due to incorrect installation, as the cable may be too tight or too loose when first installed. Finally, the cable can break due to rust and corrosion, which can occur if the cable is exposed to moisture or other elements.

What are the Signs of a Broken Garage Door Cable?

If your garage door metal cable has broken, there are a few tell-tale signs that you should look out for. One of the most common signs is a loud noise coming from the door when it is opened or closed. This noise may sound like a grinding or a snapping sound. Another sign is that the door will move more slowly than it normally does, or in some cases, it may not move at all. Finally, if you look at the cable itself, you will likely see that it is frayed or has snapped in two.

What Happens if a Garage Door Cable Breaks?

If your garage door metal cable has broken, it is important to take action immediately. Leaving a broken cable can be dangerous, as it can lead to the door coming off its tracks, or even crashing down. It can also cause damage to your car or other items in the garage. Thus, it is important to have the cable repaired or replaced as soon as possible.

How to Replace a Broken Garage Door Cable

Replacing a broken garage door cable is not a difficult task, but can be dangerous if done incorrectly. If you are not comfortable with the task, then it is best to hire a professional to do the job for you. However, if you are feeling brave, then here are the steps to replace the cable yourself. First, you will need to disconnect the power to the garage door opener. This can be done by flipping the circuit breaker. Then, you will need to carefully remove the broken cable from the pulley system. After this, you will need to measure and cut a new cable that is the same length as the old one. Finally, you will need to attach the new cable to the pulley system and reconnect the power to the garage door opener.

Tips for Extending the Life of Your Garage Door Cable

In order to keep your garage door metal cable from breaking, there are a few tips that you can follow. First, be sure to have your garage door serviced on a regular basis. This will ensure that the cables are in good condition and that they are not too tight or too loose. Additionally, you should make sure to lubricate the cables and pulley system regularly. This will help to reduce friction and wear, and will help to keep the cable from breaking. Finally, be sure to check the cable for signs of rust or corrosion, as this can lead to the cable breaking.

Conclusion

A broken garage door metal cable can be a major inconvenience, as well as a potential safety hazard. Fortunately, it is a relatively easy problem to fix, as long as you take the proper precautions. Be sure to disconnect the power to the garage door opener before attempting to replace the cable, and be sure to measure and cut the new cable correctly. Additionally, make sure to have your garage door serviced regularly, and to lubricate the cables and pulley system. Following these tips can help to extend the life of your garage door metal cable and keep it from breaking.
