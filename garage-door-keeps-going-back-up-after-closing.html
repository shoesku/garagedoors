How To Fix A Garage Door That Keeps Going Back Up After Closing

If you're having trouble with your garage door going back up after closing, then you may be wondering what the problem is. You may be concerned that the problem is serious, but the good news is that it is usually a fairly easy repair. In this article, we'll discuss the issues that may be causing your garage door to keep going back up after closing, and what you can do to fix it.

The Cause of the Problem

The most common cause of a garage door going back up after closing is an issue with the sensors. The sensors are located at the bottom of the door and they send a signal to the garage door opener to either open or close the door. If there is something blocking the sensors, such as dirt, debris, or a pet, then the door will not be able to properly close. This is usually the most common cause of the problem.

Testing the Sensors

If you think that the sensors are the cause of the problem, then the first thing you should do is to test them. To do this, you will need to locate the sensors and make sure that there is nothing blocking them. If there is, then you should remove it and then test the door again. If the sensors are still not working, then you may need to replace them.

Cleaning the Sensors

If the sensors are still not working, then you may need to clean them. To do this, you will need to use a soft cloth and some warm water. You should be careful not to get the sensors too wet, as this can damage them. Once you have cleaned the sensors, then you should try to open and close the door again to see if it is still going back up after closing.

Lubricating the Tracks

Another possible cause of your garage door going back up after closing is a problem with the tracks. If the tracks are not lubricated properly, then this could cause the door to not close properly. To fix this, you should use a lubricant specifically designed for garage doors. You should apply the lubricant to the tracks and then run the door several times to make sure that it is working properly.

Replacing the Garage Door Opener

If the other steps have not fixed the problem, then you may need to replace the garage door opener. This is a more complicated repair, but it is something that you can do yourself if you are confident that you can do it. You should make sure to purchase the correct opener for your door, as some openers are not compatible with certain doors. You should also follow all the instructions that come with the opener carefully to ensure that it is installed correctly.

Call in a Professional

If the problem persists after trying the steps above, then it is time to call in a professional. They will be able to diagnose the problem quickly and accurately, and they will also be able to tell you if the problem is serious or not. It is always best to have a professional take a look at your garage door, as this will ensure that the problem is fixed quickly and correctly.

Conclusion

A garage door going back up after closing is a common problem, but it is usually not a major issue. In most cases, the problem can be easily fixed by checking the sensors, cleaning them, and lubricating the tracks. If the problem persists after trying these steps, then it is time to call in a professional. No matter what the cause of the problem is, you should be able to get it fixed quickly and easily.
