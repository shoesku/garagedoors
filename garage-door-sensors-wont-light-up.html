Troubleshooting When Your Garage Door Sensors Won't Light Up

Introduction

If your garage door isn't opening and closing, it could be that the sensors aren't working. This is a common issue that homeowners face, but luckily, it's usually easy to fix. In this article, we'll discuss what could be causing your garage door sensors to not light up and how you can go about troubleshooting and fixing the issue so you can get your garage door working again.

What Causes Garage Door Sensors to Not Light Up?

There are a few potential causes for the sensors to not light up. One of the most common causes is that the sensor lenses are dirty or covered in debris, preventing them from detecting the infrared beam. Another potential cause is that the power isn't connected to the sensors, meaning that there is no electricity flowing to the sensors, preventing them from lighting up. Another possibility is that the sensors are misaligned and that the infrared beam isn't being detected by the sensors.

How to Troubleshoot and Fix the Problem

The first step is to check the power source. Make sure the power is connected to the sensors and that the outlet is working. If the power is connected and the outlet is working, then the next step is to check the sensors themselves. Make sure the lenses of the sensors are clean and free of any debris. If they are covered in dirt or debris, then use a soft cloth to clean them. If that doesn't work, then it's time to check the alignment of the sensors.

Checking the Alignment

To check the alignment of the sensors, you'll need to make sure that the infrared beam is connecting the two sensors. This can be done by manually opening and closing the garage door and looking for the infrared beam. If you don't see the beam, then the sensors are likely misaligned. To realign the sensors, you'll need to adjust the position of one or both of the sensors until the beam is connecting them.

Conclusion

If your garage door sensors aren't lighting up, then it could be due to a power issue, dirty lenses, or misalignment. To troubleshoot and fix the problem, first check the power source and make sure the sensors have power. Then check the lenses of the sensors and clean them if they are dirty. Finally, check the alignment of the sensors and adjust the position of one or both of the sensors until the infrared beam is connecting them. By following these steps, you should be able to get your garage door sensors working again in no time.
