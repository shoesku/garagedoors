What To Do When Your Liftmaster Garage Door Keeps Opening By Itself

Introduction

If you’re the proud owner of a Liftmaster garage door, you may have wondered why it’s been opening and closing by itself lately. You’re not alone—it’s a common problem for homeowners. Fortunately, it’s usually an easy fix. In this article, we’ll explain why your Liftmaster garage door keeps opening by itself, and how you can fix it.

Why Does My Liftmaster Garage Door Keep Opening By Itself?

There are several possible causes of your Liftmaster garage door opening and closing by itself. The most common cause is a malfunctioning safety sensor. This safety device is designed to prevent the door from closing on a person or object. When the sensors are not aligned correctly, they can cause the door to open and close when it detects a slight movement or vibration. Another common issue is a misaligned track or force setting. If the track is not aligned correctly, it can cause the door to open and close erratically.

How to Fix a Liftmaster Garage Door Opening By Itself

If you’ve determined that the safety sensors are the culprit, you can fix the problem quickly and easily. Start by checking the alignment of the sensors. They should be pointed directly at each other, and there should be no more than a 3-inch gap between them. If they are not aligned correctly, you can adjust them using the included screws. If the track is the cause of the problem, you’ll need to adjust the force setting. You can do this by turning the adjustment dial on the motor unit until the door opens and closes smoothly.

When to Call a Professional

If you’ve tried adjusting the sensors and force setting but the problem persists, it’s time to call a professional. A qualified technician will be able to diagnose and repair the issue quickly and efficiently. They can also provide advice on how to maintain your Liftmaster garage door and keep it operating smoothly. It’s always best to call a professional before attempting any repairs, as this can help prevent further damage and ensure that the job is done correctly.

Conclusion

If your Liftmaster garage door keeps opening and closing by itself, it’s usually an easy fix. The most common cause is a malfunctioning safety sensor or a misaligned track or force setting. You can try adjusting the sensors and force setting yourself, but if the problem persists, it’s best to call a professional. A qualified technician will be able to diagnose and repair the issue quickly and efficiently.
