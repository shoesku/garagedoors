Tips On Lubricating Your Garage Door

It’s always important to make sure your garage door is well maintained and lubricated. Without regular lubrication, the springs and other components of your garage door can start to wear out and will cause your garage door to malfunction. Properly lubricating your garage door can save you money in the long run by helping to reduce repair costs and extend the life of the door.

Why You Should Lubricate Your Garage Door

Lubrication is essential to reducing friction and wear on the components of your garage door. Without lubrication, the door can become noisy, difficult to open and close, and will require more energy to move. Lubrication can also protect the springs and other parts from rust and corrosion, which can cause further damage over time. Regular lubrication can also prevent your door from sticking or becoming sluggish, which can be dangerous if the door is not working properly.

What Parts of the Garage Door Need to be Lubricated?

When lubricating your garage door, it’s important to focus on the areas that need it the most. The main points of lubrication are the hinges, tracks, rollers, and springs. The hinges and tracks need to be lubricated with a light oil or lubricant, while the rollers and springs need to be lubricated with a heavy-duty lubricant. Be sure to avoid getting any lubricant on the cables or the springs as this can cause them to break.

How Often Should You Lubricate Your Garage Door?

The frequency of lubrication depends on the type of garage door you have. For those with wooden doors, lubrication should be done at least once a year. For those with metal doors, lubrication should be done every three months. It’s also important to check the door regularly for any signs of damage or wear and tear. If you notice any issues, it’s important to contact a professional for help.

What Lubricant Should You Use?

When selecting a lubricant for your garage door, there are a few things to keep in mind. It’s important to use an appropriate lubricant for the components of your garage door. For the hinges and tracks, you should use a light oil or lubricant. For the rollers and springs, you should use a heavy-duty lubricant. Be sure to avoid using any WD-40 or other petroleum-based lubricants as these can damage the components of your garage door.

How to Lubricate Your Garage Door

Before lubricating your garage door, it’s important to make sure it’s in good working order. Check for any signs of wear and tear and make sure the door is properly aligned. Once you’ve done that, you can begin the lubrication process. Start by lubricating the hinges and tracks with a light oil or lubricant. Then, lubricate the rollers and springs with a heavy-duty lubricant. Be sure to avoid getting any lubricant on the cables or the springs as this can cause them to break.

Conclusion

Lubricating your garage door is an important part of maintaining it and keeping it in good working order. It’s important to use the right type of lubricant for the components of your garage door and to avoid getting any lubricant on the cables or springs. Regular lubrication can help to reduce friction and wear, prevent the door from sticking or becoming sluggish, and protect the springs from rust and corrosion. By following these tips, you can help to extend the life of your garage door and save money in the long run.
