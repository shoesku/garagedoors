What to Look for When Buying Garage Door Cables

When shopping for garage door cables, it’s important to consider two main factors: the size of the cable and the type of cable. The size of the cable will determine the amount of weight the cable can safely handle, while the type of cable will determine the strength and durability of the cable. It’s important to choose the right size and type of cable for your garage door.

Size of Cable

The size of a garage door cable is measured in diameter and is typically measured in millimeters (mm). The most common sizes of cables used in residential garage doors are 8mm, 10mm, and 12mm. The size of the cable will determine the amount of weight the cable can safely handle. For example, a 8mm cable can usually handle up to 100kg of weight, while a 12mm cable can handle up to 200kg.

Type of Cable

The type of cable you choose will determine the strength and durability of the cable. The standard steel-reinforced cable is the most commonly used type of cable and is the least expensive. This type of cable is typically used in residential garage doors and is strong and durable enough to handle most loads. For heavier loads, such as commercial garage doors, you may want to consider a heavy-duty cable. These cables are thicker and stronger than standard cables and can handle up to 500kg of weight.

Steel-Reinforced Cables

The most common type of garage door cable is the steel-reinforced cable. These cables are made from a combination of steel and plastic and are designed to be strong and durable. They come in 8mm, 10mm, and 12mm sizes and can handle up to 200kg of weight. The standard steel-reinforced cables are the least expensive and are perfect for most residential applications.

Heavy-Duty Cables

For heavier loads, such as commercial garage doors, you may want to consider a heavy-duty cable. These cables are thicker and stronger than standard cables and can handle up to 500kg of weight. They come in 8mm, 10mm, and 12mm sizes and are designed specifically for commercial applications. The heavy-duty cables are more expensive than the standard steel-reinforced cables, but they provide added strength and durability.

Stainless Steel Cables

If you’re looking for a cable that is resistant to corrosion, you may want to consider a stainless steel cable. These cables are made from high-grade stainless steel and are designed to be strong and durable. They are more expensive than the standard steel-reinforced cables, but they provide added strength and corrosion resistance. Stainless steel cables are typically used in commercial applications.

Aluminum Cables

Aluminum cables are lightweight and strong and are often used in residential applications. They are more expensive than the standard steel-reinforced cables, but they provide added strength and corrosion resistance. Aluminum cables are typically used in residential applications where weight is a concern.
