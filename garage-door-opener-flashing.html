How To Fix A Garage Door Opener Flashing?

A garage door opener flashing indicates a problem with the electrical system that runs it. It could be something simple such as a broken belt or a more complex issue such as a loose connection. In any case, there are a few steps you can take to try and troubleshoot and fix the problem yourself, as long as you have the right tools and knowledge.

Step 1: Check the Batteries in the Remote

If the garage door opener is flashing, the first thing to check is the batteries in the remote. If the batteries are weak or dead, the remote will not be able to transmit the signal to the garage door opener. So, replace the batteries in the remote and check if the issue has been resolved. If the flashing persists, move on to the next step.

Step 2: Check the Wall Control

If the batteries in the remote are fine, the next step is to check the wall control. The wall control is the device located in the garage that allows you to open or close the garage door manually. Make sure the wall control is plugged in and that the wiring is connected correctly. If the connections are loose or disconnected, the wall control will not be able to communicate with the garage door opener correctly.

Step 3: Check the Photoelectric Sensor

The photoelectric sensor is an essential part of the safety system of the garage door opener. It is located near the bottom of the garage door and prevents the door from closing on a person or object. If the sensor is blocked or misaligned, it can cause the garage door opener to flash. Check the sensor to make sure it is not blocked or misaligned.

Step 4: Check the Belts and Chains

If all of the above steps have been checked and the issue still persists, the next step is to check the belts and chains in the garage door opener. The belts and chains are responsible for lifting and lowering the garage door. If they are loose or broken, the garage door opener will not be able to lift the door correctly, causing it to flash.

Step 5: Contact a Professional

If all the above steps have been checked and the issue still persists, it is best to contact a professional for help. A professional will be able to diagnose the issue and suggest the best course of action to fix it. It is also important to contact a professional if the garage door is not opening or closing properly, as this could be a sign of a more serious issue.

Conclusion

A garage door opener flashing is a sign of an issue with the electrical system that runs it. It could be something simple like a broken belt or a more complex issue such as a loose connection. In any case, there are a few steps you can take to try and troubleshoot and fix the problem yourself. If all of the above steps have been checked and the issue still persists, it is best to contact a professional for help.
