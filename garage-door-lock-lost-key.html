What To Do When You Lose Your Garage Door Lock Key

Best Garage doors and references for house or residential with custom material and more design. We Share more picture design with HD quality images.

Imagine this scenario: you’ve just come home from work, exhausted from a long day, only to find out that you’ve lost your garage door lock key. It’s a frustrating and all too common situation for many homeowners. Fortunately, there are several steps you can take to regain access to your garage. Here’s a closer look at what you should do if you’ve lost your garage door lock key.

Call a Professional Locksmith

The first step you should take if you’ve lost your garage door lock key is to call a professional locksmith. A locksmith can quickly assess the situation and determine the most effective way to gain entry to your garage. Depending on the security system of your garage door, they may be able to simply pick the lock, replace the lock with a new one, or even fabricate a new key for you.

Replace the Lock

If you’re unable to locate your lost garage door lock key, you may need to replace the lock altogether. Replacing the lock is the most secure option, as it ensures no one else can gain access to your garage. Before purchasing a new lock, double check to make sure the new lock is compatible with the existing garage door hardware. Once you’ve purchased a new lock, hire a professional locksmith to install it.

Check Around Your Home

Before calling a locksmith, it’s a good idea to check around your home for your lost garage door lock key. Start by looking in all the usual spots where you store keys, such as your coat pockets, handbags, and drawers. You may also want to check near the door itself, as it’s possible the key may have fallen off your keychain and is lying nearby. If you’ve recently had a houseguest, you should also check with them to see if they may have your key.

Check Your Spare Key

If you have a spare key for your garage door, now is the time to use it. Many homeowners keep a spare key in a secure location, such as with a trusted neighbor or family member. If you have a spare key, you’ll be able to regain access to your garage without having to call a locksmith.

Change the Coding System

If you have a keyless entry system for your garage door, you should consider changing the coding system. Keyless entry systems use a code that is entered into a keypad to open the door. If you’ve lost your garage door lock key, you may want to change the code to prevent someone else from using it to gain access to your garage.

Use a Credit Card

If you’ve lost your garage door lock key and can’t find a spare, you may be able to use a credit card to open the door. This trick only works with certain types of locks, so be sure to check the specifications of your lock before attempting this method. If it does work for your lock, use a credit card to wedge between the door and the frame to create enough space to open the door.

Drill Out the Lock

If all else fails, you may need to drill out the lock to gain entry to your garage. This should only be done as a last resort, as it can cause permanent damage to the lock. If you decide to go this route, be sure to hire a professional locksmith to do the job. They will have the necessary tools and expertise to do the job safely and efficiently.

Prevent Future Loss

Once you’ve regained access to your garage, take steps to prevent future loss of your garage door lock key. First, make sure you always store your keys in the same place. You may also want to consider investing in a keychain that makes it easier to keep track of your keys. Lastly, be sure to keep a spare key in a secure location, such as with a trusted neighbor or family member.

Conclusion

Losing your garage door lock key can be a frustrating experience, but with the right steps you can regain access to your garage quickly and safely. Start by checking around your home for your lost key and if that fails, consider calling a professional locksmith. You may also be able to use a credit card or drill out the lock, although these methods should only be used as a last resort. Finally, take steps to prevent future loss of your key.
