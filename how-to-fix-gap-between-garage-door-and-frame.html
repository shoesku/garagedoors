How To Fix The Gap Between Your Garage Door And Frame

Introduction

Having a gap between your garage door and frame can be a real nuisance. Not only does it let in cold air, but it can also let in pests and other unwanted visitors. Fortunately, there are a few simple steps you can take to fix the gap and make sure your garage stays secure. In this article, we’ll take a look at how to fix the gap between your garage door and frame so you can keep the cold air out and the pests away.

Step 1: Repair the Frame

The first step to fixing the gap between your garage door and frame is to repair the frame. If the frame is cracked or warped, it won’t be able to hold the door in place properly. To repair the frame, start by using a hammer and chisel to remove any loose pieces of wood. Once the loose pieces are removed, use wood glue to bond the pieces back together. Let the glue dry overnight, then sand the frame until it is smooth. Finally, use a sealant to protect the frame from moisture.

Step 2: Adjust the Rollers

Once the frame is repaired, it’s time to adjust the rollers. The rollers are what allow the door to move up and down. If they’re not adjusted properly, the door won’t move correctly, which can create a gap between the door and frame. To adjust the rollers, start by loosening the screws that hold the roller in place. Then, move the roller slightly up or down, depending on whether the gap is at the top or bottom of the door. Finally, tighten the screws to secure the roller in place.

Step 3: Adjust the Hinges

The next step is to adjust the hinges. The hinges are what hold the door in place and keep it from swinging open. If the hinges are out of alignment, the door won’t stay in place, which can create a gap between the door and frame. To adjust the hinges, start by loosening the screws that hold the hinges in place. Then, move the hinges slightly up or down, depending on whether the gap is at the top or bottom of the door. Finally, tighten the screws to secure the hinges in place.

Step 4: Add Weatherstripping

The last step is to add weatherstripping. Weatherstripping is a thin strip of material that helps keep out cold air. It’s easy to install and can be found at most hardware stores. To install the weatherstripping, start by cutting it to size. Then, peel the backing off the weatherstripping and press it firmly into place. Make sure the weatherstripping is securely attached, then use a sealant to keep it in place.

Conclusion

Fixing the gap between your garage door and frame doesn’t have to be complicated. With a few simple steps, you can make sure your garage stays secure and keep the cold air out. Start by repairing the frame, then adjust the rollers and hinges. Finally, add weatherstripping to help keep out cold air. Follow these steps and your garage will be secure and the gap will be gone.
