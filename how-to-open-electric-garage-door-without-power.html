Understanding Your Garage Door

If you are the owner of an electric garage door, then you know the convenience that it brings to your life. You donâ€™t have to leave your car to open it manually and it is much easier to access your garage without having to struggle with a heavy garage door. However, this convenience comes with a price - when the power goes out, you are unable to open the door.

Electric garage doors come with a motor that opens and closes the door when the power is on. When the power is off, however, the motor is unable to operate and the automatic door opener will not work. In this case, you will need to figure out how to open the door manually.

Using the Manual Release Mechanism

Fortunately, most electric garage door openers come with a manual release mechanism. This is a system of cables and pulleys that can be used when the power is out. To open the garage door, you will need to locate the manual release mechanism and disengage it from the motor.

The manual release mechanism can be found on the side of the garage door opener. It will typically be a small lever or knob that is connected to a cable. Pull the lever or knob to disengage the motor from the door and then you will be able to open the door manually.

Using the Emergency Release

If your electric garage door opener does not have a manual release mechanism, then you will need to use the emergency release. This is a cord that is connected to the motor and can be used to manually open the door. The emergency release is usually located near the motor and can be seen clearly when the power is out.

To use the emergency release, simply pull the cord and the motor will disengage from the door. Then you can open the door manually. Be sure to keep the emergency release cord in a safe place and out of the reach of children.

Manually Opening the Door

Once the motor is disengaged from the door, you can open it manually. Simply grab the door handle and pull it up until it is fully open. It may take some effort to open the door, especially if the door is heavy. If the door is too heavy for you to open, enlist the help of a friend or family member.

Using an Emergency Key

If your electric garage door opener is equipped with an emergency key, then you can use it to open the door when the power is out. The emergency key is a small key that fits into a slot on the motor. Insert the key into the slot and turn it to the left to disengage the motor from the door. Then you can open the door manually.

Closing the Door

Once you have opened the door, you can then close it manually. Close the door by pushing it up until it is fully closed. Make sure that all of the locks are secure and that the door is properly aligned. Once the door is closed, you can then re-engage the motor.

Conclusion

When the power goes out, you can still open your electric garage door with a few simple steps. First, locate the manual release mechanism or the emergency release cord and disengage the motor from the door. Then, you can open the door manually. Finally, make sure to close the door securely and re-engage the motor when you are done.
