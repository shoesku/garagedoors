What to Do When Your Chamberlain Garage Door Won't Shut

If you have a Chamberlain garage door, you know just how convenient they can be. You can open and close your garage door without ever having to step outside. But when your Chamberlain garage door won't close, it can be a major headache. If your Chamberlain garage door won't close, don't worry, there are a few things you can do to get it working again. In this article, we'll go over some of the most common causes of a Chamberlain garage door not closing and what you can do to fix it.

Check the Power Source

The first thing you should do if your Chamberlain garage door won't close is to check the power source. Your Chamberlain garage door opener needs power to operate, so if the power source is not working, your garage door won't shut. If you have an electric opener, make sure the power switch is on and the circuit breaker is not tripped. If you have a battery-powered opener, make sure the batteries are still charged. If the power source is working, move on to the next step.

Check the Safety Sensors

Another common cause of a Chamberlain garage door not closing is a faulty safety sensor. Chamberlain garage doors are equipped with safety sensors that detect when something is in the way of the closing garage door. If the sensors are not properly aligned, the garage door will not close. To check the sensors, look for a small light next to each sensor. If the light is off, the sensors are not aligned properly. Try adjusting the sensors until the light turns on. Once the sensors are aligned, try closing the garage door again.

Check the Track and Rollers

Another possible cause of a Chamberlain garage door not closing is a damaged track or worn-out rollers. If the track is bent or the rollers are worn out, it can prevent the garage door from closing properly. To check the track, look for any kinks or bends. If you find any, try straightening them out. For the rollers, look for any cracks or gouges. If the rollers are worn out, you'll need to replace them.

Check the Remote Control

If your Chamberlain garage door won't close, you may also need to check the remote control. If the remote control is not working properly, the garage door will not close. Try replacing the batteries in the remote control and see if that solves the problem. If not, you may need to replace the remote control.

Check the Limit Switch

The limit switch is another possible cause of a Chamberlain garage door not closing. The limit switch is a device that tells the opener when to stop closing the garage door. If the limit switch is not set correctly, the garage door will not close. To check the limit switch, look for a small switch near the motor. If the switch is not set correctly, try adjusting it.

Check the Wall Switch

If your Chamberlain garage door still won't close, you may need to check the wall switch. The wall switch is a device that allows you to open and close the garage door from inside the garage. If the wall switch is not working properly, the garage door will not close. To check the wall switch, look for a small switch near the door. If the switch is not working properly, try replacing it.

Check the Trolley

Finally, you may need to check the trolley on your Chamberlain garage door if it won't close. The trolley is a device that connects the motor to the garage door and allows it to move. If the trolley is damaged or not working properly, the garage door will not close. To check the trolley, look for a small wheel near the motor. If the wheel is not moving, try replacing it.

Conclusion

If your Chamberlain garage door won't close, don't worry, there are a few things you can do to get it working again. The most common causes of a Chamberlain garage door not closing are a faulty power source, safety sensors, track and rollers, remote control, limit switch, wall switch, and trolley. Try checking these components one by one to see which one is causing the problem. If you still can't figure it out, it's best to call a professional for help.
