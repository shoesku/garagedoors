What Is the Wayne Dalton Garage Door Emergency Release?

The Wayne Dalton garage door emergency release is a system designed to allow you to open your garage door manually in the event of a power outage or other mechanical failure. The system is composed of a cable and a manual release lever. The cable connects the garage door opener to the door itself, and the lever is located near the bottom of the door. When you pull the lever, it releases the tension in the cable and allows you to open the door manually.

How to Use the Wayne Dalton Garage Door Emergency Release

Using the Wayne Dalton garage door emergency release is fairly straightforward. Here’s what you need to do:

  • First, locate the manual release lever. It is usually located near the bottom of the garage door, and it should be clearly marked.
  • Once you have located the lever, pull it until you feel the tension in the cable release. This should allow you to open the door manually.
  • Once you have opened the door, you will need to re-engage the cable. To do this, simply push the lever back into its original position.
  • Finally, you can open the door from the inside by pressing the wall switch or using the remote control.

Why Is the Wayne Dalton Garage Door Emergency Release Important?

The Wayne Dalton garage door emergency release is an important safety feature because it allows you to manually open the garage door in the case of a power outage or other mechanical failure. This means that you won’t be stuck inside or outside your garage in an emergency situation. It also allows you to exit quickly in the event of a fire or other emergency.
