What is a Garage Door Threshold Draught Excluder?

A garage door threshold draught excluder is a device used to reduce airflow and keep the garage and other areas of the home free from cold drafts. This type of draught excluder is installed at the bottom of the garage door, and is designed to create a seal between the inside and outside of the garage door. This seal helps prevent cold air from entering the home and keeps the temperature inside the garage more comfortable. The draught excluder can also help reduce energy costs, as it keeps the heated air inside the garage, preventing it from escaping.

Benefits of Installing a Garage Door Threshold Draught Excluder

Installing a garage door threshold draught excluder can bring many benefits to the homeowner. Firstly, it helps to reduce energy costs, as it keeps the heated air from escaping from the garage. Secondly, it helps to reduce cold drafts from entering the home. This means that the temperature inside the garage and other areas of the home can remain more comfortable, without the need for additional heating. Thirdly, it helps to reduce noise from outside the home, as the draught excluder helps to reduce the amount of noise that enters the home.

Materials Needed for Installing a Garage Door Threshold Draught Excluder

When installing a garage door threshold draught excluder, you will need to have the following materials on hand: a drill, screws, a level, caulk, and the threshold draught excluder itself. You will also need to have a ladder or step-stool, as well as protective eyewear and gloves. Make sure you have all of these materials before beginning your installation.

Step-by-Step Guide to Installing a Garage Door Threshold Draught Excluder

Installing a garage door threshold draught excluder isn't as difficult as it may seem. Follow these simple steps to get the job done quickly and easily:

  • Measure the gap between the garage door and the floor to determine the size of the draught excluder you will need.
  • Mark the location of the draught excluder on the floor using a pencil.
  • Drill pilot holes for the screws that will be used to attach the draught excluder to the floor.
  • Place the draught excluder in the marked location and use a level to make sure it is even.
  • Secure the draught excluder to the floor with screws.
  • Apply caulk along the edges of the draught excluder to ensure a tight seal.
  • Allow the caulk to dry completely before testing the draught excluder.

Testing the Draught Excluder

Once the draught excluder has been installed, it's important to test it to make sure it is working properly. The easiest way to do this is to open the garage door and close it again. If the draught excluder is working properly, you should feel a tight seal between the door and the floor. If you don't feel the seal, you may need to adjust the draught excluder or reapply the caulk.

Cleaning and Maintaining the Draught Excluder

It's important to keep your draught excluder clean and in good condition. To clean the draught excluder, simply use a damp cloth or mild detergent to wipe it down. Avoid using harsh chemical cleaners, as these can damage the material. It's also important to inspect the draught excluder periodically to make sure it is still in good condition and providing a tight seal. If you find that it is not, it may be time to replace it.

Conclusion

Installing a garage door threshold draught excluder can help reduce energy costs and keep the temperature inside the garage and other areas of the home more comfortable. It's an easy project that can be done in a few simple steps. Be sure to have the right materials on hand and follow the steps above to ensure a proper installation. Also, don't forget to clean and maintain your draught excluder to ensure that it is working properly.
