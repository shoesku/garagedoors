How To Fix A Garage Door Opener Not Going All The Way Up

Best Garage doors and references for house or residential with custom material and more design. We Share more picture design with HD quality images.

Garage door openers are a convenient way to open and close your garage door. However, when your opener isn't working correctly, it can be a major hassle. If your door won't open all the way, it can be frustrating and even dangerous if you have to manually lift it open. Fortunately, there are some easy steps you can take to get your door back in working order.

Check The Wall Switch

The first step is to check the wall switch. This is the switch on the wall near the garage door opener. Make sure it is in the "up" position. If it is not, try flipping it up and down a few times to see if that helps. If it does not, it could be a sign that the switch is malfunctioning and needs to be replaced.

Adjust the Limit Switches

If the wall switch is functioning properly, the next step is to adjust the limit switches. The limit switches control how far the door opens and closes. If they are not properly adjusted, it can prevent the door from opening all the way. To adjust the limit switches, locate the two screws on either side of the motor unit. Make sure they are both turned clockwise until they are tight.

Check the Torsion Springs

Another possible cause of a garage door not going all the way up could be a problem with the torsion springs. These are the large springs on the sides of the door that help balance the weight of the door. If the springs are worn out or broken, it could prevent the door from opening all the way. If this is the case, it is best to have a professional technician replace the springs for you.

Clean and Lubricate All Parts

Sometimes, the issue could be as simple as dirt and grime buildup on the various parts of the garage door opener. Dirt and grime can cause the components to stick and not operate properly. To prevent this from happening, it is important to routinely clean and lubricate all parts of the opener. This includes the tracks, chains, springs, and any other parts that move.

Check the Drive Gear

If all other steps fail, the issue could be with the drive gear. This is the gear that is connected to the motor and is responsible for driving the chain. If this gear is stripped or damaged, it can cause the door to not open all the way. To check the gear, you will need to remove the cover of the motor unit and inspect it. If it is damaged, it needs to be replaced.

Replace The Motor Unit

If the drive gear is not the problem, the issue could be with the motor unit itself. If the motor is failing, it may not be able to lift the weight of the door. If this is the case, it is best to have a professional technician replace it for you. This is a complex job that should not be attempted by a novice.

Have a Professional Technician Inspect The Opener

If all else fails, it is best to have a professional technician inspect the opener. They will be able to diagnose the problem and recommend the best course of action. It is also important to have a professional check the opener on a regular basis to ensure it is functioning properly. This will help keep your garage door opener running smoothly and prevent any further issues.

Conclusion

If your garage door opener is not going all the way up, it can be a major hassle. Fortunately, there are some easy steps you can take to get your door back in working order. First, check the wall switch and make sure it is in the "up" position. Then, adjust the limit switches and check the torsion springs. Additionally, make sure to clean and lubricate all parts of the opener and check the drive gear for any damage. If all else fails, it is best to have a professional technician inspect the opener. With the help of these steps, you should have your garage door opener working in no time.
