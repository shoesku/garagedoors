What is a Garage Door Delatching Kit?

A garage door delatching kit is a set of components designed to help you open your garage door from the outside if the power is out or if your garage door opener fails. This is a great tool for those who have lost their remote or who have experienced a power outage. The kit usually includes a release handle, a latch, and a keyed lock. It's a fairly straightforward setup, and most kits are easy to install.

Benefits of Installing a Garage Door Delatching Kit

Having a garage door delatching kit can be beneficial for many reasons. First, it helps provide added security for your home. It also allows you to be able to open your garage door from the outside in the event of a power failure or if you have misplaced your remote. Additionally, it can be used as a backup plan if your garage door opener malfunctions or fails.

Features to Look for in a Garage Door Delatching Kit

When choosing a garage door delatching kit, there are a few features you should look for. First, find a kit that has a keyed lock so that you can ensure that only you have access to your garage. Additionally, you should look for a kit that is easy to install and that is made of durable materials that can withstand the elements. Finally, you should make sure that the kit includes a release handle so that you can easily open the garage door from the outside.

Types of Garage Door Delatching Kits

There are two main types of garage door delatching kits to choose from: surface-mounted and flush-mounted. Surface-mounted kits are installed directly onto the door and can be easily removed if needed. Flush-mounted kits are installed into the door, so they are more secure. Additionally, flush-mounted kits are less visible than surface-mounted kits, making them the preferred option for many homeowners.

Tips for Installing a Garage Door Delatching Kit

Installing a garage door delatching kit is relatively straightforward, but there are a few tips you should keep in mind. First, make sure to read all instructions carefully before beginning the installation. Additionally, you should make sure to use the correct tools and that you double-check all connections to ensure they are secure. Finally, it's a good idea to test the kit after installation to make sure it is working properly.

Cost of Garage Door Delatching Kits

The cost of a garage door delatching kit can vary depending on the type of kit you choose and the quality of the components. Generally, you can expect to pay anywhere from $20 to $100 for a basic kit. However, if you want a higher-quality kit with more features, you may need to pay more. Additionally, you may need to pay for professional installation if you don't feel comfortable installing it yourself.

Conclusion

A garage door delatching kit is a great way to provide added security and convenience for your home. When choosing a kit, make sure to look for one that has a keyed lock, is easy to install, and is made of durable materials. Additionally, make sure to read all instructions carefully before installation and test the kit after installation to make sure it is working properly. With the right kit, you can be sure that you will be able to access your garage even in the event of a power failure or if you have misplaced your remote.
