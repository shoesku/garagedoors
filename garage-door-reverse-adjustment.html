A Guide To Adjusting Your Garage Door Reversal System

Having a garage door reversal system is one of the best safety features you can have for your home. The feature is designed to prevent the door from closing and injuring someone or something that is in the way. However, from time to time, you may need to adjust the system to ensure it is working correctly. In this guide, we’ll show you how to adjust the garage door reversal system and make sure it is functioning properly.

Steps for Adjusting the Garage Door Reversal System

There are several steps you need to take to ensure that your garage door reversal system is adjusted correctly. These steps include:

Step 1: Check the Sensors

The first step is to check the sensors. These sensors are what cause the garage door to reverse when something is in its way. Make sure the sensors are in the correct position and that they are clean. If they are dirty, you can clean them with a damp cloth. It is also important to make sure the sensors are not blocked by any objects, as this could interfere with their ability to detect objects.

Step 2: Check the Alignment of the Sensors

Once you have checked the sensors, the next step is to check the alignment of the sensors. This is important to ensure that the sensors are lined up correctly and that they are able to detect any objects that may be in their way. You can use a level to check the alignment of the sensors.

Step 3: Adjust the Sensitivity of the Sensors

The next step is to adjust the sensitivity of the sensors. This is important as it affects how quickly the door will reverse when it detects an object in its way. You can adjust the sensitivity of the sensors by changing the settings on the control panel. Make sure to check the manufacturer’s instructions for the correct settings.

Step 4: Test the System

Once you have adjusted the sensitivity of the sensors, the next step is to test the system. You can do this by placing an object in the path of the door and then activating the door. If the door reverses when it detects the object, then the system is working properly. If the door does not reverse, then you may need to make further adjustments.

Step 5: Adjust the Spring Tension

The last step is to adjust the spring tension. This is important as it affects the speed of the door when it opens and closes. It is important to make sure the spring tension is not too tight or too loose, as this could affect the performance of the door. You can adjust the tension by adjusting the screws on the door.

Conclusion

Adjusting your garage door reversal system is an important part of keeping your family safe. By following the steps outlined in this guide, you can ensure that your garage door reversal system is working correctly and that it is providing the best possible protection for your family. If you have any questions, it is always best to consult a professional.
