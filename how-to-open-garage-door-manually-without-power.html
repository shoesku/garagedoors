How To Open Garage Door Manually Without Power

Garage doors are a great convenience, and can be really helpful when you need to get something out of your garage quickly. But when the power goes out, it can be a bit of a hassle to get the garage door open manually without power. Luckily, with a few simple steps, you can open your garage door manually without power and get on with your day.

Step 1: Find the Manual Release Handle

The first step in opening your garage door manually without power is to find the manual release handle. This is usually located near the bottom of the garage door, and can be identified by a red cord. The manual release handle is an emergency release that disengages the garage door opener, allowing you to open the door manually. To open the garage door manually without power, you will need to pull the handle, which will disconnect the door from the opener.

Step 2: Lift the Door Manually

Once the manual release handle has been pulled, you can then lift the door manually. To do this, you will need to find the latch located on the door, which is usually located near the center of the door. Once the latch has been located, you can then lift the door manually by grabbing onto the latch and pulling it up. Depending on the size and weight of the door, this may require some strength and effort.

Step 3: Secure the Door Open

To keep the door open, you will need to secure it in place. To do this, you can use a piece of wood, or something similar, to prop the door open. This will help to keep the door open while you are in the garage. Once you are done, you can then remove the prop and allow the door to close.

Step 4: Close the Door Manually

To close the door manually, you will need to find the latch again and pull the door down. You may need to use some strength, depending on the size and weight of the door. Once the door is closed, the manual release handle can be re-engaged and the door will be ready to be opened again with the garage door opener.

Step 5: Check the Mechanism

Once the door has been opened and closed manually, it is important to check the mechanism to make sure that it is in working order. If the mechanism seems to be stuck or is not functioning properly, it may need to be lubricated or adjusted. It is also important to make sure that the garage door opener is in good working order, as this is what will be used to open and close the door when the power is restored.

Step 6: Test the Door

Once the door is in working order, it is important to test it to make sure that it is functioning properly. To do this, you can open and close the door several times, making sure that it is operating properly. If you find that the door is still not operating properly, it is best to contact a professional for assistance.

Conclusion

Opening a garage door manually without power is a fairly easy process that can be done in a few simple steps. By locating the manual release handle, lifting the door manually, securing the door open, and closing the door manually, you can open your garage door without power in no time. It is also important to check the mechanism and test the door to make sure that it is functioning properly. With these tips, you can have your garage door open and ready to be used in no time.
