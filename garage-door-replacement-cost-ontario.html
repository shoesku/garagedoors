What You Need To Know About Garage Door Replacement Cost In Ontario

If you live in the province of Ontario, then you likely have a garage door. And if you have a garage door, then you likely have questions about the cost of garage door replacement in the province. After all, garage doors can be expensive, and you should know what you’re getting yourself into before investing in a major repair or replacement job.

In this article, we’ll explain the factors that affect garage door replacement cost in Ontario, as well as the average cost of a complete garage door replacement job. Keep reading to learn more about how much it costs to replace a garage door in Ontario.

Factors that Affect Garage Door Replacement Cost in Ontario

When it comes to garage door replacement cost in Ontario, there are a few key factors that can affect the total cost of the job. These factors include:

  • Size of the garage door
  • Type of door material
  • Installation type
  • Additional accessories or features

The size of the garage door is the most important factor when it comes to garage door replacement cost in Ontario. The larger the door, the more expensive it is likely to be. The type of door material also affects the cost of the job, with steel and aluminum garage doors being the most affordable and wood or composite materials being more expensive.

The installation type also affects the cost of a garage door replacement in Ontario. If you hire a professional to do the job, you’ll likely pay more than if you do the job yourself. Additionally, the addition of any accessories or features, such as automatic openers or additional insulation, will also increase the cost of the job.

Average Cost of Garage Door Replacement in Ontario

The average cost of a complete garage door replacement job in Ontario is around $1,200-$3,000. This cost includes the cost of the door itself, as well as any installation fees or additional accessories that you may choose to add. The exact cost of the job will depend on the size, type, and installation of the door.

If you opt to have a professional install the door, you should expect to pay around $100-$200 per hour for labor. Additionally, you may be charged a flat fee for the installation, which will vary depending on the type of door you choose and the complexity of the job. If you decide to install the door yourself, you should expect to pay for the door and any additional accessories, and you may need to rent some equipment or buy additional tools to do the job correctly.

Tips for Saving Money on Garage Door Replacement Cost in Ontario

If you’re looking to save money on garage door replacement cost in Ontario, there are a few tips you can follow. First, shop around for the best deals on doors. You may be able to find cheaper doors from a different manufacturer, or you may be able to get a better deal if you order in bulk. Additionally, you may be able to get a discount if you purchase the door and have it installed at the same time.

Second, look for discounts or promotions from local garage door companies. Many companies offer discounts or promotions throughout the year, so keep an eye out for any deals that you can take advantage of. Finally, consider doing the installation yourself. If you’re handy and have the right tools and equipment, you may be able to save money by doing the installation yourself.

Conclusion

Garage door replacement cost in Ontario can vary depending on the size, type, and installation of the door. The average cost of a complete garage door replacement job in the province is around $1,200-$3,000. To save money on the job, shop around for the best deals, look for discounts or promotions from local companies, and consider doing the installation yourself.
