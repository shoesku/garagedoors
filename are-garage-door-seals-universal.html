What Are Garage Door Seals?

Garage door seals, also known as weatherstripping, are an important part of ensuring that your garage door is properly sealed and insulated. Seals are typically made from rubber, foam, or vinyl and are installed around the edges of your garage door to prevent air, moisture, noise, and pests from entering your garage. Over time, seals can wear out and need to be replaced to ensure that your garage door is properly sealed and functioning as it should. But are garage door seals universal?

Are Garage Door Seals Universal?

The short answer to this question is no. Garage door seals are not universal and must be purchased according to the specific make and model of your garage door. This is because every make and model of garage door is slightly different and requires a specific type and size of seal to ensure a proper fit. If you try to install a universal seal, it is unlikely to fit properly and may not provide adequate protection against the elements or pests.

How To Find The Right Garage Door Seal

In order to find the right seal for your garage door, you will need to know the make and model of your garage door. It is important to make sure that you are buying the correct seal for your door, as an incorrect seal may not fit properly or provide adequate protection.

Installation Of Garage Door Seals

Once you have found the correct seal for your garage door, you will need to install it properly in order to ensure a good seal. This is best done by a professional, as it requires knowledge of the specific make and model of your garage door, as well as the proper tools for installation. If you choose to attempt the installation yourself, be sure to follow the instructions provided and use the proper tools. Improper installation of the seal can result in an inadequate seal and potential damage to your garage door.

Replacing Worn Seals

If you notice that your garage door seals are starting to wear out, it is important to replace them as soon as possible. Worn seals can allow air and moisture to enter your garage, which can lead to damage to your garage door or the items inside. Replacing the seals as soon as possible can help to prevent this damage and will ensure that your garage door is properly sealed and functioning as it should.

Conclusion

In conclusion, garage door seals are not universal and must be purchased according to the specific make and model of your garage door. In order to find the right seal for your garage door, you will need to know the make and model, and then search for the correct seal. It is best to have a professional install the seal, as improper installation can lead to an inadequate seal and potential damage to your garage door. Lastly, be sure to replace worn seals as soon as possible to ensure that your garage door is properly sealed and functioning as it should.
