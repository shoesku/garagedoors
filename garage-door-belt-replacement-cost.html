What is a Garage Door Belt?

A garage door belt is a type of opener system that uses a flexible belt, rather than a chain or screw, to open and close the door. The belt is made of sturdy nylon or steel material and is attached to a track system that runs along the length of the garage door. The belt is connected to a motor, which is operated manually or with a remote. The motor is responsible for activating the belt to open and close the door.

Cost of Replacing a Garage Door Belt

The cost of replacing a garage door belt can vary depending on the type of door, the size, and the age of the door. For a standard single-car garage door, the cost of replacing a belt is usually around $150. For a double-car garage door, the cost can range from $200 to $300. For larger or more complex doors, the cost can be higher, up to $500 or more. The cost of replacing a belt also includes the cost of the parts and labor, so it is important to factor in these costs when budgeting for the replacement.
