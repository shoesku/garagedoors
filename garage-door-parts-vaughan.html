Types of Garage Door Parts in Vaughan

Garage door parts in Vaughan come in a variety of shapes, sizes and materials. From the motor and control systems, to the sensors and springs, each part is essential for the proper functioning of your garage door. Here are some of the most common components of a typical garage door:

  • Motor and Control System: The motor and control system are the two main components of a garage door. The motor powers the door, while the control system allows you to open and close the door with a remote or a keypad.
  • Cables and Tracks: The cables and tracks are responsible for guiding the door as it opens and closes. The cables are connected to the motor, while the tracks guide the door along its path.
  • Springs: Springs are responsible for counterbalancing the weight of the door to ensure it opens and closes smoothly. Springs are typically made of steel and come in various sizes.
  • Sensors: Sensors are essential to the safety of your garage door. They detect when someone or something is in the way and stop the door from closing. Sensors are usually located on either side of the door.
  • Weather Stripping: Weather stripping is a rubber or foam seal that is installed along the bottom of the door to help keep out dust, moisture and insects. Weather stripping also helps keep the temperature in your garage more consistent.
  • Hardware: The hardware is the nuts and bolts that keep the door in place. It includes hinges, rollers, bolts, nuts and other fasteners.

Why It is Important to Maintain Your Garage Door Parts

Like any other part of your home, it is important to maintain your garage door parts to ensure they are in good working condition. Proper maintenance can help extend the life of your garage door, as well as help prevent any safety hazards. Some of the most important maintenance tasks include lubricating the moving parts, checking the balance and tension of the springs, and testing the sensors regularly.

It is also important to inspect your garage door parts regularly for signs of wear and tear. If you notice any damage, rust, or loose parts, it is a good idea to replace them as soon as possible. This can help prevent the problem from becoming worse and potentially causing further damage to your door.
