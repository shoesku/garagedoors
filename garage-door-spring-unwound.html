How To Fix A Garage Door Spring Unwound

Garage doors are an integral part of any home’s security system, and when they’re not functioning properly, it can be a cause of serious concern. One of the most common issues that homeowners face is a garage door spring unwound, which can prevent the door from opening and closing. Fortunately, this issue is relatively easy to fix and can usually be done with a few simple tools. In this article, we’ll go over how to fix a garage door spring unwound so you can get your door back up and running again in no time.

What Causes a Garage Door Spring Unwound?

Garage door springs are often the cause of a garage door spring unwound. These springs are under a lot of tension, and over time, the tension can wear down and cause the springs to become loose and unwound. This can be caused by a variety of reasons, such as the age of the springs, the weight of the door, or even a sudden jolt from a gust of wind.

What Are the Signs of a Garage Door Spring Unwound?

One of the most common signs of a garage door spring unwound is a loud, screeching noise when you open or close the door. This noise is caused by the tension of the springs being released, and it’s a good indication that something is wrong with the springs. Additionally, if you can see the springs while the door is open, you may be able to see that they’re loose or unwound.

How to Fix a Garage Door Spring Unwound

If you’ve determined that your garage door spring is unwound, the first step is to make sure that the door is completely closed and locked. Then, you’ll need to gather the necessary tools, which should include a pair of pliers, a screwdriver, and a ladder. Make sure that all of the tools are in good condition and that the ladder is stable and secure.

Next, you’ll need to open the door and gain access to the springs. This can be done by removing the mounting brackets and the screws that hold the springs in place. Be sure to use caution when doing this, as the springs are under a lot of tension and can be dangerous. Once the springs are exposed, you’ll need to use the pliers to rewind the spring until it’s tight.

After the springs are rewound, you’ll need to use the screwdriver to reattach the mounting brackets and secure the springs in place. Once the springs are securely mounted, you can either use the screwdriver or a drill to fasten the screws, and then you’ll be all set.

Tips for Avoiding a Garage Door Spring Unwound

To avoid having to repair a garage door spring unwound in the future, it’s important to take steps to keep the springs in good condition. One of the most effective preventative measures is to lubricate the springs regularly. This will help to keep them from wearing down and becoming loose over time. Additionally, you should inspect the springs periodically to make sure that they’re in good condition and that there are no signs of wear or tear.

Conclusion

Garage door spring unwound can be a major issue and can prevent your garage door from functioning properly. Fortunately, it’s a relatively easy fix and can usually be done with a few simple tools. In this article, we’ve gone over how to fix a garage door spring unwound so you can get your door back up and running again. Additionally, we’ve provided some tips for avoiding a garage door spring unwound in the future. With the right preventative measures, you can help ensure that your garage door springs remain in good condition for years to come.
