How To Resolve Your Garage Door Not Closing In Cold Weather

Best Garage doors and references for house or residential with custom material and more design. We Share more picture design with HD quality images.

If you're having trouble getting your garage door to close in cold weather, you're certainly not alone. During the winter months, when temperatures dip below freezing, it's not uncommon for garage doors to get stuck in the open position. Fortunately, there are some simple measures you can take to get your door back to normal. In this article, we'll look at some of the most common causes of stuck garage doors in cold weather and how to resolve them.

Check the Springs and Cables

One of the most common causes of a garage door not closing in cold weather is a broken spring or cable. Garage door springs are made of metal and are extremely susceptible to cold weather. If the temperature drops too low, the metal can become brittle and eventually break. The same is true for the cables, which are made of a sturdy synthetic material that can become weakened over time.

If you suspect that your springs or cables may be broken, it's important to have them inspected by a professional. A broken spring or cable can cause significant damage to the door and should not be attempted to be fixed by the homeowner.

Check the Tracks and Rollers

Another common cause of a garage door not closing in cold weather is clogged or misaligned tracks and rollers. Over time, dirt and debris can build up in the tracks, preventing the door from closing properly. In addition, the rollers can become misaligned, preventing the door from sliding smoothly. If either of these issues are present, your garage door will not close properly in cold weather.

The best way to resolve this issue is to inspect the tracks and rollers for any dirt, debris, or misalignment. If anything is found, it should be cleaned or adjusted as necessary. If the issue persists, it's best to call a professional to inspect and repair the door.

Check the Weather Stripping

Weather stripping is an important part of any garage door and is especially important in cold weather. Weather stripping helps to seal the door, preventing cold air from entering the garage. Over time, the weather stripping can become worn or cracked, allowing cold air to enter the garage, which can cause the door to not close properly.

If you suspect that your weather stripping is worn or cracked, you should have it inspected and replaced as necessary. This is a fairly simple process and can be done by most homeowners.

Check the Door Opener

In some cases, the issue may not be with the door itself, but rather with the door opener. If the door opener is not functioning properly, it may not be able to close the door properly in cold weather. In this case, you should inspect the door opener for any signs of damage or malfunction. If any issues are found, they should be repaired or replaced as necessary.

It's also important to make sure that the door opener is properly lubricated. This helps to ensure that it functions properly in cold weather, preventing the door from getting stuck in the open position.

Check the Motor and Gearbox

The motor and gearbox are two of the most important components of any garage door opener. If either of these components is malfunctioning, it can prevent the door from closing properly in cold weather. In this case, you should have both components inspected and repaired or replaced as necessary.

It's also important to make sure that the motor and gearbox are properly lubricated. This helps to ensure that they function properly in cold weather, preventing the door from getting stuck in the open position.

Conclusion

If your garage door is not closing properly in cold weather, it's important to take the necessary steps to resolve the issue. In this article, we've looked at some of the most common causes of stuck garage doors in cold weather and how to resolve them. By following the steps outlined above, you can ensure that your garage door is working properly, regardless of the temperature outside.
