How To Replace A Sears Garage Door Keypad

Introduction

If you have a Sears garage door opener with a keypad, it can be a great convenience. You can open the door without having to enter the garage. All you need to do is enter your code and the door will open. However, if the keypad is not working properly, you may need to replace it. In this article, we'll cover the steps you need to take to replace a Sears garage door keypad.

Step 1: Gather the Necessary Tools

Before you begin, you'll need to gather the tools you'll need for the replacement. You'll need a screwdriver, a pair of pliers, and a replacement keypad. You can purchase a new keypad from any hardware store or online. Make sure to get one that is compatible with your particular model.

Step 2: Locate the Keypad

The next step is to locate the keypad. On most Sears garage door openers, the keypad is located on the interior wall of the garage, near the door. It should be easy to spot â€“ it's usually a small rectangle with a number pad on it. If you have trouble locating the keypad, refer to your owner's manual.

Step 3: Remove the Old Keypad

Once you have located the keypad, the next step is to remove it. To do this, you'll need to use a screwdriver to remove the screws that hold the keypad in place. Once the screws are removed, you can then carefully pull the keypad off of the wall.

Step 4: Connect the New Keypad

Now it's time to connect the new keypad. Start by connecting the wires to the appropriate terminals. Most keypads have color-coded terminals, making it easy to identify which wire goes where. Once the wires are connected, you can then mount the new keypad to the wall using the screws you removed from the old one.

Step 5: Program the Keypad

The next step is to program the keypad. To do this, you'll need to refer to your owner's manual. It should have instructions on how to program the keypad. Once you have successfully programmed the keypad, you should be able to open and close the garage door by entering the code you programmed.

Tips for Troubleshooting

If you are having trouble programming the keypad, there are a few things you can try. First, make sure you are entering the correct code. If you are still having trouble, you may need to reset the keypad. To do this, refer to your owner's manual for instructions. If the problem persists, you may need to replace the keypad again.

Conclusion

Replacing a Sears garage door keypad is a relatively simple process. All you need to do is gather the necessary tools, locate the keypad, remove the old one, connect the new one, and program it. If you are having trouble programming the keypad, make sure you are entering the correct code and follow the instructions in your owner's manual. With these steps, you should have no trouble replacing your Sears garage door keypad.
