Troubleshooting A Garage Door Not Closing Sensor Light On

Best Garage doors and references for house or residential with custom material and more design. We Share more picture design with HD quality images.

Introduction

Are you having trouble with your garage door not closing? Is the sensor light coming on when it should not be? If so, you are not alone. Many homeowners experience this problem and are looking for a solution that will get their garage door working properly again. This article will provide you with tips on how to troubleshoot your garage door not closing sensor light on so you can get your door functioning as it should.

Check the Sensors

The first thing to check when troubleshooting your garage door not closing sensor light on is the sensors themselves. Make sure that the sensors are in the proper position and are free from any dirt or debris. If the sensors are dirty, simply use a damp cloth to clean the sensors. Also, make sure that the sensors are not blocked by any objects or furniture, as this can prevent the sensors from working properly.

Check the Cables

Another thing to check when troubleshooting your garage door not closing sensor light on is the cables. Make sure that the cables are not frayed or damaged in any way. If they are, they will need to be replaced. Also, make sure that the cables are properly connected to the motor and the sensors. If the cables are not properly connected, the motor may not be able to lift the garage door.

Check the Motor

The motor is another important component to check when troubleshooting your garage door not closing sensor light on. Make sure that the motor is in good working condition and is receiving the proper amount of power. If the motor is not receiving the proper power, it may not be able to lift the garage door. Also, make sure that the motor is not making any unusual noises or vibrations, as this could be an indication of a problem.

Check the Tracks

The tracks are another important component to check when troubleshooting your garage door not closing sensor light on. Make sure that the tracks are free from any debris or obstructions. Additionally, make sure that the tracks are in good condition and are properly lubricated with a lubricant recommended for garage door tracks. If the tracks are not properly lubricated, the motor may not be able to lift the garage door.

Check the Springs

The springs are another important component to check when troubleshooting your garage door not closing sensor light on. Make sure that the springs are in good condition and are not damaged in any way. If the springs are damaged, they will need to be replaced. Additionally, make sure that the springs are properly tensioned and that the tension is adjusted to the proper level.

Check the Lock

The lock is another important component to check when troubleshooting your garage door not closing sensor light on. Make sure that the lock is in good working condition and is not damaged in any way. If the lock is damaged, it will need to be replaced. Additionally, make sure that the lock is properly aligned and that the key is inserted correctly.

Check the Weather Stripping

The weather stripping is another important component to check when troubleshooting your garage door not closing sensor light on. Make sure that the weather stripping is in good condition and is not damaged in any way. If the weather stripping is damaged, it will need to be replaced. Additionally, make sure that the weather stripping is properly aligned and that the edges are sealed properly.

Conclusion

Troubleshooting your garage door not closing sensor light on can be a challenge, but it is not impossible. By following the tips provided in this article, you should be able to get your garage door working properly again. Before attempting to troubleshoot your garage door, it is important to remember to always use caution and to read the instructions carefully before attempting any repairs.
