Garage door molding weather seal trim is a type of weather seal that is used to protect your garage door from the elements. It is typically made of rubber or vinyl and is used to seal the gaps between the top and sides of the garage door and the door frame. This type of weather seal is designed to keep out moisture, dust, dirt and other debris that can cause damage to the garage door over time. It is also used to reduce noise from entering the garage, and to help insulate the garage from extreme temperatures.

Types of Garage Door Molding Weather Seal Trim

Garage door molding weather seal trim comes in several different styles and materials, including rubber, vinyl, metal and plastic. Rubber is the most common type of weather seal, as it is relatively inexpensive and provides a good seal against moisture, dust and other debris. Vinyl is also a popular choice, as it is more durable than rubber and provides a better seal against moisture, dust and other debris. Metal weather seals are typically used for heavier garage doors and provide a stronger seal against moisture, dust and other debris.

In addition to the different types of weather seals, there are also different sizes of trim available. This can vary depending on the size of the garage door, as well as the type of weather seal being used. Some weather seals are designed to fit into grooves or slots in the garage door frame, while others are designed to be installed on the door itself. It is important to measure the size of the garage door before purchasing any trim to ensure it fits properly.

Installing Garage Door Molding Weather Seal Trim
