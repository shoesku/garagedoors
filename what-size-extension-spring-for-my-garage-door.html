How To Determine The Right Size Extension Spring For Your Garage Door

Best Garage doors and references for house or residential with custom material and more design. We Share more picture design with HD quality images.

As technology has advanced, so has the complexity of garage doors. Many homeowners are now investing in automatic garage door openers to make life easier. While this is a great investment, it is important to note that garage doors are made up of many different parts, one of which is the extension spring.

Extension springs are designed to help offset the weight of the garage door, allowing the opener to lift it with ease. Without the right size spring, your garage door won’t open or close properly, and could even cause serious damage to the opener or other components. So, how do you determine the right size extension spring for your garage door? Read on to find out!

Step 1: Measure the Length of Your Existing Spring

The first step to determine the right size extension spring for your garage door is to measure the length of the existing spring. Extension springs come in a variety of sizes, so it’s important to get an accurate measurement. To do this, measure from the center of the spring to the end. To get an even more accurate measurement, you can measure from the center of the spring to the end of the hook. This will help you determine the size of the spring you need to purchase.

Step 2: Measure the Diameter of Your Existing Spring

The next step is to measure the diameter of the existing spring. This can be done by measuring the distance from one end of the spring to the other. Extension springs typically come in a variety of diameters, ranging from 1/4 inch to 2 inches. It is important to get an accurate measurement of your existing spring so that you purchase the right size.

Step 3: Determine the Spring’s Strength

Once you have determined the length and diameter of your existing spring, the next step is to determine its strength. Extension springs come in a variety of strengths, ranging from light duty to heavy duty. To determine the strength of your existing spring, look for the wire gauge number printed on the spring. This number will tell you the strength of the spring.

Step 4: Purchase the Right Size Extension Spring

With the length, diameter, and strength of your existing spring measured, you can now purchase the right size extension spring for your garage door. Be sure to purchase a spring that is the same length, diameter, and strength as the existing spring. If you are unsure of what size spring to purchase, you can always consult with a professional who can help you find the right size for your garage door.

Step 5: Install the New Spring

Once you have purchased the right size spring for your garage door, it is time to install it. Installing a new extension spring can be dangerous, so it is important to read the instructions carefully before proceeding. It is also a good idea to consult a professional if you are unsure of how to properly install the new spring. Doing so will help ensure that the installation is done correctly and safely.

Step 6: Test the New Spring

Once the new spring has been installed, it is important to test it to make sure it is working properly. To do this, open and close your garage door several times. If the door opens and closes smoothly, then the spring is installed correctly. If not, you may need to adjust the spring tension or consult a professional for help.

Conclusion

Determining the right size extension spring for your garage door is not always easy. However, with a little bit of knowledge and the right steps, you can determine the right size spring for your garage door. Be sure to measure the length, diameter, and strength of your existing spring to ensure you purchase the right size. Installing the spring can be dangerous, so it is important to consult a professional if you are unsure of how to do it. Once the spring is installed, be sure to test it to make sure it is working properly. Following these steps will help you determine the right size extension spring for your garage door.
