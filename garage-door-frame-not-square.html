Tips For Fixing A Garage Door Frame That Isn't Square

One of the most common problems with garage doors is when the frame isn't square. This can make it difficult to open and close the door, as well as cause it to stick or jam. Fortunately, this is a fairly easy problem to fix. Here are some tips for getting your garage door frame back in shape.

Check for Obstructions

The first thing you should do when trying to fix a garage door frame that isn't square is check for any obstructions. This includes things like debris, dirt, or objects that are blocking the door's path. If you find something that is blocking the door, remove it and see if that fixes the problem. If it doesn't, then it's time to move on to the next step.

Adjust the Rollers

If there are no obstructions, then the next step is to adjust the rollers. The rollers are what allow the garage door to open and close properly. If they are too tight or too loose, then the door won't be able to move properly. You can adjust the rollers by turning the adjusting screws located at the bottom of the door. This should help get the door back into shape.

Check for Warped Frames

If adjusting the rollers doesn't work, then you may need to check for warped frames. This is usually caused by moisture or extreme temperatures. If the frame is warped, then you may need to replace it. If it isn't, then you can try to adjust the frame by using clamps or a hammer and chisel. This should help the door fit back in place.

Replace the Springs

If the frame is still not square after adjusting it, then you may need to replace the springs. The springs are what helps the door open and close smoothly. If they are too worn out or damaged, then they won't work properly. This can cause the door to become unbalanced, which makes it hard to open and close. You can replace the springs yourself, or you can call a professional to do it for you.

Check the Tracks

The tracks are another possible cause for a garage door not being square. If the tracks are bent or damaged, then the door won't be able to open and close correctly. You can try to adjust the tracks yourself, or you can call a professional to do it for you. They will be able to straighten out the tracks and get the door back into shape.

Adjust the Hinges

If all else fails, you may need to adjust the hinges. The hinges are what allow the door to open and close smoothly. If they are too loose or too tight, then the door won't be able to move properly. You can adjust the hinges yourself, or you can call a professional to do it for you. They will be able to get the door back in shape.

Conclusion

Fixing a garage door frame that isn't square isn't as difficult as it may seem. With a little bit of patience and some basic tools, you can get your garage door back in shape in no time. Just make sure to check for obstructions, adjust the rollers, check for warped frames, replace the springs, adjust the hinges, and check the tracks. Once you do, your garage door will be back to normal in no time.
