The Cost Of Replacing A Garage Door Top Panel

It's no secret that replacing a garage door top panel can be expensive. Depending on the materials and labor involved, the price can vary greatly. While it's possible to save money by doing the job yourself, it's not advised unless you have experience with this type of repair. In this article, we'll discuss the cost of replacing a garage door top panel, as well as the factors that can influence the price.

What Affects the Cost of Replacing a Garage Door Top Panel?

The cost of replacing a garage door top panel will depend on a number of factors, such as the size and type of garage door you have, the type of panel you need, and the labor involved. It's important to note that the cost of the materials and labor can vary greatly, depending on the region you live in and the individual contractor you hire.

Size of the Garage Door

The size of your garage door will have a major impact on the cost of replacing the top panel. Generally speaking, larger garage doors will require more materials and labor than smaller ones. For example, a single-car garage door will cost less to replace than a double-car garage door. Additionally, if your garage door is made from a complex material, such as wood or steel, the cost of the labor will be higher.

Type of Panel

The type of panel you need will also affect the cost of the replacement. Generally speaking, panels made from durable materials, such as aluminum or steel, will be more expensive than composite or vinyl panels. It's also important to note that some manufacturers may offer discounts if you purchase multiple panels at once.

Labor Costs

The labor costs associated with replacing a garage door top panel will vary depending on the contractor you hire and the complexity of the job. Generally speaking, the cost of labor can range from $50 to $200 per hour, depending on the region and the specific contractor. Additionally, some contractors may charge a flat fee for the job, while others may charge an hourly rate. Be sure to ask for an estimate before hiring a contractor for the job.

Do-It-Yourself

If you're handy and have some experience with do-it-yourself projects, you may be able to save some money on the cost of replacing a garage door top panel. However, it's important to note that the job may be more complex than you anticipate. Additionally, it's important to remember that you may void the warranty on your garage door if you attempt to do the job yourself.

Finding the Right Contractor

When it comes to replacing a garage door top panel, it's important to find a contractor who is experienced and reputable. Be sure to ask for references from previous customers and read online reviews before hiring a contractor for the job. Additionally, you should get an estimate from the contractor before signing any contracts. This will ensure that you know exactly what the job will cost before committing to the project.

The Bottom Line

Replacing a garage door top panel can be expensive, but it's important to remember that the cost will vary depending on the size and type of garage door you have, the type of panel you need, and the labor involved. Additionally, it's important to find a reputable contractor who is experienced in this type of repair. By following these tips, you can ensure that you get the best price for the job.
