A Guide To Greasing Garage Door Tracks In 2023

Introduction

A squeaky and slow-moving garage door can be very frustrating, especially if you need to open and close your garage door several times a day. Fortunately, a simple solution exists to keep your garage door running smoothly: greasing the tracks. When done properly, greasing can minimize noise, increase speed, and reduce the wear and tear on your garage door. In this article, we'll explain why greasing is important, what to use, and how to do it.

Why Greasing Is Important

Garage doors are opened and closed multiple times a day, and over time, the metal components of the system can become worn and corroded. Without proper lubrication, the metal pieces can begin to grind against each other, causing the door to move more slowly and make more noise. Greasing the tracks keeps the parts moving smoothly, reducing noise and increasing the speed of the door. It can also help reduce wear and tear on the system, resulting in a longer lifespan.

What To Use To Grease Garage Door Tracks

When greasing garage door tracks, it's important to use the right product for the job. Many people opt for standard lubricants like WD-40, but these are not ideal for the job. WD-40 is made up of a combination of oil and water, and while it may provide some short-term lubrication, it will quickly evaporate, leaving the tracks unprotected. Additionally, the water in the mixture can corrode the metal, resulting in further damage.

What Is The Best Option For Greasing Garage Door Tracks?

The best option for greasing garage door tracks is a quality lubricant specifically designed for the job. These lubricants are typically made from a combination of mineral oil and wax, and they are designed to stay on the tracks longer and provide better protection. Additionally, they will not break down the metal components of the system, resulting in a longer lifespan for the garage door.

How To Grease Garage Door Tracks

Once you've selected the right lubricant for the job, it's time to start greasing. Begin by using a cloth to wipe away dirt and debris from the tracks. Once the tracks are clean, apply the lubricant along the length of the track, making sure to cover all of the metal components. Allow the lubricant to sit for a few minutes before closing the door.

When Should You Grease Garage Door Tracks?

Greasing the tracks of your garage door should be done regularly, at least once or twice a year. Additionally, if you notice that your door is opening and closing more slowly than usual, or if it is making more noise than usual, it's a good indication that the tracks need to be greased. Greasing the tracks can be a quick and easy way to get your door back to running smoothly.

Conclusion

Greasing your garage door tracks is a simple and effective way to keep your door running smoothly. By selecting the right lubricant and regularly greasing the tracks, you can reduce noise, increase the speed of the door, and reduce wear and tear on the system. Remember to wipe away dirt and debris before applying the lubricant, and make sure to cover all of the metal components of the track. With a few simple steps, you can keep your garage door running smoothly and efficiently.
