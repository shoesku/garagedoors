How To Apply Garage Door Weather Stripping For Maximum Benefits

As the temperatures start to drop, it’s important to properly seal up your garage with garage door weather stripping. Not only does this help keep out the cold air, but it can also save you money on your energy bill. Weatherstripping your garage door is an easy do-it-yourself project that can be completed in just a few hours. Here’s a step-by-step guide on how to apply weatherstripping to your garage door.

Step 1: Clean the Door

Before you begin installing the weatherstripping, make sure that the garage door is clean and free of dirt, dust, and debris. This will ensure that the weatherstripping will properly adhere to the door. Use a damp cloth and some mild soap to remove any dirt or grime that may be present.

Step 2: Measure the Door

Before you begin to buy your weatherstripping, it’s important to measure the garage door. Measure the door from the top to the bottom and from side to side. This will help you determine how much weatherstripping you need to buy. Make sure to leave a few extra inches of weatherstripping in case you need to make any adjustments.

Step 3: Purchase the Weatherstripping

Once you’ve measured the door, you can head to your local hardware store to purchase the weatherstripping. There are many different types of weatherstripping available, but most people prefer to use foam weatherstripping as it is easy to install and provides a tight seal. Make sure to buy enough to cover the entire door.

Step 4: Cut the Weatherstripping

Using a sharp utility knife, cut the weatherstripping to fit the length and width of the garage door. Make sure to cut the weatherstripping about an inch longer than the actual measurements of the door. This will leave some extra room for adjustments.

Step 5: Install the Weatherstripping

Now that you’ve cut the weatherstripping, it’s time to install it. Start by attaching the weatherstripping to the top of the garage door. To do this, use a strong adhesive or nails to secure the weatherstripping. Make sure to press down firmly so that it is properly secured to the door.

Next, attach the weatherstripping to each side of the door. Again, use a strong adhesive or nails to secure the weatherstripping to the sides. Make sure that the weatherstripping is flush against the sides of the door.

Finally, attach the weatherstripping to the bottom of the door. Make sure that it is flush against the bottom of the door and that the adhesive or nails are firmly secured.

Step 6: Test the Weatherstripping

Once you’ve installed the weatherstripping, it’s important to test it to make sure that it is properly sealed. Close the garage door and check to make sure that there are no gaps or air leaks. If there are any gaps, adjust the weatherstripping until they are sealed.

Conclusion

Weatherstripping your garage door is an easy and effective way to reduce your energy costs and keep out the cold air. By following the above steps, you can easily complete this project in just a few hours. Make sure to check the weatherstripping regularly to make sure that it is still providing a tight seal.
