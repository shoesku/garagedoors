What You Need To Know About Garage Door Humming Not Closing

Introduction

If you own a garage door, you might have experienced that sometimes it’s humming while closing but not actually closing. It’s an annoying problem and can be quite dangerous if not addressed in time. But first, it’s important to understand why the door is humming and not closing. In this article, we will explain the causes of this problem and the solutions to fix it.

What Can Cause Your Garage Door to Hum and Not Close?

There are several possible causes for a humming garage door that won't close. We’ll explain the most common ones below.

1. Obstruction

It is possible that something is obstructing the door from closing. It could be a toy or a tool that isn’t visible to you. It’s also possible that the door is hitting a large object like a car parked too close to the door. If this is the case, the door will make a humming noise while trying to close but won’t close due to an obstruction.

2. Broken Springs or Cables

Broken springs and cables can also cause the door to make a humming noise and not close. The springs and cables are responsible for lifting the weight of the door and if they are broken, the door will struggle to move. This can cause it to make a humming noise while trying to close but not actually close.

3. Misaligned Tracks

Another common cause for a humming garage door is misaligned tracks. This means that the metal tracks on which the door moves are not properly aligned. This can cause the door to make a humming noise while trying to close, but not actually close. You can check the tracks and see if they are properly aligned, or call a professional to do it for you.

4. Malfunctioning Motor

Finally, a malfunctioning motor can also be the cause of a humming garage door. The motor is responsible for opening and closing the door, and if it’s malfunctioning, it can cause the door to make a humming noise while trying to close but not actually close. You will need to call a professional to fix this issue.

How To Fix A Garage Door Humming Not Closing

Once you have identified the cause of the problem, you can start fixing it. We’ll discuss the most common solutions below.

1. Remove the Obstruction

If the door is making a humming noise due to an obstruction, the first thing to do is to remove it. If it is a toy or a tool, you can simply take it out. If it is something else, like a car parked too close to the door, you will need to move it away from the door or call a professional to do it for you.

2. Replace the Springs or Cables

If the springs or cables are broken, you will need to replace them. This is something that should be done by a professional as it can be quite dangerous. A professional will be able to identify the problem and replace the springs or cables to fix the issue.

3. Align the Tracks

If the tracks are misaligned, you will need to align them. This is a simple task that you can do yourself. You will need to use a level and a wrench to adjust the tracks until they are properly aligned. If you are not sure how to do this, you can call a professional to do it for you.

4. Replace the Motor

Finally, if the motor is malfunctioning, you will need to replace it. This is something that should be done by a professional as it can be dangerous. A professional will be able to identify the problem and replace the motor to fix the issue.

Conclusion

A humming garage door that won't close can be quite annoying and dangerous. Fortunately, it is usually a problem that can be fixed easily. The most common causes are obstructions, broken springs or cables, misaligned tracks, and a malfunctioning motor. To fix the issue, you will need to remove the obstruction, replace the springs or cables, align the tracks, or replace the motor. If you are not sure how to do this, you can call a professional for help.
