What To Do If Your Garage Door Opener Is Making Grinding Noise?

Best Garage doors and references for house or residential with custom material and more design. We Share more picture design with HD quality images.

You’re about to leave for work but you hear a loud grinding noise coming from your garage. You open the door to see what’s going on and you realize that it’s your garage door opener making the noise. You want to fix the problem, but you’re not sure what to do. Don’t worry, we’ve got you covered! Here’s our guide on what to do if your garage door opener is making grinding noise.

1. Check the Chain Tension

Grinding noises can be caused by an issue with the chain tension. Check that the chain tension is properly adjusted. If the chain is too tight, it will cause noise as it moves. If the chain is too loose, it won’t be able to move the door properly and it will make noise.

To adjust the chain tension, you will need to use the tension adjustment bolt on the motor unit. The tension should be adjusted so that the chain is tight enough to move the door, but not so tight that it causes noise. It should be adjusted so that there is about an inch of slack in the chain.

2. Check the Motor Unit

The motor unit is the part of the garage door opener that moves the chain. It can also be the cause of grinding noise. Check the motor unit for any signs of wear and tear, such as broken or worn out parts. If there are any signs of wear and tear, you will need to replace the motor unit.

The motor unit may also be making noise because it is not properly lubricated. Make sure to lubricate the motor unit regularly to keep it running smoothly and quietly.

3. Check the Chain

The chain is the part of the garage door opener that moves the door. It can also be the cause of grinding noise if it is worn out or damaged. Check the chain for any signs of wear and tear, such as rust or frayed links. If there are any signs of wear and tear, you will need to replace the chain.

It is also important to make sure that the chain is properly lubricated. This will help to reduce noise and ensure that the chain moves smoothly and quietly.

4. Check the Gears

The gears are the parts of the garage door opener that move the chain. If the gears are worn out or damaged, they can cause grinding noises. Check the gears for any signs of wear and tear, such as chips or cracks. If there are any signs of wear and tear, you will need to replace the gears.

It is also important to make sure that the gears are properly lubricated. This will help to reduce noise and ensure that the gears move smoothly and quietly.

5. Check the Motor Mounts

The motor mounts are the parts of the garage door opener that hold the motor unit in place. If the motor mounts are worn out or damaged, they can cause grinding noises. Check the motor mounts for any signs of wear and tear, such as cracks or chips. If there are any signs of wear and tear, you will need to replace the motor mounts.

It is also important to make sure that the motor mounts are properly lubricated. This will help to reduce noise and ensure that the motor mounts move smoothly and quietly.

Conclusion

If your garage door opener is making grinding noise, it could be caused by any number of issues. It is important to check the chain tension, motor unit, chain, gears, and motor mounts to make sure that everything is in good condition. If any of these parts are worn out or damaged, you will need to replace them. It is also important to make sure that all of these parts are properly lubricated to ensure that they move smoothly and quietly.

By following these steps, you can ensure that your garage door opener is running smoothly and quietly. If you are still having issues, it is best to contact a professional repair technician to help diagnose and repair the issue.
