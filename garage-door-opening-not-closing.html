Why Is My Garage Door Not Closing?

Best Garage doors and references for house or residential with custom material and more design. We Share more picture design with HD quality images.

Your garage door is an essential part of your home and when it's not closing properly, it can be quite annoying. The most common reasons why a garage door won't close properly are broken springs, blocked sensors, and misaligned tracks. No matter what the cause, it's important to get the issue taken care of quickly and correctly.

Broken Springs

The garage door springs are essential for the proper functioning of your garage door. If the springs are broken, the door won't be able to move. The best way to check if the springs are the cause of the problem is to look at them. If they are broken, you will need to replace them. This can be a difficult job and it is best to hire a professional to do the work if you don't feel confident doing it yourself.

Blocked Sensors

The sensors on your garage door are designed to detect obstacles in the path of the door so that it won't close on something. If the sensors are blocked, the door won't be able to close properly. The best way to check if the sensors are blocked is to look at them and make sure there is nothing blocking them. If there is, you will need to remove the obstacle and then test the door to make sure it closes properly.

Misaligned Tracks

The tracks on your garage door are responsible for guiding the door up and down. If the tracks are misaligned, the door won't be able to close properly. The best way to check if the tracks are misaligned is to look at them and make sure they are straight and level. If they are not, you will need to adjust them and then test the door to make sure it closes properly.

Other Causes

There are other possible causes for a garage door not closing properly. These include a faulty opener, a broken or worn garage door cable, or a problem with the garage door remote. You will need to check each of these items to make sure they are working properly. If they are not, you will need to replace them.

Conclusion

If your garage door won't close properly, there are several possible causes. It could be broken springs, blocked sensors, or misaligned tracks. You will need to check each of these items to make sure they are working properly and make any repairs or replacements necessary. It is best to hire a professional to do the work if you don't feel confident doing it yourself.
