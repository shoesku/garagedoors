Garage door alarms are designed to detect when doors are opened. When the door is opened, the alarm is triggered and an alert is sent to a monitoring center or to the homeowner. The homeowner is then able to take appropriate action to secure the property.

The most basic garage door alarms are triggered by contact switches. When the door is opened, the contact switch triggers the alarm. These alarms can be set to different levels, depending on the homeowner’s preference. For instance, the alarm can be set to sound an alert if the door is opened for longer than a certain amount of time, or if the door is opened during certain hours.

What Are Garage Door Alarm Magnets?

Garage door alarm magnets are a more advanced type of alarm. These alarms use a magnetic switch to detect when the door is opened. The switch is placed on the doorframe and the magnet is placed on the door. When the door is opened, the magnet is pulled away from the switch, which triggers the alarm.

These alarms are usually more reliable than contact switches, as they are less likely to be triggered accidentally. They can also be adjusted to detect different levels of opening, allowing the homeowner to choose the level of security they need. For example, the alarm can be set to sound an alert if the door is opened for longer than a certain amount of time, or if the door is opened during certain hours.
