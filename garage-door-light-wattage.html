What Is The Right Wattage For Your Garage Door Light?

The garage door light is an important part of your home security system. It helps to light up the area when you come home, and lets you know if there is an intruder in your garage. But what wattage should you be using for your garage door light? The answer depends on the type of light you choose and how much lighting you need for your security system.

Types of Garage Door Lights

There are several types of lights you can use for your garage door. Incandescent lights are the most common, and they use 60-watt bulbs. These lights are the least expensive and provide good lighting. Halogen lights use higher wattage bulbs, ranging from 65 to 100 watts. They are brighter than incandescent bulbs, but they are also more expensive. LED lights are the most energy efficient, and they use only 10 to 25 watts, depending on the size and type of bulb.

How Much Wattage Do You Need?

The amount of wattage you need for your garage door light depends on the size of the area you are lighting and how bright you want the light to be. If your garage is large, you will need more wattage to ensure your entire garage is well-lit. If you have a smaller area, then you can get away with using a lower wattage bulb. For example, if your garage is 12 feet by 12 feet, then you can use a 50-watt bulb. If your garage is larger than that, then you may need to use a 75-watt bulb.

Tips for Installing Your Garage Door Light

When installing your garage door light, there are a few things to keep in mind. First, make sure the light is installed in a place where it won't be blocked by objects like shelves or furniture. Second, make sure the light is pointed toward the ground. Third, make sure the light is spaced evenly throughout the area. Finally, make sure the light is not too bright or too dim.

Conclusion

When it comes to choosing the right wattage for your garage door light, the answer depends on the type of light you choose and how much lighting you need. Incandescent lights use the least wattage, while halogen and LED lights use more. The size of the area you are lighting and how bright you want the light to be are also factors to consider. When installing your garage door light, make sure it is installed in a place where it won't be blocked by objects, pointed toward the ground, spaced evenly throughout the area, and not too bright or too dim.
