What Is a Garage Door Spring?

A garage door spring is an essential component for the proper operation of a garage door. It is a metal coil that is attached to the garage door using cables and hooks. As the door is opened, the spring is stretched and exerts a tension on the cables. This tension helps to lift the door up as it is opened. As the door is closed, the spring contracts, and the tension is released, allowing the door to close.

Garage door springs come in a variety of sizes, strengths, and lengths. The size and strength of the spring you need will depend on the size and weight of your garage door. It is important to choose the right size and strength of spring for your door to ensure it works properly. You should also ensure that the spring is properly installed for it to work properly.

Types of Garage Door Springs

There are two main types of garage door springs: torsion springs and extension springs. Torsion springs are the most common type and are typically used for heavier doors. They are the most expensive type of spring and are typically installed by a professional.

Extension springs are typically used for lighter doors and are the most affordable type of spring. They are easier to install and can be done by a homeowner. They are also the most common type of spring found on residential garage doors.
