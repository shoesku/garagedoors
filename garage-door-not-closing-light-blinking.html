Garage Door Not Closing: What Does A Blinking Light Mean?

If you’ve ever experienced the frustration of a garage door not closing, you know that it can be an irritating inconvenience. Fortunately, you can often diagnose and resolve the issue yourself. One of the most common issues is when the garage door opener’s light is blinking. Here’s a look at what to do if your garage door opener light is blinking.

Common Causes of a Blinking Light

When it comes to a garage door not closing, the blinking light can mean many different things. There are several common causes of a blinking light on a garage door opener, including:

  • The door is not properly connected to the opener.
  • The opener’s sensors are blocked or obstructed.
  • The opener’s battery is low.
  • The opener’s circuit board has failed.
  • The opener’s remote control is malfunctioning.

Troubleshooting a Blinking Light

If your garage door opener’s light is blinking, the first step is to try to troubleshoot the issue. Here are the steps you should take:

  • Check that the door is properly connected to the opener. Make sure the cord is plugged in and the connections are secure.
  • Check the opener’s sensors. Make sure they are not blocked or obstructed by dirt or debris.
  • Replace the opener’s battery.
  • Check the opener’s circuit board. If it is faulty, you may need to replace it.
  • Replace the opener’s remote control.

When to Call a Professional

If you’ve tried the troubleshooting steps above and the garage door still won’t close, it may be time to call a professional. A professional can diagnose the issue and make any necessary repairs. They can also inspect the door itself to make sure it is working properly. It’s important to have a garage door that works properly, as it can be a safety hazard if it is not functioning correctly.

Conclusion

If your garage door opener’s light is blinking, it can be an irritating inconvenience. Fortunately, most of the time the issue can be resolved by troubleshooting the problem yourself. If the issue persists, however, it may be time to call a professional for assistance. With the help of a professional, you can get your garage door functioning properly once again.
