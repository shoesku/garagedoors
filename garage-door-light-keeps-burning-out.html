What Causes A Garage Door Light To Keep Burning Out?

If you’ve noticed that your garage door light keeps burning out, you’re not alone. Garage door lights are often one of the most commonly replaced parts of a garage door, and there are a few different reasons why they may burn out more often than other parts. Let’s take a closer look at what could be causing your garage door light to keep burning out, and what you can do to prevent it from happening again.

1. Poor Quality Lights

One of the most common reasons that garage door lights keep burning out is because of poor quality. Many lights installed in garages are not well-made and are prone to burning out quickly. Low quality lights with poor insulation can easily overheat and eventually fail. The best way to prevent this from happening is to invest in a high-quality garage door light. You can find some good options online or at your local home improvement store.

2. Incorrect Installation

Another common cause of garage door lights burning out is incorrect installation. If the light is not installed correctly, it can cause the wiring to be loose or the connection between the light and the wiring to be faulty. This can lead to the light overheating and eventually burning out. To avoid this, it’s important to make sure that your light is installed correctly and that the wiring is tight and secure.

3. Voltage Issues

If you’re experiencing frequent garage door light burnouts, it could be due to voltage issues. If the voltage of your light is not sufficient enough to power it, it can cause the light to overheat and eventually burn out. To avoid this, it’s important to make sure that your light is connected to the right voltage. You can check the voltage of your light by looking at the label on the light or using a multimeter.

4. Wiring Problems

Wiring problems can also be a common cause of garage door lights burning out. If there are any loose connections or faulty wiring, it can cause the light to overheat and eventually burn out. To avoid this, it’s important to make sure that the wiring is tight and secure. If you’re not sure how to do this, you can always hire a professional electrician to come and take a look at your wiring.

5. Frequent Use

Another common cause of garage door lights burning out is frequent use. If you’re using your garage door light too often, it can cause it to overheat and eventually burn out. To avoid this, it’s important to limit the amount of time that you use your light. If you’re using it more than necessary, it’s best to invest in a higher-quality light that can handle more frequent use.

6. Poor Maintenance

Finally, poor maintenance can also be a common cause of garage door lights burning out. If you’re not regularly cleaning and maintaining your light, it can cause it to overheat and eventually burn out. To avoid this, it’s important to regularly inspect and clean your light. You can use a damp cloth to clean the light and make sure that there are no loose connections or faulty wiring.

Conclusion

If your garage door light keeps burning out, it could be due to poor quality, incorrect installation, voltage issues, wiring problems, frequent use, or poor maintenance. To avoid this from happening, it’s important to invest in a high-quality garage door light and make sure that it is installed correctly and maintained regularly. If you’re unsure how to do this, you can always hire a professional electrician to come and take a look at your light.
