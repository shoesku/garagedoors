What To Do When Your Garage Door Keeps Stopping When Opening

Are you having trouble with your garage door? Does it start to open and then suddenly stop? It could be a frustrating experience and could potentially be a symptom of a larger problem. In this article, we will discuss what to do when your garage door keeps stopping when opening.

Check the Sensors

The most common cause of a garage door that stops opening mid-way is a problem with the sensors. The sensors are responsible for detecting any objects or people in the way of the door, and will send a signal to the door to stop and reverse if it detects anything in its way. Before you do anything else, you should check the sensors to make sure they are working properly. Check for any debris or dust that may be blocking the sensors, and if the sensors are blocked, clean them off with a soft cloth. Additionally, check the wires connecting the sensors to the door opener to make sure they are securely connected.

Check the Tracks

If the sensors are working properly, the next possible cause of a garage door stopping mid-way is a problem with the tracks. The tracks are the metal rails that the door moves along as it opens and closes. Over time, the tracks can become worn or damaged, and this can cause the door to stop mid-way. Check the tracks to make sure they are in good condition, and if they are not, you may need to have them professionally repaired or replaced.

Check the Springs

Another common cause of a garage door stopping mid-way is a problem with the springs. The springs are responsible for helping the door open and close, and if they are worn or damaged, the door may not be able to open or close properly. Check the springs to make sure they are in good condition, and if they are not, you may need to have them professionally repaired or replaced.

Check the Opener

If the sensors, tracks, and springs are all in good condition, the problem may be with the opener. The opener is responsible for actually lifting the door as it opens, and if it is not working properly, the door may not open all the way. Check the opener to make sure it is working properly, and if it is not, you may need to have it professionally repaired or replaced.

Check the Wiring

Finally, the problem with your garage door may be a problem with the wiring. The wiring is responsible for connecting the various components of the door, and if it is not working properly, the door may not open or close correctly. Check the wiring to make sure it is securely connected, and if it is not, you may need to have it professionally repaired or replaced.

Conclusion

If your garage door keeps stopping when opening, there are several possible causes. Check the sensors, tracks, springs, opener, and wiring to make sure they are all in good condition, and if they are not, you may need to have them professionally repaired or replaced. Remember, if you are ever unsure about what to do, it is always best to contact a professional for help.
