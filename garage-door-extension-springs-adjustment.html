How To Adjust Garage Door Extension Springs In 2023

Introduction

Garage door extension springs are one of the most important components of a garage door system. They are responsible for counterbalancing the weight of the door and allowing it to open and close with ease. If they are not properly adjusted, it can cause problems with the operation of the door and can even lead to potential safety hazards. That is why it is important to adjust your garage door extension springs every so often.

Tools You Need

Before you begin adjusting your garage door extension springs, there are a few tools you will need to have on-hand. First and foremost, you will need a pair of safety glasses. This is to protect your eyes from any debris or dust that may be kicked up during the process. You will also need to have a pair of pliers and an adjustable wrench. Finally, you will need to have a ladder or step stool to help you reach the springs.

Step 1: Remove the Spring

The first step in adjusting your garage door extension springs is to remove them from the door. The springs are typically attached to the upper end of the track near the ceiling. To remove them, you will need to use the pliers to loosen the bolts and then gently slide the spring out of the track. Be sure to keep your hands away from the spring as it is still under tension and can cause serious injury if it were to snap.

Step 2: Adjust the Spring

The next step is to adjust the tension on the spring. To do this, you will need to use the adjustable wrench to turn the adjustment nut located at the end of the spring. Turning the nut clockwise will increase the tension, while turning it counter-clockwise will reduce the tension. You want to turn the nut until the spring is just tight enough to counterbalance the weight of the door. You do not want to over-tighten the spring as this can cause it to wear out faster.

Step 3: Reattach the Spring

Once you have adjusted the tension on the spring, you can then reattach it to the track. To do this, you will need to use the pliers to tighten the bolts and then slide the spring back into the track. Again, make sure that your hands are away from the spring in case it were to snap while you are adjusting it.

Step 4: Test the Door

After you have finished adjusting the spring, you will then want to test the door to make sure that the tension is correct. To do this, you will need to open and close the door several times. If it is opening and closing with ease, then the spring is properly adjusted. If it is still having trouble, then you may need to adjust the tension a bit more.

Conclusion

Adjusting your garage door extension springs is an important part of keeping your door in good working condition. It is a relatively simple process that should not take more than an hour or so to complete. However, if you feel like you are not comfortable doing it yourself, then it is always best to call in a professional to do the job for you. Keep in mind, though, that it is important to adjust your springs every so often to ensure that your door is operating safely and efficiently.
