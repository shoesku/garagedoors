How To Adjust Garage Door Extension Springs

Introduction

Adjusting garage door extension springs is an important part of regular garage door maintenance. Extension springs are located on the sides of your garage door and are used to provide a counterbalance to the weight of the door. Over time, these springs can become worn out and need to be adjusted to prevent any issues. In this article, we will discuss the steps to properly adjust your garage door extension springs. With a few simple tools and a bit of patience, you can have your garage door safely operating in no time.

Gather the Required Tools and Supplies

Before you begin the process of adjusting your garage door extension springs, you need to gather the appropriate tools and supplies. You will need a pair of pliers, an adjustable wrench, a measuring tape, and a ladder. Additionally, you may need to purchase a pair of new extension springs for your garage door. Make sure you have these items on hand before you begin the process.

Safety First

It is important to remember that adjusting your garage door extension springs can be a dangerous process. Make sure to follow all safety guidelines and use caution when working with the springs. Wear safety goggles to protect your eyes and always keep your hands away from any moving parts. If you are unsure of any part of the process, it is best to contact a professional to complete the job safely.

Locating the Springs

Once you have gathered all the necessary tools and supplies, you can begin the process of adjusting your garage door extension springs. The first step is to locate the springs. The springs are generally located on the sides of the door and may be mounted to a bracket or directly to the door itself. If you are unsure of their location, refer to your garage door’s owner’s manual for further instructions.

Adjusting the Tension on the Springs

Once you have located the springs, you can then begin to adjust the tension on the springs. To do this, you will need to use the pliers and the adjustable wrench. Start by using the pliers to loosen the tension on the springs. Then, use the adjustable wrench to tighten the tension on the springs until you have reached the desired level. Make sure to check the tension level regularly and make adjustments as needed.

Check the Balance of the Door

Once you have adjusted the tension on the springs, you should then check the balance of the door. To do this, use the ladder to reach the top of the door and use the measuring tape to measure the distance from the top of the door to the floor. This will give you an indication of whether or not the door is balanced correctly. If the door is too low, you may need to adjust the tension on the springs again to bring the door up to the correct level.

Adjust the Counterbalance System

If the door is still not balanced correctly after adjusting the tension on the springs, then you may need to adjust the counterbalance system. To do this, use the adjustable wrench to loosen the nuts and bolts on the counterbalance system. Then, use the pliers to adjust the tension on the counterbalance system until the door is balanced correctly.

Check the Rollers and Tracks

Once you have adjusted the tension on the springs and the counterbalance system, it is important to check the rollers and tracks. Make sure that the rollers are aligned properly and are not binding or sticking. Additionally, make sure that the tracks are clear of any debris and are properly lubricated.

Conclusion

Adjusting your garage door extension springs is an important part of regular garage door maintenance. With a few simple tools and a bit of patience, you can have your garage door operating safely in no time. Make sure to follow all safety guidelines and use caution when working with the springs. If you are unsure of any part of the process, it is best to contact a professional to complete the job safely.
