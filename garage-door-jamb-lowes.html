What is a Garage Door Jamb?

A garage door jamb is a part of the frame of a garage door. It is used to provide extra support and security for the door itself. The jamb is installed on the exterior of the garage door and is typically made from either wood, steel, or aluminum. The jamb provides a strong and secure surface for the door to rest against when it is closed. It also helps to keep the door from swinging out of its tracks when the door is open.

What Does a Jamb Do?

A garage door jamb helps to ensure that the door is properly secured when it is closed. The jamb provides an extra layer of protection against anyone attempting to break into the garage by forcing the door open. Additionally, the jamb helps to keep the door on its track when it is opened and closed. Without a jamb, the door could easily be forced out of its track and become stuck.
