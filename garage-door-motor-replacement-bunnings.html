Types of Garage Door Motors

When purchasing a garage door motor, you'll be presented with four main options. These are:

  • Chain Drive Motors
  • Belt Drive Motors
  • Direct Drive Motors
  • Jackshaft Motors

Chain drive motors are the most popular option. They're relatively inexpensive and offer a good level of performance. They use a chain to move the door and require minimal maintenance. The downside is that they can be noisy, so you may want to consider an alternative if you live in a quiet area.

Belt drive motors are a step up from chain drive motors. They use a rubber belt to move the door, which makes them much quieter. They're more expensive than chain drive motors, but they're still reasonably priced and offer a good level of performance. They also require minimal maintenance.

Direct drive motors are the most expensive option, but they offer the best performance. They use a direct drive mechanism to move the door, which makes them incredibly quiet and efficient. They also require very little maintenance.

Jackshaft motors are the least popular option. They use a motor and a drive shaft to move the door, which makes them very efficient. They're also fairly quiet and require minimal maintenance. The downside is that they're more expensive than the other types.

How to Choose the Right Garage Door Motor

When selecting a garage door motor, you'll need to consider a few factors. The first is the size and weight of your door. If you have a large, heavy door, you'll need to choose a powerful motor that can handle the load. You'll also need to consider the type of door you have. Some motors are designed specifically for sectional doors, while others are designed for rolling doors.

You'll also need to consider the price. If you're on a budget, you'll want to stick to the cheaper options. However, if you can afford to spend a bit more, you'll get a better performance and longer lifespan. Finally, you'll need to consider the noise level. If you live in a quiet area, you may want to opt for a belt drive or direct drive motor.
