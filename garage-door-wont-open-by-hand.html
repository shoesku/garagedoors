How To Troubleshoot When Your Garage Door Won't Open By Hand

Garage doors are a major convenience, but they also require regular maintenance and occasional repairs. If your garage door won't open by hand, it could be a sign of a problem with the springs, cables, or other components. Fortunately, troubleshooting why your garage door won't open by hand is usually a straightforward process.

Check the Springs and Cables

The most likely culprit when your garage door won't open by hand is a problem with the springs or cables. These components are under a great deal of tension, so it's important to exercise caution when checking them. Before you begin, unplug the power to your garage door opener to prevent an accidental activation.

Start by visually inspecting the springs and cables for any signs of damage. If the cables appear frayed, stretched, or otherwise compromised, you'll need to replace them. The same is true for the springs if they appear broken, worn, or otherwise damaged.

Adjust the Springs

If the springs and cables appear to be in good condition, you may be able to fix the problem by adjusting the tension. This is done by turning the screws on the springs clockwise to make them tighter, or counter-clockwise to make them looser. Make small adjustments and test the door after each one until you find the right tension.

If you're unable to adjust the tension or the door still won't open by hand, it's time to call a professional. This is especially true if you're uncomfortable working with the springs and cables, as they can be dangerous if not handled properly.

Check the Opener

If the springs and cables appear to be in good condition and the tension is properly adjusted, you'll need to check the opener. Start by checking the power to the unit. If the plug is loose or the circuit breaker is tripped, that could be the reason why your door won't open by hand.

If the power is on, check the opener's control board for any signs of damage. If the control board is damaged, it may need to be replaced. You should also make sure the safety sensors are properly aligned and free of debris. The sensors use infrared light to detect objects in the path of the door, so any obstructions can prevent the door from opening.

Test the Motor

If the opener appears to be in good condition, you'll need to check the motor. Start by disconnecting the motor from the power source and testing it with a voltage meter. If the motor isn't operating properly, it may need to be replaced.

If the motor appears to be in good condition, you'll need to check the track. Make sure the track is properly aligned and free of debris. If the track is misaligned, you can usually adjust it with a wrench. If the track is bent or otherwise damaged, it may need to be replaced.

Call a Professional

If you're unable to identify the cause of the problem or make the necessary repairs, it's time to call a professional. A qualified technician can quickly identify the cause of the problem and make the necessary repairs. They can also perform regular maintenance to help prevent future problems.

Conclusion

Garage doors are a major convenience, but they require regular maintenance and occasional repairs. If your garage door won't open by hand, it could be a sign of a problem with the springs, cables, opener, or other components. Fortunately, troubleshooting why your garage door won't open by hand is usually a straightforward process.

Start by checking the springs and cables, then adjust the tension if necessary. If that doesn't work, check the opener and motor. If all else fails, it's time to call a professional. With the right troubleshooting and maintenance, you can keep your garage door working properly for years to come.
