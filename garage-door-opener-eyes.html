Garage door opener eyes are an important part of any home's security system. They are designed to detect the presence of an intruder, and alert the homeowner of their presence. Types of Garage Door Opener Eyes

There are three main types of garage door opener eyes available on the market today. The first is an infrared beam, which is a low-cost, low-power infrared beam mounted at the top of the garage door. When an intruder breaks the beam, the opener activates, alerting the homeowner of their presence. The second type is an optical eye, which is a more powerful, higher-cost device. It uses an optical beam to detect motion, and when the beam is broken, the opener is activated. The third type is a motion-activated device, which activates the opener when motion is detected. All three types of garage door opener eyes are effective, but the optical eye is the most reliable.

Benefits of Installing Garage Door Opener Eyes

Garage door opener eyes offer several benefits to homeowners. First, they provide an extra layer of security for your home. Things to Consider When Choosing a Garage Door Opener Eye

When selecting a garage door opener eye, there are a few things to consider. First, consider the type of opener you have. Some openers require a specific type of eye, so make sure you know what type you have before making a purchase. Additionally, you should also consider the size of your garage. If your garage is large, you may need to invest in a larger opener eye to ensure maximum coverage. Finally, you should also consider the type of motion detection you're looking for.
