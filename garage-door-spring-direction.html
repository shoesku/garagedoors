How To Change The Direction Of A Garage Door Spring

Garage door springs are an important part of the garage door system, helping to lift and lower the door with ease. They come in either a left-wound or right-wound design, depending on the direction of the torsion bar that runs through the center of the spring. It is possible to change the direction of a garage door spring, but it is important to follow the correct steps to ensure the safety of everyone in the vicinity. In this article, we will explain how to change the direction of a garage door spring in a few easy steps.

What You Will Need

Before you begin, you will need the following items:

  • A pair of safety glasses
  • A pair of work gloves
  • A socket wrench with the appropriate size socket
  • A ladder (optional)
  • A helper (optional)

Step One: Inspect the Spring

The first step is to inspect the spring to make sure it is in good working condition and that it is not damaged. If the spring is damaged or has any visible signs of wear, it should be replaced with a new spring before proceeding. Be sure to wear safety glasses when inspecting the spring as it is under tension and can cause serious injury if it suddenly releases.

Step Two: Remove the Spring

Once the spring has been inspected and is deemed to be in good working condition, the next step is to remove the spring from the door. This can be done by using a socket wrench to loosen the bolts that hold the spring in place. Be sure to wear safety glasses and work gloves when removing the spring as it is under tension and can cause serious injury if it suddenly releases.

Step Three: Change the Direction of the Spring

After the spring has been removed, the direction of the spring can be changed by turning the torsion bar that runs through the center of the spring. The torsion bar should be turned in the opposite direction of the current winding. For example, if the spring is currently wound in a left-handed direction, the torsion bar should be turned in a right-handed direction. It is important to note that the torsion bar should be turned slowly and carefully to avoid damaging the spring.

Step Four: Reinstall the Spring

Once the direction of the torsion bar has been changed, the spring can be reinstalled. This can be done by using a socket wrench to tighten the bolts that hold the spring in place. Be sure to wear safety glasses and work gloves when reinstalling the spring as it is under tension and can cause serious injury if it suddenly releases.

Step Five: Test the Spring

The final step is to test the spring by opening and closing the garage door. If the spring is functioning correctly, the door should open and close smoothly and with ease. If the door does not open or close correctly, it is important to adjust the tension on the spring. This can be done by winding or unwinding the torsion bar.

Conclusion

Changing the direction of a garage door spring is a relatively simple process, but it is important to take the necessary safety precautions when doing so. It is also important to make sure that the spring is in good working condition before attempting to change the direction. If the spring is damaged or worn, it should be replaced with a new spring. Following these steps should help ensure the safety and proper functioning of the garage door system.
