Types of Garage Door Insulation

When it comes to garage door insulation, there are several types to choose from. If you’re looking for a basic, cost-effective option, then you may want to consider foam insulation. This type of insulation is relatively inexpensive and provides an effective barrier against heat loss. It is also relatively easy to install, so you can do it yourself if you have the right tools. For a more durable and long-lasting option, consider insulated steel doors. These doors are designed to stand up to extreme temperatures and weather conditions, and they can be customized to fit your garage’s specific dimensions.

The Benefits of Insulated Garage Doors

Adding insulation to your garage door can have several benefits. For one, it can help to reduce your energy bills by keeping more of your heated or cooled air inside your home. It can also help to reduce noise from outside and from the garage itself. Additionally, an insulated garage door can help to protect your belongings from temperature fluctuations. Finally, it can add to the overall value and aesthetic of your home.

Cost of Garage Door Insulation

The cost of garage door insulation varies depending on the type of insulation you choose and the size of your garage. Generally speaking, foam insulation is the most affordable option, while insulated steel doors are usually the most expensive. At Mitre 10, we offer a wide range of insulation products at a variety of price points, so you can find the right solution for your budget.

How to Install Garage Door Insulation

Installing garage door insulation is a relatively easy process that can be completed in a few hours. The first step is to measure your garage door to determine the size of the insulation you’ll need. Once you’ve done that, you can begin to cut the insulation to fit. After that, you’ll need to attach the insulation to the door using nails, screws, or staples.
