Why Should I Lubricate My Garage Door Chain?

Garage door chains, also known as trolley chains, are the mechanism that raises and lowers your door when you press the button on your garage door opener. Without proper lubrication, these chains can become sluggish, noisy, and eventually seize up, preventing them from properly doing their job. To keep your door running smoothly, it is important to regularly lubricate your garage door chain. This guide will explain the process of how to lubricate a garage door chain and the reasons why you should do so.

What Do I Need to Lubricate a Garage Door Chain?

The first step in lubricating a garage door chain is to gather the necessary supplies. You will need a can of spray lubricant, preferably a graphite or teflon-based lubricant, as these are the least likely to attract dirt and dust. You will also need a clean rag or rag, a screwdriver, and a stepladder if your garage door is too high off the ground to reach easily.

How to Lubricate a Garage Door Chain: Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you have gathered the necessary supplies, you are ready to begin lubricating your garage door chain. The following is a step-by-step guide on how to lubricate a garage door chain:

  • Step 1: Remove the cover from your garage door opener. This cover is usually held in place by two screws, which can be removed using a screwdriver.
  • Step 2: Once the cover is removed, you will see the garage door chain and the two pulleys it runs over. Use your rag to wipe away any dirt and grime that may have built up on the chain.
  • Step 3: Spray the lubricant onto the chain. Make sure to get the lubricant in between the links of the chain, as this is where the most friction occurs.
  • Step 4: Using your rag, wipe away any excess lubricant that may have gotten onto the pulleys or other parts of the garage door opener.
  • Step 5: Replace the cover and re-tighten the screws.

When Should I Lubricate My Garage Door Chain?

It is recommended that you lubricate your garage door chain at least once a year. This helps to ensure that it is running smoothly and is less susceptible to breakage or malfunction. Additionally, if you notice that your garage door is becoming noisy or sluggish, it may be a sign that your chain is in need of lubrication.

Do I Need to Lubricate My Garage Door Chain?

The short answer is yes. Lubricating your garage door chain is important to ensure that it is running smoothly and efficiently. Additionally, regular lubrication can help to extend the life of your garage door opener, as the chain is the key component to its proper operation. If you neglect to lubricate your garage door chain, you may find yourself dealing with an issue that could have been easily avoided.

Conclusion

Lubricating your garage door chain is an important step to ensure that it is running smoothly and efficiently. By following the steps outlined in this guide, you can easily lubricate your garage door chain and keep it running like new. Additionally, regular lubrication can help to extend the life of your garage door opener, saving you money in the long run. So, don’t forget to lubricate your garage door chain when needed and keep your door running smoothly.
