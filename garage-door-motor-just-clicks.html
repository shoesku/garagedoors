Why Does a Garage Door Motor Just Click?

When you press the button to open your garage door, you want to hear the reassuring rumble of the motor as it whirs into life. But all too often, instead of the motor kicking into gear, you’re met with a loud and annoying clicking sound. This can be incredibly frustrating, but it’s not necessarily a sign of a major problem. In most cases, the cause of the clicking is simply a lack of power.

When the motor gets power, it’s able to move the door along its track. But if the power supply is interrupted or insufficient, the motor will respond by emitting a clicking noise. This is a built-in safety feature that prevents the motor from burning out or overheating.

What Can I Do About the Clicking Sound?

If your garage door motor is clicking instead of working properly, there are a few steps you can take to troubleshoot the problem. The first and most important thing is to check the power supply. Make sure the wires that connect to the motor are properly attached and that the outlet is working. If the power supply is fine, you may need to replace the garage door motor.

If the power supply is working and you’re still dealing with clicking, you may have an issue with the motor itself. It may be that the gears in the motor are worn out or jammed. In this case, you’ll need to replace the motor or contact a repairman to do it for you.

Should I Contact a Professional?

When it comes to garage door motors, it’s always best to leave the work to a professional. This is especially true if you’re dealing with a more serious problem like a stuck or jammed motor. A professional has the experience and expertise necessary to identify the cause of the problem and repair it quickly and safely.

What’s more, a repairman can also check for other potential issues that might be causing the motor to make clicking noises. This could include worn out parts, broken cables, or a damaged door track. Once the issue is identified, they can then make the necessary repairs.

When Should I Replace the Motor?

If you’ve tried troubleshooting the problem and nothing has worked, it may be time to replace the motor. If your motor is more than 10 years old, it’s probably a good idea to replace it anyway. Modern garage door motors are much more efficient and reliable than older models, so replacing it is a smart investment.

When buying a new motor, make sure to get one that’s compatible with your existing garage door system. You’ll also want to check the warranties offered by different manufacturers. This will give you an idea of what kind of protection you’ll get if the motor fails or stops working.

Conclusion

If your garage door motor is making a clicking sound instead of working properly, it’s important to take the time to troubleshoot the problem. In most cases, the issue is a lack of power or a worn out motor. If the power supply is fine and the motor still won’t work, you may need to replace it. For more serious problems like a stuck or jammed motor, it’s best to contact a professional repairman.
