Dummy Handles For Your Garage Door: A Comprehensive Guide For 2021

Garage doors are a fundamental part of your home's security and aesthetic. Choosing the right dummy handle for your garage door can make a big difference in the overall look and feel of your home. But with so many options available, it can be difficult to know which one is the best for you.

Dummy handles are an easy way to add a touch of style to your garage door. Not only do they provide a classic, timeless look, but they also give you easy access to the door. Whether you're looking for a traditional style or something more modern, there are plenty of dummy handles to choose from.

Types of Dummy Handles

The type of dummy handle you choose will depend on the look and feel you're going for. There are three main types of dummy handles to choose from:

  • Traditional: Traditional dummy handles are the most common type. They typically have a long, rectangular shape and are made from plastic, metal, or wood. They are ideal for traditional-style homes.
  • Modern: Modern dummy handles are more contemporary in design. They often have a curved or circular shape and are usually made from metal, plastic, or wood. They are perfect for modern or contemporary homes.
  • Decorative: Decorative dummy handles are designed to add a touch of style to your home. They come in a variety of shapes, sizes, and materials, and they can often be personalized with engravings or other decorative elements.

Features to Consider

When choosing a dummy handle for your garage door, there are several features to keep in mind. These include the size, construction materials, and the type of locking mechanisms available.

  • Size: Dummy handles come in a variety of sizes, so it's important to make sure you choose one that fits your door. Measure the width and height of your door before you shop to make sure you get the right size.
  • Construction Materials: The materials used to construct dummy handles vary greatly. The most common materials are plastic, metal, and wood, but there are also some that are made from composite materials or even glass.
  • Locking Mechanisms: Most dummy handles come with a variety of locking mechanisms. This can include keyless entry, keypad entry, or even a combination lock. Make sure to choose one that is suitable for your needs.

Benefits of Dummy Handles

Dummy handles are a great way to add a touch of style to your garage door. Not only do they provide a classic, timeless look, but they also come with several benefits.

  • Security: Dummy handles provide an extra layer of security for your garage door. They can help deter potential intruders and make it more difficult for them to gain access to your home.
  • Durability: Dummy handles are usually made from durable materials, so they can withstand the elements and last for many years. This means you don't have to worry about replacing them any time soon.
  • Aesthetics: Dummy handles can add a touch of style to your home. They come in a variety of shapes, sizes, and materials, so you can find one that fits your home's overall look and feel.

How to Install a Dummy Handle

Installing a dummy handle is relatively simple and can usually be done in just a few minutes. Here are the steps to follow:

  • Start by measuring the width and height of your garage door. This will help you choose the right size dummy handle for your door.
  • Choose the type of dummy handle you want. Consider the construction materials and locking mechanisms available.
  • Check the instructions that come with the dummy handle for specific installation instructions.
  • Install the dummy handle according to the instructions. Make sure to use the appropriate screws and tools.
  • Test the handle to make sure it works properly.

Conclusion

Dummy handles are a great way to add a touch of style to your garage door. Not only do they provide a classic, timeless look, but they also come with several benefits, such as increased security and durability. When choosing a dummy handle, make sure to consider the size, construction materials, and locking mechanisms available. Installing a dummy handle is relatively easy and can usually be done in just a few minutes. With the right dummy handle, you can give your garage door the perfect look and feel.
