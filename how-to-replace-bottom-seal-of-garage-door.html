Replacing The Bottom Seal Of A Garage Door

Best Garage doors and references for house or residential with custom material and more design. We Share more picture design with HD quality images.

Introduction

The bottom seal of a garage door is an important part of the door that helps keep the inside of your garage free from the elements. It is also the main protection against animals, pests, and other unwanted visitors. Over time, the seal can become worn out or damaged, leading to drafts and even water entering your garage. Replacing the bottom seal is a fairly simple job that can be accomplished with the right tools and materials.

Tools and Materials Needed

Before you begin, you will need to gather the necessary tools and materials. You will need a tape measure, a cordless drill, a utility knife, a Phillips head screwdriver, a putty knife, a level, a pair of pliers, a hammer, a block of wood and a replacement seal for your garage door. Make sure to get the exact measurements of the current seal before you purchase a new one.

Steps to Replace the Bottom Seal

Step 1: Measure the Current Seal

The first step is to take measurements of the current seal. Measure the length, width, and height of the seal. Take into account any curves or angles that may be present in the seal. Record the measurements so that you have them handy when you go to purchase a new seal.

Step 2: Remove the Old Seal

Once you have the measurements of the old seal, you can begin removing it. Start by using a utility knife to cut the seal away from the door frame. Be careful not to damage the frame as you do this. Once the seal is removed, use a putty knife to scrape away any remaining adhesive from the frame.

Step 3: Clean the Area

Once the old seal has been removed, it's time to clean the area. Use a damp cloth or sponge to remove any dirt, debris, and old adhesive from the frame. Make sure the area is completely clean and free of any residue.

Step 4: Install the New Seal

Now you can begin installing the new seal. Start by placing the seal on the frame and pressing it firmly into place. Make sure the seal is completely flush with the frame. Use a level to ensure that the seal is level. Once the seal is in place, use a cordless drill to secure it with screws.

Step 5: Test the Seal

Once the seal is installed, it's time to test it. Close the garage door and inspect the seal for any cracks or gaps. If you find any, use a block of wood and a hammer to tap them into place. If the seal appears to be secure, you can open and close the garage door a few times to make sure the seal is functioning properly.

Conclusion

Replacing the bottom seal of a garage door is a fairly easy job that can be completed in just a few short steps. By following the steps outlined above, you can easily replace the seal and ensure that your garage door is secure and functioning properly. Make sure to use the correct tools and materials and take the appropriate safety precautions when working around the garage door.
