How To Install Garage Door Decorative Hardware

Best Garage doors and references for house or residential with custom material and more design. We Share more picture design with HD quality images.

Introduction

Garage doors can be a major focal point of a home's exterior. Not only do they provide security and privacy, but they can also add a touch of style to your home. Installing decorative hardware is one way to spruce up a plain-looking garage door. Decorative hardware can range from clavos to door knockers to handles, hinges and more. With a few simple tools and some patience, you can easily install decorative hardware on your garage door.

Step 1: Choose the Right Hardware

When selecting decorative hardware for your garage door, you'll want to choose pieces that are made from high-quality materials and designed to withstand the elements. Aluminum, brass, and wrought iron are all good options. You'll also want to make sure that the hardware is designed for the type of garage door that you have. If you're not sure what type of hardware to choose, consult a professional.

Step 2: Prepare the Door

Before installing the hardware, you'll need to prepare the door. Begin by cleaning the door with a damp cloth and mild soap. This will help remove any dirt, debris, and grime that may be present. Next, sand down any rough or uneven surfaces to create an even surface. Finally, apply a coat of primer to the door to help protect against corrosion and rust.

Step 3: Mark and Drill the Holes

Once the door is prepared, you can begin to mark and drill the holes for the hardware. You'll want to make sure that the holes are aligned properly and that the hardware will fit securely. Use a drill bit that is slightly smaller than the size of the hardware so that the hardware will fit snugly.

Step 4: Install the Hardware

Now it's time to install the hardware. Begin by inserting the screws into the holes that you drilled. Make sure that the screws are tight and secure. Next, use a screwdriver to attach the hardware to the door. Do this carefully and make sure that the hardware is securely attached.

Step 5: Finish the Installation

Once the hardware is installed, you'll need to finish the installation. Start by applying a coat of paint or sealant to the hardware. This will help protect it from the elements. Then, use a damp cloth to wipe away any excess paint or sealant. Finally, stand back and admire your work!

Conclusion

Installing decorative hardware on your garage door can be a great way to add a touch of style to your home. With a few simple tools and some patience, you can easily install the hardware yourself. Just remember to choose hardware that is made from high-quality materials and designed to withstand the elements. Once the hardware is installed, you can enjoy the added curb appeal of your home.
