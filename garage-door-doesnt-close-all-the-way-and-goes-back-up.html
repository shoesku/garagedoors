What Causes a Garage Door to Go Back Up?

If your garage door doesn't close all the way and goes back up, there could be a few different things causing this issue. Most often, the problem is related to either the electrical or mechanical systems in the door. There are a few common causes for this problem. One of the most common is a misaligned or bent track. If the track is bent or misaligned, the door will be unable to travel all the way to the bottom of the track. Another common cause is when the rollers in the door are worn out or damaged. Over time, the rollers can become worn and may not be able to support the weight of the door, causing it to go back up.

How to Fix a Garage Door That Won't Close All the Way

The first step to fixing a garage door that won't close all the way is to determine the cause. If the track is bent, you'll need to straighten it out and make sure it is aligned properly. If the problem is with the rollers, you'll need to replace them. If you're unsure of what is causing the problem, you may want to call a professional garage door technician for assistance. Once you've identified the cause, you can then move on to the next step, which is to fix the problem.

Fixing a Misaligned or Bent Track

If the problem is a bent or misaligned track, you'll need to use a wrench to straighten out the track. Start by loosening the bolts that hold the track in place and then use a rubber mallet to gently tap the track back into place. Once it is straight, you can then re-tighten the bolts. This should get the door to close all the way and not go back up.

Replacing the Rollers

If the problem is with the rollers, you'll need to replace them. Start by opening the door and then removing the rollers from their track. You can then replace the old rollers with new ones. Make sure to use the same size and type of rollers as the old ones to ensure the door will work properly. Once you have the new rollers in place, you can then close the door and test to make sure it closes all the way and doesn’t go back up.

Call a Professional

If you can’t determine what is causing the problem or if you’re unable to fix it yourself, you may want to consider calling a professional garage door technician. They will be able to diagnose the problem and fix it quickly and efficiently. This can save you time and ensure that the problem is fixed correctly the first time.

Conclusion

If your garage door won't close all the way and goes back up, there can be a few different causes. Most often, the problem is either with the track or the rollers. To fix the issue, you'll need to either straighten out the track or replace the rollers. If you're unsure of what is causing the problem or if you're unable to fix it yourself, you may want to call a professional for assistance. With the right knowledge and tools, you should be able to get your garage door working properly again in no time.
