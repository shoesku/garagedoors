How To Adjust A Garage Door Spring

If you’ve been finding it difficult to close or open your garage door, chances are you may need to adjust the garage door spring. A garage door spring can become misaligned or lose tension over time, and this can be a hassle to fix. Fortunately, adjusting a garage door spring is a fairly simple task, and can be done with a few basic tools.

Gather the Materials You Need

Before you begin, it’s important to make sure you have all the necessary tools and materials. You’ll need a wrench, a pair of pliers, a screwdriver, and a ladder. Additionally, you may want to have safety glasses, gloves, and a pair of rubber-soled shoes.

You’ll also need to purchase a new garage door spring, as the old one can’t be adjusted. You can usually find these at your local hardware store. Make sure you purchase the correct size and type for your garage door.

Prepare the Area

The first step is to make sure the area is properly prepared. Clear out any debris or clutter that may be in the way. Make sure there’s plenty of light and that the area is well ventilated.

Once the area is clear, you’ll want to turn off the power to the garage door opener. This is important, as you don’t want the garage door to unexpectedly open or close while you’re working.

Remove the Old Spring

Once the area is prepared and the power is off, you can begin to remove the old garage door spring. Start by using the wrench to loosen the bolts that hold the spring in place. You may also need to use the pliers to remove any stuck bolts.

Once the bolts are removed, you can carefully remove the spring from the track. Be sure to be careful, as the spring is under tension and can be dangerous.

Install the New Spring

Now that the old spring is removed, you can install the new one. Start by carefully aligning the new spring with the track. Once it’s in place, use the wrench to tighten the bolts that hold it in place.

Once the spring is securely in place, you can adjust the tension. This can be done by using the screwdriver to turn the adjustment screw. The adjustment screw is located on the side of the spring and can be accessed from the inside of the garage.

Test the Door

Once the new spring is adjusted, it’s time to test the door. Turn the power back on and open and close the door several times. If it opens and closes smoothly, then the spring is properly adjusted. If not, then you may need to adjust the tension again.

Conclusion

Adjusting a garage door spring is not a difficult task, and can be done with a few basic tools. It’s important to be careful when working with the spring, as it’s under tension and can be dangerous. With the right tools and a little bit of time, you can easily adjust the tension on your garage door spring.
