What To Do When Your Garage Door Light Bulb Burns Out?

It’s a common occurrence—you’re fumbling with your keys in the dark, trying to get into your garage, when you notice the light bulb above your garage door is burned out. You’re not alone—garage door light bulbs burn out all the time. But what should you do?

Check the Light Bulb

The first step is to check the light bulb itself. Is it burned out? If so, you’ll need to replace it. But if not, it could be a wiring issue or a blown fuse. If you’re not a handy person, it’s probably best to call an electrician to check it out.

Choose the Right Bulb

When it comes time to replace the bulb, you’ll need to make sure you choose the right one. Make sure the wattage is the same as the old bulb, as this will determine the brightness of the light. You may also want to choose a bulb with a longer life expectancy. LED bulbs are a great option, as they usually last much longer than traditional bulbs.

Replacing the Bulb

Once you’ve chosen a new bulb, it’s time to replace the old one. First, make sure to turn off the power to the garage door before attempting to replace the bulb. Then, unscrew the old bulb and replace it with the new one. Be sure to twist it in the same direction as the old one. Once you’ve replaced the bulb, turn the power back on and test it out.

Preventative Measures

There are a few things you can do to help prevent your garage door light from burning out. First, make sure the bulb isn’t exposed to too much heat. If it’s a hot day, try to keep the garage door closed as much as possible. You should also make sure to clean the light fixture regularly, as dust and dirt can build up over time and cause the bulb to burn out sooner than it should.

Replacing the Fixture

If your light fixture is old and worn, you may want to consider replacing it. A new fixture can provide better light and can help prolong the life of your bulb. When selecting a new fixture, make sure to choose one that is compatible with the wattage of your bulb. You may also want to choose a fixture with a built-in motion sensor, which will help conserve energy.

Call an Expert

If all else fails, you may want to call in an expert. An electrician can help troubleshoot the issue and can advise you on the best course of action. They may be able to repair the wiring or the fixture, or they may recommend a new fixture altogether. Either way, an electrician can help make sure your garage door light is up and running again in no time.

Conclusion

Having a burned out garage door light bulb can be a nuisance, but it doesn’t have to be. With a few simple steps, you can replace the bulb, clean the fixture, and even install a new one if necessary. And if you’re having trouble, don’t be afraid to call in an expert. With a few simple steps, you can have your garage door light up and running again in no time.
