Introduction

Chamberlain garage doors are one of the most popular types of garage door systems. They are reliable, easy to install and operate, and require minimal maintenance. However, like any other mechanical system, they can malfunction and require repairs. One of the most common issues is the Chamberlain Garage Door Not Closing Light Blinking. This is usually caused by a faulty sensor, which can easily be fixed. In this article, we will discuss how to troubleshoot and repair this issue.

What Is Chamberlain Garage Door Not Closing Light Blinking?

Chamberlain Garage Doors are equipped with sensors that detect if there is an obstruction in the path of the door. If the sensors detect an obstruction, the light on the opener will begin to blink. This is a safety feature that prevents the door from closing if there is something in the way. If the light is blinking, it means that the garage door cannot close, and it must be fixed in order for the door to work properly.

What Causes Chamberlain Garage Door Not Closing Light Blinking?

The most common cause of the Chamberlain Garage Door Not Closing Light Blinking is a faulty sensor. The sensors are located on either side of the door and they detect obstacles in the path of the door. If the sensors are not functioning properly, they will not detect the obstacle and the light will continue to blink. This is a safety feature that prevents the door from closing if something is in the way.

How to Troubleshoot Chamberlain Garage Door Not Closing Light Blinking?

Troubleshooting the Chamberlain Garage Door Not Closing Light Blinking involves checking the sensors to make sure they are working properly. To do this, first, unplug the garage door opener from the power source. Then, check the sensors to make sure there are no obstructions or dirt that could be blocking the sensors. If the sensors appear to be clear and unobstructed, then the next step is to check the wiring of the sensors. If the wiring appears to be in good condition, then the problem may be with the sensor itself.

How to Replace Chamberlain Garage Door Not Closing Light Blinking Sensor?

If the Chamberlain Garage Door Not Closing Light Blinking is caused by a faulty sensor, then the only way to fix it is to replace the sensor. The sensor is located on either side of the door and is secured by two screws. To replace the sensor, first, remove the two screws and then carefully pull the old sensor out. Once the old sensor is removed, insert the new sensor and secure it with the two screws. Then, plug in the garage door opener and test the sensor to make sure it is working properly.

Conclusion

If your Chamberlain Garage Door Not Closing Light is Blinking, it could be caused by a faulty sensor. To fix this issue, you will need to replace the faulty sensor. This can be done by unplugging the garage door opener and then carefully removing the old sensor and replacing it with a new one. Once the new sensor is installed, plug the garage door opener back in and test the sensor to make sure it is working properly. If you follow these steps, you should be able to fix the Chamberlain Garage Door Not Closing Light Blinking issue.
