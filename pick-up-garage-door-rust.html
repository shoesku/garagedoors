What is Rust?

Rust is an oxidized iron that occurs when a reaction between iron and oxygen takes place in the presence of water or air moisture. Rust is a common problem for metal objects that are left outdoors, such as garage doors. Rust can cause issues with the door’s function and look. Luckily, there are a few ways to pick up rust from a garage door.

Why is it Important to Remove Rust from Garage Doors?

Rust is not just an eyesore; it can also damage your garage door. Rust can weaken the metal, making it more susceptible to dents and other damage. Rust can also interfere with the door’s operation, making it harder to open and close the door. It can also cause the door to become stuck in one position. Removing rust from your garage door is a must to keep it functioning properly and looking nice.

What You Need

To remove rust from your garage door, you’ll need a few supplies: steel wool, sandpaper, a wire brush, and a rust remover product. You should also wear appropriate safety gear, including gloves, goggles and a face mask, to protect yourself from the chemicals in the rust remover.

Step 1: Scrub with Steel Wool

Start by using steel wool to scrub the rust off the door. Use a circular motion to really work the steel wool into the rust. If the rust is particularly stubborn, try using a wire brush instead of steel wool. Be sure to scrub gently to avoid damaging the metal of the door.

Step 2: Sand the Area

Once you’ve removed as much of the rust as possible, use sandpaper to sand down the affected area. This will help to smooth out any ridges or bumps left behind by the rust. Use a fine-grit sandpaper to avoid damaging the metal.

Step 3: Use a Rust Remover

Next, use a rust remover product to remove any remaining rust. Follow the instructions on the product label to ensure that you use the product correctly and safely. Most rust remover products require you to apply the product to the affected area and let it sit for a few minutes before wiping it off. Once you’ve wiped off the product, you should be left with a rust-free surface.

Step 4: Coat with Paint or Primer

Finally, you'll need to coat the area with a paint or primer to protect it from future rust. Apply a thin coat of paint or primer to the affected area and let it dry. This will help to protect the door from future rust and keep it looking nice.

Conclusion

Rust can be a major problem for garage doors, but it doesn’t have to be. By following these four steps, you can easily remove rust from your garage door and keep it looking and functioning properly. It’s important to remember to wear the appropriate safety gear when using a rust remover product.
