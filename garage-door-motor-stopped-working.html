Quick Fixes When Your Garage Door Motor Stops Working

Introduction

If you rely on your garage door motor to open and close your garage door, then you know how frustrating it can be when it stops working. Luckily, there are some simple fixes you can try that may get your motor running again. Here are a few things to try before you call a professional for help.

Check the Power Source

The first thing to check is the power source. Make sure the power switch to your garage door opener is in the “on” position. If it’s in the “off” position, then your garage door motor won’t work. If you have an older garage door opener with an electrical outlet, make sure the outlet is working and that the cord is plugged in all the way.

Check the Garage Door Tracks

If the power source is working and your garage door motor still isn’t running, then you should check the garage door tracks. The tracks should be free of debris and should be properly aligned. If your tracks are clogged, then your garage door motor won’t work as it should. If you have an automatic garage door opener, then make sure the sensors are properly aligned.

Clean the Motor

If the power source and tracks are working properly and your garage door motor still won’t start, then you should clean it. Dirt and debris can build up on the motor, causing it to malfunction. Use a soft brush or cloth to gently clean the motor. Make sure you don’t get any liquid on the motor, as this can cause more damage.

Check the Fuses

If the power source, tracks, and motor are all clean and working properly, then you should check the fuses. If one of the fuses is blown, then it could be preventing the motor from running. You can find the fuses in the fuse box, usually located near the motor. Replace any blown fuses and see if that fixes the problem.

Check the Wiring

If none of the above solutions worked, then you should check the wiring. Make sure all the wires are connected properly and that there are no loose wires. Make sure all the wiring is in good condition and replace any wires that are damaged or worn. This should be done by a professional.

Check the Remote

If you’re using a remote to open and close your garage door, then you should also check the remote. Make sure the remote is properly programmed and that the batteries are working. If the remote isn’t working, then it could be preventing your garage door motor from starting.

Replace the Motor

If none of the above solutions worked, then you may need to replace the motor. Garage door motors don’t last forever, and they can become worn out over time. Replacing the motor yourself can be difficult, so you should hire a professional to do the job for you.

Conclusion

If your garage door motor has stopped working, don’t panic. There are a few simple fixes you can try before calling a professional for help. Check the power source, tracks, motor, fuses, wiring, and remote to see if any of those are the cause of the problem. If none of these solutions work, then you may need to replace the motor altogether.
