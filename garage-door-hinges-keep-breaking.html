Why Do Garage Door Hinges Keep Breaking?

Garage door hinges keep breaking because they are subject to heavy use and high levels of stress. If you're experiencing a lot of breakages, it can be a sign that your door is not properly maintained or installed. In this article, we'll take a look at some of the most common reasons for garage door hinge breakages, as well as some tips for avoiding them in the future.

Reasons for Garage Door Hinge Breakage

The most common reasons for garage door hinges breaking include:

  • Poor maintenance: If your garage door isn't regularly maintained, the hinges can become worn out and start to break. This includes lubrication, tightening of screws, and making sure the door is properly balanced.
  • Faulty installation: If your garage door is incorrectly installed, the hinges may not be able to handle the weight or stress of the door. This can result in them breaking or simply not functioning properly.
  • Rust: Rust can form on the hinges and weaken them, resulting in them breaking. Regular maintenance and lubrication can help to avoid this.
  • Improper use: If your garage door is used for activities that it's not designed for, such as pulling a trailer, it can put undue strain on the hinges and cause them to break.

How to Prevent Garage Door Hinge Breakage

To help prevent your garage door hinges from breaking, there are a few things you can do:

  • Regular maintenance: Make sure you regularly check and maintain your garage door, including lubricating the hinges and tightening any screws or bolts. This will help to keep them in good condition.
  • Check the balance: Make sure your garage door is properly balanced. If it isn't, it can put extra strain on the hinges and cause them to break.
  • Check for rust: Regularly check your garage door hinges for rust. If you spot any, make sure to remove it and lubricate the hinges.
  • Avoid improper use: Make sure your garage door is only used for what it's designed for. Avoid using it for activities such as pulling a trailer as this can put extra strain on the hinges and cause them to break.

Tips for Replacing Garage Door Hinges

If your garage door hinges are broken, you'll need to replace them as soon as possible. Here are some tips to help you do this:

  • Choose the right hinges: Make sure you pick the right type of hinges for your door. You can find out what type you need by consulting your door's manual.
  • Remove the old hinges: Remove the old hinges by unscrewing them from the door. Make sure you keep all the pieces in case you need them for future repairs.
  • Install the new hinges: Carefully install the new hinges and make sure they are secure. If your door has multiple hinges, make sure to install them in the correct order.
  • Check the balance: After installing the new hinges, check that your door is properly balanced. If it isn't, it can put extra strain on the hinges and cause them to break.

Conclusion

Garage door hinges keep breaking because they are subject to heavy use and high levels of stress. Poor maintenance, faulty installation, rust, and improper use can all lead to hinge breakage. To avoid this, make sure you regularly maintain your door and check it for rust. If your hinges do break, make sure to replace them quickly and carefully.
