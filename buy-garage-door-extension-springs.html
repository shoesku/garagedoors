Everything You Need To Know Before You Buy Garage Door Extension Springs

Garage doors are one of the most important components of any home. Not only do they provide security, but they also add a great deal of curb appeal to your property. Unfortunately, like any other mechanical component, garage doors can experience wear and tear over time. One of the most common repairs that homeowners may need is replacing the extension springs on their garage door.

Extension springs are an important part of any garage door system. They are responsible for counterbalancing the weight of the door, allowing it to open and close smoothly. Without the springs, the door won’t be able to open and close correctly, putting your home at risk. If your garage door is experiencing these issues, it’s likely time to replace the springs.

How To Know When It's Time To Replace Your Garage Door Extension Springs

It’s important to understand the symptoms of failing extension springs in order to know when it’s time to replace them. Knowing the warning signs will help you address the issue before it becomes a bigger problem. Here are some of the most common signs that it’s time to replace the extension springs in your garage door:

  • The door is not opening or closing properly, or is moving slower than usual.
  • The door is making strange noises, such as creaking or squeaking.
  • The door suddenly drops or slams shut when it is opened or closed.
  • The door is off balance, or is not opening or closing evenly.
  • The door is stuck in the open position and won’t close.

If you’re experiencing any of these issues, it’s likely time to replace your garage door extension springs. It’s important to note that it’s best to call a professional for this type of repair. Extension springs are under a great deal of tension, and can be dangerous if you don’t have the proper experience. Attempting to repair or replace the springs yourself can be dangerous, so it’s best to call a professional.

What To Look For When Buying Garage Door Extension Springs

When it’s time to replace your extension springs, you’ll need to find the right ones for your garage door system. The good news is that there are a few things you can do to ensure that you buy the right springs. Here are some tips to help you find the right garage door extension springs:

  • Measure the length and diameter of the existing springs. This will help you find the right size replacement springs.
  • Check the weight rating of the existing springs. This will help you find the right weight rating for the replacement springs.
  • Check the manufacturer’s website for the recommended replacement springs for your specific garage door system.
  • Be sure to buy the springs from a reputable dealer.

By following these tips, you can be sure that you’re buying the right springs for your garage door system. Of course, you should always consult with a professional for help with this type of repair.

How Much Do Garage Door Extension Springs Cost?

The cost of garage door extension springs will vary depending on the size, weight, and type of springs you need. Generally speaking, you can expect to pay anywhere from $20-$50 per spring, depending on the type and size. However, the cost of installation should also be taken into consideration, as this will add to the overall cost of the repair.

DIY Garage Door Extension Springs Installation

As mentioned, it’s best to call a professional for this type of repair. However, if you’re feeling confident and want to attempt the repair yourself, here are some tips to help you:

  • Be sure to wear the proper safety equipment, such as gloves, safety glasses, and work boots.
  • Be sure to loosen the tension on the existing springs before attempting to remove them.
  • Be sure to have a partner help you with the installation process.
  • Be sure to follow the manufacturer’s instructions for installation.
  • Be sure to test the door after installation to make sure it operates correctly.

By following these tips, you can ensure that you’re safely and properly installing new garage door extension springs. Remember, if you’re not sure how to proceed, it’s best to call a professional for help.

Conclusion

Garage door extension springs are an important part of any garage door system. They’re responsible for counterbalancing the weight of the door, allowing it to open and close smoothly. Knowing the warning signs of a failing spring will help you address the issue before it becomes a bigger problem. When it’s time to replace the springs, be sure to buy the right ones for your system, and call a professional for help with the installation. Following these tips will ensure that your garage door is working properly and providing the security and convenience that you need.
