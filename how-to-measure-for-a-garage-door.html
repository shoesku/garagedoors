How To Measure For A Garage Door

Introduction

Choosing the right garage door for your home is an important decision. It not only adds to the overall look and feel of your property, but it can also affect the security and energy efficiency of your home. If you’re looking to replace your existing garage door, it’s essential to measure the door opening correctly so you can choose the right size and type of door for your needs. In this article, we’ll show you how to measure for a garage door correctly and what other measurements you need to take into account.

Measure the Width

The first step when measuring for a garage door is to measure the width of the opening. To do this, use a tape measure and measure from the left side of the opening to the right side. Make sure you’re measuring from the inside of the door frame and take three measurements across the width. If the measurements differ slightly, record the shortest width. This will ensure you purchase a garage door that fits correctly.

Measure the Height

Once you’ve measured the width of the garage door opening, you need to measure the height. Measure from the top to the bottom and take three measurements. Again, if the measurements differ, record the shortest height. When measuring for a garage door, you should note that the majority of residential garage door sizes come in a range of heights, usually between 6ft and 8ft.

Measure the Headroom

When measuring for a garage door, you also need to measure the headroom. This is the space between the top of the door opening and the ceiling or any other obstructions, such as lights or beams. This measurement is essential as it will determine what type of door you can have installed. If there’s not enough headroom, you may need to consider installing a low headroom garage door, which requires more complex installation.

Measure the Backroom

The next measurement you need to take is the backroom. This is the space between the back of the door opening and any obstructions, such as walls or columns. Generally, you need 6-8 inches of backroom for a garage door to open and close correctly. If there’s not enough backroom, you may need to install a space-saving garage door, which uses a torsion spring mounted over the opening.

Measure the Side Space

The side space is the space between the left and right sides of the door opening and any walls or beams. Generally, you need at least 3-4 inches on either side of the door opening for the door to open and close correctly. If you don’t have enough space, you may need to install an overhead door, which is designed to open and close without any side space.

Measure the Track

The next step is to measure the track. This is the space between the top of the door opening and the ceiling. Generally, you need 10-12 inches of track space for a standard garage door to open and close correctly. If there’s not enough track space, you may need to install a low headroom door, which requires a more complex installation.

Measure the Opening

Finally, you need to measure the opening of the door. To do this, measure from the left side of the opening to the right side and then measure from the top to the bottom. Record your measurements and compare them to the measurements you took earlier. If they match, then you’ve measured the door opening correctly.

Conclusion

Measuring for a garage door correctly is essential to ensure you purchase the right type and size of door for your home. By following the steps outlined above, you should have no trouble measuring the door opening and finding the right garage door for your needs. If you need any help or advice, be sure to contact a reputable garage door company for assistance.
