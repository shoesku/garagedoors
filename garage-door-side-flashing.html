Side Flashing: What Is It And How To Install It

Side flashing is an important element of any garage door installation. It helps to protect the sides of the door from water and other elements that could cause damage. If you are replacing an existing garage door, it is important to make sure that you install side flashing as part of the new installation.

Side flashing is made from metal and is installed on the sides of the door. It helps to direct water away from the door and protect it from the elements. It is usually installed on the outside of the door, and should be sealed along the edges with weatherstrip or caulk.

Why Is Side Flashing So Important?

Side flashing serves a few important functions when it comes to protecting your garage door. It helps to protect the sides of the door from moisture, which can cause the door to rust and weaken over time. It also helps to prevent water from entering the garage, which can cause flooding and other damage to the structure of the building. Finally, it helps to keep out pests and animals that may try to enter the garage.

In addition to its protective functions, side flashing adds an aesthetic element to the garage door. It helps to give the door a finished look, and can help to compliment the color and style of the door.

What Materials Are Used To Create Side Flashing?

Side flashing is usually made from aluminum, steel, or vinyl. Each of these materials has its own advantages and disadvantages, so it is important to research and choose the material that is best for your specific application. Aluminum is the most common material used for side flashing, as it is lightweight, durable, and easy to install. Steel is a bit more durable and resistant to weathering, but can be more difficult to install. Vinyl side flashing is the most affordable option, but can be less durable and may need to be replaced more often.

How to Install Side Flashing

Installing side flashing is not a difficult task, but it is important to do it correctly to ensure that it is properly protected. Here are a few tips to help you with your installation:

  • Measure the area where the side flashing will be installed.
  • Cut the side flashing to fit the area.
  • Install the side flashing using screws or nails.
  • Seal the edges of the side flashing with weatherstrip or caulk.
  • Check the flashing regularly to ensure that it is in good condition.

Installing side flashing is a fairly simple task, but it is important to make sure that it is done properly. Following the steps above will help to ensure that your side flashing is installed correctly and will last for many years.

Conclusion

Side flashing is an important element of any garage door installation. It helps to protect the sides of the door from the elements, as well as adding an aesthetic element to the door. Installing side flashing is a fairly simple task, but it is important to make sure that it is done correctly. Following the steps outlined above will help to ensure that your side flashing is installed correctly and will last for many years.
