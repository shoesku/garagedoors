Genie Garage Door Openers Overview

Genie garage door openers are some of the best and most reliable on the market. They are designed to last for years and provide years of trouble-free use. With such a reliable design, it can be a surprise and a hassle when your Genie garage door opener won't close with the remote. Fortunately, this is a problem that can be solved and can be done so without having to call in a professional.

Genie garage door openers use a variety of different designs and technologies, but most of the modern models use a powerful DC motor and a belt drive system. This allows for a quieter operation and more reliable performance. The remote control system allows users to open and close the door from a distance, making it a convenient and secure way to get into and out of your garage.

Common Issues With Genie Garage Door Openers

The most common issue with Genie garage door openers not closing with the remote is a faulty remote. This is usually due to a weak or dead battery, or simply a malfunction in the remote itself. In either case, it is important to check the batteries, and if they are dead, replace them. If the batteries are fine, then it is likely that the remote itself needs to be replaced.

Another issue that can cause a Genie garage door opener not to close with the remote is a malfunctioning safety sensor. The safety sensors are designed to detect when something is blocking the path of the door. If something is blocking the path, the door will not close. It is important to check for any objects that could be blocking the path, and if there are any, remove them.

Troubleshooting Genie Garage Door Openers

The first step in troubleshooting a Genie garage door opener not closing with the remote is to check the remote itself. Make sure that the batteries are fresh and that the remote is functioning properly. If the remote is not working, then it may need to be replaced.

Once the remote is checked, it is important to check the safety sensors. Make sure that nothing is blocking the path of the door. If something is blocking the path, then remove it and try again.

If the safety sensors are functioning properly, then it is likely that the issue is with the opener itself. Make sure that the wires are firmly connected and that the power is on. If the power is not on, then check the circuit breaker or the fuse box.

Replacing Genie Garage Door Openers

If the above steps do not resolve the issue, then it may be necessary to replace the Genie garage door opener. This is best done by a professional, as it requires knowledge of wiring and installation. However, it is possible to do it yourself if you are comfortable with the process.

To replace the Genie garage door opener, first disconnect the power to the unit. Then, remove the existing opener and the wiring from the wall. Then, install the new opener according to the manufacturer's instructions. Finally, connect the wiring and power, and test the opener to ensure that it is working properly.

Conclusion

In conclusion, if your Genie garage door opener is not closing with the remote, it is likely due to a faulty remote, a malfunctioning safety sensor, or an issue with the opener itself. Check the remote first, and then check the safety sensors. If necessary, replace the opener and the wiring. With a little bit of troubleshooting, you should be able to get your Genie garage door opener working properly again.
