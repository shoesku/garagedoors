How To Adjust Your Garage Door Auto Reverse Feature

If you're like most homeowners, you already have a garage door that's equipped with a safety feature known as auto reverse. This is a great feature that will reverse the door if it senses something in its path. However, if your garage door isn't working properly, you may need to adjust your auto reverse feature. Here's how to do it.

Step 1: Check the Manual

Before you start adjusting your garage door's auto reverse feature, it's important to check your owner's manual. This will ensure that you know exactly how to adjust the feature properly. It may also contain some helpful troubleshooting tips that can help you fix the issue without needing to adjust the feature.

Step 2: Locate the Adjustment Screws

Once you've read the manual, the next step is to locate the adjustment screws. These are usually located near the bottom of the garage door, and they should be easy to spot. Once you've located the screws, you can start adjusting them.

Step 3: Adjust the Sensitivity

Once you've located the adjustment screws, you'll need to adjust the sensitivity. To do this, you'll need to turn the screws clockwise to make the auto reverse feature more sensitive, or counter-clockwise to make it less sensitive. Depending on the type of garage door you have, you may need to adjust the sensitivity in both directions.

Step 4: Test the Feature

Once you've adjusted the sensitivity, it's time to test the feature. To do this, you'll need to open the garage door and place a block of wood or a broom handle in its path. If the door reverses when it comes in contact with the object, then the auto reverse feature is working properly. If it doesn't reverse, then you'll need to adjust the sensitivity again.

Step 5: Replace the Safety Sensor Batteries

If you find that the auto reverse feature isn't working properly after adjusting the sensitivity, then you may need to replace the safety sensor batteries. These are usually located near the top of the door, and they should be easy to spot. Once you've replaced the batteries, you can test the feature again to make sure it's working properly.

Step 6: Contact a Professional

If you're still having trouble getting the auto reverse feature to work properly, then it may be time to contact a professional. A qualified technician can inspect the garage door and make any necessary adjustments or repairs that may be needed. This is the best way to ensure that your garage door is safe and secure.

Conclusion

Adjusting your garage door's auto reverse feature can be a relatively simple process, but it's important to follow the steps above carefully. Doing so will help ensure that your garage door is safe and secure at all times. If you're still having trouble, then it may be time to contact a professional for help.
