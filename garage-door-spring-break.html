What To Do When Your Garage Door Spring Breaks

Best Garage doors and references for house or residential with custom material and more design. We Share more picture design with HD quality images.

If you’ve ever heard a loud bang and then found that you can’t open your garage door, chances are, you’ve experienced a broken garage door spring. Garage door springs are an essential part of your garage door and can be difficult to repair. It’s best to leave the repair to a professional technician since it can be dangerous to handle a broken spring. This article will give you an overview of what happens when a garage door spring breaks and what to do in the event of a break.

What Are Garage Door Springs?

Garage door springs are a type of torsion spring that are located on either side of your garage door. They’re responsible for powering the lifting and lowering of the garage door. They also help to counterbalance the weight of the garage door, making it easier to lift. Without them, it would be much more difficult to open and close the door.

What Causes Garage Door Springs to Break?

Garage door springs are made of high-strength steel and are designed to last for many years. However, they can break due to a variety of reasons. Some of the most common causes of a broken garage door spring include:

  • Excessive wear and tear
  • Frequent use
  • Improper installation
  • Rust and corrosion
  • Extreme weather

What Happens When a Garage Door Spring Breaks?

When a garage door spring breaks, it can cause the garage door to become unbalanced and unable to open or close. This is because the spring is responsible for powering the lifting and lowering of the door. When the spring breaks, the door is no longer able to lift the weight of the door, which can cause the door to become stuck in place.

What Should I Do if My Garage Door Spring Breaks?

If you’ve noticed that your garage door is stuck in place and you think that the spring may be the cause, it’s best to contact a professional repair technician. This is because attempting to repair the spring yourself can be dangerous, as the springs are under a lot of tension. A professional technician will be able to safely repair or replace the broken spring.

How Can I Prevent My Garage Door Spring From Breaking?

The best way to prevent a garage door spring from breaking is to make sure it’s properly maintained. This includes regularly lubricating the hinges, rollers, and springs, as well as checking for signs of corrosion or rust. Additionally, it’s important to make sure the springs are properly installed and that they are of the correct size and tension for your garage door.

Conclusion

Garage door springs are a crucial part of your garage door’s operation and when they break, it can be a hassle to repair them. It’s best to leave the repair to a professional technician since attempting to do it yourself can be dangerous. Additionally, regular maintenance can help to prevent the springs from breaking in the first place. Hopefully, this article has given you a better understanding of what happens when a garage door spring breaks and what you should do if it happens to you.
