Troubleshooting Your Garage Door When It Makes Noise But Doesn't Open

Best Garage doors and references for house or residential with custom material and more design. We Share more picture design with HD quality images.

Having a noisy garage door can be quite a nuisance. If you’re dealing with a garage door that makes plenty of noise when you try to open it, but fails to open, then you’re in the right place. In this article, you will learn how to troubleshoot your noisy garage door, in order to get it back up and running. It’s important to remember that you should never attempt to repair or replace any part of your garage door on your own, as this could be dangerous. Always contact a professional for any repairs or replacements.

Locate the Source of the Noise

The first step to troubleshooting your noisy garage door is to locate the source of the noise. Most garage doors make noise when they are opened or closed, but if the noise is unusually loud, it could be a sign of a problem. The most common sources of noise are the rollers, the hinges, and the tracks. If you find that the noise is coming from one of these areas, it’s important to take action right away.

Check the Rollers

The rollers are one of the most common culprits when it comes to noisy garage doors. If the rollers are worn or broken, it can cause the garage door to make a loud noise when it is opened or closed. If the rollers are covered in dirt, dust, or debris, this can also cause the noise. To fix this problem, you will need to replace the rollers. This is a job that should be done by a professional.

Inspect the Hinges

The hinges on your garage door can also cause a loud noise when it is opened or closed. If the hinges are worn or broken, it can cause the door to make an unpleasant, loud noise. To fix this problem, you will need to replace the hinges. This is a job that should be done by a professional.

Check the Tracks

Another possible source of the noise is the tracks. If the tracks are bent or damaged, this can cause the door to make a loud noise when it is opened or closed. To fix this problem, you will need to replace the tracks. This is a job that should be done by a professional.

Check the Springs

The springs on your garage door can also cause a loud noise when it is opened or closed. If the springs are worn or broken, it can cause the door to make an unpleasant, loud noise. To fix this problem, you will need to replace the springs. This is a job that should be done by a professional.

Check the Opener

The opener is another possible source of the noise. If the opener is worn or broken, it can cause the door to make a loud noise when it is opened or closed. To fix this problem, you will need to replace the opener. This is a job that should be done by a professional.

Check the Safety Sensors

The safety sensors on your garage door can also cause a loud noise when it is opened or closed. If the sensors are worn or broken, it can cause the door to make an unpleasant, loud noise. To fix this problem, you will need to replace the sensors. This is a job that should be done by a professional.

Check the Cables

The cables on your garage door can also be a source of noise. If the cables are worn or broken, it can cause the door to make a loud noise when it is opened or closed. To fix this problem, you will need to replace the cables. This is a job that should be done by a professional.

Conclusion

If your garage door is making noise but not opening, you should take action right away. The most common causes of noise are the rollers, the hinges, the tracks, the springs, the opener, the safety sensors, and the cables. All of these parts can be replaced by a professional. If you have any questions or concerns, it’s best to contact a professional right away.
