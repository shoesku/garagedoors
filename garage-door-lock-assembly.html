How To Assemble A Garage Door Lock

Overview

Assembling a garage door lock is a relatively straightforward process that requires only basic tools and a few minutes of your time. With the right supplies and steps, you can quickly and easily install a new lock on your garage door.

The average homeowner can complete this process in 15-30 minutes, depending on the type of lock they are installing and their level of experience. In this guide, we'll cover the supplies you'll need and the steps to follow to properly assemble your garage door lock.

What You'll Need

Before you start assembling your lock, make sure you have the following supplies.

  • Garage door lock kit
  • Drill and drill bits
  • Screwdriver
  • Tape measure
  • Level
  • Pencil

Once you have all the supplies, you're ready to start assembling your garage door lock.

Step One: Measure the Door

The first step is to measure the door to ensure that the lock you purchased will fit properly. Using a tape measure, measure the width and height of the door from the outside. Also, measure the distance from the door to the edge of the frame.

Write down the measurements and compare them to the specifications on the lock you purchased. Make sure that the lock will fit the door properly before proceeding.

Step Two: Mark the Locations

The next step is to mark the locations for the lock and the strike plate. Place the lock on the door and use a pencil to mark the location of the screws. Then, measure and mark the location of the strike plate on the door jamb.

Make sure that the lock and the strike plate are properly aligned and that the screws will fit into the holes in the door and the door jamb.

Step Three: Install the Lock

The next step is to install the lock. Start by placing the lock on the door and lining it up with the marks you made in step two. Then, use a drill and the appropriate drill bit to create pilot holes for the screws.

Once the pilot holes are drilled, use a screwdriver to secure the lock to the door. Make sure the lock is level before tightening the screws.

Step Four: Install the Strike Plate

The next step is to install the strike plate. Place the strike plate on the door jamb and line it up with the marks you made in step two. Then, use a drill and the appropriate drill bit to create pilot holes for the screws.

Once the pilot holes are drilled, use a screwdriver to secure the strike plate to the door jamb. Make sure the strike plate is level before tightening the screws.

Step Five: Test the Lock

The final step is to test the lock. Open and close the door several times to make sure the lock is working properly. Make sure the lock is engaging and disengaging correctly and that the door is properly secured when the lock is engaged.

If the lock is not working properly, double-check the installation to make sure everything is installed correctly. If needed, adjust the screws to ensure the lock is properly secured.

Conclusion

Assembling a garage door lock is a relatively straightforward process that can be completed in 15-30 minutes. With the right supplies and steps, you can quickly and easily install a new lock on your garage door.

Follow the steps outlined in this guide and you'll be able to assemble your garage door lock in no time.
