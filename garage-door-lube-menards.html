Tips To Keep Your Garage Door Lubricated With Menards

As one of the most used elements of your home, your garage door needs regular maintenance and lubrication to keep it running smoothly. Menards offers a wide selection of lubricants to help you keep your garage door in good condition. In this article, we’ll go over some of the best types of lubricants for your garage door and some tips for using them. Keep reading to learn more about the best garage door lubricants from Menards and how to use them!

Types of Garage Door Lubricants

When it comes to lubricating your garage door, there are a few different types of lubricants to choose from. The type of lubricant you choose will depend on the type of garage door you have and the type of parts that need to be lubricated. Here are a few of the most common types of lubricants that Menards carries.

WD-40

One of the most popular lubricants is WD-40. WD-40 is a multi-purpose lubricant that can be used for a variety of different applications. It can be used to lubricate door tracks, springs, and other moving parts. It can also help to clean and protect the metal surfaces on your garage door.

White Lithium Grease

White lithium grease is a great option for lubricating the moving parts of your garage door. It’s a thick, viscous lubricant that can help to keep your door running smoothly. It can be used on hinges, tracks, and other metal parts. White lithium grease is a good choice for garage doors that are exposed to the elements, as it’s resistant to heat, water, and rust.

Silicone Spray

Silicone spray is another popular lubricant for garage doors. It can be used to lubricate the door track, springs, and other metal parts. It’s easy to apply and it won’t leave a greasy mess. Silicone spray can also help to protect the metal surfaces from rust and corrosion.

Tips for Using Garage Door Lubricants

Now that you know a bit more about the different types of lubricants available, let’s go over some tips for using them properly. Here are a few key tips to keep in mind when lubricating your garage door.

Clean the Area First

Before you apply any lubricant, make sure to clean the area first. This will help to remove any dirt and debris that could interfere with the lubricant’s effectiveness. Use a damp cloth or a brush to clean the area before you apply the lubricant.

Apply Lightly

When you’re applying the lubricant, use only a light amount. Too much lubricant can actually create a sticky, gummy residue that can attract dirt and debris. So it’s best to use only a light amount and to make sure you don’t overapply.

Reapply Regularly

It’s important to reapply the lubricant on a regular basis. This will help to keep your garage door running smoothly and reduce the risk of it becoming stuck or jammed. A good rule of thumb is to reapply the lubricant every three months or so.

Conclusion

Keeping your garage door lubricated is an important part of its maintenance. Menards offers a wide selection of lubricants to choose from, so you can easily find the right one for your garage door. Just make sure to apply the lubricant properly and to reapply it regularly for optimal performance. With the right lubricant and a little bit of effort, your garage door can stay in great condition for years to come!
