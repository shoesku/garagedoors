Troubleshooting Garage Door Closes, Then Reopens

Garage doors are large, complex mechanisms that require consistent maintenance and attention. When a garage door closes, then reopens, it can be frustrating and cause a disruption in your day. It is important to understand why your garage door may be experiencing this issue and how to fix it. In this article, we will discuss the common issues that can cause a garage door to close, then reopen, and how to troubleshoot them.

Common Causes of a Garage Door Closing, Then Reopening

There are several common causes of a garage door closing, then reopening. These include:

  • Obstruction in the track: If there is an obstruction in the track, the garage door may close, then reopen as it encounters the obstruction. This could be caused by debris, a pet, or even a person walking in front of the door. If you notice that your garage door is closing, then reopening, check the track for any obstructions.
  • Sensor issues: Garage doors have sensors that prevent them from closing if something is in the way. If the sensors are not aligned properly, or if they are dirty or damaged, the garage door may not sense the obstruction and will close, then reopen.
  • Limit switch: The limit switch is a safety device that prevents the door from closing if it senses that the door is not closed completely. If the limit switch is not functioning properly, the garage door may close, then reopen when it senses that the door is not completely closed.
  • Worn out parts: Worn out parts can cause the garage door to close, then reopen. If the rollers are worn out, the door may not slide properly on the track. If the springs are worn out, the door may not lift properly. If the opener is worn out, it may not be able to lift the door properly.

How to Troubleshoot a Garage Door Closing, Then Reopening

Once you have identified the potential cause of the issue, you can begin troubleshooting your garage door. Here are some tips for troubleshooting a garage door that closes, then reopens:

  • Check the track: If you notice that your garage door is closing, then reopening, check the track for any obstructions. If you find an obstruction, remove it and see if the issue is resolved.
  • Clean and adjust the sensors: If the sensors are dirty or misaligned, they may not be able to sense an obstruction. Clean the sensors with a damp cloth and adjust them so that they are pointing in the same direction.
  • Adjust the limit switch: If the limit switch is not functioning properly, you can adjust it to ensure that the door is completely closed before it will close. Consult your owner’s manual for instructions on how to adjust the limit switch.
  • Replace worn out parts: If the rollers, springs, or opener are worn out, they may be causing the issue. Replacing these parts can help resolve the issue and ensure that your garage door operates properly.

Conclusion

Garage doors that close, then reopen can be frustrating, but they can usually be fixed with a few simple troubleshooting steps. Be sure to check the track, clean and adjust the sensors, adjust the limit switch, and replace any worn out parts. With these tips, you can ensure that your garage door operates properly and stays closed.
