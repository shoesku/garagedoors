Types of Garage Door Extension Springs

There are different types of garage door extension springs that can be used depending on the size and weight of your garage door. The most common type is the standard spring that is usually found in residential garages. It is a simple coil spring that is designed to be able to take the weight of the door and help it to open and close properly. Other types of springs include the torsion spring, which is a bit more difficult to install, and the extension spring, which is designed to help the door move up and down.

Installing Garage Door Extension Springs White

When installing garage door extension springs, it is important to make sure that the springs are properly aligned. This can be done by using a level or a tape measure to check that the springs are level with the ground. Once the springs have been aligned, the next step is to attach the springs to the door. This is done by using screws and nuts, and the springs must be tightened down so that the door is securely attached.

Maintaining Garage Door Extension Springs White

Once the garage door extension springs have been installed, it is important to make sure that they are maintained properly. This means that the springs should be checked regularly to make sure that they are not becoming loose or damaged. If there is any damage, it is important to replace the springs as soon as possible. It is also important to make sure that the springs are lubricated regularly in order to keep them in good working order.

Replacing Garage Door Extension Springs White

If the garage door extension springs are not working properly, it may be necessary to replace them. This should be done by an experienced professional, as it can be a very dangerous job. Safety Tips for Garage Door Extension Springs White
