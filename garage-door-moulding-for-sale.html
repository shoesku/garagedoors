Types of Garage Door Moulding

There are many different types of garage door moulding available. The type of moulding you choose will depend on the look and feel you’re trying to achieve. Some of the most popular options include:

  • Traditional mouldings, which are usually made from wood and feature a classic, timeless look.
  • Contemporary mouldings, which are usually made from plastic or metal and feature a modern, sleek look.
  • Rustic mouldings, which are usually made from wood and feature a rustic, natural look.
  • Vintage mouldings, which are usually made from wood and feature an antique, classic look.
  • Custom mouldings, which can be made from any material and feature a unique, one-of-a-kind look.

No matter which type of moulding you choose, you’ll want to make sure it’s compatible with your existing garage door and any other components, such as door handles, locks, and hinges.

Materials Used for Garage Door Moulding

Garage door moulding is typically made from wood, plastic, or metal. Each type of material offers its own advantages and disadvantages, so it’s important to consider your needs before making a purchase.

  • Wood moulding is a classic look and is typically more affordable. It’s also easy to install and can be painted or stained to match your existing garage door.
  • Plastic moulding is lightweight and easy to maintain. It’s also relatively affordable, but it may not be as durable as other materials.
  • Metal moulding is strong and durable. It’s usually more expensive than other materials, but it can last for years with minimal maintenance.

When selecting a material for your garage door moulding, you should also consider the climate in your area. For example, if you live in an area with extreme temperatures, you may want to opt for metal moulding, as it is more resistant to extreme temperatures.
