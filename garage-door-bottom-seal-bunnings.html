The Benefits of a Garage Door Bottom Seal

A garage door bottom seal is designed to prevent water, dust, and pests from entering your garage. It is an essential part of your garage door, as it helps to protect your home from the elements. Without this seal, water can get in through the bottom of your garage door, causing damage to the walls and floors. This can lead to expensive repairs in the future, not to mention the cost of energy loss from the drafts that can come in through the bottom of the door.

The seal also helps to keep out dust, debris, and pests. Without a garage door bottom seal, dust can get into the garage and clog up the air filter, causing your air conditioning and heating systems to work harder and waste energy. Pests can also enter your garage through the bottom of the door, which can lead to infestations that are difficult to get rid of.

How to Install a Garage Door Bottom Seal Bunnings

Installing a garage door bottom seal is a relatively easy project that most people can do themselves. Start by measuring the length and width of your garage door. Then, use a tape measure to measure the area where you want to install the seal. Once you have the measurements, you can purchase the correct size seal from Bunnings.

The next step is to install the seal. Start by attaching the seal to the bottom of the door with the adhesive provided. Then, use a hammer and nails to secure the seal to the door frame. Make sure to use long nails that will penetrate deep into the frame so the seal is secure.

Finally, you need to check the seal for any gaps or holes. If you find any, use a sealant or caulk to fill them in. This will ensure that the seal is airtight and watertight.
