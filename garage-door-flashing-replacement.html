Garage Door Flashing Replacement - A Comprehensive Guide

Garage Door Flashing is an important part of your home’s exterior, providing protection from the elements for your garage door and the items stored inside. Over time, the flashing can become worn out and require replacement. If this happens, it’s important to know how to replace your garage door flashing correctly, as improper installation can lead to water damage and other costly repairs. In this guide, we’ll show you the steps you need to take to replace your garage door flashing.

Tools and Materials Needed

Before you can start replacing your garage door flashing, you’ll need to gather the necessary tools and materials. You’ll need a utility knife, a caulking gun, a measuring tape, a putty knife, a hammer, a screwdriver, and roofing tar. You’ll also need to purchase new flashing material. You can usually find this at your local home improvement store.

Step 1: Remove the Old Flashing

The first step in replacing your garage door flashing is to remove the existing flashing. This can be done using a utility knife or a putty knife. Carefully cut the old flashing away from the door frame and then remove it. Make sure to remove any nails or screws that were used to attach the old flashing to the frame.

Step 2: Measure and Cut the New Flashing

Once you’ve removed the old flashing, you’ll need to measure and cut the new flashing to fit in its place. Measure the area where the flashing will go and then cut the flashing to fit. Be sure to leave a few inches of extra material so that you can secure it to the door frame.

Step 3: Secure the Flashing to the Door Frame

Once you’ve cut the new flashing to size, you’ll need to secure it to the door frame. Begin by hammering a few nails into the flashing along the bottom edge. Then, use a screwdriver to attach the flashing to the door frame. Make sure the flashing is secure and that there are no gaps between the flashing and the door frame.

Step 4: Seal the Edges with Roofing Tar

Once the flashing is secure, you’ll need to seal the edges with roofing tar. Using a caulking gun, apply a generous amount of roofing tar around the edges of the flashing. Allow the tar to dry and then check to make sure the edges are properly sealed.

Step 5: Test the Flashing

Once you’ve sealed the edges of the flashing, it’s time to test it. Close your garage door and spray it with a hose. If the water runs off and doesn’t pool around the edges of the door, then your flashing is installed correctly. If water does pool around the edges, then you’ll need to go back and seal the edges more securely.

Conclusion

Replacing your garage door flashing may seem daunting, but it’s actually a fairly simple process. All you need is the right tools and materials, and you can get the job done quickly and efficiently. Just remember to take your time and double check your work to ensure that the flashing is secure and properly sealed.
