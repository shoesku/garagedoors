Understanding The Different Garage Door Lock Position

The garage door lock position is an important security feature that can help protect your home from unauthorized access. It is essential to understand the various lock positions so you can ensure the safety of your family and property. This article provides an overview of the different types of garage door locks, their positions, and how to choose the right one for your needs.

The first type of garage door lock is the traditional deadbolt lock. This lock is a cylindrical lock with a keyhole in the center. When the key is inserted and turned, it locks the door in place. This lock is typically used on single car garages and is the most secure and reliable option. It is also available in different finishes, allowing you to match the style of your home.

Lever Handle Locks

The second type of garage door lock is the lever handle lock. This lock is a lever-style lock with a handle that is turned to unlock the door. This type of lock is typically used on double car garages and is less secure than the deadbolt lock. It is also available in different finishes, allowing you to match the style of your home.

Electronic Keypad Locks

The third type of garage door lock is the electronic keypad lock. This lock is a keypad-style lock with a code that is entered to unlock the door. This type of lock is typically used on multiple car garages and provides a higher level of security than the deadbolt and lever handle locks. It is also available in different finishes, allowing you to match the style of your home.

Remote Controlled Locks

The fourth type of garage door lock is the remote controlled lock. This lock is a keyless entry system with a remote control that is used to open the door. This type of lock is typically used on commercial garages and offers a higher level of security than the other locks. It is also available in different finishes, allowing you to match the style of your home.

Choosing the Right Lock for Your Garage Door

When choosing the right lock for your garage door, it is important to consider the security level that you require and the type of door you have. Deadbolt locks are the most secure option for single car garages, while lever handle locks are the best choice for double car garages. Electronic keypad locks and remote controlled locks are more secure options for multiple car garages. Additionally, it is important to choose a lock that matches the style of your home.

Installing the Lock in the Right Position

Once you have chosen the right lock for your garage door, the next step is to install it in the correct position. The lock should be installed so that the keyhole is facing downwards and the handle is facing away from the door. Additionally, the lock should be installed at a height that is easily accessible for you to use. This will ensure that the lock is secure and will prevent the door from being opened accidentally.

Conclusion

The garage door lock position is an important security feature that can help protect your home from unauthorized access. It is essential to understand the different types of locks and their positions, as well as to choose the right one for your needs. Additionally, it is important to install the lock in the right position to ensure that it is secure and that the door cannot be opened accidentally. By following these tips, you can ensure the safety of your home and family.
