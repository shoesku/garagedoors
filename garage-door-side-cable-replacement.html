How To Replace Garage Door Side Cables In 2023

Replacing the side cables on your garage door can seem like a daunting task. But, in reality, it's not only possible, but easy to do with the right tools and knowledge. Keep in mind that safety is always the number one priority when it comes to any kind of home repair or maintenance. Take the necessary precautions to ensure that your family is safe at all times.

Tools Needed for Garage Door Side Cable Replacement

Before you get started, make sure you have the right tools for the job. Here's what you'll need:

  • Socket Set
  • Wrench
  • Tape Measure
  • Screwdriver

You may also need a stepladder if you can't reach the garage door tracks, but it's not essential. Once you've gathered the supplies, you're ready to get started.

Step-by-Step Guide for Replacing Garage Door Side Cables

Follow these steps to replace your garage door side cables:

  1. Unplug the garage door opener and turn off the power.
  2. Place a stepladder under the door if necessary.
  3. Using the socket set, loosen the bolts that hold the cable to the door track.
  4. Remove the old cable and discard it.
  5. Measure the new cable and cut it to the same length as the old one.
  6. Thread the new cable through the pulley and attach it to the door track.
  7. Tighten the bolts to secure the new cable in place.
  8. Check the cable tension and adjust as needed.
  9. Plug in the garage door opener and turn the power back on.
  10. Test the garage door to make sure it is operating smoothly.

Tips for Replacing Garage Door Side Cables

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when replacing garage door side cables:

  • Always wear safety glasses when working with tools.
  • Don't overexert yourself while working on the door. Ask for help if needed.
  • Be sure to check the tension of the cable and adjust as needed.
  • After installation, test the door to make sure it is operating properly.

Conclusion

Replacing the side cables on your garage door is a fairly simple task that can be done in just a few steps. With the right tools and knowledge, you can have your garage door up and running again in no time. Just remember to always take safety precautions to ensure the safety of your family.
