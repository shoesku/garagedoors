What To Do If Your Garage Door Motor Is Clicking

Introduction

Garage door motors are an essential part of a functioning garage door system. Without a working motor, the door can't open, and the system won't work. Unfortunately, motors can be prone to problems, such as clicking. If your motor is clicking, it is important to take the necessary steps to ensure that the motor is functioning properly, so that your garage door system runs smoothly and safely.

Identifying the Problem

If you hear a clicking sound coming from your garage door motor, you should first identify the source of the sound. The clicking sound can be caused by a number of different problems, such as a bad motor capacitor, a faulty switch, or a broken gear. Identifying the source of the problem can help you determine the best course of action for fixing the issue.

Check the Motor Capacitor

One of the most common causes of clicking in a garage door motor is a bad motor capacitor. A motor capacitor helps the motor start and run smoothly. If the capacitor is faulty, the motor will not be able to start properly, and it may make a clicking sound. To check the motor capacitor, disconnect the power to the motor and remove the capacitor. If the capacitor is damaged or worn out, it will need to be replaced.

Replace the Switch

Another common cause of clicking in a garage door motor is a faulty switch. A switch is responsible for controlling the motor, and if it is damaged or worn out, the motor will not be able to function properly. To replace the switch, you will need to remove the old switch and install a new one. It is important to make sure that the new switch is compatible with your garage door motor, so that the motor will be able to function correctly.

Check the Gears

The gears in a garage door motor can also be a source of clicking. If the gears are damaged or worn out, they may cause the motor to make a clicking sound. To check the gears, you will need to remove the motor and inspect the gears for signs of damage. If the gears are damaged, they will need to be replaced with new ones.

Conclusion

If your garage door motor is clicking, it is important to take the necessary steps to identify the source of the problem and fix it. In most cases, the source of the problem is either a faulty motor capacitor, a faulty switch, or damaged gears. To ensure that your garage door motor is functioning properly, it is important to take the time to inspect the motor and replace any faulty or damaged parts.
