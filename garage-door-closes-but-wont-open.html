How To Fix A Garage Door That Closes But Won't Open

It's a common problem for many homeowners - the garage door closes, but won't open. If you're in this situation, you're probably looking for some quick answers to get your door back in working order. Fortunately, most of the time, this problem is quite easy to diagnose and fix.

Check the Safety Sensors

One of the most common culprits behind a garage door that won't open is an issue with the safety sensors. These are the small sensors mounted at the bottom of your garage door frame. Their job is to detect when something is blocking the path of the door, and to keep the door from closing when that happens. If the sensors are misaligned or blocked, they won't be able to do their job properly, and the door won't open.

This is an easy problem to diagnose. First, check to make sure the sensors are mounted properly. They should be aligned and pointing directly at each other. If they're not, you'll need to adjust them. Then, make sure that there isn't any debris or other objects blocking the sensors. If nothing is blocking them, then the problem may be with the wiring or a faulty sensor, in which case you'll need to call a professional.

Check the Remote Control

Another possible cause of a garage door that won't open is an issue with the remote control. If your remote control isn't working, then your garage door won't open. This can be caused by a variety of things - the batteries may be dead, the remote may be out of range, or the antenna may be damaged. The first step is to replace the batteries. Then, make sure that the antenna is in good condition and that the remote is within range.

If the problem still persists, then it's likely that the remote is damaged or malfunctioning. In that case, you'll need to purchase a new remote control. Most garage door openers come with a remote control, but you can also purchase aftermarket remotes from many home improvement stores.

Check the Garage Door Motor

If neither of the above solutions works, then the problem may be with the garage door motor. The motor is what actually lifts and lowers the door, so if it's malfunctioning, then the door won't open. This is a more difficult problem to diagnose, so it's best to call a professional if you suspect this is the issue.

Check the Springs and Rollers

The springs and rollers on your garage door can also be a cause of the door not opening. If the springs are worn or damaged, then the door won't be able to open properly. The same is true for the rollers. You'll need to inspect the springs and rollers for any signs of wear or damage. If you find any, then you'll need to replace them.

In some cases, the springs may be too tight or too loose. This can cause the door to open and close erratically. If you suspect this is the case, then you'll need to adjust the tension on the springs. This is a job for a professional, as it can be dangerous to adjust the tension yourself.

Conclusion

A garage door that won't open is a common problem, but it's usually an easy one to diagnose and fix. Most of the time, the problem is with the safety sensors, the remote control, or the motor. However, it can also be caused by a problem with the springs or the rollers. If you're unable to fix the problem yourself, then it's best to call a professional.
