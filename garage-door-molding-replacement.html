How To Replace Garage Door Molding In 2023

Best Garage doors and references for house or residential with custom material and more design. We Share more picture design with HD quality images.

Introduction

Garage door molding is an important part of your home’s exterior, as it helps to protect it from the elements and make it look good. It’s not uncommon for molding to become damaged over time, due to wear and tear or accidental damage. However, replacing garage door molding is a relatively straightforward process that can be completed in just a few steps. In this article, we’ll show you how to replace garage door molding in 2023.

What You’ll Need

Before you get started, you’ll need a few items to help make the process easier. Be sure to gather the following:

  • Replacement molding
  • Measuring tape
  • Caulking gun
  • Caulk
  • Miter saw or coping saw
  • Drill with drill bits
  • Screwdriver
  • Sandpaper

You may also want to consider purchasing some gloves and safety glasses to protect yourself from any sharp edges.

Step 1: Measure

The first step in the process is to measure the existing garage door molding. This will allow you to purchase the right size of replacement molding. Measure the length, height, and width of the molding, and make sure to note any angles or curves.

Step 2: Purchase Replacement Molding

Once you’ve taken your measurements, it’s time to purchase the replacement molding. Be sure to purchase the correct size, as well as any other necessary pieces, such as corner pieces or trim.

Step 3: Remove Old Molding

The next step is to remove the old molding. Start by using a drill to remove any screws that are holding the molding in place. Once the screws are removed, use a putty knife to pry the molding away from the garage door frame.

Step 4: Cut Replacement Molding

Once the old molding has been removed, it’s time to cut the replacement molding to size. Using either a miter saw or a coping saw, cut the molding to the correct length, width, and height. Be sure to account for any angles or curves.

Step 5: Install Replacement Molding

Now that you’ve cut the replacement molding to size, it’s time to install it. Start by applying a thin layer of caulk to the back of the molding. This will help to seal any gaps and prevent moisture from seeping in. Then, using a drill, attach the molding to the garage door frame with screws.

Step 6: Finishing Touches

The final step is to make sure that the molding looks good. Use a piece of sandpaper to lightly sand down any rough edges. Then, apply a coat of paint to the molding, if desired.

Conclusion

Replacing garage door molding is a relatively easy task that can be completed in just a few steps. With the right tools and materials, you can easily replace your garage door molding in 2023. Just be sure to take your time and measure correctly to ensure a perfect fit.
