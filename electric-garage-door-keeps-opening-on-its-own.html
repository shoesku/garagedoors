Is Your Electric Garage Door Opening On Its Own?

If your electric garage door is opening on its own, then you may be wondering what could be causing this. Whether you recently installed a new electric garage door or youâ€™ve had one for a while, it can be a bit disconcerting when it starts to act up. Fortunately, there are a few common causes that can be easily fixed.

Check the Sensors

Most modern electric garage doors come equipped with sensors at the bottom of the door that detect when an object is in its path. If the sensors are blocked, the door will not open or close. They can also become misaligned, causing the door to open on its own. To check the sensors, look for two small lights on the bottom of the door. If the lights are off, then the sensors are likely blocked or misaligned. You can try relocating the sensors, cleaning them, or replacing them if they are broken.

Check the Remote Control

If you have a remote control, then it could be the cause of your electric garage door opening on its own. If the remote control is malfunctioning, then it could be sending out a signal that is causing the door to open. To check the remote control, try replacing the batteries or replacing the remote control if it is broken. If the remote control is working properly, then the issue may be with the wiring or the motor.

Check the Wiring

If the wiring is loose or worn, then it could be the cause of your electric garage door opening on its own. You can check the wiring by looking for any exposed wires or broken connections. If the wiring is in good condition, then the issue may be with the motor itself.

Check the Motor

The motor is responsible for opening and closing the electric garage door. If the motor is malfunctioning, then the door may open on its own. To check the motor, you can try replacing it or having it serviced. If the issue is with the motor, then replacing it may be the best solution.

Check the Wall Switch

It is also possible that the wall switch is the cause of your electric garage door opening on its own. If the switch is stuck in the open position, then the door will open. To check the switch, try replacing it or having it serviced.

Contact a Professional

If you are unable to pinpoint the cause of your electric garage door opening on its own, then it is best to contact a professional. A professional will be able to diagnose the issue and provide you with the best solution. They can also provide you with tips on how to maintain and keep your electric garage door in top condition.

Conclusion

If your electric garage door is opening on its own, then it is best to check the sensors, remote control, wiring, motor, and wall switch. If you are unable to pinpoint the cause, then contact a professional for assistance. With the right maintenance and repairs, you can keep your electric garage door in top condition.
