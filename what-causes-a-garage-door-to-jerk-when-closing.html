Why Do Garage Doors Jerk When Closing?

Garage doors are a convenient and essential part of any home. But when your garage door jerks when closing, it can be a major headache for any homeowner. Not only is it an uncomfortable experience, but it can also be a sign that something is wrong with your garage door system. To help you understand why your garage door is jerking when closing, we’re going to explain what could be causing the issue and how you can fix it.

What Causes a Garage Door to Jerk When Closing?

There are a few potential causes for a garage door to jerk when it’s closing. One of the most common causes is a problem with the springs. Over time, the springs of a garage door can become worn out, causing them to be unable to support the weight of the door. This can cause the door to jerk when it’s closing, as it’s unable to move smoothly.

Another potential cause is a problem with the tracks. The tracks of a garage door system should be perfectly aligned to ensure that the door moves smoothly. If there is a problem with the alignment of the tracks, the door may jerk when it’s closing.

Finally, a problem with the cables may be the culprit. The cables of a garage door system are responsible for lifting the door up and down. If the cables are worn out or become loose, the door may jerk when it’s closing.

How to Fix a Jerking Garage Door

Once you’ve identified the cause of your jerking garage door, you can take steps to fix the issue. If the problem is due to worn out springs, you should have them replaced by a professional.

If the problem is due to an alignment issue with the tracks, you can try to realign them yourself. However, if you’re not comfortable doing this, you should have a professional take care of it.

Finally, if the problem is due to worn out or loose cables, you should have them replaced or tightened by a professional.

Preventing Garage Door Jerking

The best way to prevent your garage door from jerking when closing is to keep up with regular maintenance. This includes regularly lubricating the springs and tracks and checking the cables for any signs of wear and tear. Additionally, it’s a good idea to have a professional inspect your garage door system at least once a year. This will help to ensure that any potential problems are caught and repaired before they can cause any serious damage.

Conclusion

Garage doors can be a major source of frustration when they jerk when closing. To understand why this is happening, you need to identify the cause of the issue. Common causes include worn out springs, an alignment issue with the tracks, and worn out or loose cables. Once you’ve identified the cause, you can take steps to fix the issue. Additionally, it’s a good idea to keep up with regular maintenance to help prevent any future issues.
