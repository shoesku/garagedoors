A garage door balance is a system that helps keep your garage door in a balanced position when it’s open and closed. This prevents the door from falling apart or wobbling when it’s in motion. The system typically consists of two cables, a torsion spring, and a counterbalance system. The cost of a garage door balance is dependent on the type of door you have and the cost of the parts.

Cost of Balance for Different Types of Garage Doors

The cost of a garage door balance can vary greatly depending on the type of door you have. The most common types of garage doors are sectional doors, roll-up doors, and tilt-up doors. Depending on the size and weight of the door, the cost of a garage door balance can range from a few hundred dollars to several thousand.

Sectional Doors

Sectional doors are the most common type of garage door, and they usually require two torsion springs and two cables for the balance system. The cost of a balance system for a sectional door can range from $150 to $350, depending on the size and weight of the door. The cost can go up if the door is made of heavy-duty material such as wood or steel.

Roll-Up Doors

Roll-up doors are the second most common type of garage door, and they usually require one torsion spring and two cables for the balance system. The cost of a balance system for a roll-up door can range from $150 to $400, depending on the size and weight of the door. The cost can go up if the door is made of heavy-duty material such as wood or steel.

Tilt-Up Doors

Tilt-up doors are the least common type of garage door, and they usually require one torsion spring and two cables for the balance system. The cost of a balance system for a tilt-up door can range from $200 to $500, depending on the size and weight of the door. The cost can go up if the door is made of heavy-duty material such as wood or steel.

Additional Costs to Consider

In addition to the cost of the balance system, there are a few other costs to consider when calculating the cost of a garage door balance. You may need to purchase additional hardware such as brackets, rollers, and hinges. You may also need to hire a professional to install the balance system, which can cost between $100 and $200.

Tips for Saving Money on Garage Door Balance
