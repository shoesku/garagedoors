Troubleshooting Your Garage Door Keeps Stopping And Reversing

Understanding the Problem

If you have noticed that your garage door keeps reversing and stopping, chances are you are having issues with your garage door opener. This is a common issue that many people experience and it can be frustrating. To help you understand your issue and how to fix it, we have created this guide. Read on to learn more about why your garage door keeps reversing and stopping, and what you can do to fix it.

Common Causes of Garage Door Reversing and Stopping

There are several common causes as to why your garage door might be reversing and stopping. Some of the most common causes include: the door is out of balance, the garage door opener sensors are misaligned, the garage door opener isn't receiving power, or the limit settings are off. Before you can properly troubleshoot the problem, you need to determine what the cause of the issue is.

Checking the Balance of the Garage Door

If you suspect that the issue is that the garage door is out of balance, then you will want to check the balance of the door. You can do this by disconnecting the opener from the door and manually opening and closing the door. If the door is difficult to open and close, then it is likely due to an issue with the balance of the door. You can try to adjust the balance of the door by adjusting the springs, but if you are not comfortable doing this then it is best to call a professional to do it for you.

Inspecting the Opener Sensors

If you have ruled out the balance of the door being the issue, then it is time to inspect the opener sensors. The sensors are designed to detect any obstructions in the path of the door and will cause the door to stop and reverse if it detects an obstruction. Inspect the sensors and make sure that they are properly aligned and that there is nothing blocking them. If the sensors are misaligned or blocked, then this is likely the issue and you will need to adjust the sensors or remove the obstruction.

Checking the Power Source

If you have ruled out the sensors as the cause, then you will want to check the power source for the garage door opener. Make sure that the circuit breaker hasn't tripped and that the power cord is connected properly. If the power cord is not connected properly, then this could be the cause of your issue and you will need to reconnect it.

Adjusting the Limit Settings

The limit settings on your garage door opener determine how far the door will open and close. If the limit settings are off, then this could be causing the door to stop and reverse. To adjust the limit settings, refer to your garage door opener manual for instructions. If you are not comfortable doing this, then it is best to call a professional to do it for you.

Contacting a Professional

If you are still having issues and are unable to determine the cause, then it is best to contact a professional. A professional will be able to inspect your garage door and opener and determine the cause of the issue. They will also be able to fix any issues that you are having, such as adjusting the balance of the door, and aligning the sensors. It is best to contact a professional if you are still having issues with your garage door reversing and stopping.

Conclusion

If your garage door keeps reversing and stopping, then it is likely due to an issue with the balance of the door, the sensors, the power source, or the limit settings. To troubleshoot the issue, you should check the balance of the door, inspect the sensors, check the power source, and adjust the limit settings. If you are still having issues, then it is best to contact a professional to inspect and fix the issue.
