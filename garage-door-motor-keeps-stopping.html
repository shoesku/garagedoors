Troubleshooting A Garage Door Motor That Keeps Stopping

Best Garage doors and references for house or residential with custom material and more design. We Share more picture design with HD quality images.

If your garage door won't open or close without stopping, it could be due to a few issues. Whether it's a broken sensor, a loose part, or a worn out motor, you can troubleshoot and resolve the problem yourself. Here are a few tips to help you figure out why your garage door motor keeps stopping.

Check The Sensors

The first thing you should do when troubleshooting a garage door motor that keeps stopping is to check the sensors. In many cases, the cause of the problem is a broken or misaligned sensor. The sensors are located on either side of the garage door and are responsible for detecting any obstructions in the way of the door. If one of the sensors is not working properly, the motor will keep stopping as it attempts to open or close the door.

To check the sensors, start by cleaning the lenses and making sure they are pointing directly at each other. If they are misaligned, adjust them so they are pointing in the right direction. If the sensors are still not working, you may need to replace them.

Check The Torsion Springs

Another potential cause of a garage door motor that keeps stopping is a broken or worn out torsion spring. The torsion spring is responsible for providing tension to the motor, and if it is not working properly, the motor will not be able to open or close the door. To check the torsion spring, you will need to locate it and then check for any signs of damage. If you find any, you should replace it as soon as possible.

Check The Tracks

The tracks of the garage door are also an important factor in the performance of the motor. If the tracks are dirty or damaged, the motor will not be able to move the door up or down. Start by cleaning the tracks to remove any dirt or debris that may be preventing the door from moving. If you find any damage to the tracks, you should replace them as soon as possible.

Check The Rollers

The rollers of the garage door are responsible for providing smooth movement to the door. If the rollers are worn out, it can cause the motor to keep stopping. To check the rollers, you will need to inspect them for any signs of wear and tear. If you find any, you should replace them as soon as possible.

Check The Motor

Finally, if all of the other components are working properly, the issue may be with the motor itself. If the motor has become worn out or is not operating correctly, it can cause the door to keep stopping. To check the motor, you should inspect it for any signs of damage or wear and tear. If you find any, you should replace the motor as soon as possible.

Conclusion

If your garage door motor keeps stopping, it could be due to a few different issues. Start by checking the sensors, torsion spring, tracks, rollers, and motor for any signs of damage or wear and tear. If you find any, you should replace the parts as soon as possible. With a little bit of troubleshooting, you can get your garage door back to working order in no time.
