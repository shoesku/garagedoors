How To Repair A Wayne Dalton Garage Door

Introduction

If you own a home, chances are you have a garage door. Many people rely on their garage door to provide the convenience of leaving their home without having to back out of their driveway or take the time to close and lock the door. An even better convenience is having an automated garage door opener, especially if you have a Wayne Dalton garage door. Wayne Dalton is a trusted name in garage door openers and has been around for nearly 50 years. Unfortunately, things eventually break down or need repair and your Wayne Dalton garage door is no exception. Knowing how to repair a Wayne Dalton garage door is essential for any homeowner, so here are a few tips on how to repair a Wayne Dalton garage door.

Inspect the Tracks and Rollers

One of the most common reasons your Wayne Dalton garage door won't open or close is due to obstructions in the tracks or rollers. Look at the tracks and rollers and make sure they are free from any dirt, debris, or other obstructions. If they are not, use a damp cloth to clean them out. Make sure that the rollers are also in good condition and haven't worn down. If they are worn down or broken, you may need to replace them.

Check the Springs

If the tracks and rollers appear to be in good condition, you should next check the springs on the garage door. Most Wayne Dalton garage doors use torsion springs, which should have a lifetime of about 10,000 cycles (roughly 3-5 years). If your springs are worn out, you may need to replace them. You can usually tell if your springs are worn out by looking for signs of corrosion or if the springs are stretched out. If your springs need to be replaced, you should call a professional to do the job.

Adjust the Force Settings

If your garage door still isn't opening or closing properly, you may need to adjust the force settings. This is especially true if your door is not closing completely or if it seems to be having difficulty opening. To adjust the force settings, you will need to locate the opener's travel limit switch. This should be located in the garage door opener's control box. Once you have located the switch, you can adjust the force settings as needed.

Check the Cables

Another common issue with Wayne Dalton garage doors is worn out or broken cables. The cables are responsible for lifting and lowering the garage door and should be checked regularly for any signs of wear or damage. If the cables are worn out or broken, you will need to replace them. Replacing the cables can be a tricky job, so you may want to call a professional to do the job for you.

Check the Remote

If your Wayne Dalton garage door is still not responding, you should check the remote control. Make sure the batteries are working and are not too low. You should also check to make sure the remote is programmed correctly. If the remote is not working, you may need to reprogram it or purchase a new one.

Conclusion

Knowing how to repair a Wayne Dalton garage door is essential for any homeowner. While some repairs can be done by a homeowner, many repairs should be done by a professional. If you find yourself in need of a repair, it is best to call a professional to make sure the repair is done correctly and safely. With the proper maintenance and care, your Wayne Dalton garage door should last for many years to come.
