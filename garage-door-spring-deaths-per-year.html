Garage door spring accidents are a surprisingly common occurrence, with thousands of people suffering serious injuries each year, and a shocking number of those accidents resulting in death. Every year, thousands of people are injured due to faulty garage door springs, and unfortunately, many of those injuries are fatal. While the exact number of deaths due to garage door spring accidents is uncertain, the numbers are likely in the hundreds, if not thousands.

Garage door springs have the potential to cause serious injury, and even death, when they fail. The springs are under a great deal of tension, and when they break, they can cause the garage door to suddenly and forcefully come down. If someone is standing close to the door when this happens, they can suffer catastrophic injuries.

The most common type of injury from garage door spring accidents is a broken bone or a head injury. People can also suffer serious lacerations from the sharp edges of the broken springs, as well as crushing injuries if the door is heavy. In the most severe cases, people can suffer fatal injuries, such as a broken neck or a traumatic brain injury.

What Causes Garage Door Spring Accidents?

Garage door spring accidents are usually caused by one of two things: either the spring has been improperly installed or it has worn out over time. If the spring is installed incorrectly, it can become weak and break more easily, leading to a sudden, dangerous fall of the garage door. Similarly, if the spring is not properly maintained, it can become weakened and break sooner than expected, resulting in the same dangerous situation.

How To Prevent Garage Door Spring Accidents?

The best way to prevent garage door spring accidents is to make sure that the springs are installed and maintained properly. If you have an old garage door, it is important to have a professional inspect it at least once a year to make sure that the springs are in good condition and not at risk of breaking.

In addition to having regular inspections, it is also important to exercise caution when operating the garage door. Never stand directly beneath the door when it is in motion, and be sure to keep any children or pets away from the door when it is being operated.

What To Do If You Have Been Injured In A Garage Door Spring Accident?

If you or someone you love has been injured in a garage door spring accident, it is important to seek medical attention immediately. In some cases, the injuries sustained can be extremely serious and require long-term treatment or rehabilitation. Additionally, you may be entitled to financial compensation for your medical bills, lost wages, and pain and suffering.

If you believe that the accident was caused by a faulty garage door spring, it is important to contact an experienced personal injury lawyer as soon as possible. An experienced lawyer can help you determine who is liable for the accident and can help you pursue the compensation you deserve for your injuries.

Conclusion

Garage door spring accidents can be extremely dangerous, and unfortunately, they can result in death. It is important to make sure that your garage door springs are installed and maintained properly in order to prevent such accidents from occurring. Additionally, if you or someone you love has been injured in a garage door spring accident, it is important to seek legal representation in order to pursue the compensation you deserve.
