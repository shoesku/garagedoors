What To Do When Your Garage Door Won't Even Close

When You Should Call a Professional

If you are having problems with your garage door not even closing, it is best to call a professional for help. A professional will be able to diagnose the issue and provide you with a solution. It is important to remember that garage doors are complex mechanical devices and require specialized knowledge in order to properly diagnose and repair them. If you attempt to repair the garage door yourself without the proper knowledge or expertise, you could end up damaging the door or causing further damage to it.

Common Reasons for Garage Door Not Closing

There are several common reasons why your garage door may not be closing properly. One of the most common causes is an obstruction in the track. This could be something as simple as a rock or a piece of debris that has become lodged in the track, preventing the door from closing properly. Another common reason is a broken spring. If the spring is broken, it will prevent the door from closing properly and will need to be replaced by a professional.

Inspecting the Tracks

If you suspect that an obstruction is the cause of your garage door not closing, you should inspect the tracks to check for any debris or obstructions. You should also check the track for any damage, such as dents or bends. If you find any damage, it is best to call a professional for repairs. If the track is clear and undamaged, you can move on to the next step.

Check for Broken Springs

If an obstruction is not the cause of the issue, you should check for a broken spring. The spring is located at the top of the garage door and is responsible for providing the necessary tension to lift and close the door. If the spring is broken, it will need to be replaced by a professional. It is important to note that replacing a broken spring is a complex and potentially dangerous task, so it is best to leave it to a professional.

Inspect the Rollers and Cables

If the spring is not the cause of your garage door not closing, you should check the rollers and cables. The rollers are what allow the door to move up and down, while the cables are responsible for providing the necessary tension to close the door. If the rollers or cables are damaged or worn, they will need to be replaced by a professional.

Check the Motor

If the rollers and cables are in good condition, then you should check the motor. The motor is responsible for powering the garage door and can sometimes be the cause of the issue. If the motor is not working properly, it will need to be replaced by a professional. It is important to note that replacing the motor is a complex and potentially dangerous task, so it is best to leave it to a professional.

Replace the Entire Door

If all the above steps have failed to fix the issue, then it may be time to replace the entire door. Replacing the entire door can be expensive, but it is the only way to ensure that your garage door is working properly. Replacing the entire door is a complex and potentially dangerous task, so it is best to leave it to a professional.

Conclusion

If your garage door is not closing properly, it is best to call a professional for help. A professional will be able to diagnose the issue and provide you with a solution. It is important to remember that garage doors are complex mechanical devices and require specialized knowledge in order to properly diagnose and repair them. If you attempt to repair the garage door yourself without the proper knowledge or expertise, you could end up damaging the door or causing further damage to it.
