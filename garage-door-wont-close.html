What To Do When Your Garage Door Won't Close

Best Garage doors and references for house or residential with custom material and more design. We Share more picture design with HD quality images.

Garage doors have become an integral part of many homes and can be a hassle when they don't work correctly. If your garage door won't close, it can cause more than just a minor inconvenience. It can also be a potential safety hazard if you can't secure your garage. Knowing what to do when your garage door won't close can help you get your door working again quickly and safely.

Check the Safety Reversing Sensors

The safety reversing sensors are designed to prevent the door from closing when something is blocking the opening. These sensors are located at the bottom of the garage door tracks on either side. Check the sensors to make sure they are aligned and functioning properly. If the sensors are blocked or misaligned, they won't be able to function properly and the door won't close. Move any objects that are blocking the sensors and make sure the sensors are in line with each other.

Check the Track for Debris

If the sensors are in good shape, you should check the track for debris. Debris, such as dirt and leaves, can get caught in the track and prevent the door from closing properly. Vacuum or brush the track to remove any debris that may be blocking the door. If you have a chain-drive garage door opener, you should also inspect the chain and make sure it is not damaged or stretched.

Inspect the Garage Door Opener

If the track is clear and the sensors are functioning properly, you should inspect the garage door opener. Check the remote to make sure the batteries are working properly. If the batteries are dead, replace them with fresh ones. You should also check the opener itself and make sure the gears and pulleys are not worn out. If they are, you will need to replace them.

Check the Springs

The springs on the garage door are responsible for lifting and lowering the door. If the springs are broken, the door won't be able to open and close properly. Inspect the springs and if they are broken, you will need to replace them. This is a job best left to a professional, as the springs can be very dangerous to work with.

Check the Balance

If the door is still not closing, you may need to check the balance of the door. An unbalanced door can cause the door to close incorrectly or not at all. To check the balance, you can disconnect the garage door opener and manually open the door. If the door is too heavy, you will need to adjust the springs to balance the door.

Check the Cables and Rollers

The cables and rollers on the garage door can become worn out over time. If the cables and rollers are worn out, the door won't be able to open and close properly. Inspect the cables and rollers and if they are damaged, you will need to replace them. This is another job best left to a professional.

Call a Professional

If you have checked all of the above and the garage door still won't close, it may be time to call a professional. A professional technician can diagnose the problem and repair or replace any parts that are damaged or worn out. A professional can also provide advice on how to prevent similar problems in the future.

Conclusion

Garage doors are an essential part of many homes and can be a hassle when they don't work properly. If your garage door won't close, it is important to take steps to diagnose and repair the problem quickly and safely. Checking the safety reversing sensors, the track for debris, the garage door opener, the springs, the balance, and the cables and rollers can all help to get your garage door working properly again. If all else fails, it may be time to call a professional to get the job done right.
