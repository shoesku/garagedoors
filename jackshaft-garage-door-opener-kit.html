What is a Jackshaft Garage Door Opener?

A jackshaft garage door opener is a type of garage door opener that uses a jackshaft drive system instead of a traditional motorized system. The jackshaft system is designed to take up less space, allowing it to be mounted to the side of the garage door, rather than on the ceiling. This makes it perfect for garages with low ceilings, or those that don’t have enough space for a traditional garage door opener.

The jackshaft system works by using a series of gears, pulleys, and cables to lift and lower the garage door. The gears and pulleys are connected to the jackshaft, which is then connected to the garage door opener. When the garage door opener is activated, it will cause the jackshaft to turn, which will in turn cause the gears and pulleys to move, lifting and lowering the garage door.

Benefits of a Jackshaft Garage Door Opener

The jackshaft system offers a number of benefits over traditional motorized garage door openers. Firstly, since it takes up less space, it can be mounted to the side of the garage door, giving you back valuable headroom in your garage. Secondly, since the jackshaft system uses fewer moving parts, it is typically quieter than a traditional motorized garage door opener.

Finally, the jackshaft system is also much easier to install than a traditional motorized garage door opener. The jackshaft system only requires a few tools for installation, and most kits come with detailed instructions for installation. This makes it perfect for DIYers who want to save money on installation costs.

Jackshaft Garage Door Opener Kit Options

If you’re looking for a jackshaft garage door opener kit, there are a few different options available. The first option is a kit that includes all the necessary parts for installation, such as the jackshaft, the pulleys, the cables, and the garage door opener. The second option is a kit that includes only the jackshaft and the pulleys, and requires you to purchase the garage door opener separately. This is the best option for those who already have a garage door opener, but want to upgrade to a jackshaft system.
