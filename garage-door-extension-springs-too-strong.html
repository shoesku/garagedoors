What Are Garage Door Extension Springs?

Garage door extension springs are an essential component of any garage door. They are responsible for counterbalancing the weight of a garage door when it is opened or closed, and they can make the difference between a safe, secure garage door and one that is unsafe and vulnerable to damage. Extension springs can be found on most overhead garage doors, and they are usually located on either side of the door. They are made up of two parts: the coil spring and the cable.

Problems With Strong Garage Door Extension Springs

When a garage door extension spring is too strong, it can cause a number of problems. The most common problem is that the garage door may be difficult to open and close. This is because the spring is providing too much tension, which makes it difficult to lift the door. Additionally, the spring may be overly stiff and make the door vibrate when it is opened or closed. This vibration can be annoying and can even cause damage to the door and its components.

How to Fix Garage Door Extension Springs That Are Too Strong

If your garage door extension springs are too strong and you're having problems opening and closing your door, there are a few things that you can do to fix the problem. The first thing to do is to adjust the tension on the springs. This can be done by either loosening or tightening the coils on the springs. You can also adjust the tension by adjusting the cables that attach the springs to the track.

Adjustment the Extension Spring

If the extension spring is too strong, it can be adjusted by either loosening or tightening the coils on the spring. To loosen the coils, you can use a pair of pliers to gently bend the coils away from each other. To tighten the coils, you can use a pair of pliers to gently bend the coils closer to each other. It is important to be careful when adjusting the coils, as too much adjustment can cause the spring to break.

Adjustment the Cable

The cable can also be adjusted if the springs are too strong. This can be done by adjusting the length of the cable. To adjust the length of the cable, you can use a pair of pliers to loosen the cable from the track. Then, you can adjust the length of the cable by either shortening or lengthening it. When adjusting the length of the cable, it is important to be careful, as too much adjustment can cause the cable to break.

Conclusion

Garage door extension springs are an essential component of any garage door, as they are responsible for counterbalancing the weight of the door when it is opened or closed. If the extension springs are too strong, it can cause problems such as difficulty in opening and closing the door, as well as vibrations. The problem can be fixed by adjusting the tension on the springs or the length of the cable. It is important to be careful when adjusting the springs or the cable, as too much adjustment can cause them to break.
