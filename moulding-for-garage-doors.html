How To Mould Your Garage Door For A Unique Look

Best Garage doors and references for house or residential with custom material and more design. We Share more picture design with HD quality images.

Garages are often one of the largest features of a home and can be the first thing visitors see when they come to your house. As such, you want it to look its best and be as appealing as possible. To help you achieve this, you may want to consider moulding your garage door. Moulding can add a unique look to your door and help to improve the overall aesthetic of the area.

Moulding your garage door is actually quite easy. It’s a simple DIY job that anyone can do with minimal tools and a few supplies. To begin, you’ll need to gather a few supplies. You’ll need some wood moulding, a saw, a drill, a measuring tape, and some wood glue. Once you have gathered these supplies, you’ll be ready to start moulding your garage door.

Step 1: Measure the Door

The first step is to measure your garage door. You want to make sure that the moulding will fit properly. Measure the width and height of the door and make sure to note any curves or angles. Measure twice to make sure you get it right. It’s also a good idea to take a photo of the door as a reference.

Step 2: Cut the Moulding

Once you’ve measured the door, you can then begin to cut the moulding. You want to make sure that the moulding is the right size, so it’s important to measure twice. Cut the moulding to the size of the door using the saw. If you’re not comfortable using a saw, you can also have the moulding cut at a local hardware store.

Step 3: Glue the Moulding

Once the moulding is cut to size, you can then start to glue it onto the door. Use wood glue to attach the moulding to the door. Make sure to spread the glue evenly to ensure a strong bond. Hold the moulding in place for several minutes to make sure that it’s secure. You may also want to use clamps to keep the moulding in place while the glue dries.

Step 4: Drill Pilot Holes

Once the glue is dry, you can then begin to drill the pilot holes. This will make it easier to install the screws. Use a drill bit that is slightly larger than the screws that you’re using. Drill the pilot holes in the moulding and then insert the screws to secure the moulding to the door.

Step 5: Paint the Moulding

Once the moulding is secure, you can then begin to paint it. Use a good quality outdoor paint that is designed to withstand the elements. You can choose any color you want, so you can make the moulding match the color of your garage door or choose a different color for a more unique look. Allow the paint to dry before you use the door.

Step 6: Enjoy Your Unique Look

Once the paint is dry, you can step back and admire your work. Your garage door now has a unique, custom look that will be sure to catch the eye of passersby. With a little bit of work, you can mould your garage door to achieve the look you want. It’s a great way to add some character to your home and make it stand out from the rest.
