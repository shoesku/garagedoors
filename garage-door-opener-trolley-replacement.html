Replacing A Trolley On Your Garage Door Opener

What is a Trolley?

A trolley is a mechanical device that is part of the garage door opener system. It is a steel bar that runs along the length of the tracks of the garage door opener and is connected to the openerâ€™s motor. The trolley is responsible for pushing and pulling the door up and down. It is a critical part of the system and if it malfunctions or breaks, the door will not open or close correctly. It is important to regularly check the trolley and replace it when needed.

Why Should You Replace the Trolley?

A trolley should be replaced as soon as it starts to malfunction or make strange noises. The trolley is a metal-on-metal part, so it will wear out over time. It is important to replace it before it completely fails, as this could cause damage to the tracks and other parts of the opener system. It is also important to replace it if it has been bent or damaged in some way, as this could cause further damage to the system.

What Tools Do You Need to Replace a Trolley?

Replacing a trolley is not a difficult task, but it does require some basic tools. You will need a socket set, a screwdriver, pliers, and a wrench. You may also want to use some lubricant to help the trolley slide more easily on the tracks. If you are not comfortable with the tools or lack the knowledge to do the job, it is best to call a professional to do the job.

How to Replace a Trolley?

Before you start, make sure that the power to the garage door opener is turned off. This will prevent any accidents from occurring. Then, locate the trolley on the tracks and remove it by unscrewing the bolts with the socket set. Once it is loose, slide it out of the tracks and remove any old lubricant from the tracks. Now you can install the new trolley in its place.

Step 1: Slide the New Trolley onto the Tracks

Start by sliding the new trolley onto the tracks, making sure that it is aligned correctly. Once it is in place, use the wrench to tighten the bolts that hold it in place. Make sure they are tight but not overly tight, as this could damage the tracks.

Step 2: Lubricate the Tracks

Now that the trolley is in place, you need to lubricate the tracks. Use a lubricant specifically designed for garage door openers, as this will help the trolley move more smoothly. Apply the lubricant to the tracks, making sure to cover all the moving parts. Once you are done, use a cloth to wipe away any excess lubricant.

Step 3: Test the Opener

Once you have completed the installation, it is time to test the opener. Turn the power back on and check that the opener is working properly. Try opening and closing the door several times to make sure it is working correctly. If all is working properly, you are done!

Conclusion

Replacing a trolley on your garage door opener is not a difficult job. However, it is important to take your time and make sure that you are doing it correctly. If you are not comfortable doing the job yourself, it is best to call a professional to take care of it. With the proper tools and some patience, you should be able to have your garage door opener working properly in no time!
