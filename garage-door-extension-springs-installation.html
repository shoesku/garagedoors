What Are Garage Door Extension Springs?

Garage door extension springs are one of the main components of a garage door system. They are located above the top of the garage door, usually above the horizontal tracks of the door. Extension springs are used to counterbalance the weight of the garage door when it is opened or closed. Without them, the door would be too heavy to open or close manually. Extension springs are usually made of steel wire, and are designed for a particular garage door size and weight.

How Do Extension Springs Work?

When the garage door is opened, the extension springs contract, pulling the cables which are connected to the bottom brackets of the door. This pulls the door up, counterbalancing its weight. When the door is closed, the springs stretch, pushing the door down. Extension springs are generally sized according to the weight of the garage door they are supporting. If they are too weak, they won’t be able to open and close the door properly. On the other hand, if they are too strong, they could cause the door to slam shut.

Types of Extension Springs

There are two types of extension springs, standard duty and heavy duty. Standard duty springs are typically used on residential garage doors, while heavy duty springs are used for heavier commercial doors. Standard duty springs have a shorter life expectancy than heavy duty springs, as they are designed for lighter doors. Both types of springs are usually sold in pairs.

Determining the Right Size of Extension Springs

Before installing extension springs, it is important to determine the right size. To do this, you will need to measure the width and height of your garage door. You should also measure the diameter of the extension spring and the length of the spring when it is at rest. Once you have these measurements, it is easy to determine the correct size of extension spring for your door.

Installing Extension Springs Safely

Installing extension springs can be a dangerous job, as they are under a lot of tension. It is important to use the proper safety precautions when installing them. Always wear safety glasses, gloves, and other protective gear when handling the springs. Avoid standing directly underneath the springs when they are under tension. Instead, stand to the side of the springs. If you don’t feel comfortable doing this job yourself, it is best to hire a professional.

Replacing Extension Springs

If your existing extension springs are worn out or broken, they should be replaced as soon as possible. It is important to use the same size and weight of springs that were originally installed. Replacing the springs is a job that should be left to a professional, as it requires special tools and knowledge. It is also important to replace both springs at the same time, to ensure they work properly.

Maintenance and Safety Tips

To ensure your extension springs last as long as possible, it is important to perform regular maintenance. This includes lubricating the springs and tracks, as well as checking for signs of wear and tear. If you notice any problems, it is best to have them fixed as soon as possible. Additionally, for safety reasons, it is important to check the tension of the springs regularly. If the tension is too low, the door may not open or close properly.

Conclusion

Garage door extension springs play an important role in keeping your garage door functioning properly. It is important to choose the right size and weight of springs, and to have them installed correctly. Additionally, regular maintenance and safety checks should be performed to ensure the springs last as long as possible. If you don’t feel comfortable doing this job yourself, it is best to hire a professional.
