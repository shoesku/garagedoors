Why Your Garage Door Won't Close All The Way?

Best Garage doors and references for house or residential with custom material and more design. We Share more picture design with HD quality images.

If you’re like most homeowners, you want your garage door to close completely each and every time you use it. But what happens when your garage door won’t close all the way? This can be a major annoyance and can leave you feeling frustrated. Fortunately, there are a few things you can do to try to get your garage door to close all the way again.

Check the Sensors

One of the most common reasons that a garage door won’t close all the way is that the sensors are out of alignment. All garage doors come with sensors that detect if an object is in the way of the door closing. If the sensors are blocked or out of alignment, the garage door will not close all the way. To fix this problem, you should check to make sure the sensors are in the proper position. Also, check to make sure that nothing is blocking the sensors from detecting when an object is in the way.

Check the Tracks

Another reason for your garage door not closing all the way could be that the tracks are dirty or bent. The tracks should be clean and free of debris so that the door can move smoothly along them. If they are dirty, use a cloth to wipe away any dirt or debris. If the tracks are bent or damaged, you can try to straighten them out. If that doesn’t work, you’ll likely need to replace the tracks.

Check the Springs

The springs on your garage door are important for helping the door move up and down. If the springs have become worn out, they may not be able to lift the door all the way up, which can cause it to not close all the way. You can check the springs to see if they are worn out by looking for signs of wear and tear. If the springs are worn out, you’ll need to replace them.

Check the Door Balance

The balance of your garage door is another important factor for it to close all the way. If the door is not balanced properly, it can cause the door to not close all the way. To check the balance, you can manually lift the door halfway up and let go. If the door stays in place, it is balanced properly. If the door falls to one side or the other, then it is not balanced and you’ll need to adjust the springs.

Check the Limit Switch

The limit switch is a device that tells the garage door when to stop moving. If the limit switch is not set properly, it can cause the door to not close all the way. To check the limit switch, you can open the garage door all the way and then press the wall button to close it. If the door stops a few inches before it is all the way down, then the limit switch needs to be adjusted.

Check the Door Opener Chain

The chain on your garage door opener is what pulls the door up and down. If the chain is loose or worn out, it can cause the door to not close all the way. To check the chain, you can open the garage door and then manually lift the door up and down. If the chain is loose or worn out, you’ll need to replace it.

Check the Rollers

The rollers on your garage door are what help the door move up and down. If the rollers are worn out or damaged, it can cause the door to not close all the way. To check the rollers, you can open the garage door and then manually lift the door up and down. If the rollers are worn out or damaged, you’ll need to replace them.

Call a Professional

If you’ve tried all of the above steps and your garage door still won’t close all the way, it’s time to call a professional. A professional can come to your home and assess the situation and provide you with a solution. They can also make any necessary repairs or adjustments to get your garage door working properly again.

Conclusion

If your garage door won’t close all the way, there could be a few different causes. You can try to diagnose the problem yourself by checking the sensors, tracks, springs, balance, limit switch, chain, and rollers. If none of these steps work, it’s time to call a professional for help. By following these steps, you can hopefully get your garage door working properly again.
