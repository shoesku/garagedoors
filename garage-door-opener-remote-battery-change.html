How To Change The Battery In Your Garage Door Opener Remote

Best Garage doors and references for house or residential with custom material and more design. We Share more picture design with HD quality images.

Having a garage door opener remote makes it convenient to open and close your garage door from inside your car or from the comfort of your home. But, like all electronics, it requires some maintenance, and one of the most important tasks is changing the garage door opener remote battery. In this article, we will explain how to do it.

Choose the Right Battery

The first step to changing the battery in your garage door opener remote is to choose the correct battery. Typical garage door opener remotes use a 12-volt battery, which is a small, round, silver-colored battery. These batteries can be found at most stores that stock household items. Some remotes may require a different type of battery, so check your garage door opener manual to make sure you get the right one.

Open the Remote

Once you have the right battery, you can begin the process of changing it. Start by opening the remote. This can be done by carefully prying up the plastic cover on the back of the remote. Be sure to use a flathead screwdriver to pry it open, as a screwdriver with a pointed edge could damage the remote. Be gentle and don’t force the cover off if it’s stuck.

Remove the Old Battery

Once the cover is off the remote, you will see the battery inside. Carefully remove the old battery and set it aside. It is important to note that the old battery may still contain some charge, so be sure to handle it with care.

Install the New Battery

Take the new battery and insert it into the remote, making sure the positive and negative terminals are aligned correctly. Once the battery is in place, replace the cover and press down firmly to make sure it is secure. If the cover does not snap back into place, double-check the battery to make sure it is properly aligned.

Test the Remote

The final step is to test the remote to make sure it is working properly. Do this by pressing the “open” and “close” buttons on the remote. If the garage door opens and closes, then the battery change was successful. If it does not work, double-check the battery to make sure it is properly installed.

Clean the Remote

When changing the battery, it is a good idea to take a few minutes to clean the remote. Use a damp cloth to wipe away any dirt or dust that may have accumulated on the remote over time. This will help ensure that the remote is working properly and will extend its life.

Conclusion

Changing the battery in your garage door opener remote is a simple and straightforward process. All you need is the right battery and a few minutes of your time. With the right tools and a little bit of effort, you can ensure that your garage door opener remote is working properly and ready to go.
