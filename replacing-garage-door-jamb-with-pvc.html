Replacing Your Garage Door Jamb With Pvc

Introduction

Replacing your garage door jamb with PVC is one of the most popular DIY projects for homeowners today. PVC, or polyvinyl chloride, is a strong and durable material that is easy to work with and can be used to create a variety of shapes and sizes. It is also very affordable, making it an attractive option for those who want to make their own custom garage door jamb. In this article, we will discuss the steps involved in replacing your existing jamb with PVC.

Gather the Supplies

Before you begin, you will need to gather the supplies needed for the job. You will need PVC pipe, a hacksaw, a drill, screws, and a jigsaw. You should also have some sandpaper or a carbide-tipped saw blade to smooth out any rough edges. If you don’t have all of these items, you can purchase them from your local hardware store.

Measure Your Garage Door Opening

The next step is to measure your garage door opening. This will help you determine the size of the PVC pipe that you need to purchase. It is important to measure the width, height, and depth of your doorway. Once you have these measurements, you can purchase the right size PVC pipe.

Cut the PVC Pipe

Once you have the right size PVC pipe, you will need to cut it to the desired length. You can use a hacksaw to do this, but it is best to use a jigsaw if possible. This will ensure that the cuts are cleaner and more precise. After the cuts are made, you should use a piece of sandpaper or a carbide-tipped saw blade to smooth out any rough edges.

Attach the PVC Pipe to the Frame

The next step is to attach the PVC pipe to the frame of your garage door. You will need to use screws to secure the pipe to the frame. Be sure to pre-drill the holes for the screws before you insert them. This will ensure that the screws hold tightly and securely.

Cut Out the Existing Jamb

Once the PVC pipe is secured, you will need to cut out the existing jamb. You will need to use a jigsaw to do this. Make sure to measure twice and cut once to ensure that the new jamb is the right size. Once the jamb is cut out, you can then attach the PVC pipe to the frame using screws.

Seal the Joints

Once the PVC pipe is attached to the frame, you will need to seal the joints between the pieces of pipe. This will help prevent moisture from entering the garage and will also help to keep the pipes secure. You can use a special sealant or caulk to do this. Be sure to read the instructions on the package carefully before applying the sealant.

Finish Installing the PVC Pipe

Once the joints are sealed, you can finish installing the PVC pipe. You will need to use screws to secure the pipes to the frame. Again, be sure to pre-drill the holes for the screws before inserting them. This will ensure that the screws hold tightly and securely.

Conclusion

Replacing your garage door jamb with PVC is a great way to update the look of your home. It is also an affordable and easy DIY project that can be completed in a few hours. Be sure to follow all the steps outlined in this article to ensure a successful installation. Good luck!
