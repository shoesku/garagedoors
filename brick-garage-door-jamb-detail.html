Installing a Brick Garage Door Jamb

Installing a brick garage door jamb is a relatively straightforward process that can be completed by most DIYers. Before beginning the installation, it is important to make sure that the area is properly prepared. This includes ensuring that the area is level and free of debris. The next step is to lay the bricks for the jamb. This can be done by starting from the bottom and working up, ensuring that each brick is butted up against the one before it. To ensure a secure and level jamb, it is important to check the alignment of each brick as it is laid. Once the bricks have been laid, it is important to secure them in place. This is usually done by using mortar or concrete to fill in the gaps between the bricks. This will ensure that the jamb is solid and secure and will provide long-lasting durability.
