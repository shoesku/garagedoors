Introduction

Garage doors are an important part of owning a home. They are used to store vehicles, tools, and other items, as well as provide security to the home. Proper maintenance of the garage door is important to ensure its longevity and to prevent any potential accidents. One of the most important parts of the garage door is the garage door spring. The springs are the ones responsible for lifting and lowering the door. Without them, the door will be very heavy and difficult to open. Therefore, it is important to understand the different types of springs, how to maintain them, and when it is time to replace them. This guide will provide you with the necessary information to help you maintain your garage door and keep it working properly.

Types of Garage Door Springs

There are two main types of garage door springs: torsion springs and extension springs. Torsion springs are the most common type used today and are found on the top of the door. They are wound tightly and when the door is opened, they twist and lift the door up. Extension springs are located on the side of the door and are connected to the door track. When the door is opened, the springs extend and pull the door open. Both types of springs are designed to last about 10,000 cycles before needing to be replaced.

Torsion Springs

Torsion springs are made of high-grade steel and are wound together to create tension. This tension is what allows the springs to open and close the door. Torsion springs are available in various sizes, which are measured in inch-pounds. It is important to choose the correct size for your door, as a spring that is too weak or too strong can cause damage to the door or the opener. Torsion springs should be inspected every few months for any signs of wear and tear, and should be replaced if any damage is found.

Extension Springs

Extension springs are made of steel wire and are connected to the door track. They are designed to extend when the door is opened and contract when the door is closed. Extension springs should be inspected every few months for signs of wear and tear and should be replaced if any damage is found. Extension springs are available in various sizes, which are measured in inch-pounds. It is important to choose the correct size for your door, as a spring that is too weak or too strong can cause damage to the door or the opener.

Maintaining Garage Door Springs

Garage door springs are under a lot of tension and require regular maintenance to keep them in good condition. It is important to inspect them at least once a month for any signs of wear and tear, such as cracks, fraying, or rust. If any of these signs are present, the springs should be replaced immediately. Additionally, the springs should be lubricated every few months to ensure they are operating properly.

Replacing Garage Door Springs

Replacing garage door springs can be dangerous and should only be done by a professional. A professional will have the correct tools and equipment to safely replace the springs, and will also be able to make sure that the springs are the correct size for your door. When replacing the springs, it is important to use the same type of spring that was originally installed on the door.

Conclusion

Garage door springs are an important part of the garage door system and require regular maintenance and inspection to keep them in good condition. It is important to understand the different types of springs, how to maintain them, and when it is time to replace them. This guide has provided you with the necessary information to help you maintain your garage door and keep it working properly.
