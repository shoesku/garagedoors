What is Garage Door Travel?

Garage door travel is a technical phrase for the amount of vertical movement your garage door has when it opens and closes. The travel distance of a garage door is controlled by two devices in your garage door opener, the force adjuster and the travel limit switch. The force adjuster helps to ensure that the door opens and closes smoothly, and the travel limit switch is responsible for setting the amount of travel the door has. Adjusting the travel of your garage door can be an important step in helping to keep your door running smoothly and safely.

Reasons to Adjust Garage Door Travel

Adjusting the travel of your garage door can be necessary for a number of reasons. If your garage door is not opening and closing properly, adjusting the travel can help to ensure that the door is not hitting the ground or opening and closing too quickly. If your door is opening and closing too quickly, it could be a sign that the travel limit switch needs to be adjusted so that the door has more travel. Additionally, if your door is hitting the ground or other objects when it is opening and closing, it could be a sign that the force adjuster needs to be adjusted.

Tools Needed to Adjust Garage Door Travel

In order to adjust your garage door travel, you will need a few basic tools. You will need a flat-head screwdriver, a Phillips-head screwdriver, and an adjustable wrench. Additionally, you may need a ladder or a step stool if you are unable to reach the travel limit switch or force adjuster from the ground.

Steps to Adjust Garage Door Travel

The first step in adjusting your garage door travel is to locate the force adjuster and the travel limit switch. The force adjuster is typically located near the motor of the garage door opener, while the travel limit switch is usually located near the top of the door. Once you have located these two devices, you can begin to adjust the travel of your garage door.

Adjusting the Force Adjuster

The force adjuster is responsible for controlling the amount of force that the garage door opener applies to the door when it opens and closes. To adjust the force of the door, use the adjustable wrench to turn the adjuster counterclockwise. This will decrease the amount of force applied to the door and will cause the door to open and close more slowly. Conversely, turning the adjuster clockwise will increase the amount of force applied to the door, causing it to open and close more quickly.

Adjusting the Travel Limit Switch

The travel limit switch is responsible for controlling the amount of travel the garage door has when it opens and closes. To adjust the travel limit switch, use a flat-head screwdriver to turn the switch counterclockwise. This will decrease the amount of travel the door has and will cause it to open and close more quickly. Conversely, turning the switch clockwise will increase the amount of travel the door has, causing it to open and close more slowly.

Conclusion

Adjusting the travel of your garage door can be an important step in helping to keep your door running smoothly and safely. By following the steps outlined above, you can easily adjust the travel of your garage door and ensure that it is opening and closing properly. Remember to always use caution when adjusting the garage door travel, as improper adjustments can lead to injuries or damage to the door.
