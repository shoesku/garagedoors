What is Rubber Flashing?

Rubber flashing is a common material used to protect against water damage in residential and commercial buildings. It is a flexible material that can be applied to walls, roofs, windows, and doors. It is typically made of rubber, vinyl, or neoprene. Rubber flashing has a variety of benefits, such as being waterproof and durable. It also protects against air and water infiltration, which can cause damage to the home or building. Furthermore, it is also an effective soundproofing material. Rubber flashing can be used to seal around garage doors to help keep out elements such as rain, snow, and wind. In this article, we will discuss the importance of using rubber flashing for garage doors and how it can help protect your home or business.

Why is Rubber Flashing Important for Garage Doors?

Garage doors are one of the most vulnerable areas of a home or building when it comes to water damage. They are exposed to the elements and can quickly become damaged due to moisture or other factors. Rubber flashing is essential for protecting the garage door from water damage. It helps to prevent water from entering the garage and damaging the door, as well as the other items inside. In addition to protecting the garage door, rubber flashing also helps to reduce air and noise infiltration, which can be an issue when the garage is attached to the house.

How to Install Rubber Flashing on Your Garage Door

Installing rubber flashing on your garage door is relatively simple and can be done in a few steps. First, measure the area where the flashing will be installed and cut the necessary pieces. Next, apply a strong adhesive to the back of the flashing and press it firmly onto the door. Finally, use a caulking gun to seal the edges of the flashing and ensure a tight fit. It is important to ensure that all gaps and cracks are sealed to prevent water from entering the garage.

Benefits of Rubber Flashing on Your Garage Door

Using rubber flashing on your garage door has a variety of benefits. It helps to keep out moisture, which can cause mold, mildew, and other problems. It also helps to reduce air infiltration, which can make the garage more comfortable during the summer months. Lastly, it also helps to reduce noise infiltration, which can be an issue when the garage is attached to the house.

Types of Rubber Flashing for Garage Doors

There are several different types of rubber flashing available for garage doors. The most common types are vinyl, neoprene, and EPDM (ethylene propylene diene monomer). Each type of rubber flashing has its own benefits and drawbacks, so it is important to research the different types to determine which is best for your needs. Vinyl is the most cost effective option and is the easiest to install. Neoprene is more durable and can withstand extreme temperatures. EPDM is the most expensive option, but it is also the most durable and can withstand the harshest weather conditions.

Conclusion

Rubber flashing is an essential material to use when installing a garage door. It helps to protect the door from water damage and air infiltration. It is important to research the different types of rubber flashing available to determine which is best for your needs. Installing rubber flashing is relatively simple and can be done in a few steps. Once installed, it will help to keep your garage door in good condition for years to come.
