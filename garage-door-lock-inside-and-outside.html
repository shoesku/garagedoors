How To Secure Your Garage Door Lock Inside And Outside

Buying a home can be a stressful and demanding process. In the midst of all the paperwork and inspections, it's easy to overlook the importance of garage door locks. But if you want to keep your home and belongings secure, you need to make sure that all your locks, including your garage door locks, are properly maintained and secure. Read on to learn more about how to protect your garage door lock inside and outside.

Identifying Your Garage Door Lock

Before you can start securing your garage door lock, you need to know what kind of lock you have. Most garage door locks consist of a keyed deadbolt and a handle lock. The deadbolt is the more secure of the two locks, as it requires a key to unlock. The handle lock can usually be opened with a simple twist of the handle. Additionally, many garage door locks also have a button or switch that can be used to lock and unlock the door from the inside.

Locking Your Garage Door from the Outside

When you leave your home, it's important to make sure that your garage door is securely locked from the outside. To do this, you need to make sure that your deadbolt is securely in place. If you have a handle lock, you should also make sure that it is locked. Additionally, you should also make sure that the button or switch that locks the door from the inside is in the locked position.

Securing Your Garage Door from the Inside

When you're inside your home, it's important to make sure that your garage door is secure from the inside. To do this, you should make sure that your deadbolt is locked. Additionally, if you have a handle lock, you should make sure that it is securely locked. And if you have a button or switch that locks the door from the inside, you should make sure that it is in the locked position.

Installing Additional Security Measures

In addition to the locks on your garage door, you should also consider installing additional security measures. If you have a window in your garage door, you should consider installing window bars or grates to prevent someone from breaking in. You should also consider installing a motion-sensor light outside your garage door to deter intruders. Additionally, you should make sure that your garage door is equipped with an automatic opener that is connected to an alarm system.

Upgrading Your Garage Door Lock

If you want to make sure that your garage door is as secure as possible, you might want to consider upgrading your existing locks. If your deadbolt is old or worn, you should consider replacing it with a more secure model. Additionally, you should consider replacing your handle lock with a keyed deadbolt. This will make it much more difficult for someone to break into your garage.

Maintaining Your Garage Door Lock

In addition to upgrading your locks, it's also important to make sure that your locks are properly maintained. You should periodically check your locks to make sure that they are in good working order. Additionally, if you have a keyed deadbolt, you should periodically change the key to make sure that it is secure. Finally, if you notice any signs of damage or wear on your locks, you should consider replacing them.

Conclusion

Garage door locks are an essential part of keeping your home secure. It's important to make sure that your locks are properly maintained and secure. You should also consider installing additional security measures such as window bars or motion-sensor lights. Finally, if your locks are old or worn, you should consider upgrading them to more secure models.
