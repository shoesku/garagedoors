Everything You Need To Know About Garage Door Cable Replacement Parts

Garage door cables are a crucial part of your door’s overall functioning, and when they break, it can be a huge hassle. Luckily, the process of replacing them is relatively straightforward, and it’s easy to find the parts you need online or at your local hardware store. In this article, we’ll provide you with all the information you need to replace your garage door cables on your own.

What Are Garage Door Cables?

Garage door cables are the two cables that run along the sides of the garage door, connecting the springs and the lift drums. These cables are responsible for lifting and closing the door, and they need to be strong and durable in order to do their job properly. Over time, the cables can become worn or frayed, and they may need to be replaced in order to keep the door working properly.

Why Are Garage Door Cables Important?

Garage door cables are important for several reasons. First, they provide the tension that’s necessary for the door to lift and close properly. Without them, the door won’t be able to open and close as it should. Additionally, garage door cables can also be safety devices. If one of the cables were to break, it would prevent the door from slamming down, which could cause serious injury.

What Are the Signs of a Broken Garage Door Cable?

If your garage door cables are broken, there are several signs that you can look for. First, you might notice that the door isn’t able to open or close properly. Additionally, you may also notice that the door is sagging or off-balance. If you’ve noticed any of these signs, it’s important to replace the cables as soon as possible.

How to Replace Garage Door Cables

Replacing your garage door cables is a relatively simple process. The first thing you’ll need to do is disconnect the power to the door. This will prevent the door from opening or closing while you’re working on it. Next, you’ll need to remove the old cables and replace them with new ones. Make sure to use the same size and type of cable that was originally installed on the door.

Step 1: Disconnect the Power

The first step is to disconnect the power to the door. This will prevent the door from opening or closing while you’re working on it. To do this, simply turn off the power supply to the garage door opener.

Step 2: Remove the Old Cables

Now that the power is disconnected, you can begin to remove the old cables. Start by removing the bolts that attach the cables to the lift drums. Then, remove the cables from the springs. Once the cables are removed, you can discard them.

Step 3: Measure the New Cables

Before you can install the new cables, you’ll need to measure them. Measure the length of the cables from end to end, and then cut them to the appropriate length. Make sure to leave a few inches of extra cable in case you need to make any adjustments.

Step 4: Install the New Cables

Now it’s time to install the new cables. Begin by attaching one end of the cable to the lift drum. Then, attach the other end to the spring. Make sure that the cables are properly aligned and that they are not twisted.

Step 5: Test the Door

Once the cables have been installed, it’s time to test the door. Reconnect the power to the opener and then open and close the door a few times to make sure that it’s working properly. If everything is working correctly, you’re done!

Where to Buy Garage Door Cable Replacement Parts

Garage door cable replacement parts can be found at most hardware stores and online. When shopping for the parts, make sure to get the right size and type of cable for your door. Additionally, be sure to double check that the cable is rated for the weight of your garage door.

Conclusion

Replacing your garage door cables is a relatively simple process, and it’s important to do so if you’ve noticed any signs of wear or damage. By following the steps outlined in this article, you should be able to replace your garage door cables on your own. Additionally, it’s easy to find the parts you need online or at your local hardware store.
