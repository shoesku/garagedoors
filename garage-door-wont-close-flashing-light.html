What To Do When Your Garage Door Won't Close And Flashes A Light?

Best Garage doors and references for house or residential with custom material and more design. We Share more picture design with HD quality images.

Having problems with your garage door? If it's not closing and flashing a light, it can be a major inconvenience. You may be wondering what to do and how to fix the problem. Luckily, there are a few common causes of this issue and easy steps you can take to fix it. This article will explain what to do if your garage door won't close and flashes a light.

Steps To Take When Your Garage Door Won't Close and Flashes a Light

1. Check the Motor and Door Sensors

The first step is to check the motor and the door sensors. The motor is responsible for opening and closing the door, and if it's not working correctly, it could be the reason why your garage door won't close and flashes a light. Additionally, the door sensors can also cause this issue. The door sensors are responsible for telling the motor when the door is open and when it's closed. If the sensors are not working correctly, they can prevent the door from closing.

2. Check the Tracks and Rollers

The next step is to check the tracks and rollers. The tracks and rollers are responsible for allowing the door to move up and down. If the tracks or rollers are damaged, they can prevent the door from closing and cause the light to flash. Additionally, if the tracks are not correctly aligned, it can also cause the same issue.

3. Check the Remote Control

The next step is to check the remote control. If the remote control is not working correctly, it can prevent the door from closing and cause the light to flash. Additionally, if the batteries are low, it can also cause this issue.

4. Check the Springs

The next step is to check the springs. The springs are responsible for balancing the weight of the door. If the springs are worn out or broken, they can prevent the door from closing and cause the light to flash.

5. Clean the Tracks and Rollers

The next step is to clean the tracks and rollers. If the tracks and rollers are dirty or covered in debris, it can prevent the door from closing and cause the light to flash. To clean the tracks and rollers, use a damp cloth to wipe away any dirt or debris.

6. Lubricate the Tracks and Rollers

The next step is to lubricate the tracks and rollers. If the tracks and rollers are not properly lubricated, it can prevent the door from closing and cause the light to flash. To lubricate the tracks and rollers, use a lubricant specifically designed for garage doors. Do not use any other type of lubricant as it could damage the tracks and rollers.

7. Adjust the Limit Settings

The next step is to adjust the limit settings. The limit settings are responsible for telling the motor when to stop opening and closing the door. If the limit settings are not properly adjusted, it can prevent the door from closing and cause the light to flash. To adjust the limit settings, refer to the owner's manual for your specific garage door opener.

8. Replace the Circuit Board

The next step is to replace the circuit board. The circuit board is responsible for controlling the motor and the door sensors. If the circuit board is not working correctly, it can prevent the door from closing and cause the light to flash. To replace the circuit board, refer to the owner's manual for your specific garage door opener.

9. Reset the Motor

The next step is to reset the motor. If the motor is not working correctly, it can prevent the door from closing and cause the light to flash. To reset the motor, refer to the owner's manual for your specific garage door opener.

10. Contact a Professional

If you have tried all of the steps above and your garage door still won't close and flashes a light, it may be time to contact a professional. A professional garage door repair technician can diagnose the problem and find a solution. It's always best to contact a professional as they are experienced and knowledgeable in garage door repair.

Conclusion

If your garage door won't close and flashes a light, it can be a major inconvenience. Luckily, there are a few common causes of this issue and easy steps you can take to fix it. The steps include checking the motor and door sensors, checking the tracks and rollers, checking the remote control, checking the springs, cleaning the tracks and rollers, lubricating the tracks and rollers, adjusting the limit settings, replacing the circuit board, resetting the motor, and contacting a professional. If you have tried all of these steps and your garage door still won't close and flashes a light, it may be time to contact a professional.
