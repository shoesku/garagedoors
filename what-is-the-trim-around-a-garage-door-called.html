If you are looking to spruce up the look of your garage, one of the best things you can do is to add trim around the opening of your garage door. This trim is often referred to as a “garage door jamb” and can be a great way to add a touch of style and sophistication to your home. But what exactly is the trim around a garage door called?

The trim around a garage door is typically referred to as a “door jamb” or “door trim”. This trim is typically made from wood, vinyl, aluminum, or steel, and is installed along the sides and top of the garage door opening. It is designed to provide a finished look to the opening and can also help to protect the door from the elements.

In addition to providing a finished look, door trim can also help to protect the garage door from the elements. It is designed to provide a barrier between the outside weather and the garage, thus keeping the door from rotting or getting rust. Additionally, the trim can be used to hide any gaps or cracks that may have developed over time.

Installing door trim is not a difficult task and can be done by a do-it-yourselfer with some basic tools. The materials needed are quite affordable and widely available, so you won't have to break the bank to add this finishing touch to your garage. The trim can also be painted or stained to match the look of your home and garage.

When installing the trim, it is important to make sure that it is properly sealed. This will help to prevent moisture from entering the garage and will also help to keep the trim looking great over time. Additionally, it is important to make sure that the trim is properly secured to the walls of the garage. This will help to ensure that it is firmly in place and will not easily be pulled off.

Once the trim is installed, it is important to regularly maintain it to ensure that it stays looking its best. This includes regularly cleaning and sealing the trim to protect it from the elements. Additionally, it is important to inspect the trim for any signs of damage or wear and tear and make repairs as needed.
