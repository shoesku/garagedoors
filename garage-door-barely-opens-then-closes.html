How To Fix A Garage Door That Barely Opens Then Closes

Having a garage door that barely opens then closes can be a nuisance and lead to a lot of frustration, especially when you’re expecting to get in and out of the garage quickly. But, don’t worry, this is a common problem and it’s actually quite easy to fix. In this article, we’ll show you how to diagnose and repair the issue so you can get your garage door functioning again in no time.

Steps to Fix a Garage Door That Barely Opens Then Closes

The first step to fixing a garage door that barely opens then closes is to identify the source of the problem. There are several possible causes, so it’s important to determine which one is causing your specific issue.

1. Check the Springs and Cables

The most common cause of a garage door that barely opens then closes is an issue with the springs. The springs are responsible for counterbalancing the weight of the door and allowing it to open and close smoothly. If the springs are too weak or too strong, it can cause the door to open and close too quickly or too slowly. As a result, you’ll likely experience the door barely opening then closing again.

To check your springs, open the door manually and observe how it moves. If it opens and closes quickly, you may need to replace the springs. If it moves slowly, you may need to adjust the tension on the springs. Either way, you’ll want to contact a professional to do the work for you.

In addition to the springs, you should also check the cables. The cables are responsible for holding the door up and are connected to the springs. If the cables are worn or frayed, they may need to be replaced.

2. Check the Opener

If the springs and cables seem to be functioning properly, you may have an issue with the opener. Sometimes, the opener can malfunction or become damaged, resulting in the door opening and closing too quickly or too slowly. If this is the case, you’ll need to call a professional to diagnose and repair the issue.

3. Check the Tracks

Another possible cause of a garage door that barely opens then closes is an issue with the tracks. If the tracks are damaged or misaligned, it can cause the door to open and close too quickly or too slowly. To check the tracks, you’ll need to open the door and look for any signs of damage. If you notice any damage, you should call a professional to repair the issue.

4. Check the Rollers

The last possible cause of a garage door that barely opens then closes is an issue with the rollers. If the rollers are worn or damaged, it can cause the door to move too quickly or too slowly. You can check the rollers by opening the door and inspecting them for any signs of wear or damage. If you notice any damage, you should call a professional to replace the rollers.

Conclusion

A garage door that barely opens then closes can be an annoying issue, but it’s actually quite easy to fix. The most common cause of this issue is an issue with the springs, cables, opener, tracks, or rollers. To diagnose and repair the issue, you’ll need to contact a professional. With a few simple repairs, you’ll be back to using your garage door in no time.
