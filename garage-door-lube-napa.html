How To Lubricate Your Garage Door With Napa Lube

Garage door maintenance can sometimes be a hassle, but it is essential to keep your garage door opening and closing properly. Lubrication is usually part of the routine maintenance you should give your garage door, and NAPA Lube is a great option to get the job done. NAPA Lube has a wide range of lubricants to choose from and is easily accessible in stores and online. Read on to learn how to lubricate your door with NAPA Lube.

Step 1: Choosing the Right Lubricant

The first step to lubricating your garage door with NAPA Lube is to select the right lubricant for your door. Every garage door is different, so it is important to choose a lubricant that is specifically made for garage doors. NAPA Lube offers a variety of different lubricants for garage doors, so you should have no problem finding the one that is right for you. Make sure to read the label carefully and select a product that is specifically designed for garage doors.

Step 2: Preparing the Door

Before you apply the lubricant, you need to make sure that your garage door is prepared. Start by cleaning off any dirt or debris that may be on the door. This will help ensure that the lubricant is applied evenly and will not be blocked by dirt or debris. You should also make sure that the door is in the closed position before you start the lubrication process.

Step 3: Applying the Lubricant

Once you have chosen the right lubricant and prepared the door, it is time to apply the lubricant. Start by spraying a light coating of the lubricant on all of the moving parts of the door. Make sure to get into all of the hard to reach spots as well. Once you have applied the lubricant, you should open and close the door a few times to spread the lubricant around.

Step 4: Clean Up

After you have lubricated your garage door, it is important to clean up any excess lubricant that may have been left behind. NAPA Lube lubricants are designed to be non-toxic and non-flammable, so you can easily wipe up any excess with a damp cloth. Make sure to wipe down the door and all of the moving parts to ensure that all of the lubricant has been removed.

Step 5: Finishing Up

Once you have finished lubricating your garage door with NAPA Lube, you should test out the door to make sure that it is working properly. Open and close the door several times to make sure that it is opening and closing smoothly. If the door is not functioning properly, you may need to apply more lubricant. If the door is still not working correctly, you should contact a professional for assistance.

Conclusion

Lubricating your garage door with NAPA Lube is a great way to keep your door in good condition. NAPA Lube has a wide selection of lubricants specifically designed for garage doors, so you should have no trouble finding the right one for your door. Make sure to follow the steps outlined above to get the best results from your lubrication. If you find that your door is still not functioning properly after lubricating it, contact a professional for assistance.
