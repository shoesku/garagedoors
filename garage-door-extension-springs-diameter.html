When you’re shopping for garage door extension springs, the diameter is one of the most important aspects to consider. The diameter of the spring you choose will determine the amount of tension it can handle, as well as how much lift it can provide. To ensure that you get the right spring for your garage door, it’s important to understand the different types of garage door extension springs and their corresponding diameters.

Types of Garage Door Extension Springs

Garage door extension springs come in two basic types: standard and high-cycle. Standard springs are designed to handle a lower number of cycles, while high-cycle springs are designed to handle more cycles. Generally, standard springs are more affordable, while high-cycle springs are more durable and have a longer lifespan. Depending on the type of spring you choose, it will come in a variety of sizes and diameters.

Standard Garage Door Extension Springs Diameter

Standard garage door extension springs typically come in two different diameters: 1 1/4 inch and 1 3/4 inch. A 1 1/4 inch spring is designed to lift a single-car garage door, while a 1 3/4 inch spring is designed to lift a two-car garage door. The 1 3/4 inch spring will also provide more tension than the 1 1/4 inch spring, so if you have a heavier single-car door, it may be a better option.

High-Cycle Garage Door Extension Springs Diameter

High-cycle garage door extension springs come in two different diameters: 2 inch and 2 1/4 inch. The 2 inch spring is designed for a single-car garage door, while the 2 1/4 inch spring is designed for a two-car garage door. As with standard springs, the 2 1/4 inch spring will provide more tension than the 2 inch spring, so it may be a better option if you have a heavier single-car door.

Important Considerations

When shopping for a garage door extension spring, it’s important to consider the size and weight of your garage door, as well as how often it’s used. If your door is on the heavier side, you may want to opt for a higher-cycle spring, as it will provide more tension and last longer. Additionally, if your door is used frequently, you may want to opt for a higher-cycle spring to ensure that it is able to handle the increased number of cycles.

Safety First

It’s important to note that garage door extension springs can be dangerous if not handled properly. To ensure your safety, it’s important to read the instructions that come with the springs and follow them carefully. Additionally, it’s recommended that you hire a professional to install the springs, as they are trained to do so safely and correctly.

Conclusion

Choosing the right garage door extension springs diameter is essential for ensuring that your garage door is both safe and effective. By understanding the different types of springs and their corresponding diameters, you can ensure that you choose the right spring for your needs. Additionally, it’s important to consider the size and weight of your door, as well as how often it’s used, to ensure that you get the right spring.
