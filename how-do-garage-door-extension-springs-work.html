Understanding How Garage Door Extension Springs Work

Most homeowners have a basic understanding of the workings of a garage door, but they may not be aware of the important role played by the extension springs. Extension springs are a critical piece of the puzzle when it comes to opening and closing a garage door, and a thorough understanding of how they work is essential. In this article, we will discuss the basic components of a garage door extension spring system and how it works to open and close the door.

What are Extension Springs?

Extension springs are a type of torsion spring, which are designed to store and release energy. They are usually made from high-strength steel and are formed into an arc shape. Extension springs are attached at one end to an anchor point and the other to the garage door itself. The springs are then wound to increase tension, which helps to open and close the door. The tension in the springs is adjusted to ensure that the door is properly balanced, which can be done by adding or removing coils.

How Do Extension Springs Work?

The extension springs are connected to the garage door and the anchor point, which is usually the ceiling. When the door is closed, the springs are tensioned and store energy. When the door is opened, the springs are released, and the energy stored in them is used to open the door. As the door is opened, the springs unwind and release their energy, which helps to lift the door. The same process occurs in reverse when the door is closed, and the springs store the energy again.

What are the Benefits of Extension Springs?

Extension springs provide several benefits, including increased safety and convenience. The tension in the springs helps to ensure that the door is properly balanced, which reduces the risk of injury or damage to the door and its components. Additionally, the springs make it easier to open and close the door, as the energy stored in them helps to lift the door. Finally, extension springs can last for many years with proper maintenance and care.

How to Maintain Extension Springs?

Extension springs can last for many years if they are properly maintained. Regular inspection and lubrication of the springs and the other components of the door system is essential to ensure that everything is in good working order. Additionally, it is important to make sure that the tension of the springs is adjusted to the correct level. If the tension is too low, the door may not open properly, and if it is too high, the door may be difficult to open or close.

What to do If the Extension Springs Break?

If the extension springs break, it is important to call a professional garage door repair service as soon as possible. Attempting to repair the springs on your own can be dangerous, as the springs are under a great deal of tension. A professional service can replace the springs and ensure that the tension is properly adjusted. Additionally, they can inspect the other components of the door system to make sure that everything is functioning properly.

Conclusion

Extension springs are an important component of a garage door system, and understanding how they work is essential for proper maintenance and repair. Extension springs are designed to store and release energy, which helps to open and close the door. It is important to inspect and lubricate the springs regularly to ensure that they are in good working order. Additionally, if the springs break, it is important to call a professional garage door repair service for assistance.
