Capping Your Garage Door Trim: A Step-By-Step Guide

If you’re looking to give your garage door trim a facelift, capping is an affordable and easy way to spruce up the look of your home. Capping the trim around your garage door helps seal out water and prevents rust, rot and decay. Not only does capping your garage door trim look great, but it also protects your garage door from the elements and adds value to your home.

Capping your garage door trim is a fairly straightforward process and can be done in a few short hours. In this step-by-step guide, we’ll take you through the process of capping your garage door trim so you can get the job done quickly and efficiently.

Step 1: Measure Your Garage Door Trim

The first step in capping your garage door trim is to measure the trim accurately. This will ensure that you purchase the right size and length of trim for the job. Measure the height and width of each side of the trim and record the measurements. This will ensure that you purchase the right size and length of trim for the job.

Step 2: Purchase Your Trim Capping Materials

Once you’ve taken your measurements, it’s time to purchase your trim capping materials. When selecting your trim capping materials, make sure to choose a material that is waterproof and durable. Vinyl, aluminum, and steel are all excellent choices for trim capping materials. Make sure to purchase enough material to cover the entire length of your garage door trim.

Step 3: Prepare the Trim for Capping

Before you begin capping your garage door trim, it’s important to make sure that the trim is properly prepared. Start by cleaning the trim with a mild detergent and warm water. Once the trim is clean, use a utility knife to remove any paint, caulk, or debris from the trim. This will help ensure that the trim capping material will adhere properly.

Step 4: Install the Trim Capping

Now that your trim is prepared, it’s time to install the trim capping. Start by measuring and cutting the trim capping material to size. If you’re using aluminum or steel trim capping, use tin snips to make the cuts. If you’re using vinyl trim capping, use a utility knife.

Once the trim capping has been cut to size, you’re ready to install it. Start by applying a thin layer of construction adhesive to the back of the trim capping material. Then, press the trim capping onto the garage door trim and hold it in place with clamps until the adhesive is dry.

Step 5: Finish the Job

Once the trim capping has been installed, it’s time to finish the job. Start by caulking any gaps or seams between the trim capping and the garage door trim. Once the caulk has dried, you can use a damp cloth to wipe away any excess caulk.

Finally, you’re ready to paint the trim capping. Before painting, make sure to prime the trim capping with an exterior primer. This will help ensure that the paint adheres properly. Once the primer has dried, you can apply two coats of exterior paint to the trim capping.

Conclusion

Capping your garage door trim is a great way to give your home a facelift and protect your garage door from the elements. It’s an easy and affordable way to add value to your home. By following the steps outlined in this guide, you can quickly and easily cap your garage door trim and give your home a fresh, new look.
