What You Need to Replace the Springs

Replacing the extension springs on your garage door can be a tricky task, but with the right tools and a few tips, it can be done. To start, you will need a few items to make sure the job is done safely and correctly. This list includes a tape measure, adjustable wrench, screwdriver, locking pliers, garage door lubricant, and a stepladder.

You will also need a few items specific to replacing the extension springs. This includes two replacement springs of the same size and weight as the ones currently on your door. If you're not sure of the size, take one of the old springs with you when you go to purchase the replacements. You will also need pliers, a clamp, and a pair of safety glasses.

Safety Tips for Replacing the Springs

Before you start replacing the springs, it's important to keep safety in mind. Make sure that you wear the safety glasses for protection and keep your hands away from the springs. Make sure that the door is closed and that the emergency release cord is disconnected before you start working on the springs.

If you are replacing the springs yourself, it's important to remember that they are under tension. When you remove the old springs, the tension will be released and the springs may fly off. To help contain the springs, you can use a clamp or vise grip. This will help keep the springs in place while you are working on them.

Steps for Replacing the Springs

Step 1: Measure the Current Springs

The first step in replacing the extension springs is to measure the current springs. Measure the length, diameter, and wire size of the springs. Record these measurements so that you can purchase springs of the same size and weight. Make sure that the springs you purchase have the same wire size, length, and diameter as the old ones.

Step 2: Disconnect the Old Springs

Once you have the new springs, it's time to disconnect the old ones. Start by loosening the bolts that hold the springs in place. Once the bolts are loose, you can disconnect the springs from the door. Make sure to keep a firm grip on the springs, as they will be under tension and may fly off once they are disconnected.

Step 3: Install the New Springs

Once the old springs are disconnected, you can start installing the new ones. Start by attaching one end of the spring to the door. Make sure that the spring is securely attached, then attach the other end of the spring to the door frame. Once both ends of the spring are attached, you can tighten the bolts that hold the springs in place.

Step 4: Test the Door

Once the springs are installed, it's time to test the door. Start by opening and closing the door a few times. Make sure that the door opens and closes smoothly and that the springs are providing the right amount of tension. If the door isn't working properly, you may need to adjust the tension of the springs.

Maintenance Tips for Your Garage Door Springs

Once you have replaced your garage door springs, it's important to keep them properly maintained. Make sure to lubricate the springs and the other moving parts of the door on a regular basis. This will help keep the springs from rusting and will help keep the door operating smoothly. Make sure to check the tension of the springs periodically to make sure that it is still correct.

Replacing the extension springs on your garage door is a job that can be done with the right tools and a few safety tips. Make sure to measure the current springs, disconnect the old springs, install the new springs, and test the door. Finally, make sure to keep the springs properly maintained to ensure that they last as long as possible.
