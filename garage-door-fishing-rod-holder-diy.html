Diy Garage Door Fishing Rod Holder

Fishing is one of the most popular recreational activities in the world, and for good reason. It’s a great way to relax and enjoy nature, and it can be a great hobby for people of all ages. Whether you’re a beginner or a seasoned pro, having the right gear can make all the difference. One of the most important pieces of gear is a fishing rod holder. But if you don’t have the space or the budget for a store-bought rod holder, you’re in luck. You can build your own with this easy DIY garage door fishing rod holder.

Steps to Make a DIY Garage Door Fishing Rod Holder

Making your own fishing rod holder is easier than you may think. All you need is a few basic tools and materials, and you can have it finished in just a few hours. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • Two 2x4s
  • Four 2-inch screws
  • Four 3-inch screws
  • Drill
  • Saw
  • Measuring tape
  • Pencil

Once you have all of your materials, you’re ready to begin. Here’s how to make your own DIY garage door fishing rod holder:

Step 1: Measure and Cut

First, you’ll need to measure and cut your 2x4s. You’ll need two pieces that are 12 inches long and two pieces that are 6 inches long. Once you’ve cut the pieces to size, you’re ready to move on to the next step.

Step 2: Drill and Screw

Next, you’ll need to drill and screw the pieces together. Start by using the 2-inch screws to attach the two 12-inch pieces together. Then, use the 3-inch screws to attach the two 6-inch pieces to the 12-inch pieces, making sure to leave a gap between the two 6-inch pieces. This gap will be where the fishing rods will be stored.

Step 3: Attach to the Garage Door

Once the pieces are all connected, it’s time to attach the holder to the garage door. Make sure the gap is facing the inside of the garage, then use the 3-inch screws to secure the holder to the door. Make sure to drill the screws into the studs in the door for extra support.

Step 4: Add Fishing Rods

Once the holder is securely attached to the door, you’re ready to add your fishing rods. Simply slide them into the gap between the two 6-inch pieces, and you’re ready to go. Now you have a custom fishing rod holder that you can take with you wherever you go.

Conclusion

Making your own DIY garage door fishing rod holder is a great way to save money and get creative with your fishing gear. With just a few basic tools and materials, you can have your own custom rod holder in no time. So what are you waiting for? Get started on your own DIY garage door fishing rod holder today.
