What To Do When Your Garage Door Keeps Opening After Closing

Best Garage doors and references for house or residential with custom material and more design. We Share more picture design with HD quality images.

Having a garage door that keeps opening after you close it can be a major nuisance, especially if you're in a hurry and you just want to leave your house. Fortunately, there are a few steps you can take to figure out what's causing the issue and fix it. In this article, we'll take a look at what could be causing your garage door to keep opening after you close it and what you can do to fix it. Read on to learn more.

Common Causes of Garage Doors Opening After Closing

There can be a few different culprits behind your garage door opening after you've closed it. One of the most common causes is a misaligned sensor. If the sensors are not aligned correctly, the garage door opener will think that something is in the way and will keep opening the door. Another possible cause is an obstruction. If there is something blocking the door's path, it can cause the door to open again after it has been closed. Finally, if the door is not properly balanced, it can cause it to open after it has been closed.

Troubleshooting Your Garage Door Problem

The first step in troubleshooting your garage door problem is to check the sensors. Make sure that the sensors are properly aligned and that there is nothing blocking them. If the sensors appear to be in good condition, then you should check the door itself. Make sure that the door is properly balanced and that there are no obstructions blocking the door's path. If you still can't figure out what's causing the issue, then it's time to call a professional.

Replacing Garage Door Components

If the sensors are not the issue, then you may need to replace some of the components on your garage door. For example, if the door is not properly balanced, then you may need to replace the springs or the rollers. You may also need to replace the cables, the opener, or the chain if they are worn or broken. If you're not sure how to replace these components, then it's best to consult a professional.

Cleaning and Lubricating the Tracks

If the sensors and components are in good condition, then you should check the tracks for debris and dirt. Dirt and debris can cause the door to open after it has been closed. Make sure to clean the tracks with a damp cloth and then lubricate them with a silicone-based lubricant. This should help solve the issue.

Checking the Wall Switch

If you're still having trouble with your garage door opening after it has been closed, then you should check the wall switch. The wall switch is located near the door and it is used to manually open and close the door. Make sure that it is in the "off" position when you close the door. If it is still in the "on" position, then the door will keep opening after it has been closed.

Upgrading Your Garage Door Opener

If none of the above steps have solved the issue, then you may need to upgrade your garage door opener. Many of the newer openers have safety sensors that will prevent the door from opening after it has been closed. These openers can be more expensive, but they are worth the investment if you want to avoid having your garage door open after it has been closed.

Call a Professional

If none of the above steps have solved the issue, then it's time to call a professional. A professional can take a look at your door and figure out what's causing the issue. They can then either fix the problem or recommend a replacement. If you're not comfortable with doing the work yourself, then it's best to leave it to the professionals.

Conclusion

A garage door that keeps opening after you've closed it can be a major nuisance. Fortunately, there are a few steps you can take to figure out what's causing the issue and fix it. Check the sensors, replace any components that are worn or broken, clean and lubricate the tracks, check the wall switch, and upgrade your garage door opener if necessary. If none of these steps have solved the issue, then it's time to call a professional.
