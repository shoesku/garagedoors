Everything You Need To Know About Garage Door Extension Spring Size

It’s no surprise that garage door extension springs are an integral part of your garage door system. They hold the door up and provide the counterbalance when the door opens and closes. If you’re in the market for a new garage door, you’ll need to select the right size of extension springs for your system. In this article, we’ll discuss the basics of garage door extension spring size and how to select the right one for your needs.

What is a Garage Door Extension Spring?

Garage door extension springs are long and coiled springs that are attached to either side of the garage door. They are designed to provide a counterbalance to the weight of the door and help to support the door while it is in use. Without extension springs, the door would be unable to open and close correctly.

What Factors Affect the Size of the Extension Springs?

The size of the extension spring you need will depend on a variety of factors, including the size and weight of the garage door. If you have a larger and heavier door, you’ll need to select a larger and stronger spring. On the other hand, if you have a smaller and lighter door, you’ll need to select a smaller and lighter spring.

How to Determine the Right Size for Your Door

The easiest way to determine the right size for your garage door is to consult a professional. A garage door technician can measure your door and select the right extension spring size for you. However, if you’d like to do it yourself, there are a few things you can do. First, measure the height and width of the door. Then, weigh the door using a scale. Next, add a few extra pounds to account for the weatherstripping and hardware. Finally, multiply the weight of the door by the height and width to get the total weight of the door.

What is the Correct Extension Spring Size?

Once you’ve determined the weight of your door, you can select the correct extension spring size. Generally, you’ll want to select a spring that is rated for at least twice the weight of your door. For example, if your door weighs 120 pounds, you’ll want to select a spring that is rated for at least 240 pounds. This will ensure that the spring can handle the weight of the door and provide a good counterbalance.

How to Install the Extension Spring

Once you’ve selected the right size of extension spring, it’s time to install it. Installing the spring can be dangerous, so it’s important to take the necessary safety precautions. First, make sure to wear safety glasses and gloves. Then, disconnect the power to the garage door opener. Next, loosen the bolts that hold the extension spring in place. Finally, remove the old spring and replace it with the new one. Make sure to tighten the bolts securely when you’re done.

What Else Do I Need to Know?

It’s important to note that garage door extension springs are under a great deal of tension. If you don’t feel comfortable installing the springs yourself, contact a professional for assistance. Furthermore, it’s important to check the springs regularly for signs of wear or damage. If you notice any frayed or broken wires, replace the spring immediately.

Conclusion

Garage door extension spring size is an important factor to consider when purchasing a new garage door. The size of the spring you need will depend on the size and weight of your door. To ensure that you select the right size, consult a professional or measure the door yourself. Once you’ve selected the right size, it’s important to install the spring correctly and inspect it regularly for signs of wear or damage. By following these tips, you can ensure that your garage door operates correctly for many years to come.
