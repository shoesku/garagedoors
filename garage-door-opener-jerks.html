Garage Door Openers Jerks: How To Reduce Unwanted Movement

Having a garage door opener that jerks and moves erratically can be a major annoyance and a risk to your family. Fortunately, there are a few ways to reduce the amount of unwanted movement in your garage door opener, allowing your family to use the door safely and comfortably.

Adjust the Adjustable Force Settings

One of the easiest and most effective ways to reduce the jerking of your garage door opener is to adjust the adjustable force settings. Every garage door opener includes adjustable force settings, which can be adjusted for different levels of tension and resistance. By adjusting the settings, you can reduce the amount of jerking and movement in the door.

To adjust the adjustable force settings, you'll need to consult your garage door opener's manual for the proper procedure. You may also need to purchase a tension spring or other hardware to make the adjustments, depending on the type of opener you have. Once the adjustments have been made, you should notice a significant reduction in the amount of jerking and movement in your garage door.

Check the Balance of the Door

Another common cause of jerking and movement in a garage door opener is an unbalanced door. If the door is not properly balanced, it can cause the opener to jerk and move erratically. To check the balance of your door, you'll need to consult your opener's manual for the proper procedure. In some cases, you may need to purchase specific tools to complete the process.

Once the balance of the door has been established, you should notice a reduction in the amount of jerking and movement in the opener. If the problem persists, you may need to adjust the adjustable force settings again, or contact a professional for further assistance.

Check the Track and Rollers

If the balance of your door and the adjustable force settings have been adjusted and the problem persists, then you should check the track and rollers for any obstructions or damage. If the rollers are worn or the track is bent, then the opener will jerk and move erratically. To check the track and rollers, you should consult your opener's manual for the proper procedure.

If you find any obstructions or damage, then you should replace the rollers and/or straighten the track. Once the track and rollers are in good condition, you should notice a reduction in the amount of jerking and movement in your garage door opener.

Replace the Springs and Cables

If the track and rollers are in good condition and the problem persists, then you should consider replacing the springs and cables. Over time, these components can become worn and cause the opener to jerk and move erratically. To replace the springs and cables, you'll need to consult your opener's manual for the proper procedure.

Replacing the springs and cables is a relatively simple procedure, but it should only be done by a professional. If the springs and cables are not replaced correctly, it can cause serious injury or damage to your garage door opener. Once the springs and cables have been replaced, you should notice a significant reduction in the amount of jerking and movement in your garage door opener.

Conclusion

Having a garage door opener that jerks and moves erratically can be a major annoyance and a risk to your family. Fortunately, there are a few ways to reduce the amount of unwanted movement in your garage door opener, allowing your family to use the door safely and comfortably.

Adjusting the adjustable force settings, checking the balance of the door, checking the track and rollers, and replacing the springs and cables are all effective ways to reduce the amount of jerking and movement in your garage door opener. While some of these procedures can be done yourself, others should only be done by a professional.
