Troubleshooting An Overhead Garage Door Not Closing All The Way

Best Garage doors and references for house or residential with custom material and more design. We Share more picture design with HD quality images.

Having an overhead garage door that won't close all the way can be an annoying, and even dangerous, problem. You may find yourself constantly having to manually close the door or make sure that it is closed all the way. Fortunately, there are some steps you can take to troubleshoot the problem and get it fixed quickly.

Check the Springs

The first thing you should do is check the springs on your overhead garage door. There are two types of springs that are typically used: torsion springs and extension springs. Torsion springs are located at the top of the garage door, while extension springs are located on each side of the door. If you find that one of the springs is broken or worn, you should replace it immediately to avoid further damage to the door.

Check the Tracks

Another possible cause of an overhead garage door not closing all the way is an issue with the tracks. If the tracks are blocked, misaligned, or bent, the door may not be able to close properly. You should inspect the tracks for any signs of damage and if necessary, straighten or replace them. You should also check to make sure that the tracks are clear of any debris or dirt. If they are not, you should clean them and then lubricate them with a silicone-based lubricant.

Check the Sensors

The sensors on your overhead garage door can also be a potential cause of the problem. The sensors are designed to detect any objects or people that may be in the way of the door. If the sensors are not working properly, the door may stop closing before it is all the way shut. You should check the sensors for any signs of damage and if necessary, replace them.

Check the Motor

The motor on your overhead garage door is responsible for powering the springs and opening and closing the door. If the motor is not working properly, the door may not close all the way. You should inspect the motor for any signs of damage and if necessary, replace it. You should also make sure that it is properly lubricated with a silicone-based lubricant.

Check the Limit Switch

The limit switch on your overhead garage door is responsible for regulating the amount of force that is applied to the door as it closes. If the limit switch is not working properly, the door may not close all the way. You should inspect the limit switch for any signs of damage and if necessary, replace it.

Check the Cables

The cables on your overhead garage door are responsible for helping to keep the door in place as it moves. If the cables are worn or broken, the door may not be able to close properly. You should check the cables for any signs of damage and if necessary, replace them.

Check for Loose Screws

Another possible cause of an overhead garage door not closing all the way is loose screws. Loose screws can cause the door to become misaligned, which will prevent it from closing properly. You should check all of the screws on the door for any signs of looseness and if necessary, tighten them.

Check the Weather Stripping

The weather stripping on your overhead garage door is responsible for providing a seal between the door and the frame. If the weather stripping is worn or damaged, the door may not be able to close properly. You should check the weather stripping for any signs of damage and if necessary, replace it.

Conclusion

If your overhead garage door is not closing all the way, there are a few steps you can take to troubleshoot the problem. You should check the springs, tracks, sensors, motor, limit switch, cables, screws, and weather stripping to make sure that everything is working properly. If any of these components are worn or damaged, you should replace them to get your door working again.
