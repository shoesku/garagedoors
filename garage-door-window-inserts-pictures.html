What Are Garage Door Window Inserts?

Garage door window inserts are pieces of glass or plastic that are inserted into the door itself. They come in a variety of sizes, shapes, and styles, and can be used to give your garage a more modern look. They may also be used to provide a bit of extra insulation to the area.

Types of Garage Door Window Inserts

The two main types of window inserts are fixed and sliding. Fixed inserts are permanently attached to the door and can’t be moved once they’re in place. Sliding inserts can be moved up and down to provide more ventilation or privacy. The most popular types of inserts are acrylic, polycarbonate, and tempered glass. Acrylic is the most affordable option and is often used for decorative purposes. Polycarbonate is a bit more expensive, but it’s also more durable and offers better insulation. Tempered glass is the most expensive option, but it provides the most insulation and is the strongest type of insert.

Choosing the Right Insert for Your Garage Door

When choosing a window insert for your garage door, you’ll need to consider a few factors. First, you’ll need to decide whether you want a fixed or sliding insert. Then, you’ll need to decide which type of material you want. Finally, you’ll need to measure the area where the insert will be placed and make sure you get the right size.

Installing Your Garage Door Window Insert

Installing a window insert is fairly easy and can usually be done in a few hours. First, you’ll need to measure the area where the insert will be placed. Then, you’ll need to make sure the insert fits properly. Finally, you’ll need to attach the insert to the door itself. Most inserts come with instructions, so make sure to read them carefully before attempting the installation. It’s also a good idea to wear safety glasses and gloves during the process.

Caring for Your Garage Door Window Insert

Once your insert is installed, you’ll need to take care of it. Regularly clean the insert with a soft cloth or sponge and mild detergent. You should also avoid using harsh chemicals, as this could damage the insert.
