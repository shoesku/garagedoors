Garage Door Sensor Not Working In Sunlight: What To Do?

Summer is a great time of year to get outside and enjoy the sunshine. Unfortunately, the same summer weather can wreak havoc on your garage door sensor. If you have noticed that your garage door isn’t responding to your remote control, or if it’s been getting stuck, then you may have a problem with your garage door sensor. Here are a few tips for dealing with a garage door sensor that’s not working in the sunlight.

Check the Connections

The first step is to check the connections and make sure that everything is plugged in properly. If there’s something wrong with the wiring, then it can cause your sensor to malfunction in the sunlight. Check the connections and make sure that everything is properly connected. If you find something wrong, then it’s best to call in a professional to take a look at it. Trying to fix it yourself can be dangerous, and it can also void the warranty on your garage door.

Clean the Sensor

Another thing you can do is to clean the sensor. Over time, dust and dirt can accumulate on the sensor, which can cause it to malfunction in the sunlight. Use a soft cloth and some warm water to gently clean the sensor. Make sure to thoroughly dry the sensor before plugging it back in.

Check the Cables

If the connections and the sensor are fine, then the next step is to check the cables. The cables can become loose or damaged over time, which can affect the sensor’s performance. Check the cables and make sure that they’re firmly secured. If you find any damaged cables, then it’s best to replace them.

Adjust the Settings

If all else fails, then you may want to try adjusting the settings on your garage door opener. Many openers have an option to adjust the sensitivity of the sensor. Try adjusting the settings to see if it helps. If you still can’t get your garage door to work properly in the sunlight, then it’s best to call in a professional.

Check the Sun’s Position

The last tip is to check the sun’s position. If the sun is shining directly onto the garage door sensor, then it can cause it to malfunction. Make sure to keep the sun out of the sensor’s direct line of sight. If that doesn’t work, then you may need to install a sunshade or awning to keep the sun off of the sensor.

Conclusion

If your garage door sensor isn’t working in the sunlight, then there are a few things you can try. First, check the connections and make sure that everything is plugged in properly. Second, clean the sensor and check the cables. Third, adjust the settings on your garage door opener. Finally, check the sun’s position and make sure that it’s not shining directly onto the sensor. If all else fails, then it’s best to call in a professional to take a look at it.
