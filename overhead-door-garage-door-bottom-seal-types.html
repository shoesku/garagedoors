Top 5 Overhead Door Garage Door Bottom Seal Types For 2023

The garage door is an important part of any home, and keeping it sealed is essential to keeping your home safe and secure. One of the best ways to ensure your garage is properly sealed is by investing in a quality garage door bottom seal. By selecting the right seal for your garage, you can ensure that your garage is well protected from the elements, as well as from pests, debris, and other unwanted intruders.

There are a variety of overhead door garage door bottom seals available in the market, each with their own set of features and benefits. The type of seal that you choose should be determined by the size and shape of your garage door, as well as the climate and weather conditions in your area. Here are some of the most popular overhead door garage door bottom seals for 2023:

1. Brush Seals

Brush seals are a common choice for overhead door garage door bottom seals, as they are easy to install, and can provide great protection against drafts and debris. Brush seals consist of a brush strip that is attached to a plastic or metal holder. The brush strip is made of nylon or polypropylene bristles, and is designed to create a tight seal against the garage door. Brush seals are available in a variety of sizes, and can be easily trimmed to fit. Brush seals are also relatively inexpensive, making them a great option for those on a budget.

2. Foam Seals

Foam seals are a great option for those looking for an effective way to seal their garage door. Foam seals are made from a flexible foam material, and are designed to fit tightly against the door. They are available in a variety of sizes, and can be easily trimmed to fit. Foam seals can provide a great seal against wind and moisture, and are relatively inexpensive. However, they may not be as effective against debris and pests as some of the other seal types.

3. Vinyl Seals

Vinyl seals are a popular choice for overhead door garage door bottom seals, as they are durable and long lasting. Vinyl seals are made of a heavy-duty vinyl material, and are designed to provide a tight seal against the garage door. Vinyl seals are available in a variety of sizes, and can be easily trimmed to fit. They are relatively inexpensive and can provide a great seal against wind and moisture, but may not be as effective against pests and debris as some of the other seal types.

4. Magnetic Seals

Magnetic seals are becoming more popular for overhead door garage door bottom seals, as they provide a great seal against wind and moisture. Magnetic seals consist of a strong magnetic strip that is attached to a plastic or metal holder. The magnetic strip is designed to create a tight seal against the garage door. Magnetic seals are available in a variety of sizes, and can be easily trimmed to fit. They are relatively inexpensive, and can provide a great seal against wind and moisture, but may not be as effective against pests and debris as some of the other seal types.

5. PVC Seals

PVC seals are a great option for those looking for an effective way to seal their garage door. PVC seals are made from a flexible PVC material, and are designed to fit tightly against the door. They are available in a variety of sizes, and can be easily trimmed to fit. PVC seals can provide a great seal against wind and moisture, and are relatively inexpensive. However, they may not be as effective against debris and pests as some of the other seal types.

No matter which type of overhead door garage door bottom seal you choose, it is important to measure your garage door carefully before purchasing. This will ensure that you select the right size and type of seal for your garage door. With the right seal, you can be sure that your garage door will be properly sealed and secure.
