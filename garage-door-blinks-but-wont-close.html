How To Fix A Garage Door That Blinks But Won't Close In 2023

Introduction

It's a common problem for homeowners: you press the button on your garage door opener and the door starts to close, but then it stops and blinks. This can be a frustrating issue, especially since you know it can be easily fixed. Fortunately, the solution is usually simple and can be done with just a few tools. In this article, we'll show you how to fix a garage door that blinks but won't close in 2023.

Steps for Fixing a Garage Door That Blinks But Won't Close

The first step is to identify the problem. If the garage door opener is blinking but won't close, it's most likely because the safety sensors are not aligned. The safety sensors are located on either side of the door and should be aligned for the door to close properly. If the sensors are not aligned, the door won't close and the opener will blink.

The next step is to check the photo eyes. The photo eyes are located on either side of the door and act as safety sensors. They should be clean and unobstructed in order for the door to close properly. If the photo eyes are dirty or obstructed, they won't detect the door properly and the opener will blink.

The third step is to check the power. Make sure the power is on and that the garage door opener is plugged in. If the power is off, the opener won't work properly and the door won't close.

The fourth step is to check the limit switch. The limit switch is located on the motor and is responsible for setting the limit of how far the door can open and close. If the limit switch is not set properly, the door won't close and the opener will blink.

The fifth step is to check the remote control. Make sure the remote control is working properly. If the remote control is not working, the door won't close and the opener will blink.

The sixth step is to check the wall control. The wall control is located on the wall and is responsible for controlling the door. If the wall control is not working properly, the door won't close and the opener will blink.

The seventh step is to check the wiring. Make sure all the wiring is connected properly and that there are no loose wires. If the wiring is not connected properly, the door won't close and the opener will blink.

The eighth step is to check the chain. The chain is located on the motor and is responsible for lifting the door. If the chain is not working properly, the door won't close and the opener will blink.

The ninth step is to check the spring. The spring is located on the door and is responsible for providing tension to lift the door. If the spring is not working properly, the door won't close and the opener will blink.

The tenth step is to check the rollers. The rollers are located on the door and are responsible for providing smooth operation. If the rollers are worn or damaged, the door won't close and the opener will blink.

Conclusion

Fixing a garage door that blinks but won't close in 2023 can be a frustrating and time consuming task. However, with a few simple steps, you can quickly identify and fix the problem. Just remember to check the safety sensors, photo eyes, power, limit switch, remote control, wall control, wiring, chain, spring, and rollers. With these steps, you'll be able to fix your garage door in no time.
