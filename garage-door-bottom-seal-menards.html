Garage door bottom seals are a crucial part of any garage door system. They help to keep out dirt, dust, water, and other debris that could otherwise be harmful to the garage door and its components. Menards is a popular home improvement store with a variety of garage door bottom seals to choose from.

Garage door bottom seals come in a variety of sizes, shapes, and materials, so it’s important to make sure you’re getting the right one for your needs. Here’s a comprehensive guide to garage door bottom seals at Menards to help you make an informed decision.

Types of Garage Door Bottom Seals

The first step in choosing the right garage door bottom seal is to understand the different types of seals available. Menards offers several different types of seals, including vinyl, rubber, and metal seals.

Vinyl seals are a popular choice because they’re easy to install and relatively inexpensive. They’re also long-lasting, durable, and weather-resistant. However, they may not be the best choice for high-traffic areas because they can be easily damaged.

Rubber seals are another popular choice. They’re flexible, durable, and resistant to most weather conditions. They’re also easy to install and relatively inexpensive. However, rubber seals may not be the best choice for areas that receive a lot of sunlight because they can become brittle over time.

Metal seals are the most durable and long-lasting choice, but they’re also the most expensive. They’re resistant to weather, temperature, and wear and tear, and they’re easy to install. However, they can be difficult to maintain and they may not be the best choice for areas that receive a lot of sunlight.

Garage Door Bottom Seal Sizing

Once you’ve chosen the type of seal you want, it’s time to consider size. Menards offers a variety of sizes to fit almost any garage door. The first step in getting the right size is to measure your garage door opening. Measure the width and the height of the opening, and then use the following formula to determine the correct size seal:

Width of the opening + height of the opening = size of the seal

For example, if the width of your opening is 8 feet and the height is 7 feet, the size of the seal you need is 15 feet.

Garage Door Bottom Seal Installation

Once you’ve chosen the right size seal, it’s time to install it. Menards offers a variety of installation options, including adhesive strips, screws, and nails. If you’re not comfortable with any of these options, you can hire a professional to install the seal for you.

To install the seal yourself, start by cleaning the area around the door opening. Remove any dirt, dust, or debris that may be present. Then, cut the seal to the appropriate size and attach it to the door using adhesive strips, screws, or nails.

Finally, test the seal to make sure it is properly installed. Open and close the door several times to make sure the seal is properly sealed and that it does not move or become loose. If you find any issues, adjust the seal and test again until it is installed correctly.

Garage Door Bottom Seal Maintenance

Once your seal is installed, it’s important to keep it in good condition. Regularly inspect the seal for signs of wear and tear, and replace it when necessary. Also, make sure to regularly clean the seal with a damp cloth to keep it free of dirt, dust, and debris.

Finally, it’s important to regularly lubricate the seal to keep it in good condition. Use a lubricant specifically designed for garage door seals, such as WD-40 or silicone-based lubricant, and apply it to the seal every few months.

Conclusion

Garage door bottom seals are an essential part of any garage door system. Menards offers a variety of seals in different sizes, shapes, and materials, so you can find the perfect seal for your needs. Make sure to choose the right size, install the seal correctly, and maintain it regularly to keep your garage door working properly.
