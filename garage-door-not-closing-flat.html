How To Fix A Garage Door Not Closing Flat

Introduction

Garage door not closing flat is a common problem for many homeowners. The garage door is the largest entry point into your home, so it’s important to ensure it is secure. If the door does not close flat, it can be an easy target for burglars. It is also important to ensure that the door is properly aligned so that it will close and open properly. In this article, we will discuss the common causes of a garage door not closing flat and how to fix it.

Common Causes of a Garage Door Not Closing Flat

The most common causes of a garage door not closing flat are misaligned rollers, worn out hinges, and a bent track. If the rollers are misaligned, they can cause the door to become off balance and not close flat. If the hinges are worn out, they can cause the door to be difficult to close. A bent track can also cause the door to be off balance and not close properly. Other causes can include a lack of lubrication, poor maintenance, and a broken spring.

Inspecting the Door

Before attempting to repair the issue, it is important to inspect the door for any visible signs of damage. Check the rollers to see if they are bent or misaligned. Also check the hinges to make sure they are in good condition. Lastly, check the track to make sure it is not bent or twisted. If any of these components are damaged, they should be replaced before attempting to repair the door.

Lubricating the Parts

If the door is not closing flat and all of the components appear to be in good condition, it may be necessary to lubricate the parts. The rollers, hinges, and track should all be lubricated with a light oil or grease. This will help reduce friction and make it easier for the door to close flat. It is important to use a lubricant that is designed for use on garage doors.

Adjusting the Rollers

If the rollers are misaligned, they can cause the door to be off balance and not close flat. To adjust the rollers, you will need to loosen the screws that hold them in place. Then, carefully move the roller into the proper position. Once the rollers are in the proper position, the screws should be tightened and the door tested to make sure it is closing properly.

Replacing the Hinges

If the hinges are worn out, they can cause the door to be difficult to close. To replace the hinges, you will need to purchase new ones from a hardware store. Then, remove the old hinges and replace them with the new ones. Once the hinges are installed, the door should be tested to make sure it is closing properly.

Replacing the Track

If the track is bent or twisted, it can cause the door to be off balance and not close properly. To replace the track, you will need to purchase a new one from a hardware store. Then, remove the old track and replace it with the new one. Once the track is installed, the door should be tested to make sure it is closing properly.

Replacing the Springs

If the springs are broken, they can cause the door to be difficult to open and close. To replace the springs, you will need to purchase new ones from a hardware store. Then, remove the old springs and replace them with the new ones. Once the springs are installed, the door should be tested to make sure it is closing properly.

Conclusion

Garage door not closing flat is a common problem for many homeowners. Fortunately, this issue can usually be fixed without the need to call a professional. If the rollers are misaligned, they can be adjusted. If the hinges are worn out, they can be replaced. If the track is bent or twisted, it can be replaced. And, if the springs are broken, they can be replaced. With the proper inspection and maintenance, you can ensure your garage door is secure and functioning properly.
