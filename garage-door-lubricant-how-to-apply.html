Garage Door Lubricant: How To Apply It Like A Pro

Best Garage doors and references for house or residential with custom material and more design. We Share more picture design with HD quality images.

Keeping your garage door in good working order is an important factor in home maintenance. Not only does it provide security for your home, but it also helps to keep a consistent temperature in your home. One way to ensure that your garage door is operating to its full potential is to make sure that it is properly lubricated. Lubricating a garage door may seem like a daunting task, but it doesn’t have to be. In this article, we’ll discuss the steps you need to take to lubricate your garage door like a pro.

What You Need to Know Before You Start

Before you begin the process of lubricating your garage door, it’s important to understand the parts of the garage door that need to be lubricated. Most garage doors have two parts that need to be lubricated: the hinges and the rollers. The hinges are the metal joints that connect the panels of the door, while the rollers are the small wheels that help the door move along the tracks.

It’s important to note that not all garage doors are the same. Some may have additional bearings or springs that need to be lubricated as well. If you are unsure which parts of your garage door need to be lubricated, it’s best to consult a professional before you start.

What Type of Lubricant Should You Use?

Once you know what parts of your garage door need to be lubricated, it’s time to decide which type of lubricant to use. There are a variety of lubricants available on the market, but the best type for garage doors is a silicone-based lubricant. This type of lubricant won’t attract dirt and dust, which can cause your garage door to malfunction over time. It is also designed to withstand extreme temperatures, which makes it ideal for garage doors.

How to Lubricate Your Garage Door

Once you have the necessary lubricant, you can begin the process of lubricating your garage door. Start by using a cloth to wipe down all of the hinges and rollers. This will help to remove any dirt or dust that may be present. Then, take the lubricant and apply a small amount to each hinge and roller. Make sure to get into all of the nooks and crannies so that the lubricant is evenly distributed.

Once you have applied the lubricant, use a clean cloth to wipe away any excess. Then, open and close the garage door several times to ensure that the lubricant is evenly distributed. If you hear any squeaking or grinding noises, it’s a good indication that the lubricant is being distributed properly.

How Often Should You Lubricate Your Garage Door?

Now that you know how to lubricate your garage door, it’s important to understand how often it should be done. It’s generally recommended that you lubricate your garage door at least once every three months. This will help to ensure that the hinges and rollers are properly lubricated and aren’t in danger of malfunctioning.

What If You Have Additional Parts?

If you have additional parts on your garage door that need to be lubricated, such as bearings or springs, it’s best to consult a professional. These parts can be difficult to lubricate and can be easily damaged if done improperly. A professional will be able to properly lubricate these parts and ensure that your garage door is functioning properly.

Conclusion

Lubricating your garage door is an important part of home maintenance. By following the steps outlined in this article, you will be able to lubricate your garage door like a pro. Make sure to use a silicone-based lubricant for the best results and to consult a professional if you have any additional parts that need to be lubricated.
