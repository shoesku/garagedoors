Install Garage Door Trim Seal In Minutes

With the summer heat baking down on your garage door, you’re likely thinking about ways to keep your garage cooler. One of the best solutions is to install a trim seal. Trim seals are designed to block air from entering your home, giving you a cooler garage and a more energy efficient home. But how do you install them? We’re here to help. In this article, we’ll give you step-by-step instructions for installing a garage door trim seal.

Tools and Supplies Needed for Installation

There are a few tools and supplies you’ll need to install your trim seal. First, you’ll need a pair of scissors or a utility knife to cut the seal to size. You’ll also need a tape measure, a screwdriver, and a drill. Finally, you’ll need a trim seal kit, which should include the trim seal, mounting screws, and installation instructions. If you don’t have all of these items, you can buy them at your local hardware store.

Step One: Measure the Door

The first step is to measure the door. You’ll need to measure the height and width of the door. Make sure to measure from the top of the door to the bottom, and from side to side. Once you have these measurements, you can cut the trim seal to the correct size. It’s important to make sure the seal is the correct size, as an improperly sized seal won’t block air from entering your garage.

Step Two: Cut the Seal

Once you’ve measured the door, you can use the scissors or utility knife to cut the seal to the correct size. Make sure to measure twice and cut once, as you don’t want to end up with a seal that’s too small or too large. You should also be careful not to cut too close to the edge of the door.

Step Three: Attach the Seal

Once you’ve cut the seal to size, you’re ready to attach it to the door. Start by using the drill to attach the mounting screws to the door frame. These screws should be spaced evenly and should be in line with the edge of the door. Once the screws are attached, you can then place the trim seal over the screws. Make sure the seal is flush with the door frame, and use the screwdriver to secure the seal.

Step Four: Test the Seal

Once you’ve attached the trim seal to the door, you’ll want to test it out. Open the door and feel for any air coming in. If you feel a draft, you may need to adjust the seal or move the mounting screws. Once you’ve got the seal sealed up tight, you’re ready to enjoy a cooler garage.

Conclusion

Installing a trim seal is a great way to keep your garage cooler and reduce your energy costs. With the right tools and supplies, you can easily install a trim seal in just minutes. Now that you know how to install a trim seal, you can enjoy a cooler garage and a more energy efficient home.
