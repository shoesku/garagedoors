How To Stop Rain Coming Under The Garage Door

Best Garage doors and references for house or residential with custom material and more design. We Share more picture design with HD quality images.

Garage doors provide essential protection for your vehicle, tools, and other belongings. But, when rain begins to enter the garage, your possessions and car can be damaged. You could end up spending thousands of dollars in repairs and replacements. That’s why it’s important to understand how to stop rain coming under the garage door.

Check the Seals Around the Garage Door

The first thing you should check when trying to stop rain coming under the garage door is the seals. These are the rubber or plastic strips that line the sides and the bottom of the door. If the seals are damaged, they won’t be able to keep out the water. You should examine them carefully and make sure they are in good condition. If not, you may need to replace them.

Look for Cracks in the Door

Another potential source for water entering the garage is a crack in the door. This is especially common in older garage doors. To check for cracks, look closely at the door. You should pay special attention to the edges and corners, as these are the areas most likely to have small cracks. If you find any, use a caulk or sealant to fill them in.

Check the Weather Stripping

The weather stripping is the strip of foam or rubber that runs along the bottom of the garage door. It helps keep out moisture, dirt, and debris. Over time, it can get worn out and start to crack. You can replace it fairly easily. Just measure the door and purchase the appropriate size. Then, simply remove the old weather stripping and replace it with the new one.

Check the Door Threshold

The threshold of the door is also an important factor when it comes to keeping out water. The threshold is the metal strip that lines the bottom of the door. If it’s not installed correctly, water can run underneath it and into the garage. To check the threshold, just look at it and make sure it’s flush with the door. If it’s not, remove it, and adjust it so that it is.

Install a Rain Gutter

If you’re struggling to keep water out of your garage, you could consider installing a rain gutter. This is a metal trough that is installed above the garage door. It collects rainwater and diverts it away from the door. It’s a fairly simple job to install one, and they are relatively inexpensive. It’s a great way to ensure that water stays away from your garage.

Check the Door for Damage

The garage door itself can also be a source of water entering the garage. If the door is made of wood, it can become warped over time. Or, it can become cracked or have holes in it. If you find any damage, you should repair or replace the door as soon as possible. This will help stop water from coming in.

Check the Sill Plate

The sill plate is the board that sits between the door and the floor of the garage. It helps to keep out water and debris. If it’s damaged or missing, water can easily enter the garage. To check the sill plate, just look at it and make sure it’s in good condition. If it’s not, you may need to replace it.

Check the Tracks of the Door

The tracks of the garage door can also be a source of water entering the garage. If the tracks are bent or damaged, water can easily run through them. To check the tracks, just look at them and make sure they are in good condition. If they are not, you may need to repair or replace them.

Conclusion

Keeping water out of the garage is essential to protecting your vehicle and belongings. By following these steps, you can help stop rain coming under the garage door and keep your garage dry. Make sure to check the seals, weather stripping, door threshold, and tracks regularly to ensure they are in good condition. If you find any damage, repair or replace it as soon as possible.
