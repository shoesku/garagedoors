How To Fix A Garage Door That Gets Stuck

Best Garage doors and references for house or residential with custom material and more design. We Share more picture design with HD quality images.

Introduction

Garage doors are a great convenience and are used in almost every home. But, like any other mechanical device, they can sometimes malfunction and stick when you open or close them. If your garage door is stuck, it can be a real hassle. This article will explain a few possible causes of a stuck garage door and how you can fix it. Read on to learn some useful tips on how to fix a garage door that gets stuck.

Check the Tracks and Roller

The first thing you should do when trying to fix a stuck garage door is to check the tracks and roller. If the tracks have become bent or misaligned, it can cause the garage door to get stuck. To check the tracks, stand outside the garage and look at the tracks. Make sure the tracks are properly aligned and there are no bent pieces that could be causing the issue. If the tracks are bent, you may need to call a professional to come and repair them.

Examine the Garage Door Opener

The next step is to examine the garage door opener. If the opener is not functioning properly, it could be the cause of the stuck garage door. Check the wiring and connections to make sure everything is in working order. If the opener is not functioning properly, you may need to replace it. If you do not feel comfortable doing this yourself, you should call a professional.

Clean the Tracks and Rollers

If the tracks and rollers are not the cause of the stuck garage door, then you should clean them. Dirt, debris, and other particles can build up on the tracks and rollers, causing them to become stuck. To clean them, you can use a damp cloth and some mild detergent. This should help to loosen any dirt and debris that may be causing the issue.

Replace the Rollers

If cleaning the tracks and rollers does not help, then you should consider replacing them. Over time, the rollers can become worn and may need to be replaced. You can purchase new rollers from most hardware stores. If you do not feel comfortable replacing the rollers yourself, you can call a professional.

Check the Springs

The next step is to check the springs. If the springs are not functioning properly, it can cause the garage door to get stuck. If you are not sure how to inspect the springs, you should call a professional to come and take a look. A professional will be able to tell if the springs need to be replaced or adjusted.

Adjust the Limiters

If the springs are functioning properly, then you should adjust the limiters. The limiters control the speed and force of the garage door. If they are not set properly, it can cause the garage door to get stuck. To adjust the limiters, you should consult the manufacturer's instructions. If you are not sure how to adjust the limiters, you should call a professional.

Conclusion

Garage doors can be a real hassle when they get stuck. If your garage door is stuck, you should check the tracks and rollers, examine the garage door opener, clean the tracks and rollers, replace the rollers, check the springs, and adjust the limiters. If none of these steps work, you may need to call a professional for help. With these tips, you will be able to fix a garage door that gets stuck quickly and easily.
