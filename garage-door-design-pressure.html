What is Garage Door Design Pressure?

Garage door design pressure is the amount of force required to open and close a garage door. This pressure is measured in pounds per square inch (psi). The higher the psi rating, the more force is needed to open and close the door. It is important to note that the most secure garage door designs will require a higher psi rating in order to be effective.

Why is Garage Door Design Pressure Important?

Garage door design pressure is important for many reasons. First, it helps to ensure that the garage door is secure and that it will remain closed when not in use. This can be especially important if the garage is used to store valuable items or to protect them from the elements. Additionally, a higher psi rating can help to make the garage door more efficient and reduce energy costs.

What Factors Affect Garage Door Design Pressure?

There are several factors that can affect the overall design pressure of a garage door. These include the type of material used in the construction of the door, the size of the door, the weight of the door, and the overall design of the door. It is important to note that the heavier the door, the more force will be needed to open and close it. Additionally, the materials used in the construction of the door can also affect the design pressure. For example, steel doors tend to require a higher psi rating than wooden doors.
