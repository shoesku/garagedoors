Easy Guide To Adjust Your Craftsman Garage Door Height

If you want to make sure that your Craftsman garage door is working properly, you'll need to adjust the door height. This is an easy task that can be done in a few simple steps. So, if you're ready to get started, here's how you can adjust the height of your Craftsman garage door.

Step 1: Measure the Current Height of Your Garage Door

Before you can adjust the height of your garage door, you'll need to measure it. Using a tape measure, measure the distance between the bottom of the door and the floor. This will help you to determine how much you need to adjust the height.

Step 2: Adjust the Door Height

Once you have measured the current height of your garage door, you can start adjusting it. To do this, you'll need to use a Phillips head screwdriver. Locate the adjustment screws on the sides of your garage door, and then turn them clockwise to raise the door, or counterclockwise to lower it. Make sure to turn the screws an equal amount on both sides.

Step 3: Test the Door

Once you've adjusted the height of your garage door, it's important to test it to make sure it is working properly. Open and close the door several times, and make sure that it is opening and closing smoothly. If it isn't, you may need to make further adjustments.

Step 4: Repeat as Necessary

If the door isn't opening and closing properly after the first adjustment, you may need to make further adjustments. Continue to make small adjustments until the door is operating smoothly. If you find that the door is still not operating properly, you may need to consult a professional.

Tips for Adjusting Your Garage Door Height

Make Small Adjustments

When adjusting the height of your garage door, it's important to make small adjustments. Turning the screws too much can cause the door to become too high or too low, so try to limit each adjustment to a quarter of a turn.

Check the Alignment of the Tracks

In addition to adjusting the height of your garage door, it's important to make sure that the tracks are properly aligned. If the tracks are misaligned, it can cause the door to open and close unevenly. Check the alignment of the tracks periodically to ensure that the door is operating properly.

Check the Springs

If you find that the door is still not operating properly after adjusting the height and the tracks, you may need to check the springs. Over time, the springs can become weak, and this can cause the door to operate improperly. If the springs are weak, you may need to replace them.

Conclusion

Adjusting the height of your Craftsman garage door is an easy task that can be done in a few simple steps. Just make sure to measure the current height of the door, adjust the height using a Phillips head screwdriver, and then test the door to make sure it is operating properly. If you find that the door is still not operating properly, you may need to make further adjustments or consult a professional.
