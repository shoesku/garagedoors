Everything You Need To Know About Lubricating Garage Doors

If you’re a homeowner, it’s important to keep your garage door in top working condition all year round. Apart from regular maintenance, you should also make sure that you lubricate your garage door regularly. Lubricating your garage door will help to prevent it from becoming stuck, and it will also reduce the amount of noise it makes when it moves. In this article, we’ll take a look at what you need to know about lubricating your garage door in 2023.

What Kind of Lubricant Should I Use?

The most important thing to remember when it comes to lubricating your garage door is that you should always use a lubricant specifically designed for garage doors. You should avoid using WD-40, motor oil, or any other types of lubricants as they can cause damage to your garage door. There are a variety of garage door lubricants available, so make sure to pick one that is designed for the type of garage door you have.

Where Should I Apply the Lubricant?

When it comes to lubricating your garage door, you should always start by lubricating the hinges. This is because the hinges are the parts of the door that are under the most stress. Once you’ve lubricated the hinges, you should then move on to the rollers, tracks, pulleys, and springs. Make sure to avoid spraying the lubricant on the bottom of the door as this can cause damage to the seal.

How Often Should I Lubricate My Garage Door?

The frequency with which you should lubricate your garage door will depend on a few factors. The first is the type of garage door you have. If you have a wooden garage door, then you should lubricate it every three to six months. If you have a metal garage door, then you should lubricate it every six to twelve months. Additionally, you should also lubricate your garage door every time you perform maintenance on it.

Tips for Lubricating Your Garage Door

When you’re lubricating your garage door, there are a few tips you should keep in mind to ensure that you get the best results. First, make sure to use a lubricant specifically designed for garage doors. Second, make sure to avoid spraying the lubricant on the bottom of the door. Third, make sure to lubricate the hinges, rollers, tracks, pulleys, and springs. Fourth, make sure to lubricate your garage door every three to twelve months, depending on the type of garage door you have.

Conclusion

Lubricating your garage door is an important part of keeping it in top working condition. Make sure to use a lubricant specifically designed for garage doors, and make sure to lubricate the hinges, rollers, tracks, pulleys, and springs. Additionally, make sure to lubricate your garage door every three to twelve months to ensure that it remains in top working condition. Following these tips will help you to keep your garage door in top working condition for years to come.
