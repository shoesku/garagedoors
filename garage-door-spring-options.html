A Comprehensive Guide To Garage Door Spring Options

Best Garage doors and references for house or residential with custom material and more design. We Share more picture design with HD quality images.

Garage doors are an integral part of keeping your home safe and secure. They not only provide protection from unwanted intruders but also help regulate the temperature inside your home, as well as protect your car from the elements. As such, it's important to understand the various types of garage door springs available today. In this guide, we'll cover the different types of springs and the pros and cons of each.

Torsion Springs

Torsion springs are the most common type of garage door spring. They are designed to help lift the weight of the door and are wound in a counterclockwise direction. As the door opens, the spring unwinds and provides the necessary torque to open the door. Torsion springs typically last between 7-10 years, however, they can be replaced if they become worn or damaged. The downside to torsion springs is that they require more force to open and close the door, which can be difficult for some people.

Extension Springs

Extension springs are a less common type of garage door spring, but they are still widely used. They work by extending when the door is opened and contracting when the door is closed. Extension springs are designed to provide a smoother opening and closing motion than torsion springs, however, they can be more difficult to install. Extension springs typically last between 5-7 years and require replacement when they become worn or damaged.

Roller Springs

Roller springs are a newer type of garage door spring that has become increasingly popular in recent years. They are designed to be quieter and more efficient than torsion or extension springs. Roller springs work by rolling up and winding when the door is opened and rolling down and unwinding when the door is closed. They are designed to be more energy-efficient than torsion or extension springs and can last up to 10 years with proper maintenance. The downside to roller springs is that they require more force to open and close the door, which can be difficult for some people.

Galvanized Springs

Galvanized springs are a more expensive type of garage door spring but are designed to be more durable than other types of springs. They are made from galvanized steel, which is designed to resist corrosion and rust. Galvanized springs are also designed to be quieter than other types of springs and can last up to 15 years with proper maintenance. The downside to galvanized springs is that they are more expensive than other types of springs.

Springs Maintenance

Regardless of the type of garage door spring you choose, it's important to regularly inspect and maintain them. Springs should be inspected at least once a year and lubricated with a light oil or grease to prevent rust and corrosion. Additionally, the tension should be checked to ensure the door is properly balanced and the springs are not too loose or too tight.

Conclusion

Garage door springs are an essential part of any garage door system. Understanding the different types of springs available and knowing how to properly maintain them can help ensure your garage door system is functioning properly and safely. Torsion springs, extension springs, roller springs, and galvanized springs are all viable options for your garage door system. Additionally, regular maintenance and inspection will help extend the life of your garage door system.
