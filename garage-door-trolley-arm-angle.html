If you're in the market for a new garage door, you may have seen the term "trolley arm angle" and wondered what it means. Trolley arm angle is simply the angle of the arm that attaches the door to the motorized trolley that opens and closes it. In other words, it's the angle of the arm that connects the door to the motorized track system.

It's important to understand the concept of trolley arm angle if you're considering purchasing a new garage door. A trolley arm angle that is too steep can cause the door to stick or jam when it's opening or closing, while an angle that is too shallow can cause the door to be too loose or to not close properly. The ideal angle will depend on the type of door you have, as well as the size of your garage. It is important to measure the angle correctly to ensure the best possible performance from your garage door.

How to Measure the Trolley Arm Angle

Measuring the trolley arm angle is relatively simple. To do so, you will need a measuring tape, a level, and a protractor or angle finder. First, measure the distance between the center of the door and the center of the trolley arm. This distance is known as the “arm length.”

Next, place the level on the garage floor at the center of the door, then measure the angle of the arm from the level. This angle is the trolley arm angle. Depending on the type of door you have and the size of your garage, the ideal angle can range from 45 degrees to 55 degrees.

Factors That Can Affect the Trolley Arm Angle

When measuring the trolley arm angle, there are several factors that can affect the accuracy of the measurement. These include the weight of the door, the tension of the springs, and the type of track system used. It is important to take all of these factors into consideration when measuring the trolley arm angle to ensure the best possible performance from your garage door.

Benefits of the Right Trolley Arm Angle

When the trolley arm angle is correctly set, the door will be able to open and close smoothly and quietly. The door will also be better able to withstand the weight of the door, which can be especially important if you have a heavy door, such as a double-wide garage door. Setting the trolley arm angle correctly will also help to prevent the door from sticking or jamming when it is opening or closing.

Garage Door Trolley Arm Angle Maintenance

It is important to periodically check and adjust the trolley arm angle to ensure the best possible performance from your garage door. It is also important to check the tension of the springs, as this can affect the trolley arm angle as well. If you are unsure of how to adjust the trolley arm angle or check the tension of the springs, it is best to contact a professional garage door technician for assistance.

Conclusion

Understanding the concept of trolley arm angle can help you choose the right garage door for your home. Taking the time to measure the angle accurately and adjust it as needed will ensure the best possible performance from your garage door. If you need any assistance with setting the trolley arm angle or adjusting the tension of the springs, it is best to contact a professional garage door technician for assistance.
