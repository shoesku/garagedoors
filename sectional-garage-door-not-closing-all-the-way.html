Troubleshooting A Sectional Garage Door Not Closing All The Way

Best Garage doors and references for house or residential with custom material and more design. We Share more picture design with HD quality images.

Introduction

Having a sectional garage door not closing all the way can be a source of major frustration. Whether you’re trying to keep cold air from entering your home or simply want to ensure the security of your garage and the items inside, a door that doesn’t close correctly can be a major inconvenience. In this blog, we’ll discuss common causes of a sectional garage door not closing all the way and provide tips for troubleshooting this issue.

Common Causes of Malfunction

The most common causes of a sectional garage door not closing all the way are typically related to either the door’s operation or its construction. In the case of the former, issues such as worn rollers and tracks, misaligned photo eyes, or a faulty opener can be to blame. With regard to construction, damaged or misaligned panels, sagging springs, or a mis-leveled door can be the culprit.

Steps to Take

Before taking any steps to address the issue, it’s important to make sure the garage door is properly lubricated. In some cases, a lack of lubrication can be the cause of a sectional garage door not closing all the way. If lubrication does not solve the issue, there are several steps you can take to troubleshoot the problem. First, check for any obstructions in the track and ensure that the door is properly balanced. If the door is balanced and there are no obstructions, check the door’s springs, rollers, and tracks for any damage or misalignment. Finally, ensure that the photo eyes and the garage door opener are properly aligned and functioning correctly.

Potential Solutions

The specific solution for a sectional garage door not closing all the way will depend on the underlying cause. In some cases, the issue may be able to be resolved quickly and easily using basic tools such as a level and a wrench. In other cases, it may be necessary to replace items such as the springs or opener. In the event that the door must be replaced, it’s important to measure the existing opening and purchase the correct size door.

When to Call a Professional

If you’re unable to diagnose and address the issue yourself, it’s important to call a professional. A door that doesn’t close properly can be a safety hazard and should be addressed as quickly as possible. A reputable professional can quickly diagnose the issue and recommend necessary repairs or replacements. Additionally, a professional can help ensure the job is done correctly and can provide helpful tips for maintaining the door in the future.

Conclusion

Having a sectional garage door not closing all the way can be a source of major frustration. By understanding the common causes of this issue, you can quickly identify the underlying cause and make the necessary repairs or replacements. However, if you’re unable to diagnose or repair the issue yourself, it’s important to call a professional to ensure the job is done correctly and to avoid any potential safety issues.
