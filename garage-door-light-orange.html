What Does It Mean When Your Garage Door Light Is Orange?

Having a garage door is a convenient way to get in and out of your home. It’s especially useful in the winter months when you don’t want to open your door and let in the cold air. But like all home appliances, you may run into some issues with your garage door. One common issue is when the light on your garage door is orange. What does this mean and how do you fix it? In this article, we’ll answer all your questions about orange garage door lights.

What Does an Orange Garage Door Light Mean?

An orange garage door light typically means there’s a problem with the door’s safety sensors. The sensors are located at the bottom of the door and they detect when there’s something blocking the door’s path. If something is blocking the sensors, the light will turn orange to let you know there’s an issue. If the light is green, then the sensors are functioning properly.

Why is the Light on My Garage Door Orange?

The most common reason the light on your garage door is orange is because something is blocking the safety sensors. This could be anything from a pile of leaves to a large item that has been placed close to the door. If the sensors are blocked, the door will not open or close, and the light will turn orange.

How Can I Fix the Orange Light on My Garage Door?

If the light on your garage door is orange, the first step is to check the safety sensors. Make sure there’s nothing blocking the sensors. If there is, remove the obstruction and the light should turn green. If the light doesn’t turn green, then the sensors may need to be adjusted. Check the user manual for instructions on how to adjust the sensors.

What If the Light Is Still Orange After Adjusting the Sensors?

If the light is still orange after adjusting the sensors, then the sensors may need to be replaced. This is not a complicated job, but it’s best left to a professional. A professional garage door technician can diagnose the problem and replace the sensors if necessary.

How Can I Prevent the Light on My Garage Door From Turning Orange?

The best way to prevent the light on your garage door from turning orange is to regularly check the safety sensors. Make sure there’s nothing blocking them and that they’re adjusted properly. You should also check the sensors every few months to make sure they’re functioning properly. If the sensors are not functioning properly, the light will turn orange.

What Are the Benefits of Having a Garage Door with a Light?

Having a garage door with a light is beneficial for a few reasons. First, the light acts as a safety measure by letting you know when something is blocking the sensors. Second, the light is a good way to know whether or not the door is open or closed. Finally, the light can act as a deterrent for burglars, as it will let them know that someone is home or that the garage door is in use.

Conclusion

If the light on your garage door is orange, it typically means there’s a problem with the safety sensors. Check the user manual for instructions on how to adjust the sensors. If the light is still orange after adjusting the sensors, then the sensors may need to be replaced. The best way to prevent the light from turning orange is to regularly check the sensors and make sure they’re functioning properly. Having a garage door with a light is beneficial for a few reasons, so it’s important to make sure it’s working properly.
