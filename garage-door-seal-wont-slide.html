Tips To Fix A Garage Door Seal That Won't Slide Easily

Best Garage doors and references for house or residential with custom material and more design. We Share more picture design with HD quality images.

Are you having trouble with your garage door seal? Is it not sliding as smoothly as it should? It can be a real pain when you’re trying to open and close your garage door, and the seal isn’t cooperating. If this is the case, you’re in luck, as there are a few steps you can take to try and fix the issue.

In this article, we’ll be looking at a few tips for fixing a garage door seal that won’t slide easily. Read on to learn how you can get your garage door functioning properly again in no time.

Check The Track

The first step to take when trying to fix a garage door seal that won’t slide is to check the track. Sometimes debris or grime can accumulate on the track, causing the seal to stick as it moves. To check, start by inspecting the track for any visible dirt or debris. If there’s any stuck on the track, use a damp cloth to wipe it away.

You should then take a look at the track itself. If it’s bent or warped, it could be causing the seal to stick. If this is the case, you’ll need to get a professional to repair the track, as it’s not something you should attempt to do yourself.

Lubricate The Track

Once you’ve checked the track for any dirt, debris, or warping, you should lubricate it. This will help the garage door seal to slide more easily. To do this, simply apply a lubricant to the track, such as WD-40 or a silicone spray. Let the lubricant sit for a few minutes, then wipe away any excess. This should help to get the seal sliding again.

Check The Rollers

Another thing you can check to see if the garage door seal is sticking is the rollers. Sometimes, the rollers can become worn out or damaged, causing them to stick. To check if this is the case, take a look at the rollers and see if they’re cracked, worn, or otherwise damaged. If they are, you’ll need to replace them to get the seal sliding again.

Check The Spring

The next step is to check the spring. The spring is what helps to lift the garage door, and if it’s not functioning properly, it can cause the seal to stick. To check the spring, open the garage door and check if the spring is loose or broken. If it is, you’ll need to replace it to get the seal functioning properly again.

Check The Weather Stripping

Finally, you should check the weather stripping. The weather stripping is what helps to keep the seal in place, and if it’s worn or damaged, it can cause the seal to stick. To check the weather stripping, take a look at the seal and see if it’s cracked or worn. If it is, you’ll need to replace it to get the seal sliding smoothly again.

Conclusion

Garage door seals that won’t slide easily can be annoying, and it’s important to take the time to fix the issue. By following the tips outlined in this article, you should be able to get your garage door seal sliding again in no time. Be sure to check the track, lubricate the track, check the rollers, check the spring, and check the weather stripping to ensure the seal is functioning properly.
