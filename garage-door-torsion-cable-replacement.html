Replacing Your Garage Door Torsion Cable In 2023: A Step-By-Step Guide

Garage door torsion cables are an integral part of your garage door system. They are responsible for balancing the weight of the door and allowing it to be opened and closed with ease. Unfortunately, these cables can become worn over time and need to be replaced. Fortunately, replacing your torsion cable is relatively easy, and this guide will provide you with the steps necessary to do so.

Step 1: Prepare Your Work Area

Before you start, make sure to clear the area around your garage door so you have plenty of room to work. Make sure to remove any items that may get in the way of you completing the job safely. Once you have cleared the area, you should also be sure to shut off the power to your garage door opener.

Step 2: Remove the Old Cable

Once you have prepared the area, you can begin removing the old torsion cable. Start by locating the cable drum on the garage door. It is usually located at the top of the door and is a large, cylindrical object. Then, you will need to loosen the cable from the cable drum. After this, slowly begin to pull the cable from the drum. Make sure to take your time and be careful not to damage the drum.

Step 3: Measure the New Cable

Once you have removed the old cable, you will need to measure the new cable for your garage door. Start by measuring the length of the old cable and make sure to add an extra foot or two to the measurement. This will ensure that the new cable will be the right size to fit your door. Once you have measured the new cable, you can cut it to size.

Step 4: Install the New Cable

Now that you have measured and cut the new cable to size, you can start to install it. Begin by wrapping the new torsion cable around the cable drum. Make sure to wind it evenly around the drum and be sure to leave a few inches of slack at the end. Once you have wound it around the drum, you can then fasten the cable in place with the included hardware.

Step 5: Adjust the Cable Tension

Once you have installed the new cable, you will need to adjust the tension. This is an important step as it will ensure that the garage door is balanced properly. Start by lifting the door halfway and then adjusting the tension on the cable. You will need to use a wrench to do this. Once you have adjusted the tension, you can then close the door and make sure it is working properly.

Step 6: Test the Garage Door

Once you have finished replacing the torsion cable, you can then test the garage door. Start by opening and closing the door a few times to make sure that it is working properly. If the door is not opening or closing properly, you may need to adjust the tension on the cable once again. Once you have tested the door, you can then turn the power back on to the garage door opener.

Conclusion

Replacing a torsion cable in your garage door is relatively easy and can be done by anyone with basic DIY skills. All it takes is a little patience and the right tools, and you can have your garage door working like new in no time. Just remember to follow the steps outlined in this guide and you should have no problems replacing your torsion cable.
