How To Fix A Garage Door That Won't Open

Best Garage doors and references for house or residential with custom material and more design. We Share more picture design with HD quality images.

A garage door that won't open can be a major inconvenience and a great source of stress. Not being able to access your garage can be a real problem, especially if you need to get out for work or errands. Fortunately, you don't have to spend a lot of money to fix it. There are some simple solutions that you can try to get your garage door working again.

Check your remote and wall switch

The first thing to do is to check the remote and the wall switch. Make sure that the batteries in the remote are working, and if they are, try to open the door manually. If the wall switch is not working, it could be due to a power outage, a blown fuse, or a bad switch. If this is the case, you may need to call a professional electrician to fix the issue.

Check the sensors

If the wall switch and the remote are working, then you need to check the sensors. The sensors are the photo eyes that are located near the bottom of the garage door on both sides. These are safety devices that make sure that the garage door does not close when there is something in the way. If the sensors are not working correctly, then the door will not open. Check the wiring and make sure that there is no debris blocking the sensors. You may need to clean the sensors to make sure that they are working properly.

Check the tracks and rollers

If the remote and wall switch are working, and the sensors are not the issue, then you need to check the tracks and rollers. Make sure that there is no debris blocking the tracks and that the rollers are not damaged. If there is any damage, you may need to replace the rollers. If the tracks are not aligned properly, you may need to use a level to make sure that they are aligned correctly.

Check the springs

The springs are what help to lift the garage door. If the springs are not working properly, then the door will not open. Check the springs for any signs of damage and make sure that they are properly lubricated. If the springs are damaged, you may need to replace them.

Check the motor

If all of the above steps have been completed and the garage door still won't open, then you need to check the motor. Make sure that the motor is plugged in and that it is receiving power. If the motor is not working, it could be due to a bad motor or a bad circuit board. You may need to call a professional to repair the motor.

Check the cables and pulleys

If the motor is working, then you need to check the cables and pulleys. Make sure that the cables are not frayed or broken and that the pulleys are not damaged. If the cables or pulleys are damaged, you may need to replace them.

Call a professional

If all of the steps above have been completed and the garage door still won't open, then it may be time to call a professional. A professional can inspect the garage door and determine what the issue is and what needs to be done to fix it. They have the tools and the expertise to get your garage door working again quickly and safely.

Conclusion

A garage door that won't open can be a major inconvenience and a great source of stress. Fortunately, there are some simple solutions that you can try to get your garage door working again. Check the remote and wall switch, the sensors, the tracks and rollers, the springs, the motor, and the cables and pulleys. If all of these steps have been completed and the garage door still won't open, then it may be time to call a professional.
