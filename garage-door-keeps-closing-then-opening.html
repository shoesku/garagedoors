Troubleshooting Your Garage Door Keeps Closing Then Opening

Best Garage doors and references for house or residential with custom material and more design. We Share more picture design with HD quality images.

If your garage door opens and closes on its own, it can be an annoying and potentially dangerous issue. This problem is usually caused by a faulty opener, or a misaligned door track. Fortunately, the fix is usually straightforward and can be done without the help of a professional. In this article, we’ll explain why your garage door keeps opening and closing, and how to fix the problem. Read on for tips and troubleshooting advice.

1. Check Your Garage Door Opener

The first thing you should do if your garage door keeps opening and closing is check the opener. If the opener is not functioning properly, your door may be opening and closing randomly. Make sure the opener is plugged in and that all connections are secure. If it’s not, replace the opener or contact a professional to do the job.

2. Check the Sensors

Your garage door opener has two sensors located on either side of the door. These sensors detect any objects that may be blocking the door and prevent it from closing. If the sensors are misaligned, the door will not close properly. To fix this issue, you need to adjust the sensors so that they are pointing directly at one another. If the sensors are not the issue, you may need to replace them.

3. Check the Door Track

If the sensors and opener are working properly, the next step is to check the door track. If the track is bent or misaligned, the door may not be able to close properly. To check the track, you should look for any gaps or bends in the rail. If you find any, you can try to realign the track using a hammer or pry bar. If the track is severely damaged, you may need to hire a professional to replace it.

4. Check the Springs

Your garage door is held in place by two springs, which are located on either side of the door. If the springs are worn out or damaged, they may be unable to hold the door in place. This can cause the door to open and close randomly. To check the springs, you should look for any signs of wear or damage. If you find any, you can either replace the springs yourself or contact a professional for help.

5. Check the Cables

If the springs and tracks are in good condition, the next step is to check the cables. The cables are responsible for lifting and lowering the door. If the cables are frayed or broken, they won’t be able to lift the door properly. To check the cables, you should look for any signs of wear or damage. If the cables are worn out, you should replace them as soon as possible.

6. Check the Rollers

If the cables and springs are in good condition, the next step is to check the rollers. The rollers are responsible for smoothly opening and closing the door. If the rollers are worn out, they may not be able to open and close the door properly. To check the rollers, you should look for any signs of wear or damage. If the rollers are worn out, you should replace them as soon as possible.

7. Check the Weather Stripping

If the rollers and cables are in good condition, the next step is to check the weather stripping. The weather stripping is responsible for keeping the door sealed against the elements. If the weather stripping is worn out, it may not be able to keep the door sealed properly. To check the weather stripping, you should look for any signs of wear or damage. If the weather stripping is worn out, you should replace it as soon as possible.

8. Check the Lubrication

If the weather stripping and rollers are in good condition, the next step is to check the lubrication. The lubrication is responsible for keeping the door moving smoothly. If the lubrication is not properly applied, the door may not be able to open or close properly. To check the lubrication, you should look for any signs of wear or damage. If the lubrication is worn out, you should reapply it as soon as possible.

Conclusion

If your garage door keeps opening and closing, there are several potential causes. The most common cause is a faulty opener or misaligned door track. To fix the issue, you should check the opener, sensors, track, springs, cables, rollers, weather stripping, and lubrication. If any of these components are worn out or damaged, you should replace or repair them as soon as possible. With the right troubleshooting steps, you should be able to fix the issue quickly and easily.
